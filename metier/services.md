# Metier Services — Data Center Context

This file describes Metier's service offerings relevant to data center operators. It serves as the "product knowledge" the agent uses when mapping operator pain points to Metier services.

**Source:** [Metier service catalog on Notion](https://www.notion.so/2ab64f448283809ebc32c13eab316b0a)

---

## 1. Contract, Procurement & Equipment Planning

**What it is:** Advisory on contract strategy (EPC, EPCM, CM, design-build), procurement planning, tender processes, supplier qualification, and equipment specification for large capital projects.

**DC-specific framing:** Data center construction involves specialized MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing) packages, long-lead equipment (generators, transformers, cooling systems, UPS), and complex interfaces between civil, electrical, and IT infrastructure. Operators without construction DNA often underestimate the importance of contract packaging — how you split scopes between contractors determines your risk exposure, schedule, and cost.

**Typical client need:**
- First-time DC developers who need to select and manage EPC/EPCM contractors
- Operators planning multiple simultaneous builds who need a scalable procurement framework
- High-security operators who need contractors with security clearance (sikkerhetsklarering)

**Relevance signals:**
- Rapid expansion plans or aggressive MW targets
- PE-backed with tight timelines (need to get procurement right the first time)
- No visible procurement/contracts team in the org chart
- High-security segment requiring cleared contractors and suppliers
- Multiple planned sites requiring standardized procurement processes

## 2. Project Execution & Project Management

**What it is:** Hands-on project management for capital projects — project directors, project managers, cost controllers, progress planners, ITB coordinators. Metier has quality-assured projects worth over 800 billion NOK.

**DC-specific framing:** Data center construction projects are fast-paced (12-18 months typical for a shell), have tight commissioning windows, and involve parallel workstreams (civil, power, cooling, IT fit-out). Metier provides the project management backbone — from a single project manager to a full PMO — so operators can focus on commercial and technical decisions.

**Typical client need:**
- Operators with ambitious build programs but no internal PM capability
- Companies scaling from 1 site to multiple sites needing PM frameworks
- Operators experiencing schedule or cost overruns

**Relevance signals:**
- Hiring for VP Construction but not for project managers or planners
- Announced timelines that seem aggressive relative to team size
- Multiple sites under development simultaneously
- No visible project controls function (cost control, scheduling)

## 3. Early-Phase Advisory (Concept Selection & Project Optimization)

**What it is:** Front-end advisory using Metier's proprietary frameworks — Verdistyrt Prosjektutvikling (VPU / Target Value Design), concept selection, Pre-FEED/FEED support, PDRI assessments. Helps owners make the right investment decisions before committing capital.

**DC-specific framing:** Site selection, power capacity planning, phasing strategy, and technology choices (air cooling vs. liquid cooling, tier level, redundancy architecture) are consequential decisions made early. Getting these wrong is expensive. Metier helps operators optimize their concept before breaking ground.

**Typical client need:**
- Operators in early planning stages evaluating sites or technologies
- Companies needing to justify investment decisions to boards or PE investors
- Operators choosing between phased development strategies

**Relevance signals:**
- Company is pre-construction or in feasibility phase
- Multiple potential sites being evaluated
- Board or investor pressure for rigorous decision-making frameworks
- Publicly announced plans but no visible construction activity yet

## 4. Owner's Engineer / PMC (Project Management Consultancy)

**What it is:** Acting as the owner's technical representative — reviewing contractor deliverables, managing interfaces, ensuring quality, representing the owner's interests during design and construction.

**DC-specific framing:** Many DC operators are commercial or financial entities (PE funds, real estate developers, energy companies) entering data centers for the first time. They need a trusted technical advisor who can represent their interests when dealing with EPC contractors, equipment suppliers, and design consultants.

**Typical client need:**
- Financial investors or non-technical owners building their first DC
- Operators who want to maintain a lean internal team but need oversight
- Companies working with international EPC contractors unfamiliar with Norwegian regulations

**Relevance signals:**
- Ownership by PE/financial investors without construction expertise
- Very small internal team relative to project size
- Using international contractors who need local oversight
- "Asset-light" business model (outsource everything, need someone to manage it)

## 5. Portfolio & Project Owner Governance

**What it is:** Setting up governance frameworks, stage-gate processes, reporting structures, and decision-making models for organizations managing multiple projects or a project portfolio.

**DC-specific framing:** Operators building multiple data centers across different sites need consistent governance — standardized reporting, stage-gate decisions, risk management frameworks. This is especially important for operators backed by institutional investors who require portfolio-level visibility.

**Typical client need:**
- Multi-site operators needing consistent project governance
- Companies transitioning from single-project to portfolio management
- Organizations where the board or investors require structured reporting

**Relevance signals:**
- Multiple sites in development or pipeline
- Institutional/PE investors requiring portfolio reporting
- Rapid organizational growth creating governance gaps
- History of project issues (delays, cost overruns) suggesting governance gaps

## 6. Strategy & Business Development Advisory

**What it is:** Strategic advisory including market analysis, competitive positioning, organizational design, and go-to-market strategy. Metier combines strategic competence with execution capability.

**DC-specific framing:** The Norwegian DC market is evolving rapidly — new entrants, changing regulations, AI-driven demand shifts. Operators need to position themselves clearly (hyperscale vs. colocation vs. high-security vs. edge) and build organizations that can deliver on their strategy.

**Typical client need:**
- New entrants defining their market position
- Established operators pivoting strategy (e.g., from colocation to AI/HPC)
- Companies needing organizational design to match their growth ambitions

**Relevance signals:**
- Unclear or broad market positioning ("we do everything")
- Rapid hiring suggesting organizational design hasn't been formalized
- New market entrants without DC industry experience
- Strategy announcements that don't match visible capabilities

## 7. Competence Development & Training

**What it is:** Metier Academy — the Nordics' largest project school. Customized corporate training, PRINCE2/PMI certifications, leadership development, project onboarding programs. 16,000 annual participants.

**DC-specific framing:** Fast-growing DC operators hire rapidly but often lack structured onboarding and competence development. Metier can build project management capability internally — from individual certifications to full corporate training programs — reducing dependency on external consultants over time.

**Typical client need:**
- Operators hiring large teams quickly who need structured onboarding
- Companies wanting to build internal PM capability rather than always outsourcing
- Organizations where project delivery quality varies across teams

**Relevance signals:**
- Rapid hiring (10+ project-related roles open)
- Mix of experienced and junior hires
- No visible internal training or development programs
- Desire to reduce consultant dependency over time

---

## 8. Digital Initiatives & Technology Advisory

**What it is:** IT architecture, solution architecture, enterprise architecture, agile coaching, product development, data science, change management for digital transformations.

**DC-specific framing:** Less directly relevant to physical DC construction, but applicable for operators building digital platforms (customer portals, capacity management systems, DCIM), or for operators whose parent companies are undergoing digital transformations.

**Typical client need:**
- Operators building custom DCIM or customer-facing platforms
- Companies implementing BIM for DC design and construction
- Digital transformation of operational processes

**Relevance signals:**
- Hiring for software/IT roles alongside construction roles
- Building proprietary technology platforms
- Parent company undergoing digital transformation
