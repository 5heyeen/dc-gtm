# Research Breakdown: IPD/Samspill Feasibility for Hospital Megaprojects (~20B NOK, >100,000 m²)

**Scope:** Is an IPD / "samspill" contract model feasible for hospital projects larger than 100,000 m² (~20B NOK), and if so what contract model structure would make it workable?
**Project:** Bedre Megaprosjekter — Metier AS
**Date:** 2026-04-25

**Output goal:** Evidence landscape — let the client draw conclusions
**Key risk to flag throughout:** International comparators may not translate directly to Norwegian context (legal, cultural, market differences)
**Special instruction:** Prioritise reference hospital megaprojects delivered on or below budget and schedule — these are the proof points.

---

### Task 01: Norwegian Hospital Megaproject Track Record
Research the major Norwegian hospital megaprojects delivered under traditional contract models: Ahus (Akershus universitetssykehus), OUS (Oslo universitetssykehus), Nye Rikshospitalet, and Sykehuset Innlandet Mjøssykehuset. For each project: planned vs. final budget, planned vs. actual schedule, contract model used, identified causes of overrun, and any post-project reviews or Riksrevisjonen findings. This task establishes the "problem to solve" baseline — what has gone wrong and why in the Norwegian hospital megaproject context. Search Norwegian sources (Riksrevisjonen, Statsforvalteren, NRK/Aftenposten, Sykehusbygg, Helse- og omsorgsdepartementet). Task is complete when each project has a documented cost outcome and a cited cause of overrun.

### Task 02: Norwegian Samspill Experience and Scale
Research Norwegian "samspill" (collaborative/relational contracting) practice: who has used it, at what project scale (NOK value and m²), and with what documented outcomes. Focus on Statsbygg, Sykehusbygg HF, Bane NOR, and Nye Veier. Search for any Norwegian hospital projects that have used samspill or a samspill-adjacent model (early contractor involvement, partneringavtaler, etc.). Also search Concept research programme (NTNU), Standard Norge, and Prosjekt Norge for guidance documents or research on samspill. Document the largest samspill contract by value in Norway and note whether any reached the 5B NOK+ range. Search Norwegian-language sources: "samspillskontrakt", "samspillsmodell", "relasjonell kontrahering", "partneringavtale sykehus". Task is complete when the upper bound of demonstrated Norwegian samspill scale is established with citations.

### Task 03: Global Hospital Megaprojects — On/Below Budget Reference Cases
Identify and document hospital megaprojects globally (>100,000 m² or budget >$1B USD / ~10B NOK) that were delivered on or below budget and/or ahead of schedule. For each: project name, country, size, budget, actual cost, contract model used, and key success factors. Priority targets: Karolinska University Hospital Nya (Sweden — notable for cost overruns, PPP issues), QEUH (Queen Elizabeth University Hospital, Glasgow, UK — NEC3 / traditional), Bispebjerg Hospital and Nyt OUH (Odense University Hospital, Denmark — large samspill/alliance elements), and any Australian health infrastructure projects. Also search for US IPD hospital examples (Sutter Health, UC Health). Task is complete when at least 3–5 megaproject case studies are documented with budget outcome and contract model.

### Task 04: IPD and Alliance Contract Models at Large Scale — International Evidence
Review academic literature, government reports, and industry publications on IPD, project alliance, and multi-party collaborative contracting at large scale (>$500M / >5B NOK). What does the evidence say about: (a) whether IPD has been successfully used at this scale; (b) what model variants have been used — "IPD-lite", multi-party alliance, progressive design-build, NEC4 Alliance Contract, two-stage open book, ECI (Early Contractor Involvement); (c) what structural conditions make it work at scale. Key sources: Lean Construction Institute, AIA (American Institute of Architects) IPD guidance, Australian Alliance Contracting (Department of Infrastructure, Infrastructure Australia), UK Cabinet Office / NEC Users Group, academic journals (IJPM, Construction Management & Economics), KPMG / Deloitte infrastructure advisory reports. Task is complete when each major model variant has a documented definition, scale range, and at least one cited case.

### Task 05: Norwegian Legal and Procurement Constraints for Multi-Party IPD
Assess whether Norwegian public procurement law and EEA/EU procurement rules permit multi-party IPD or full-alliance structures for public hospital projects. Research: Lov om offentlige anskaffelser (LOA), Forskrift om offentlige anskaffelser (FOA), relevant EEA directives (2014/24/EU), and how these interact with integrated multi-party contracts (single contract spanning design + construction + key trade contractors). What procurement procedures could accommodate IPD: competitive dialogue (konkurransepreget dialog), innovation partnership (innovasjonspartnerskap), restricted procedure with BAFO? Have Statsbygg, Sykehusbygg, or Bane NOR published any guidance or legal opinions on this? Search DFØ (Direktoratet for forvaltning og økonomi), Anskaffelser.no, and any published Sykehusbygg HF contract strategy documents. Task is complete when the legal permissibility of multi-party IPD for Norwegian public hospitals is assessed with cited sources, and the most viable procurement procedure identified.

### Task 06: Structural Provisions Enabling Megaproject-Scale IPD
Research the specific contract provisions and governance structures that make IPD or alliance models workable at megaproject scale (>$500M). Cover: (a) pain/gain sharing mechanisms — typical cap structures (e.g., ±10–15% of target cost), how caps scale with project size, whether the risk pool remains meaningful at 20B NOK; (b) big room / integrated operations — how co-location and joint decision-making governance is structured at large scale (sub-teams, cluster alliances); (c) target cost setting — how target costs are negotiated and validated when scope complexity is very high; (d) integrated insurance / liability waivers — whether Integrated Project Insurance (IPI) or similar is available in European/Norwegian context; (e) how many parties can realistically join a multi-party agreement and maintain alignment. Key sources: AIA IPD Agreements, ConsensusDocs 300, NEC4 Alliance Contract guidance, Infrastructure Australia Alliance Contracting guidance, academic work by Martin Fischer (Stanford CIFE), Glenn Ballard, and Yosef Shaked. Task is complete when each of the five structural elements above is addressed with cited evidence on scalability.
