# Research Scope

**Scope:** Is an IPD (Integrated Project Delivery) / "samspill" contract model feasible for hospital projects larger than 100,000 m² with a budget of approximately 20 billion NOK, and if so what contract model structure would make it workable?

**Project:** Bedre Megaprosjekter (standalone — no Notion project match, Notion tools not available in this environment)

**Date:** 2026-04-25

## Full Research Brief

This research is for Metier AS, Norway's largest project consultancy (owned by Tetra Tech), which advises on large physical infrastructure projects including hospitals. Metier has worked on projects like Nye Rikshospitalet. The "Bedre megaprosjekter" (Better Megaprojects) initiative is relevant context — Norway has a track record of hospital megaprojects delivered under traditional contracts (Ahus, OUS, Nye Rikshospitalet, Sykehuset Innlandet Mjøssykehuset) with cost overruns and complexity challenges.

## Key Questions

1. What is the current evidence base for IPD / samspill models at megaproject scale (>100,000 m², >10B NOK / ~$1B USD)?
2. Have any hospital projects of this size globally been delivered under IPD or IPD-like (alliance, samspill) models? What were the outcomes?
3. What are the structural challenges of applying IPD to a project this large (complexity, number of parties, risk-sharing mechanism size)?
4. What contract model variants make large-scale IPD workable — e.g., "lite" IPD, project alliance, multi-party IPD, Progressive Design-Build with collaborative provisions?
5. What does Norwegian "samspill" (collaborative contracting) experience tell us — what scale has it been used at in Norway, and are there hospital examples?
6. Are there specific contract provisions or structural elements (pain/gain sharing caps, big room governance, co-location, integrated insurance, target cost) that enable IPD at this scale?
7. What are the key risks and preconditions for IPD to work at megaproject scale?
