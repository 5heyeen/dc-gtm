## ⚡ KEY FINDING UPFRONT

**"EYP Architecture+Engineering" ≠ "EYP Mission Critical Facilities"**

The entity acquired by Ramboll is **EYP Mission Critical Facilities (EYP MCF)** — a US data center specialist consultancy headquartered in Valhalla, NY. This is distinct from the broader EYP Architecture+Engineering brand. EYP MCF was spun out of EYP A+E's parent company (Einhorn Yaffee Prescott), acquired by HP in 2007, spun back out in a 2018 MBO, backed by TA Associates PE in May 2021, then acquired by Ramboll on January 31, 2022. There is no single published "playbook" document — the methodology is described across service pages, thought leadership, and project references.

## Section: Acquisition Intelligence

### When did Ramboll acquire EYP MCF, and what were the terms?

**Close date:** January 31, 2022. Financial terms: undisclosed.

**Revenue target disclosed:** USD 60 million combined data center consulting revenue by 2025.

**Size:** EYP MCF had ~50 professionals; Ramboll's pre-existing DC practice had ~50, bringing combined headcount to ~100 dedicated data center specialists.

**Corporate history of EYP MCF:**
- 1996: Founded within Einhorn Yaffee Prescott Architecture and Engineering
- 2007: Acquired by HP (to bundle DC design with hardware sales)
- 2018: MBO by eight former EYP principals (Rick Einhorn, Brian Whelan, et al.) from Hewlett Packard Enterprise
- May 2021: TA Associates PE investment (~two-thirds stake)
- January 31, 2022: Acquired by Ramboll Group A/S

**Ramboll's stated rationale (COO Michael Simmelsgaard):** "Two huge trends are shaping our world today: the climate crisis and the digitalization of society... data centers have a critical role to play in supporting a green circular economy... our role is to ensure that the facilities we design, build and operate on behalf of our clients are at the cutting edge of environmental performance."

**EYP MCF's stated rationale (Managing Partner Rick Einhorn):** "Joining Ramboll is a great opportunity to expand our consulting and engineering services to our existing global client base while bringing our technical expertise into Ramboll's existing and future clients across 35 countries around the world."

### Post-acquisition brand and integration

EYP was NOT fully rebranded under Ramboll. It operates as **"EYP Mission Critical Facilities, Part of Ramboll"** with a separate website at eypmcfinc.com. This dual-brand approach is the textbook correct A/E M&A strategy: preserving client relationships and key personnel incentives while building Ramboll brand association.

Ramboll also hosts a data center services page at ramboll.com/en-us/technology/data-centers-mission-critical-facilities and a thought leadership hub at ramboll.com/transforming-the-future-of-data-centres.

**Follow-on acquisition (September 2024):** Ramboll acquired **i3 Solutions Group** (UK/Singapore, founded by Ed Ansett) — a data center sustainability consultancy with European and Asian market focus. This directly addressed EYP MCF's US-centric geographic gap. The combined Ramboll data center practice now claims "hundreds of dedicated data centre design experts globally."

Sources: [eypmcfinc.com](https://www.eypmcfinc.com/), [Ramboll DC page](https://www.ramboll.com/en-us/technology/data-centers-mission-critical-facilities), [Ramboll acquires i3 — DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/ramboll-acquires-data-center-consulting-firm-i3-solutions/)

## Section: The EYP "Playbook" — What It Actually Is

### There is no single published "playbook" document

EYP MCF does not publish a document titled "playbook." The methodology is described across service pages, press releases, and conference presentations. The term "EYP playbook" used in industry discussions refers to their proprietary delivery approach, which consists of:

### 1. Hybrid IT and Adaptive Stack Strategic Advisory Framework

The entry-point, upstream-of-design service. EYP advises clients on the optimal strategy across technology, facilities, and operations — answering "should you build, lease, colo, or cloud?" before engineering begins.

Named methodology: **Sourcing and Demand Analysis** — "a powerful analysis methodology to create a set of comparative solutions for a multiyear data center sourcing solution, allowing for the collaborative creation of a strategy that uses the best alternatives for managing your application portfolio and investing in the right options over time."

Source: [DCD — EYP MCF new service delivery model](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/eyp-mission-critical-facilities-adds-new-service-delivery-model-focused-on-strategic-technology-planning/)

### 2. End-to-End Engineering Design

Site selection → concept design → detailed engineering (structural, MEP, power, cooling) → construction documentation → procurement support → construction administration. Described as "maintenance-friendly, scalable and energy-efficient design" customized per project. Tier III and Tier IV design experience. Claimed: "first Tier IV facilities in the US, Saudi Arabia, and China."

Cooling architectures used: adiabatic, chilled water, evaporative, liquid immersion, rear-door heat exchangers. CFD thermal modeling as standard.

Scale benchmarks: 100+ MW of power capacity designed; 700,000+ sq ft of raised floor.

### 3. Probability Risk Analysis (PRA)

Named methodology for site auditing and existing infrastructure assessment. Determines condition of electrical and mechanical infrastructure, directing limited capital toward areas of greatest benefit. Used for Operational and Reliability Risk Assessments covering: reliability analysis, operational risk analysis, basic capacity assessment, infrastructure condition analysis, policies/procedures development, staff training.

Source: [eypmcfinc.com About](https://www.eypmcfinc.com/about)

### 4. Modular / Prefabricated Deployment Capability

- "Massively modular" 700,000 sq ft colocation facility in Dallas — highly standardized elements
- 45 containerized units (30 EcoPODs + 15 Water Cooled 40' PODs) deployed in 6 months for 130,000 servers
- 34 modular units using liquid immersion cooling for data mining / high-density use case

### 5. IUE Metric (Co-Developed with The Green Grid/TGGC)

Yigit Bulut (EYP MCF) co-developed the **IUE (Infrastructure Usage Effectiveness)** metric — extends PUE to measure power delivery, cooling generation, cooling flow, and rack capacity utilization. Industry-standard metric contribution, not just thought leadership.
Source: [IUE White Paper — DCD](https://media.datacenterdynamics.com/media/documents/IUE_White_Paper-Final.pdf)

### 6. Sustainability Framework (Net-Zero Roadmap — led by i3/Ramboll)

Published September 2025 at Climate Week NYC: "Developing Sustainable Data Centres: A Strategic Roadmap to Achieve Net Zero Carbon and Reduce Environmental Impact" — first-of-its-kind roadmap by Ed Ansett (Innovation Director, Ramboll / former i3 founder). Key benchmarks: operational net zero achievable, zero waste/landfill, water neutrality achievable.
Source: [Ramboll news](https://www.ramboll.com/en-us/news/nycw-2025-developing-sustainable-data-centers)

## Section: Competitive Differentiation vs. Generalist Firms

### Most Differentiated Elements (vs. Arup, WSP, Arcadis)

1. **Integrated strategy-to-commissioning scope under a single specialist brand** — rare in firms of EYP MCF's size
2. **Sourcing and Demand Analysis methodology** — proprietary strategic advisory, upstream of engineering design
3. **Probability Risk Analysis (PRA)** for existing infrastructure — quantitative risk methodology not common in design firms
4. **IUE metric co-development** — practitioner-level influence on industry measurement standards
5. **Modular/containerized deployment expertise** — documented at scale (45 units, 700K sq ft)
6. **HPC/supercomputing track record** — directly relevant to AI campuses

### What Competitors Would Argue

- **Arup:** Deeper multi-disciplinary technical integration, longer mission-critical history, global reach, internal R&D
- **WSP:** Larger global DC practice, more hyperscaler relationships, multi-continent scale
- **Arcadis:** Stronger owner's representative / program management capability, cost-control expertise
- **All three:** Ramboll/EYP is geographically US-centric; European DC credentials are thin; combined European practice (with i3) is still developing

## Section: Gaps and Weaknesses (Competitive Intelligence for Metier)

### Gap 1: European/Nordic Market Depth — Directly Relevant to Metier

EYP MCF's 25+ year track record is almost entirely North American. As of 2025, the combined Ramboll/EYP/i3 practice has no confirmed European data center project references under the combined brand. In the Norwegian and Nordic market, Ramboll/EYP's data center credentials are developing, not established.

**Implication for Metier:** Metier's local Norwegian presence, regulatory knowledge, and operator relationships represent a durable advantage over Ramboll/EYP in the near term.

### Gap 2: AI / Liquid Cooling Technical Framework

No published white paper or methodology for AI data center design (80-150 kW/rack, direct liquid cooling) from EYP MCF specifically. The Texas campus win (2025) shows market engagement but published technical frameworks for this segment are absent.

**Implication for Metier:** Metier could position as more current on Norwegian AI infrastructure context (cold climate free cooling, fjord cooling, Norwegian renewable energy grid).

### Gap 3: Complementary Roles, Not Direct Competition

Metier is a **project management / owner's advisory firm**. EYP MCF is primarily an **engineering design and technical advisory firm**. These are adjacent, not directly competing, roles. The primary risk is if Ramboll/EYP expands into owner's representative or program management services in the Norwegian market — which is not their current positioning.

**Most productive framing:** Metier as the owner's PM advisor; EYP MCF/Ramboll as a potential engineering design partner. Competitive threat primarily in strategic advisory overlap (EYP MCF's Hybrid IT/Adaptive Stack framework vs. Metier's project advisory mandate).

### Gap 4: Sustainability Thought Leadership is i3-Dependent

Ramboll's sustainability content (net-zero roadmap, GHG abatement work) is primarily attributable to Ed Ansett (i3/Ramboll), not to EYP MCF's native capability. If Metier has Norwegian-specific sustainability expertise (Norwegian energy system, sector coupling, fjord cooling, Norwegian permitting), this is an area to differentiate.
