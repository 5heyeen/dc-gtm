# Prompt 05: core-components-dc-design-playbook

> **Prompt:** What are the core components of a data center design playbook — covering site selection, concept design, detailed engineering (structural, MEP, power, cooling), sustainability frameworks, commissioning, and handover?

---

A comprehensive data center design playbook covers the full asset lifecycle from pre-investment through operations:

**Site Selection and Feasibility**
Power availability assessment (utility feeds, substation capacity, transmission constraints), land area and geometry for required IT load and expansion, fiber connectivity (carrier diversity), water availability for cooling, natural hazard assessment (flood zone, seismic, wind), planning and permitting risk, labor market assessment, and financial modeling of total cost of ownership by site scenario.

**Concept Design**
Campus layout, building configuration (single-story vs. multi-story), IT room module sizing, power architecture (utility voltage, number of feeds, transformer strategy, generator strategy), cooling architecture (air-side economization viability by climate zone, chilled water vs. DX, free cooling hours modeling), PUE target, tier/redundancy level, sustainability targets, and basis of design document.

**Detailed Engineering — Structural**
Floor loading for high-density deployments (AI clusters at 100+ kW/rack require structural assessment), raised floor vs. slab-on-grade, seismic design category, roof loading for cooling plant, column grid optimization for IT room layout.

**Detailed Engineering — MEP and Power**
HV/MV switchgear, transformers, UPS topology (centralized vs. distributed, online double-conversion vs. modular), battery or flywheel backup, PDU and busway design, generator sizing and fuel system, automatic transfer switching, power monitoring and DCIM integration.

**Detailed Engineering — Cooling**
Chiller plant or direct-free-cooling design, cooling tower or dry cooler selection, CRAH/CRAC vs. in-row cooling, hot aisle/cold aisle containment, rear-door heat exchangers, direct liquid cooling (DLC) for high-density, thermal modeling and CFD validation.

**Sustainability**
PUE and WUE targets, carbon intensity of power supply, renewable energy procurement strategy (PPAs, on-site generation), embodied carbon assessment, waste heat recovery options, LEED/BREEAM/DGNB certification pathway.

**Commissioning**
Level 1–5 commissioning plan (component, assembly, integrated, seasonal, operations), loadbank test matrices, integrated systems testing scripts, acceptance criteria, and issue tracking.

**Handover and Operations Support**
As-built documentation, O&M manual standards, CMMS integration, staff training requirements, and post-occupancy performance review protocol.
