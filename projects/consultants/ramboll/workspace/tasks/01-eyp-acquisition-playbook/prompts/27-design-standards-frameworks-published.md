# Prompt 27: design-standards-frameworks-published

> **Prompt:** What specific design standards or internal frameworks has EYP/Ramboll published for data center design (power density targets, cooling architecture preferences, modular build approaches)?

---

## Scale and Track Record Benchmarks (Published)

- Designed data centers with over **100 MW of power capacity** and over **700,000 sq ft of raised floor**
- Designed numerous **Tier III and Tier IV** (Uptime Institute) data centers
- Claim to have designed **"the first Tier IV facilities in the US, Saudi Arabia, and China"**
- **20+ LEED-certified** data center projects across North America, Latin America, Europe, and Asia
- Sources: [eypmcfinc.com About](https://www.eypmcfinc.com/about); [EYP MCF DCD company page](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/company/eyp-mission-critical-facilities/)

## Cooling Architecture Preferences

Published references cover multiple cooling architectures:
- Adiabatic cooling
- Chilled water cooling
- Evaporative cooling
- **Liquid immersion cooling** — for high-density/crypto mining/HPC use cases
- **Rear-door heat exchangers**
- **CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics) analysis** to optimize airflow and setpoint temperatures

Documented project: 34 modular data center units using liquid immersion cooling to support heat-intensive data mining technologies.

Sources: [EYP Data Center Trends 2021](https://webuilddatacenters.com/2021/03/26/eyp-mission-critical-discusses-data-center-trends-for-2021/); [eypmcfinc.com Data Center Design](https://www.eypmcfinc.com/data-center-design)

## Modular/Prefabricated Build Approach

EYP MCF has developed **prefabricated and pre-populated containerized solutions**:
- Designed a "massively modular" 700,000 sq ft colocation facility in Dallas using "highly standardized elements"
- Deployed 45 containerized units (30 EcoPODs + 15 Water Cooled 40' PODs) in 6 months to support 130,000 servers
- Mix of adiabatic and chilled water cooling with central UPS and in-rack critical power battery backup

Source: [EYP MCF DCD company page](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/company/eyp-mission-critical-facilities/)

## Rack Density and Densification Data (Published Thought Leadership)

EYP has published benchmarks showing:
- Rack load density has increased **17% annually over the past 10 years**
- Chip power consumption has doubled since 2000
- Data center footprint per year has shrunk by 30%

Source: [Densification in Mission Critical Lab Environments — Data Center Frontier](https://www.datacenterfrontier.com/voices-of-the-industry/article/11428335/densification-in-mission-critical-lab-environments)

## Risk Assessment Framework

EYP uses **Probability Risk Analysis (PRA)** methodology for site auditing:
- Determines condition of electrical and mechanical infrastructures
- Directs limited capital toward areas of greatest benefit
- "Operational and Reliability Risk Assessment" service covers: reliability analysis, operational risk analysis, basic capacity assessment, infrastructure condition analysis, policies/procedures development, and staff training

Source: [eypmcfinc.com About](https://www.eypmcfinc.com/about)

## IUE Metric (Co-Developed)

EYP's Yigit Bulut co-authored the **IUE (Infrastructure Usage Effectiveness)** metric through The Green Grid/TGGC (China). IUE extends PUE to measure how effectively infrastructure resources are being used across power delivery, cooling generation, cooling flow, and rack capacity utilization.

Source: [IUE White Paper — DCD Media](https://media.datacenterdynamics.com/media/documents/IUE_White_Paper-Final.pdf)
