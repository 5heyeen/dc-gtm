# Prompt 03: what-separates-leading-dc-engineering-consultancy

> **Prompt:** What separates a leading data center engineering consultancy from an average one — what do hyperscaler and colo clients look for in an engineering partner?

---

Hyperscaler and colocation clients apply a short but uncompromising filter when selecting engineering partners.

**Hyperscalers** (Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon) typically have highly developed internal standards and want an engineering partner who can execute against those standards with precision, speed, and minimal hand-holding. They care about: throughput (can you handle 20 projects simultaneously?), reliability (will the drawings be right the first time?), and security clearance/NDAs. Leading firms for hyperscalers have dedicated account teams, embedded knowledge of the client's internal standards, and the operational scale to staff up quickly.

**Colocation operators** (Equinix, Digital Realty, NTT, CyrusOne) want more design input because they are building for multi-tenant use with undefined future customers. Flexibility — higher power density readiness, modular expansion, diverse cooling topology options — matters more. They also value engineering firms that understand financial modeling of capital expenditure, because colo decisions are fundamentally IRR calculations.

**What separates leaders from average:**

1. Speed of deliverable production without quality loss — the best firms have invested in templates and BIM workflows that compress drawing production time.
2. Commissioning and testing expertise — firms with a commissioning practice embedded in the design team produce more buildable, testable designs.
3. Technology currency — the transition from traditional air-cooled designs to liquid cooling (direct-to-chip, immersion) is a current dividing line. Firms that understand the thermal and structural implications of high-density AI clusters are in a different tier.
4. Geographic reach with local execution — a global framework delivered by local licensed engineers.
5. Reference projects at the relevant scale — a firm that has designed a 100 MW campus is in a different conversation than one whose largest project was 20 MW.
