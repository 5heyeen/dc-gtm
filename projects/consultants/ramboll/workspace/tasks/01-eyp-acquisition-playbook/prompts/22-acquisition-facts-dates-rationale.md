# Prompt 22: acquisition-facts-dates-rationale

> **Prompt:** Search for "Ramboll acquires EYP" OR "Ramboll EYP Architecture Engineering acquisition" — what year did the acquisition close, what was the stated rationale, and what financial terms (if any) were disclosed?

---

## Key Finding: EYP MCF, not EYP Architecture+Engineering

**Important clarification:** The entity acquired by Ramboll is **EYP Mission Critical Facilities (EYP MCF)**, headquartered in Valhalla, NY — a data center-focused consultancy. This is distinct from the broader "EYP Architecture+Engineering" brand. The two EYP entities share historical roots but diverged; EYP MCF is the data center-focused spinoff. This is a common source of confusion in industry discussions of the "EYP playbook."

## Acquisition Facts

**Acquisition close date:** January 31, 2022

**Financial terms:** Undisclosed. No purchase price, revenue multiple, or deal structure was made public.

**Revenue target disclosed:** Ramboll projected combined data center consulting revenue of USD 60 million by 2025 (implying ~$30–40M combined at time of deal with ~100 dedicated staff globally post-merger).

**Size of EYP MCF at acquisition:** ~50 professionals. Ramboll's pre-existing data center practice also had ~50 staff.

## Corporate History of EYP MCF

- **Founded:** 1996 as independent consultancy
- **Acquired by HP:** November 2007 (HP sought to bundle data center design with hardware sales for large enterprise and emerging-market clients)
- **Spun back out:** Early 2018 — a group of eight former EYP principals (including Rick Einhorn as Managing Partner) led a management buyout from Hewlett Packard Enterprise
- **PE investment:** TA Associates completed a PE round on May 15, 2021 (undisclosed amount, two-thirds stake)
- **Acquired by Ramboll:** January 31, 2022

## Stated Rationale

**Ramboll (COO Michael Simmelsgaard):** "Two huge trends are shaping our world today: the climate crisis and the digitalization of society. As digitalization advances...data centers have a critical role to play in supporting a green circular economy... our role is to ensure that the facilities we design, build and operate on behalf of our clients are at the cutting edge of environmental performance."

Ramboll framed the deal as: strengthening its position in the "international premier league of data center consultancies," adding deep mission-critical technical domain knowledge, and combining EYP MCF's North American client base with Ramboll's 35-country global footprint.

**EYP MCF (Managing Partner Rick Einhorn):** "Joining Ramboll is a great opportunity to expand our consulting and engineering services to our existing global client base while bringing our technical expertise into Ramboll's existing and future clients across 35 countries around the world."
