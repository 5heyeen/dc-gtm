# Prompt 10: expert-evaluation-playbook-differentiation

> **Prompt:** What would a data center engineering expert ask to evaluate whether a firm's "playbook" is genuinely differentiated vs. marketing language?

---

A credible technical evaluator would probe in several directions:

**On specificity and currency**
"Show me your basis-of-design template for a 32 MW Tier III air-cooled campus designed for 20 kW/rack average with DLC readiness for 40+ kW zones. What are your default assumptions for UPS topology, generator fuel reserve, and cooling redundancy?" A firm with a real playbook answers this in minutes. A firm with a marketing playbook deflects to a general description of their process.

**On AI/high-density currency**
"How does your playbook handle the transition from 10 kW/rack air-cooled to 100 kW/rack direct liquid cooling? What changes in your structural, mechanical, and electrical approach?" This tests whether the playbook has been updated for the current AI infrastructure buildout or is still calibrated for the 2018-era hyperscaler campus.

**On commissioning integration**
"At what design stage do you write the Level 4 and Level 5 IST scripts, and who authors them?" If the answer is "the commissioning agent does that," the firm's playbook does not include commissioning integration.

**On failure modes and lessons learned**
"What was the last significant commissioning failure your firm encountered, what caused it, and how did it change your design templates?" A firm with a real learning loop can answer this.

**On client-specific adaptation**
"How does your playbook change when the client is a hyperscaler with their own internal standards versus a regional colo operator building to Uptime Tier III?" This tests whether the methodology is genuinely modular or a single rigid template.

**On speed**
"What is your typical elapsed time from design kickoff to IFC package for a 20 MW single-story data hall, and what in your process drives that timeline?" Speed is a proxy for how well-developed the templates actually are.

**On staffing**
"How much of your playbook execution depends on specific named individuals versus being transferable to a newly onboarded senior engineer?" This tests institutional capture versus personal capture of the methodology.
