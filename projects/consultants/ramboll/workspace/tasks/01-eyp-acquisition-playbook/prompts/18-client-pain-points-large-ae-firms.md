# Prompt 18: client-pain-points-large-ae-firms

> **Prompt:** What do clients typically complain about when working with large A/E firms on data center projects — what pain points does a specialist playbook aim to solve?

---

**Bait-and-switch staffing**
The senior partners who won the project are replaced by less experienced staff during execution. The client who bought the capability of specific individuals gets a team without the same depth. This is endemic in large A/E firms and is the most commonly cited frustration.

**Slow response times**
Large firms have approval chains, review processes, and utilization pressures that slow RFI responses, submittal reviews, and design revisions. In fast-moving data center construction programs where schedule compression is constant, a 10-day RFI turnaround from the engineer can collapse a 3-day construction schedule window.

**Generalist engineers on specialist problems**
When a large firm's data center practice is understaffed, it deploys engineers from other practice areas without mission-critical design experience. A mechanical engineer excellent at hospital HVAC may not understand the implications of hot aisle containment failure on UPS battery temperatures.

**Lack of commissioning integration**
Design engineers who do not understand commissioning produce designs that are technically correct on paper but create expensive problems during functional testing — missing isolation valves, inaccessible test points, no provisions for temporary power connections during loadbank testing.

**Documentation quality**
Large firms with high throughput sometimes produce drawing packages with inconsistencies between disciplines, missing details, or specification sections copied from previous projects with client-specific information not updated.

**Cost overruns from scope creep in design**
Large firms may under-scope initial engagements to win work, then add scope through change orders.

**A specialist playbook addresses these pain points** by: providing templates that reduce errors, embedding commissioning requirements in design from concept stage, maintaining specialist depth throughout the project (not just in pursuit), and standardizing deliverable formats that reduce review time.
