# Prompt 06: standards-certifications-dc-playbook

> **Prompt:** What standards and certifications (Uptime Institute, TIA-942, EN 50600, LEED, BREEAM) would a credible data center engineering playbook reference and comply with?

---

A credible playbook embeds standards as decision frameworks, not just credential logos.

**Uptime Institute Tier Classification (I–IV)**
Most widely recognized availability framework. A playbook specifies which Tier level is the design target, what topology requirements follow (e.g., Tier III requires concurrent maintainability), and how commissioning verification (TCCF) is planned.

**TIA-942**
Widely used in North America and internationally. Rated-1 through Rated-4 maps loosely to Uptime Tier I–IV but uses different methodology.

**EN 50600**
European standard for data centre facilities and infrastructures. More granular than TIA-942 in some subsystems. Essential for EU regulatory contexts and increasingly referenced in EU Code of Conduct for Data Centres.

**LEED**
US Green Building Council standard. Data center LEED projects typically target Silver or Gold. Relevant for corporate sustainability reporting.

**BREEAM**
UK/European equivalent of LEED. BREEAM Data Centres is a specific scheme. Required by some UK and European public sector clients.

**DGNB**
German sustainability standard, gaining traction in Scandinavia.

**EU Code of Conduct for Energy Efficiency in Data Centres**
Voluntary but increasingly expected by large European operators.

**ISO/IEC 27001**
Information security management — relevant at the design stage for physical security zoning and access control design.

**NFPA 75 / NFPA 76**
Fire protection standards for IT equipment areas (US context).

A genuine playbook references these not in a table of logos but as specific decision points: "At concept design stage, redundancy tier is confirmed against Uptime Institute Tier Topology requirements; TIA-942 Rated level is confirmed with client; EN 50600 classification is documented in the Basis of Design."
