## Assessment Framework

An integration expert would evaluate four dimensions: people retention, commercial performance, brand strategy, and capability deepening.

## Dimension 1: People Retention

**Evidence available (positive indicators):**
- Rick Einhorn (Managing Partner) — confirmed present as of 2024-2025, active in client-facing roles
- Brian Whelan (Managing Partner) — confirmed present, active in commissioning/PM leadership
- Yigit Bulut (CTO, Electrical Engineering) — confirmed present, presenting at 7x24 Exchange Fall 2024
- Spiros Liolis (Chief Technologist, Strategy) — confirmed present, presenting at 7x24 Exchange Fall 2023
- Jim Davis (Sustainability & Resilience) — confirmed present, panelist at DCD>Connect March 2024

**Assessment:** Core leadership team appears intact 3 years post-acquisition. This is a positive signal vs. the typical A/E M&A failure pattern (key persons depart within 18 months). INFERENCE: Earn-out structures and/or equity participation likely aligned incentives.

**Unknown:** Whether the ~50 non-leadership staff from EYP MCF are still present or have turned over. Senior staff retention is necessary but not sufficient.

## Dimension 2: Commercial Performance

**Evidence available (positive indicators):**
- October 2025: EYP MCF (Part of Ramboll) selected for full-scope engineering of **438-acre AI campus** in Ector County, Texas — a major new client win 3+ years post-acquisition, suggesting commercial health
- DCS Awards 2023 win for sustainability innovation — competitive visibility maintained
- Continued conference presence (DCD, 7x24 Exchange) — market presence maintained

**Unknown:** Whether the USD 60 million revenue target by 2025 (stated at acquisition) was achieved. No public financial reporting on this.

## Dimension 3: Brand Strategy

**Evidence available (positive indicators):**
- Separate website (eypmcfinc.com) maintained — brand preserved rather than absorbed
- "EYP Mission Critical Facilities, Part of Ramboll" identity — dual branding executed correctly
- Ramboll also hosts a data center services page on ramboll.com — cross-referencing building parent brand recognition

**Assessment:** Brand strategy is well-executed. EYP name preserved (protecting client relationships and key person incentives), while Ramboll association builds global credibility. This is the textbook correct approach for A/E M&A.

## Dimension 4: Capability Deepening

**Evidence available:**
- Ramboll acquired **i3 Solutions Group** (September 2024) — adding European/Asian data center capability to supplement EYP MCF's North American focus. This is evidence of continued investment in the practice, not stagnation.
- Combined practice now described as having "hundreds of dedicated data centre design experts globally"
- September 2025 net-zero data center white paper (Ed Ansett, i3/Ramboll) demonstrates thought leadership capability extended beyond EYP MCF's historical North American focus

**Assessment:** The i3 Solutions acquisition in September 2024 is the strongest evidence that Ramboll's integration of EYP MCF was successful enough to justify continued investment in the practice. If EYP MCF integration had failed, Ramboll would not have doubled down with a second data center acquisition.

## Overall Integration Assessment

**Verdict: Successful integration, with strong evidence of people retention, commercial activity, and capability investment.** The acquisition appears to have avoided the most common failure modes (key person departure, brand dilution, knowledge loss). The i3 Solutions follow-on acquisition is the strongest positive signal.

**Caveats:** Non-leadership staff retention and actual revenue performance are unknown from public sources. The playbook's currency for AI/liquid cooling is unverified — EYP MCF's track record is primarily pre-AI buildout.
