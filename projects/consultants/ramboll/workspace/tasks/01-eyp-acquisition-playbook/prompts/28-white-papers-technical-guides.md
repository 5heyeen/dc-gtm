## Confirmed Published Documents

### 1. "Infrastructure Sustainability Options and Revenue Opportunities for Data Centres" (March 2021)
**Authors:** EYP Mission Critical Facilities Inc. + i3 Solutions Group
**Topics:** GHG abatement, Demand Side Response, Data Centre Power Generation, Energy Storage, Sustainable Energy Trading, Sustainability Performance Indices (SPIs)
**Description:** First in a planned series on GHG abatement for data center operators moving toward carbon net-zero operations by 2030. Covers how GHG reduction targets and revenue generation are not mutually exclusive.
Planned follow-up papers: gas reciprocating engines and turbines, fuel cells, GHG reduction with blended hydrogen/natural gas.
**Sources:**
- [einpresswire.com announcement](https://www.einpresswire.com/article/536786036/eyp-mission-critical-facilities-and-i3-solutions-group-launches-first-data-center-sustainability-white-paper)
- [Datacentre Solutions](https://datacentre.solutions/news/60967/i3-solutions-group-and-eyp-mission-critical-facilities-launch-data-centre-sustainability-white-paper)

### 2. "Developing Sustainable Data Centres: A Strategic Roadmap to Achieve Net Zero Carbon and Reduce Environmental Impact" (September 2025)
**Publisher:** Ramboll (EYP MCF + i3 Solutions integrated)
**Lead author:** Ed Ansett, Innovation Director at Ramboll (former i3 Solutions Group founder)
**Released at:** Climate Week NYC, September 2025
**Description:** "First-of-its-kind strategic roadmap" covering embodied carbon, operational carbon, biodiversity, circularity, energy, and water.

**Key benchmarks published:**
- Operational carbon: **net zero achievable** through energy efficiency optimization, renewable energy procurement, energy reuse/export, and demand response
- Embodied carbon: reducible via low-carbon steel/concrete, locally sourced or reused materials
- Circularity benchmark: **all materials reused, reusable, or recyclable; zero output to landfill or incineration**
- Water: **water neutrality** achievable by avoiding water-based cooling, maximizing cycles of concentration, using alternative sources (rainwater)

**Sources:**
- [Ramboll news announcement](https://www.ramboll.com/en-us/news/nycw-2025-developing-sustainable-data-centers)
- [Ramboll insights page](https://www.ramboll.com/insights/decarbonise-for-net-zero/how-to-develop-sustainable-data-centres)
- [Intelligent Data Centres coverage](https://www.intelligentdatacentres.com/2025/09/23/report-from-ramboll-finds-that-data-centres-can-become-net-zero-and-sustainable/)
