# Prompt 09: ae-engagement-phases-dc-client

> **Prompt:** What are the typical phases of an A/E firm's engagement with a data center client — from feasibility through to operations support — and how would a specialist playbook map to those phases?

---

A specialist firm's engagement typically follows these phases:

**Phase 1: Strategic Advisory / Pre-Feasibility**
Market demand analysis, site search criteria, power and land market assessment, financial modeling of build-vs-lease-vs-colo options. Playbook contribution: site scoring matrix, power market assessment templates, financial model framework.

**Phase 2: Site Selection and Due Diligence**
Technical due diligence on shortlisted sites — utility infrastructure, substation capacity studies, permitting risk, environmental constraints, geotechnical assessment. Playbook contribution: site assessment checklist, utility engagement process guide, permitting risk matrix by jurisdiction.

**Phase 3: Concept Design**
Campus masterplan, building configuration, power and cooling architecture selection, basis of design, capital cost estimate (Class 4 or 3). Playbook contribution: basis of design template, reference design options library, cost benchmarking data.

**Phase 4: Schematic Design and Design Development**
Progressive engineering across all disciplines (architectural, structural, civil, electrical, mechanical, low voltage, fire protection, security). BIM model development. Playbook contribution: BIM standards, discipline-specific calculation templates, specification section library.

**Phase 5: Construction Documentation**
Complete permit and construction drawing sets. Playbook contribution: drawing standards, QA/QC checklist, constructability review process.

**Phase 6: Procurement Support**
Technical specification review for bid packages, RFI response support, bid leveling for mechanical, electrical, and structural packages. Playbook contribution: bid leveling templates, contractor qualification criteria.

**Phase 7: Construction Administration**
Submittal review, RFI management, site observation, change order technical review. Playbook contribution: submittal log templates, site observation report formats.

**Phase 8: Commissioning and Testing**
Commissioning plan execution, witness testing, integrated systems testing (IST), performance verification. Playbook contribution: commissioning plan template, IST scripts, deficiency tracking system.

**Phase 9: Handover and Operations Support**
As-built documentation package, O&M manuals, staff training, CMMS data population, post-occupancy PUE and WUE measurement against design targets.

A genuinely differentiated playbook has explicit, documented tools for each phase — not just a process diagram.
