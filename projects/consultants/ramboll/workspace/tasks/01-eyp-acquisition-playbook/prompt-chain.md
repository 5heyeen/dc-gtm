# Socratic Research Prompts: EYP Acquisition by Ramboll & the EYP Data Center Playbook

> **Topic:** Research the EYP Architecture|Engineering acquisition by Ramboll, the EYP data center design playbook/methodology, capabilities brought to Ramboll, and how Ramboll markets these since the acquisition.
> **Use these prompts in sequence in any AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) for deep, structured research.**

---

## Principles

- `[independent]` What makes a consultancy/engineering firm's acquisition of a specialist data center design firm strategically valuable — what capabilities are hardest to build organically vs. acquire?
- `[independent]` What defines a "playbook" in the context of engineering and design for mission-critical facilities — what elements does a repeatable methodology typically include (standards, templates, checklists, delivery stages)?
- `[independent]` What separates a leading data center engineering consultancy from an average one — what do hyperscaler and colo clients look for in an engineering partner?
- `[independent]` What role does brand continuity play after an acquisition in the A/E (Architecture/Engineering) sector — do firms typically rebrand acquired units or preserve the acquired name?

---

## Framework

- `[independent]` What are the core components of a data center design playbook — covering site selection, concept design, detailed engineering (structural, MEP, power, cooling), sustainability frameworks, commissioning, and handover?
- `[independent]` What standards and certifications (Uptime Institute, TIA-942, EN 50600, LEED, BREEAM) would a credible data center engineering playbook reference and comply with?
- `[independent]` What does "mission-critical design" mean in the context of EYP/Ramboll — how does it differ from standard commercial building design?
- `[sequential]` Given the components above, which aspects of EYP's methodology are most differentiated from what generalist engineering firms (Arup, WSP, Arcadis) offer?
- `[independent]` What are the typical phases of an A/E firm's engagement with a data center client — from feasibility through to operations support — and how would a specialist playbook map to those phases?
