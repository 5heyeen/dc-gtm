## Executive Summary

**Headline finding:** Despite Ramboll's extensive Norwegian office network (14 locations, ~1,600 experts), **no publicly named Norwegian data center project credits Ramboll as the lead engineering consultant**. Their documented Nordic DC references are in Denmark (Meta Odense) and Finland (TeliaSonera Helsinki), not in Norway. This gap is significant for Metier's competitive positioning — Ramboll markets Norwegian DC services, but their Norwegian track record appears limited.

## Section 2: Named Nordic DC Project References

### 2.1 Meta Odense Data Center (Denmark) — WASTE HEAT TO DISTRICT HEATING

**Ramboll role:** Design of the energy centre that redistributes surplus heat from Meta's data centre to Odense's district heating network.

**Significance:** The **largest Danish heat pump installation utilising surplus heat from a data centre**. This is a marquee Nordic reference for Ramboll's combined data center + district heating capability — a service area directly relevant to Norwegian municipal contexts.

**Relevance to Norway:** Demonstrates Ramboll's proven capability to link hyperscaler DC waste heat to Nordic district heating systems. Norway's cities have extensive district heating networks (Oslo, Bergen, Trondheim), making this capability directly applicable.

Source: [Ramboll — Meta: surplus heat to district heating](https://www.ramboll.com/projects/energy/meta-surplus-heat-to-district-heating)

### 2.2 TeliaSonera Helsinki Data Center (Finland) — 2018

**Ramboll role:** Management + lead consulting services, from feasibility study to final handover.

**Facility specs:**
- 24 MW IT power capacity
- 15,000 m² (200,000 servers)
- Heat recovery system using heat pumps
- Supplies >20 GW of heating energy to 9,000 households

**Significance:** This is a **full-lifecycle Nordic DC engagement** — feasibility → design → construction → handover — and it demonstrates Ramboll's capability to lead complete DC programs (not just specialist engineering). The 9,000-household district heating contribution is a strong sustainability credential.

Source: [Sustainable Data Centers: Location, Location, Location — Ramboll Insights](https://c.ramboll.com/sustainable-data-centers-location-location-location)

### 2.3 Google Norway (Skien) — NO CONFIRMED RAMBOLL ROLE

Google is building a ~EUR 600 million hyperscale data center in Norway, operational from 2026. However, searches did not return any confirmed Ramboll involvement in this project. The DLA Piper insight piece discusses the Google project without naming Ramboll as an engineering partner.

Source: [DLA Piper — Google EUR600 million Norway DC](https://www.dlapiper.com/en-us/insights/publications/2024/05/construction-of-googles-eur600-million-hyperscale-data-centre-in-norway)

## Section 5: Inference — Why No Named Norwegian Project References?

Several possible explanations, ranked by probability:

### 5.1 (Most Likely) Commercial Confidentiality
Many data center operators — particularly hyperscalers, colo operators, and those in competitive markets — do not publicly credit engineering consultants on their projects. Ramboll may have Norwegian DC engagements that are simply not publicly attributable. This is the most common pattern in the DC consulting sector.

### 5.2 (Possible) Limited Norway Track Record
Ramboll's data center practice grew primarily through the EYP MCF acquisition (2022, US-focused) and i3 Solutions (2024, UK/Asia-focused). Neither acquisition added Norway-specific DC references. Ramboll's Norwegian offices have ~1,600 staff across many practice areas, but data center-specific capability may be primarily exported from other geographies rather than locally-grown.

### 5.3 (Possible) Partnership/Sub-Consultant Role
Ramboll may play sub-consultant roles (e.g., on sustainability, waste heat recovery, or specific engineering disciplines) on Norwegian DC projects where a larger contractor (Skanska, Veidekke, AF Gruppen) holds the primary engineering contract. Sub-consultants are rarely named in public project announcements.

### 5.4 (Speculative) Emerging Market Position
Given Ramboll's September 2024 acquisition of i3 Solutions and stated ambition to expand European DC presence, Norway may be a target market where Ramboll is positioning rather than having established references.

## Section 6: Implications for Metier

### 6.1 Competitive Advantage — Local Norway Track Record
Metier likely has more documented Norwegian DC project experience than Ramboll, specifically in Norwegian project management contexts. This is a **defensible competitive advantage** for Metier in Norwegian PM advisory mandates.

### 6.2 Partnership Opportunity — Waste Heat to District Heating
Ramboll's Meta Odense project is a **best-in-class Nordic reference** for linking DC waste heat to municipal district heating. For Norwegian projects where a client prioritizes waste heat recovery, Ramboll could be a valuable engineering partner that Metier (as owner's PM) could recommend.

### 6.3 Watch-List — Hyperscale Growth
If hyperscale operators (Google, Microsoft, Meta) expand in Norway beyond Google's Skien facility, Ramboll + Meta Odense + TeliaSonera Helsinki references position them to win engineering mandates. Metier should monitor whether Ramboll's Nordic staff expand their Norway-specific data center capability over 2026-2027.

### 6.4 Differentiation
Metier's core differentiators vs. Ramboll in the Norwegian DC market:
- **Norwegian PM methodology and local project culture fluency**
- **Owner-side advisory positioning** (not conflicted with engineering scope)
- **Established Norwegian operator relationships** (if any exist)
- **Norwegian language and regulatory fluency at the working level**

Ramboll's advantages:
- **Global DC engineering depth** (via EYP MCF)
- **Nordic sustainability and district heating integration credentials** (Meta Odense, TeliaSonera Helsinki)
- **i3/Ansett sustainability thought leadership** applicable to European markets
- **Scale** — 300+ dedicated DC experts globally
