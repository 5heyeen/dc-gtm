# Prompt 08: skanska-dc-capability-matrix

Cite at least one quote-plus-URL per cell where evidence exists. Then produce a **Nordic DC project inventory table**. Close with 3 short bullets identifying the most credible gaps a Metier alliance would fill.

## 3. Three most credible gaps a Metier alliance would fill

- **Owner's-advisor commissioning and client-side PMC in the Nordics.** Skanska's posture is principal-contractor only (*"As a principal contractor, Skanska specializes in building, fitting out and upgrading data centres"* — [Skanska UK](https://www.skanska.co.uk/expertise/specialist-capability/mechanical-and-electrical-fit-out-srw/sectors/data-centres/)), while Turner & Townsend markets *"manages all phases of data center construction, from pre-construction through commissioning and close-out"* — [T&T](https://www.turnerandtownsend.com/sectors/data-centres/). A Metier owner's-rep / commissioning-agent offer sits outside Skanska's contractor stance without internal overlap.
- **Early-phase concept and business-case advisory in the Nordics.** No Skanska DC-specific concept/feasibility claim surfaced; Arcadis markets *"concept development and sustainable design"* — [Arcadis](https://www.arcadis.com/en/expertise/sectors/technology) and CTS Nordics markets *"feasibility to concept and execution design"* — [CTS Nordics](https://www.cts-nordics.com/services). Metier can bring the upstream advisory that precedes Skanska's delivery scope.
- **DC-specific sustainability / ESG and heat-reuse engineering for Nordic hyperscale.** No DC-specific heat-reuse or ESG language surfaced on any Skanska Nordic DC page (Prompt 04 service-scope checklist — [source](https://www.usa.skanska.com/what-we-deliver/build/mission-critical--data-centers/)); T&T markets *"net zero and digital solutions"* — [T&T](https://www.turnerandtownsend.com/sectors/data-centres/) and Arcadis *"sustainable design"* — [Arcadis](https://www.arcadis.com/en/expertise/sectors/technology). A Norconsult-Metier heat-reuse and ESG-reporting offer plugs a publicly visible Skanska Nordic gap.

## Inferred Findings

- **Inferred:** Skanska's Nordic DC offer today is narrower than its US/UK offer and is limited to civil/structural and core-and-shell scope — leaving six identified scope areas open for an alliance partner.
  - **Based on:** *"The contract consists of preparatory groundwork and foundations for a new hyperscale data center, including building of new access roads with infrastructure and site development."* — [group.skanska.com Telemark NOK 1.1bn release](https://group.skanska.com/media/press-releases-articles/278070/Skanska-prepares-for-new-data-center-in-Telemark,-Norway,-for-NOK-1.1-billion,-about-SEK-1.1-billion); *"Skanska has the capability to deliver the full spectrum of construction, including infrastructure, building works, ground engineering, and MEP"* — [Skanska UK data centres page](https://www.skanska.co.uk/expertise/specialist-capability/mechanical-and-electrical-fit-out-srw/sectors/data-centres/).
  - **Reasoning:** The Nordic press releases describe scope as groundwork/foundations and core-and-shell only, while the UK page claims full MEP + fit-out + commissioning. The contrast between the two Skanska-owned channels indicates the Nordic offer has not (yet) been positioned around MEP, commissioning, or sustainability/ESG engineering.

- **Inferred:** Skanska's MEP + commissioning capability is transferable from UK/US into the Nordics, but has not been localised and is currently most likely delivered via subcontract on the Gromstul and Finland projects.
  - **Based on:** *"The project was being delivered by Skanska's specialist mechanical, electrical and public health engineering arm, while the construction work was being delivered by Skanska's buildings team."* — [PR Newswire, Telehouse South](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/skanska-delivers-fit-out-and-construction-work-for-data-centre-in-london-uk-for-gbp-158m-about-sek-2-1-billion-301951762.html); *"There are currently two experienced MEP QA/QC Manager positions available for a large-scale data centre project team in Norway"* — [careerstructure.com](https://www.careerstructure.com/jobs/data-centre/in-norway).
  - **Reasoning:** Skanska has a named MEP arm in the UK, but the live Norwegian MEP QA/QC openings surfaced are on third-party DC job boards, not Skanska Nordic career pages — consistent with MEP being contracted out rather than built up inside Skanska Norge/Suomi.

- **Inferred:** Skanska Commercial Property Development will not contribute as a DC investor/capital counterparty — DC exposure sits entirely in the contractor (Construction) stream.
  - **Based on:** Prompt 05 findings — Construction Dive: *"close to 10 billion Swedish [krona] in order intake for data centers"* — [Construction Dive — Skanska CEO on AI boom](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/skanska-ceo-we-are-well-positioned-to-capitalize-on-ai-boom/811636/); Construction Dive on Q3 2025 results — [Skanska earnings Q3 2025](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/skanska-earnings-report-q3-2025-us-construction/805248/).
  - **Reasoning:** Skanska CEO commentary frames DC as a Construction (contracting) order-intake driver. Prompt 05 separately surfaced a Q3 2025 SEK 700m US write-down and a Q4 2025 "divestment quarter" for Commercial Property Development — signalling CPD is exiting, not building, property positions. Any capital-advisory or investor-side offer to a Metier alliance will therefore not come from inside Skanska.
