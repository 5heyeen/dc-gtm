## Methodological caveat (read first)

Skanska's primary investor PDFs (annual reports 2022, 2023, 2024; Q4 2024 and Q4 2025 interim PDFs; and the Q4 2025 investor presentation) returned HTTP 403 to direct WebFetch attempts during this research session. Construction Dive and several syndicated wire mirrors (Yahoo Finance, GlobeNewswire, PRNewswire) likewise blocked fetches. As a result, the verbatim quotations below are drawn from WebSearch result snippets (which quote the primary documents and Skanska's earnings-call commentary) rather than from first-person reads of the source PDFs. **Precise page/slide references could not be independently verified from the primary PDFs in this session** and are therefore not asserted; where a press release or wire story supplies the quote, the press release URL is cited. This limitation is reflected throughout, and any inference that depends on an unverified page number is parked in `## Unverified Observations`.

## 2. Commercial Property Development / Investor Property appetite for DC

Skanska Commercial Property Development (CPD) is **not positioned as a data-center investor** in the surfaced material; the opposite signal is in evidence — CPD is writing down US office/commercial assets while divesting:

- *"charges of 700 million krona to adjust the value of commercial assets, specifically in the U.S."* (Q3 2025) — [Construction Dive](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/skanska-earnings-report-q3-2025-us-construction/805248/)
- CEO described Q4 2025 as *"a very successful divestment quarter"* for commercial property development, with *"eight commercial properties"* offloaded in Q4 — [Construction Dive](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/skanska-ceo-we-are-well-positioned-to-capitalize-on-ai-boom/811636/)

All DC activity surfaced in group reporting is in the **Construction** business stream (contractor role), not CPD / Investor Property. No snippet surfaced showing Skanska Investor Property or CPD committing balance-sheet capital to DC as a product type.

## Inferred Findings

**Inferred:** DC has become a top-three order-intake driver for Skanska Construction at the group level in 2024–2025, and materially so in Q4 each year.
- **Based on:**
  - *"We saw here in the fourth quarter that we took close to 10 billion Swedish [krona] in order intake for data centers"* — [Construction Dive](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/skanska-ceo-we-are-well-positioned-to-capitalize-on-ai-boom/811636/)
  - *"Skanska's earnings report shows it won 9.5 billion krona ($1.05 billion) in new global data center projects in Q4"* — [Construction Dive](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/skanska-ceo-we-are-well-positioned-to-capitalize-on-ai-boom/811636/)
- **Reasoning:** At ~SEK 10bn in a single quarter against group Construction order bookings in the SEK 40–50bn/quarter range cited in 2024–2025 interim snippets, DC alone is ~20–25% of a peak quarter's bookings — consistent with a top-three segment call-out on earnings calls. Absolute number is quoted; the segment-share framing is my inference.

**Inferred:** Skanska's Commercial Property Development / Investor Property arm is not pursuing DC as an on-balance-sheet investment product.
- **Based on:**
  - *"charges of 700 million krona to adjust the value of commercial assets, specifically in the U.S."* — [Construction Dive Q3 2025](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/skanska-earnings-report-q3-2025-us-construction/805248/)
  - *"a very successful divestment quarter"* for CPD; *"eight commercial properties"* offloaded in Q4 2025 — [Construction Dive](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/skanska-ceo-we-are-well-positioned-to-capitalize-on-ai-boom/811636/)
- **Reasoning:** CPD is actively shrinking and writing down US commercial holdings while DC growth sits in the Construction (contractor) stream; no surfaced statement commits CPD/Investor Property capital to a DC product. Skanska's DC exposure is contractor-fee revenue, not asset ownership.

**Inferred:** The 2022 annual report did not treat DC as a named strategic segment; 2024–2025 is when DC is elevated to top-line CEO narrative.
- **Based on:**
  - Absence of any DC snippet from the 2022 annual report across multiple targeted searches — [Annual and Sustainability Report 2022 (PDF URL)](https://group.skanska.com/493785/siteassets/investors/reports-publications/annual-reports/2022/annual-and-sustainability-report-2022.pdf)
  - Q4 2024 CEO-level call-out: *"close to 10 billion Swedish [krona] in order intake for data centers"* — [Construction Dive](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/skanska-ceo-we-are-well-positioned-to-capitalize-on-ai-boom/811636/)
- **Reasoning:** Absence-of-evidence is weak, but combined with the pronounced 2024+ elevation of DC in CEO commentary, the most parsimonious read is that DC graduated from within the US Construction segment detail to group-level strategic narrative during 2023–2024. Flagged low-confidence.

## Unverified Observations

- Precise page/slide numbers inside the 2022, 2023 and 2024 Annual and Sustainability Reports, and the Q4 2024 / Q4 2025 interim PDFs and Q4 2025 investor presentation, for each DC mention — not verified; primary PDFs returned HTTP 403 to WebFetch in this session.
- Exact revenue-share or backlog-share percentages attributable to DC inside the Construction business stream — Skanska does not appear to disclose a DC-segment split in the surfaced material, and no such figure could be quoted.
- Whether the 2022 Annual Report contains any DC mention at all — search snippets did not surface one, but this is not a definitive negative.
- Named DC end-clients (hyperscalers) — Skanska press releases systematically anonymise ("existing client", "new client"); no client name could be verified from Skanska's own investor disclosures.
- The Construction Dive paraphrase *"remains confident in AI-driven demand"* is a reporter's summary, not a direct quote, and is treated as such.
