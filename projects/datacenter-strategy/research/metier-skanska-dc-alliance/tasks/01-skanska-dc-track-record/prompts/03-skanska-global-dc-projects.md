# Prompt 03: skanska-global-dc-projects

> **Prompt:** Enumerate Skanska's data center projects in the United States (Skanska USA Building Inc.) and the United Kingdom / continental Europe (Skanska UK, Skanska Poland, Skanska Czech). Capture: (a) client / operator name, (b) location, (c) stated scale (MW or m²), (d) Skanska's contracted role and scope, (e) contract value if disclosed, (f) year awarded and completion year, (g) delivery model. Cross-check Skanska USA project portfolio pages, Bisnow, ENR (Engineering News-Record), Construction Dive, and DCD. Highlight any hyperscale (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta, Oracle) references. Also identify Skanska's typical contracted role on hyperscale projects: full GC, design-build, civil-only, shell-only, or tenant fit-out, and which scopes are self-performed vs. subcontracted.

- Corporate positioning: *"As a principal contractor, Skanska specializes in building, fitting out and upgrading data centres"* and *"Skanska has the capability to deliver the full spectrum of construction, including infrastructure, building works, ground engineering, and MEP (mechanical, electrical and public health) systems"* — [Skanska UK data centres capability page](https://www.skanska.co.uk/expertise/specialist-capability/mechanical-and-electrical-fit-out-srw/sectors/data-centres/)

- Telehouse South demonstrates a split self-perform model: *"The project was being delivered by Skanska's specialist mechanical, electrical and public health engineering arm, while the construction work was being delivered by Skanska's buildings team"* — [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/skanska-delivers-fit-out-and-construction-work-for-data-centre-in-london-uk-for-gbp-158m-about-sek-2-1-billion-301951762.html)

## Inferred Findings

**Inferred:** Skanska's largest US data-center contracts (the Arizona and Georgia repeat-client awards above USD 150M) are most likely hyperscale build-to-suit projects rather than colocation.
- **Based on:**
  - *"a single-story data center, totalling approximately 22,800 square meters (245,000 square feet)"* — [PR Newswire, Arizona USD 241M](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/skanska-builds-data-center-in-arizona-usa-for-usd-241m-about-sek-2-5-billion-302265173.html)
  - *"the construction of a 22,700 square-meter data center, associated site work, and underground utilities, with a scope that includes a fit out of an administration space and five data halls"* — [Data Centre Magazine, Georgia USD 90M](https://datacentremagazine.com/data-centres/skanska-to-build-us-90m-data-centre-in-georgia-usa)
- **Reasoning:** Single-story ~22,000 m² data halls with five-hall layouts in Arizona and Georgia, awarded repeatedly to an "existing client," match the footprint/pattern typical of hyperscale self-build campuses (AWS/Meta/Microsoft clusters in those markets) rather than enterprise or mid-sized colo.

**Inferred:** Skanska's default delivery posture on data centers is full GC / design-build with self-perform MEP in the UK and self-perform concrete/structure capacity flexed through subcontract in the US.
- **Based on:**
  - *"The project was being delivered by Skanska's specialist mechanical, electrical and public health engineering arm, while the construction work was being delivered by Skanska's buildings team"* — [PR Newswire, Telehouse](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/skanska-delivers-fit-out-and-construction-work-for-data-centre-in-london-uk-for-gbp-158m-about-sek-2-1-billion-301951762.html)
  - *"an exoskeleton erected around the center bay using subcontractor SME Steel"* — [ENR, Aligned PHX](https://www.enr.com/articles/41524-a-new-approach-to-building-data-centers)
- **Reasoning:** The Telehouse South split shows Skanska's UK MEP fit-out capability self-performed alongside buildings works, while the Aligned ENR case shows structural steel procured from a subcontractor — consistent with a GC/design-build default that self-performs where in-house trades exist and subcontracts specialist packages.
