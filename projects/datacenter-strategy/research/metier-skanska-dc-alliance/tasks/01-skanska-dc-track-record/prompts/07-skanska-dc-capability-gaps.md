# Prompt 07: skanska-dc-capability-gaps

> **Prompt:** What data center capabilities does Skanska visibly NOT claim or demonstrate in the Nordics — in-house DC mechanical / electrical design, white-space fit-out, IT infrastructure, owner's-advisor commissioning, operational support, energy-market / PPA advisory, early-phase concept and business-case advisory, investor / capital-structuring advisory? Contrast against the stated scope of CTS Nordics, Mercury Engineering, Exyte, Turner & Townsend, and Arcadis to reveal the gap. Also search for any public DC loss, replacement, or withdrawal for Skanska (DCD, Byggeindustrien, Bisnow). This is the critical input for identifying alliance fit with Metier.

---

**Method note:** WebSearch used 5/5 per budget. WebFetch attempts on `cts-nordics.com/services` and `turnerandtownsend.com/sectors/data-centres/` both returned HTTP 403 and were not retried. All verbatim competitor quotes below are therefore drawn from publicly indexed search-result snippets that reproduce on-page text of the competitor's own domains. Where the snippet paraphrases rather than quotes, the material is treated as an Inferred Finding and labelled accordingly.

## 2. Capability gap — Skanska (Nordics) vs. the five competitors

Skanska's claimed-scope baseline comes from Prompt 04 in this task folder (`04-skanska-stated-capabilities.md`): Nordic Skanska-owned communications describe groundwork, core & shell, site/envelope; modular and MEP fit-out claims are US (SAT) and UK only; no DC-specific heat-reuse, ESG, energy-integration, operational-support, concept/business-case, or investor-advisory language was surfaced on Skanska-owned Nordic pages. Prompt 02 (`02-skanska-dc-job-ads.md`) and Prompt 06 (`06-skanska-dc-leaders.md`) did not surface in-house DC M/E design, commissioning-advisor, or PPA/energy-markets roles as publicly advertised Nordic positions.

| Capability | Skanska Nordics — publicly claimed? | Competitor(s) publicly claiming it |
|---|---|---|
| In-house DC mechanical / electrical **design** (not fit-out) | Not claimed on group.skanska.com DC pages; only core-and-shell + site | Exyte ("foundational and detailed engineering"), Arcadis ("A&E services"), CTS Nordics ("design, execution, commissioning and production") |
| White-space fit-out / MEP fit-out in the Nordics | Claimed on UK page; not claimed on Nordic Skanska pages | Mercury ("construction and fit-out programmes"), Exyte ("Data Center Fitout") |
| IT infrastructure (cabinets, structured cabling, IT racks) | Not claimed anywhere surfaced | Not explicitly claimed by T&T/Arcadis either; CTS Nordics closest via "full digitalization from concept to commercial operations" |
| Owner's-advisor commissioning (Cx) — client-side, not contractor-side | Not claimed; Skanska UK commissioning is contractor self-delivery on Virtus | Turner & Townsend ("pre-construction through commissioning and close-out"), Arcadis ("project management and construction services (PMC)") |
| Operational / run-phase support | Not claimed | Arcadis ("asset management", "operations and maintenance"), CTS Nordics ("to commercial operations") |
| Energy-market / PPA advisory | Not claimed on any Skanska page surfaced | None of the five competitors explicitly claim PPA advisory in the snippets surfaced — see Gap row below |
| Early-phase concept / business-case advisory | Not claimed on Nordic pages | Arcadis ("concept development"), CTS Nordics ("feasibility to concept"), T&T ("financial forecasting and executive reporting") |
| Investor / capital-structuring advisory | Not claimed | Not explicitly claimed by the five; nearest is T&T advisory ("contract negotiation, financial forecasting") |

**Headline gaps unique to Skanska Nordics (publicly):** owner's-advisor commissioning, early-phase concept/business-case advisory, investor/capital-structuring advisory, energy-market/PPA advisory, operational-phase support, DC-specific sustainability positioning. Each is a capability that Metier — or a Metier–Skanska alliance — could slot into without internal overlap.

## Inferred Findings

**Inferred:** In the Nordic market specifically, Skanska's publicly claimed DC scope is narrower than any of the five reference competitors — limited to civil/structural and envelope works, with no published advisory, commissioning-advisor, or lifecycle-operations offer.
**Based on:**
- "The contract consists of preparatory groundwork and foundations for a new hyperscale data center, including building of new access roads with infrastructure and site development." — [group.skanska.com — Telemark NOK 1.1bn release](https://group.skanska.com/media/press-releases-articles/278070/Skanska-prepares-for-new-data-center-in-Telemark,-Norway,-for-NOK-1.1-billion,-about-SEK-1.1-billion)
- "comprehensive data center services, from concept development and sustainable design to delivery and asset management" — [Arcadis — Technology Solutions](https://www.arcadis.com/en/expertise/sectors/technology)
**Reasoning:** Skanska's own Nordic press releases describe scope as groundwork, core & shell, and site/envelope; Arcadis, T&T, CTS Nordics and Exyte describe end-to-end scope including concept/advisory/operations. Even if Skanska performs some of these internally, the absence from public communications signals either non-capability or unwillingness to market them — either way an alliance partner is unopposed publicly.

**Inferred:** Energy-market / PPA advisory and investor / capital-structuring advisory are whitespace for *all* six parties (Skanska plus the five competitors) in their public DC communications.
**Based on:**
- "programme management, cost and commercial management, as well as net zero and digital solutions" — [Turner & Townsend — Data centre management](https://www.turnerandtownsend.com/sectors/data-centres/)
- "due diligence, project management, cost management, design, stakeholder engagement, and digital capabilities" — [Arcadis — Technology Solutions](https://www.arcadis.com/en/expertise/sectors/technology)
**Reasoning:** The most-advisory-leaning competitor pages (T&T, Arcadis) list cost, programme, net zero, due diligence, and digital — but do not claim PPA/power-purchase advisory or equity/debt structuring services. This suggests Metier's investor-facing and energy-structuring value propositions face a thin competitive field in the Nordics, and an alliance offering them alongside Skanska's delivery muscle would be genuinely differentiated.

**Inferred:** Skanska's modular/prefab DC narrative is not yet localised to the Nordics and is therefore a visible gap versus Mercury's Schönebeck manufacturing investment and CTS Nordics' NordicEPOD joint venture.
**Based on:**
- "Mercury has invested €25 million in a state-of-the-art off-site manufacturing facility in Schönebeck, Germany" — [Mercury Engineering — Nordics article](https://www.mercuryeng.com/2026/03/12/data-centre-growth-across-the-nordics/)
- "NordicEPOD secures investment from Eaton and CTS Nordics to develop its capacity to manufacture standardised power modules for the European data centre industry" — [Eaton release](https://www.eaton.com/gb/en-gb/company/news-insights/news-releases/2024/eaton-expands-reach-into-european-data-centre-market-nordic-epod.html)
**Reasoning:** Skanska's modular/prefab DC language lives on the US SAT press release (Prompt 04); no equivalent Nordic-localised modular statement was surfaced. Mercury and CTS Nordics both publicly anchor prefab to European-market manufacturing. If the Metier-Skanska alliance needs a prefab story in the Nordics, it cannot rely on an existing Skanska Nordic claim.
