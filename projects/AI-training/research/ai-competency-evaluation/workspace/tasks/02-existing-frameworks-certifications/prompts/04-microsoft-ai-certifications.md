# Prompt 04: microsoft-ai-certifications

> **Prompt:** Microsoft AI certifications — structure, domains, exam format, passing scores

---

## Overview

Microsoft offers a layered AI credential ecosystem spanning Fundamentals, Associate, and role-specific tracks. Credentials fall into three categories: **Role-based Certifications** (proctored exams), **Applied Skills** (lab-based assessments), and **Microsoft Copilot / Business AI Certifications** (new tracks launched 2025–2026). Several flagship exams are retiring mid-2026 and being replaced by a new generation of generative-AI-focused credentials.

---

## Core AI Certifications

### AI-900 — Microsoft Azure AI Fundamentals (Fundamentals level)

**Target role:** Non-technical professionals, beginners entering AI/cloud, business decision-makers.
**Experience level:** No prerequisites; "basic familiarity with technology and cloud concepts" recommended.

**Exam structure:** 40–60 questions; 45-minute time limit.
"Questions are presented with four answer options where one answer is correct, often testing your ability to identify the correct Azure AI service or concept for a given scenario. The exam also includes questions with five or more options where you select two or three correct answers, commonly for identifying multiple responsible AI principles or Azure AI service capabilities." — [OpenExamPrep AI-900 Study Guide](https://open-exam-prep.com/exams/azure-ai-900/introduction/about-the-ai-900-exam)

**Passing score:** 700 on a 100–1000 scaled scoring model. — [OpenExamPrep AI-900 Study Guide](https://open-exam-prep.com/exams/azure-ai-900/introduction/about-the-ai-900-exam)

**Skill domains (% of exam):**
- Describe AI workloads and considerations (15–20%)
- Describe fundamental principles of machine learning on Azure (20–25%)
- Describe features of computer vision workloads on Azure (15–20%)
- Describe features of NLP workloads on Azure (15–20%)
- Describe features of generative AI workloads on Azure (15–20%)

Source: [k21academy Azure AI Certification Guide](https://k21academy.com/azure-aiml/azure-ai-ml-certification-guide-k21-academy/)

**Retirement:** Retires June 30, 2026. Replacement: AI-901 (Azure AI Fundamentals Refreshed), launched in beta April 2026. — [PassITExams AI-900 Study Guide 2026](https://passitexams.com/study-guide/ai-900/)

### AI-102 — Designing and Implementing a Microsoft Azure AI Solution (Associate level)

**Target role:** Azure AI Engineers who build, manage, and deploy AI solutions using Azure Cognitive Services, Azure AI Search, Azure OpenAI Service, and related services.
**Experience level:** Hands-on technical role; proficiency in Python or C#, 1–2 years Azure experience recommended; 60–150 hours preparation depending on background.

**Exam structure:** 100-minute time limit; interactive components possible.
"The exam allows 100 minutes to complete. The exam will be proctored and may have interactive components." — [Microsoft Q&A on AI-102 Exam Pattern](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/answers/questions/1419143/exam-pattern-for-ai-102)

**Passing score:** 700 (Microsoft standard scaled score). — [k21academy Azure AI Certification Guide](https://k21academy.com/azure-aiml/azure-ai-ml-certification-guide-k21-academy/)

**Skill domains (% of exam):**
- Plan and manage an Azure AI solution (15–20%)
- Implement decision-support solutions (10–15%)
- Implement Azure AI vision solutions (15–20%)
- Implement natural language processing solutions (20–25%)
- Implement knowledge mining and document intelligence solutions (10–15%)
- Implement generative AI solutions (10–15%)

Source: [Microsoft Learn AI-102 Study Guide](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/resources/study-guides/ai-102)

**Retirement:** Retires June 30, 2026. Replacement: AI-103. — [Whizlabs: Microsoft Next-Gen AI Certifications 2026](https://www.whizlabs.com/blog/microsofts-next-generation-ai-certifications/)

---

### DP-100 — Designing and Implementing a Data Science Solution on Azure (Associate level)

**Target role:** Azure Data Scientists who apply data science and machine learning to implement and run ML workloads on Azure, primarily using Azure Machine Learning.
**Experience level:** Intermediate; data science background expected; Python, MLflow, Azure ML SDK experience recommended.

**Exam structure:** 100-minute time limit; 40–60 questions; question types include multiple choice, sequence-ordering, scenario-based single answer, and drop/drag formats; interactive components possible.
"The exam will have 40–60 questions that may be in a format like multiple-choice questions, arranged in the correct sequence type questions, scenario-based single answer questions, or drop type of questions. Additionally, you may have interactive components to complete as part of this exam." — [k21academy DP-100 Self Study Guide](https://k21academy.com/azure-data/dp100-self-study-guide/)

**Passing score:** 700 (Microsoft standard scaled score). — [k21academy DP-100 Certification Guide](https://k21academy.com/microsoft-azure/dp-100/azure-data-scientist-certification-guide/)

**Skill domains (% of exam):**
- Design and prepare a machine learning solution (20–25%)
- Explore data and run experiments (35–40%)
- Train and deploy models (20–25%)
- Optimize language models for AI applications (10–15%)

Source: [Microsoft Learn DP-100 Study Guide](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/resources/study-guides/dp-100)

**Retirement:** Retires June 1, 2026. Replacement: DP-750. — [Whizlabs: Microsoft Next-Gen AI Certifications 2026](https://www.whizlabs.com/blog/microsofts-next-generation-ai-certifications/)

## Copilot-Specific Certifications (New 2025–2026)

Microsoft launched a dedicated Microsoft 365 Copilot and Business AI credential track targeting business users and administrators — no coding required.

"Microsoft launched five brand-new Microsoft 365 Copilot credentials — including three certifications (AB-900, AB-730, AB-731) and two Applied Skills." — [Vlad Talks Tech: 5 New Microsoft 365 Copilot Credentials](https://vladtalkstech.com/microsoft-learning-and-credential-news/5-new-microsoft-365-copilot-credentials-explained-ab-900-ab-730-ab-731-applied-skills/)

| Exam | Credential | Level | Target Audience |
|------|-----------|-------|----------------|
| AB-900 | Copilot and Agent Administration Fundamentals | Fundamentals | IT admins, M365 administrators |
| AB-730 | AI Business Professional | Associate | Business users automating workflows |
| AB-731 | AI Transformation Leader | Advanced | Executives and change leaders |

- **AB-900** "proves skills in configuring, securing, and supporting AI-powered Microsoft 365 environments." — [Microsoft Learn: Copilot and Agent Administration Fundamentals](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/copilot-and-agent-administration-fundamentals/)
- **AB-730** "validates the use of generative AI and Copilot to streamline workflows without coding." — [Vlad Talks Tech](https://vladtalkstech.com/microsoft-learning-and-credential-news/5-new-microsoft-365-copilot-credentials-explained-ab-900-ab-730-ab-731-applied-skills/)
- **AB-731** "verifies the ability to define AI business value and lead company-wide adoption strategies." — [Vlad Talks Tech](https://vladtalkstech.com/microsoft-learning-and-credential-news/5-new-microsoft-365-copilot-credentials-explained-ab-900-ab-730-ab-731-applied-skills/)

There is also a **GitHub Copilot** certification available through Microsoft Learn. — [Microsoft Learn: GitHub Copilot Certification](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/github-copilot/)

## Applied Skills (Lab-Based, Role-Specific)

Applied Skills are a newer, shorter-format credential type — lab assessments rather than traditional proctored exams.

"The new credentials are faster to earn, with most lab-based assessments letting you prove specific skills in just 45 minutes." — [Vlad Talks Tech](https://vladtalkstech.com/microsoft-learning-and-credential-news/5-new-microsoft-365-copilot-credentials-explained-ab-900-ab-730-ab-731-applied-skills/)

Key AI-focused Applied Skills include:
- **Create agents in Microsoft Copilot Studio** — "addressing real-world business scenarios." — [Microsoft Learn: Applied Skills – Create agents in Copilot Studio](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/applied-skills/create-agents-in-microsoft-copilot-studio/)
- **Streamline business workflows with AI chat** — showcases ability to use Copilot automation in daily work, manage meetings, draft content, and coordinate teamwork.

Applied Skills do **not** use a pass/fail score in the traditional sense; they assess hands-on task completion in a live lab environment. They are scoped to a single skill area rather than a broad domain, making them stackable and faster to earn than full certifications.

## Inferred Findings

**The Copilot credential track (AB-900/730/731) signals Microsoft's intent to certify business-layer AI adoption separately from cloud-engineering skills.** Based on: (1) the non-coding framing of AB-730/731 — [Vlad Talks Tech](https://vladtalkstech.com/microsoft-learning-and-credential-news/5-new-microsoft-365-copilot-credentials-explained-ab-900-ab-730-ab-731-applied-skills/); (2) Microsoft's "AI Business Professional" page targeting workflow automation without development skills — [Microsoft Learn: AI Business Professional](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/ai-business-professional/). Reasoning: This creates a parallel track for the much larger population of business users vs. the narrower Azure engineer market, likely accelerating enterprise Copilot adoption.

**The 2026 retirement cycle reflects a strategic shift toward generative AI and agentic architectures.** Based on: (1) retirement of AI-900, AI-102, DP-100 all by mid-2026 — [Whizlabs](https://www.whizlabs.com/blog/microsofts-next-generation-ai-certifications/); (2) replacements (AI-901, AI-103, DP-750) explicitly described as "focused on generative AI, agentic architectures, and AI-powered cloud solutions." Reasoning: The existing exams were designed pre-LLM era and do not adequately cover prompt engineering, RAG architectures, or multi-agent systems.
