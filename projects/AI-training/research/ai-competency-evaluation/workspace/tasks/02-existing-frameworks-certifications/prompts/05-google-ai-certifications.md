# Prompt 05: google-ai-certifications

> **Prompt:** Google AI certifications — structure, domains, exam format, comparison to Microsoft

---

## Google's AI Certification Portfolio

Google operates two parallel certification tracks: a **workplace/productivity track** (Google AI Essentials and Generative AI Leader) aimed at general professionals, and a **cloud-engineering track** (Professional Machine Learning Engineer, Cloud GenAI Engineer) aimed at technical practitioners on Google Cloud Platform.

---

### 1. Google AI Essentials

Google AI Essentials is a non-exam, course-based certificate delivered through Coursera and Grow with Google. "Learners can complete the course in under 15 hours, and many learners complete their certificate within one month." — [Google AI Essentials — Grow with Google](https://grow.google/ai-essentials/)

It consists of five modules:

- Introduction to AI
- Maximize Productivity With AI Tools
- Discover the Art of Prompting
- Use AI Responsibly
- Stay Ahead of the AI Curve

Skill domains covered include writing effective AI prompts (few-shot prompting, iterative refinement), identifying AI biases and ethical risks, recognising privacy and security considerations, and determining whether an AI tool is appropriate for a given task. — [Google AI Essentials — Credly](https://www.credly.com/org/googlecareercertificates/badge/google-ai-essentials)

**Exam format:** No formal exam. Completion is through hands-on activities and in-course assessments at a foundational/awareness level. A digital badge is awarded on completion via Credly.

**Target audience:** General workforce, non-technical professionals. No prerequisites.

---

### 2. Generative AI Leader Certification

This credential sits above AI Essentials but below the engineering certifications. "Cloud GenAI Leader is for non-technical roles covering fundamentals and use cases." — [Google Cloud AI ML Learning Paths Overview — Google Skills](https://www.skills.google/paths/1951)

It validates strategic and conceptual understanding of generative AI without requiring coding skills.

---

### 3. Professional Machine Learning Engineer (PMLE)

This is Google Cloud's flagship technical AI certification. The exam assesses six domains:

1. Architect low-code AI solutions
2. Collaborate within and across teams to manage data and models
3. Scale prototypes into ML models
4. Serve and scale models
5. Automate and orchestrate ML pipelines
6. Monitor AI solutions

"About 60% of the exam is about MLOps — the operational side of machine learning." — [Google Professional ML Engineer Quick Facts 2026 — CertificationPractice](https://certificationpractice.com/exam-overviews/google-professional-machine-learning-engineer-quick-facts)

**Exam format:** 50–60 questions (multiple choice and multiple select), 2-hour duration, $200 registration fee, available online-proctored or at test centres, offered in English and Japanese. Recommended experience: 3+ years industry experience including 1+ years on Google Cloud. — [Professional ML Engineer Certification — Google Cloud](https://cloud.google.com/learn/certification/machine-learning-engineer)

**2026 update:** The exam now explicitly covers generative AI tasks including Vertex AI Agent Builder, Model Garden, and RAG architectures. — [Google Professional ML Engineer 2026 Guide — FlashGenius](https://flashgenius.net/blog-article/google-professional-machine-learning-engineer-certification-2026-guide)

**Skill depth:** Expert/practitioner level. Python and SQL proficiency expected for interpreting code snippets, though coding is not directly assessed.

---

### 4. Cloud GenAI Engineer Certification

A professional-level credential focused specifically on generative AI engineering: designing, building, and deploying GenAI solutions using Vertex AI, Gemini API, RAG architectures, model tuning, prompt engineering, evaluation, and production deployment. — [Google AI Certifications in 2026 — NetCom Learning](https://www.netcomlearning.com/blog/breaking-into-ai-and-machine-learning-essential-skills-and-google-cloud-ai-certifications)

## Google vs. Microsoft: Comparative Overview

| Dimension | Google | Microsoft |
|---|---|---|
| **Breadth of audience** | Primarily technical (ML engineers, cloud developers) with one general-workforce entry certificate | Wide spectrum: non-technical managers, business analysts, developers, architects, data scientists |
| **Non-technical coverage** | Google AI Essentials + GenAI Leader (light) | Extensive: AI Business Professional (AB-series, launched Feb 2026), AI-900, Microsoft 365 Copilot credentials |
| **Technical depth** | High: PMLE requires 3+ years GCP experience, covers full MLOps lifecycle | Moderate: DP-100 and AI-102 are strong, but Microsoft is recognised as having "less depth in pure ML compared to rivals" |
| **Platform tie-in** | GCP-specific (Vertex AI, BigQuery, Dataflow, TFX) | Azure-specific (Azure ML, Cognitive Services, OpenAI Service) |
| **Exam format** | Mostly proctored MCQ exams (50–60 questions, 2 hrs); AI Essentials is course-based only | Proctored MCQ exams + Applied Skills (scenario-based labs, no exam) |
| **Generative AI focus** | Strong in 2026 PMLE update; dedicated Cloud GenAI Engineer cert | AI-102 updated, plus separate Azure OpenAI Applied Skills |

"Google Cloud leads in accessible, practical AI training… Google's certifications prioritize cloud-scale ML engineering, best for technical experts handling production systems on GCP." — [How to Choose Between AI Certifications — Learni Group](https://learni-group.com/en/blog/how-to-choose-ai-certifications-google-aws-microsoft-march-2026)

"Microsoft balances accessibility and business application, excelling for Azure users and non-coders aiming for leadership, though with less depth in pure ML compared to rivals." — [How to Choose Between AI Certifications — Learni Group](https://learni-group.com/en/blog/how-to-choose-ai-certifications-google-aws-microsoft-march-2026)

"Microsoft's February 2026 launch wins decisively on business-role coverage with AB-series certifications for non-technical managers and leaders unmatched by Google Cloud, while Google Cloud maintains an edge for professionals in data-heavy, ML-engineering workflows via Vertex AI and BigQuery ML." — [AWS vs Google vs Microsoft AI Certification Comparison — FutureSkillGuides](https://www.futureskillguides.com/aws-vs-google-vs-microsoft-ai-certification-comparison/)

## Inferred Findings

**Google's portfolio has a narrower entry ramp for non-technical learners than Microsoft's.**
- Based on: [Google AI Essentials — Grow with Google](https://grow.google/ai-essentials/) (single general-audience certificate, course-only, no exam) and [How to Choose Between AI Certifications — Learni Group](https://learni-group.com/en/blog/how-to-choose-ai-certifications-google-aws-microsoft-march-2026) (Microsoft "wins decisively on business-role coverage… unmatched by Google Cloud").
- Reasoning: Google has one foundational course-based certificate for general workers and one leadership-level conceptual credential, but no proctored exam-based credential for non-technical roles analogous to Microsoft's AI-900 or the new AB-series certifications. Organisations seeking to certify mixed technical/non-technical cohorts will find Microsoft's ladder more complete at the lower levels.
