# Skanska Data Centers — Research Synthesis

**Date:** April 2026 | **Purpose:** Metier BD intelligence on Skanska as a CSA partner

---

## 1. What Skanska Does in Data Centers (Globally & Norway)

Skanska is one of the world's largest general contractors ($17B revenue, ENR #10 US) and has been building data centers for 30+ years. Their DC business is now the fastest-growing segment, with ~SEK 9.5B (~USD 1.05B) in DC orders in Q4 2025 alone.

**Globally:** Active in US (dominant market; 200+ projects, $6B+ value, ~150 MW/year), UK (via Skanska Rashleigh Weatherfoil MEP subsidiary), Norway (Google/Telemark, NOK 1.7B), and Finland (EUR 95M, 2025). No confirmed DC in Germany, Poland, or Czech Republic.

**Norway specifically:** Skanska Norge and wholly-owned subsidiary Marthinsen & Duvholt won the civil groundworks (NOK 1.1B) and core & shell (NOK 569M) contracts for Google/WS Computing's 240 MW, €600M hyperscale campus in Skien, Telemark. This is Norway's first hyperscale DC and Skanska Norway's first confirmed major DC project. Scope is **civil + structural only — no MEP fit-out**.

**Delivery models used:** Core & shell (Norway, Finland); shell + interior fit-out (US hyperscale); MEP fit-out (UK via SRW); design-build (UK colocation); CMAR (US government).

## 2. Skanska's Ambitions in DC

**Strategic intent is explicit and aggressive:**
- CEO Anders Danielsson: "We are well-positioned to capitalize on the AI boom" (Q4 2025 earnings). "Lots of data centers in the pipeline" (CNBC, May 2025).
- Skanska reorganized its DC capability into **Skanska Advanced Technology (SAT)** in February 2025, expanded in September 2025 to 285+ dedicated professionals, merging DC + semiconductor delivery.
- CFO Jonas Rickberg: "Following our customers very closely" — growth driven by repeat hyperscaler relationships.
- No published numeric DC revenue target, but ~$1B/quarter order intake implies this is a major strategic pillar.
- Nordic DC market = explicit target: Norway confirmed, Finland active, Sweden pursuing.

**Differentiators Skanska claims:** 30-year track record, sustainability leadership (MSCI 'A', SBTi, FT Climate Leader), repeat-client relationships, selective bidding for higher margins (4.1% operating margin vs. industry 2–3%), and a mobile expert deployment model (SAT expertise exported to local units).

## 3. What Triggers Skanska to Pursue DC Projects

**External triggers:**
1. **Hyperscaler investment announcement in a market where Skanska has a local entity** — Google announcing €600M Norway → Skanska Norge wins NOK 1.7B. This is the most documented pattern.
2. **AI-driven capex cycle** — hyperscaler capex projected $443B (2025) → $602B (2026); Skanska sees no slowdown.
3. **Renewable energy availability** — Norway's hydropower and Finland's clean energy are prerequisites; Skanska enters markets where power is committed.

**Internal triggers:**
1. **Existing client relationship** — Almost all large contracts are "repeat client" or "existing client." CFO explicitly: "following our customers."
2. **SAT team utilization** — 285+ specialists need deployment; mobile expert model enables geographic scaling.
3. **Margin discipline** — Only bids where it has competitive advantage, track record, and right organization. Supplier prices locked before bidding.

**Entry pathway:** Preconstruction services → early works → main award → supplemental awards. Never speculative.

## 5. Skanska's Gaps & Weaknesses

### Critical for Metier's BD context:

**A. No MEP capability in Norway** — Skanska Norway does civil + core/shell only. MEP fit-out is sourced separately by Google. The UK has SRW; Norway has nothing equivalent. This creates a structural gap in DC program coordination that the owner (Google/WS Computing) must manage.

**B. Single-client concentration in Norway** — Google/WS Computing is the only confirmed Norwegian DC client. No multi-client portfolio. If Google diversifies its contractor mix for Phase 2, Skanska Norway's DC pipeline is at risk.

**C. Scale gap in US** — Behind Turner, Holder, DPR, Clayco, and HITT in US DC revenue. Cannot compete for the very largest single-award mega-campus programs.

**D. Prefab/modular lag** — DPR leads in hyperscale prefabrication; Skanska SAT is developing this capability but is behind.

**E. Geographic gaps** — No DC capability in Germany (Europe's largest DC market), Poland/Czech Republic (growing at 21% CAGR), or Asia-Pacific.

**F. Late mover in Sweden** — Despite being Swedish HQ, no large-scale DC wins in Sweden until very recently. Now catching up via SAT knowledge transfer.

**G. Not a full turnkey provider in Norway** — Cannot offer civil + MEP + IT as a single scope. Owner must coordinate multiple contractors.

**Consistent 3-stage pattern across all geographies:**
1. A global or repeat client selects Skanska in a new geography
2. Skanska leverages existing local entity + exports SAT expertise via mobile expert groups
3. Repeat/supplemental work follows the initial contract

**No acquisitions for DC capability.** SRW came via a broader conglomerate acquisition (Kvaerner). Growth is entirely organic and client-led.

## 7. Metier's Strategic Positioning vs. Skanska

### Skanska is NOT a competitor to Metier

Skanska builds structures (civil, structural). Metier manages programs on the owner's behalf (PMO, cost/schedule control, governance). Different roles, different commercial relationships.

### The Gap Skanska Creates = Metier's Opportunity

Skanska Norway does civil + core/shell. The owner (Google/WS Computing) must:
- Coordinate the handoff to MEP fit-out contractors
- Manage the overall multi-phase campus program timeline
- Verify cost and schedule independently
- Navigate Norwegian regulatory complexity
- Ensure program governance across 5+ work streams (civil, shell, MEP, IT, commissioning)

This is **precisely Metier's value proposition** — and it's a gap that grows as Norway's DC market expands from one hyperscale campus to multiple projects from multiple operators.

### Primary Targets for Metier in this Context

1. **WS Computing AS (Google)** — the current owner at Gromstul. Already in execution but Phase 2 is coming.
2. **Future hyperscalers entering Norway** — each will face the same coordination challenge.
3. **Norwegian DC operators** (Green Mountain, Lefdal, atNorth) — if they commission large expansion programs with multiple contractors.
