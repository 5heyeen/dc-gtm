# DC Customer Personas & Value Props

## Capital-led DC Developers ("Finansgutta")

**Priority:** 🔴 High

**Examples:** PE/infra funds, family offices, developer-led SPVs

**Pain Points:**
- Bankable feasibility
- Credible delivery plan
- Risk visibility
- Contracts/procurement strategy
- Stakeholder legitimacy

**Typical Needs per Build Phase:** Feasibility, permitting, grid, tender-readiness

---

## Domestic Operators scaling but capacity-constrained

**Priority:** 🔴 High

**Examples:** Bulk, Green Mountain, DigiPlex

**Decision Makers:**
- VP of Construction
- Head of Program Management
- CTO/COO
- Procurement leads

**Pain Points:**
- Delivery bandwidth
- Cost/schedule control across multiple builds
- Governance
- Design-to-build handover consistency
- Procurement & contractor performance
- Program controls

**Typical Needs per Build Phase:** PMO, controls, engineering capacity

---

## Global Operators entering Norway (hyperscalers / global colo)

**Priority:** 🔴 High

**Examples:** Meta, Microsoft, Google, Equinix, DLR, Vantage

**Pain Points:**
- Local permitting/zoning realities
- Grid connection process
- Norwegian supplier market capacity
- Local HSE/labor practices
- Community acceptance
- Local codes/authorities

**Typical Needs per Build Phase:** Local execution partner, permitting, supply chain

---

## High-density AI/HPC compute specialists

**Examples:** AI infra companies, HPC users, research consortia, private AI enterprises

**Pain Points:**
- Extreme power density & heat rejection
- Accelerated timelines
- Supply-chain constraints (electrical/MEP long lead)
- Grid readiness
- Performance validation

**Typical Needs per Build Phase:** Front-end technical definition, long-lead procurement, delivery controls

---

## Utility / Energy-company backed DC platforms ("power-to-data")

**Examples:** Power producers, grid-adjacent developers, energy investors

**Pain Points:**
- Translating "power advantage" into viable DC product
- Grid + site infrastructure definition
- Interface mgmt (utility–developer–tenant)
- Bankability
- ESG/heat reuse claims that survive scrutiny

**Typical Needs per Build Phase:** Powered-site product definition, enabling infra design, interface mgmt

---

## Real-estate / Industrial park developers pivoting to DC

**Examples:** Traditional industrial/logistics developers entering DC

**Pain Points:**
- Underestimating technical requirements (redundancy, MEP, security, fire, resilience)
- DC-specific contracting
- Schedule risk
- Campus infrastructure integration

**Typical Needs per Build Phase:** Owner's engineer, delivery governance, technical requirements

---

## Regulated / Sovereign workloads (finance, health, gov, defense)

**Examples:** Orgs with strict data residency, security classification, audit requirements

**Pain Points:**
- Assurance & compliance documentation
- Security-by-design
- Lifecycle risk
- Vendor governance
- Change control
- Commissioning/validation rigor

**Typical Needs per Build Phase:** Requirements traceability, governance, validation/commissioning

---

## Telecom / Edge / Subsea-adjacent operators

**Examples:** Telcos, ISPs, CDN/edge players, subsea landing ecosystem

**Pain Points:**
- Speed-to-market
- Standardized repeatable designs
- Site constraints
- Network infrastructure integration
- Operational continuity during upgrades

**Typical Needs per Build Phase:** Template-based design, rapid permitting/construction, cutover mgmt

---

## Brownfield expansions, retrofits & life-extension

**Examples:** Operators expanding/upgrading existing sites

**Pain Points:**
- Building while operating (zero downtime)
- Phased execution
- Legacy documentation gaps
- Hidden conditions
- Commissioning complexity
- Capacity management

**Typical Needs per Build Phase:** Phased delivery, risk controls, operational continuity, commissioning
