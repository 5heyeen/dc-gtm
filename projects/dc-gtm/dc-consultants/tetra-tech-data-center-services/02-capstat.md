## 5. Assessment — What the CapStat Changes

### Confirms / Strengthens

- **Project references are real and substantial**: Named clients include VIRTUS, Global Switch, Telehouse/LINX, China Mobile International, Oracle, IBM, NTT RagingWire. The "no named client" finding from prior web research was an artefact of limited public disclosure, not absence of work.
- **European DC portfolio is extensive**: UK is clearly the core of their European DC practice — VIRTUS ×3, Global Switch, North Two/Telehouse, 600MW campus, Project Concorde, Bidder Street, HV routes, Infinity. Continental Europe: NL (800MW campus), Germany (China Mobile Frankfurt), Spain (Microsoft/Mercury).
- **HPBG MEP bench is very large**: 3,500 MEP engineers across brands — Hoare Lea, Glumac, Cosentini, NDY are all credible delivery vehicles, not just the Tetra Tech brand.
- **Uptime Tier III delivery is confirmed**: Multiple named projects to Uptime standards.
- **Immersion / liquid cooling is delivered, not just designed**: The Hub (oil immersion, London), 360MW Texas campus (DTC liquid-cooled), KLON06 Phase 4 (CDU-ready hybrid).

### Does Not Change

- **Nordic / Norway DC presence remains zero**: No Nordic project of any kind in the CapStat.
- **The delivery vehicle is multi-brand HPBG**: Work in the UK and Europe runs through Hoare Lea, RPS, NDY — not the "Tetra Tech" brand directly. This explains why web search found nothing.
- **Metier was the exception, not the rule**: Metier's inclusion in the HPBG list (now struck through) confirms it was a portfolio asset, not integrated into DC delivery.

### Raises New Question

- **Which HPBG brand would cover Norway?** RPS is the European engineering vehicle; Hoare Lea is UK-focused. Neither has Norwegian DC relationships. This is now the key operational question for any alliance.
