# International Engineering Consultancies in the Data Center Market: Service Models and Positioning

## Executive Summary

Major international engineering consultancies have established dedicated data center practices, overwhelmingly positioning themselves **on the owner's side** of the table -- as design consultants, EPCM providers, or owner's engineers. The dominant entry strategy has been **acquisition of specialist DC engineering firms** (WSP/kW MCE, Ramboll/EYP MCF, Accenture/DLB Associates), followed by organic growth of multidisciplinary teams (Arup, COWI). The most common commercial models are fee-for-service design consultancy and EPCM, with owner's engineer/representative roles growing. For Norconsult, the clearest precedents are COWI (Nordic, similar size, design consultant model) and Ramboll/EYP (Nordic parent, acquired specialist US capability).

---

## 1. Arup

**Positioning:** Design consultant and technical advisor, working **directly for DC owners and operators** (not on the contractor side). Arup serves as a global partner to operators including Digital Realty and Equinix.

**Services:** Full lifecycle -- site selection and due diligence, masterplanning, MEP/architectural design, sustainability consulting (net-zero pathways, whole-life carbon), commissioning, and operations optimization. They have Uptime Institute accredited tier designers on staff.

**Scale:** Over **2,000 MW of IT capacity designed** globally for hyperscale and colocation providers. Dedicated DC business leads per region (e.g., Stephen Griffin for Europe). ([Arup Data Centres](https://arup-group.com/expertise/industry/science-industry-and-technology/data-centres.html); [Data Centre Magazine Top 10](https://datacentremagazine.com/top10/top-10-data-centre-consultants))

**Organization:** Multidisciplinary teams within the Buildings Engineering group, with regional DC business leads. MEP delivery led by associate directors.

**Commercial model:** [Inference] Primarily fee-for-service design consultancy on a per-phase basis, consistent with Arup's partnership model across all sectors. Likely also retainer-based advisory for repeat hyperscale clients.

## 2. WSP (post kW MCE + POWER Engineers)

**Positioning:** Multidisciplinary design consultant and engineering services provider, **direct-for-owner**. Ranked among the **top 5 engineering firms serving DC clients** in the US.

**Acquisition path:** Acquired **kW Mission Critical Engineering** (175 employees, Troy NY, founded 2012) in January 2021 for undisclosed sum. kW MCE specialized in MEP, fire protection, controls, telecom, and security design for 24/7 uptime facilities. WSP also acquired POWER Engineers, which brought power plant and grid interconnection expertise. ([WSP acquisition announcement](https://www.wsp.com/en-us/news/2021/wsp-acquires-kw-mission-critical-engineering-and-tk1sc-in-the-united-states); [DCD coverage](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/wsp-acquires-data-center-engineering-firm-kw-mce/))

**Services:** Site analysis and screening, power system design (including dedicated power plants for hyperscale), MEP engineering, liquid cooling design for AI facilities, sustainability/LCA, and speed-to-market delivery. The combined kW MCE + POWER Engineers entity delivers "end-to-end energy solutions" from grid to rack. ([POWER & WSP Data Centers](https://pages.powereng.com/data-centers))

**Organization:** Operates a **South Africa-based Centre of Excellence** (26 engineers) supporting global projects, plus core US teams from kW MCE. [Inference] The kW MCE brand has been maintained as a recognized sub-brand within WSP's Property & Buildings business.

**Commercial model:** [Inference] Design consultant (fee-for-service per phase) for most engagements. For hyperscale clients, likely program-level retainer arrangements given the repeat nature of the work.

## 3. HDR

**Positioning:** Full-service design consultant working **directly for owners**, including Fortune 500 companies, government agencies, and startups. US-headquartered, employee-owned (13,000+ employees globally).

**Services:** Architecture, MEP engineering, site design and permitting, power system design, cybersecurity infrastructure, commissioning, energy management. Notable portfolio includes the **first fuel-cell-powered data center** and simultaneous delivery of multiple DCs nationwide. Also delivers highly secure C4I command centers. ([HDR Data Centers](https://www.hdrinc.com/markets/techmediatelecom-data/data-centers))

**Organization:** Data centers sit within the Tech/Media/Telecom & Data market sector. Active in the DC space since the late 1990s.

**Commercial model:** [Inference] Fee-for-service design consultancy, consistent with HDR's model across architecture and engineering. Likely lump-sum design packages for government/defense clients.

## 4. Jacobs Engineering

**Positioning:** The most explicitly **EPCM-oriented** of the firms studied. Jacobs works on the **owner's side**, providing full engineering, procurement, and construction management as an integrated service.

**Key project:** Selected by **Hut 8** as EPCM lead for the **River Bend AI data center** in Louisiana -- a **245 MW, ~$10 billion campus**. Initial data hall completion Q1 2027, with ~1,000 construction workers at peak. Jacobs also provides EPCM for a confidential HPC facility in the southeastern US. ([Jacobs press release](https://www.jacobs.com/newsroom/press-release/jacobs-appointed-engineering-procurement-and-construction-management-lead); [Construction Dive](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/jacobs-louisiana-data-center-hut8/808553/))

**Services:** "End-to-end EPCM and program management" -- engineering design, procurement management, construction management, commissioning. Over 30 years in mission critical facilities.

**Scale:** $16B annual revenue, 60,000+ employees company-wide. Among the top-ranked DC engineering firms per BD+C rankings.

**Commercial model:** EPCM -- typically a percentage of construction cost or cost-reimbursable with a management fee. [Inference] For a $10B campus like River Bend, the EPCM fee could be in the range of 3-8% of construction value, potentially $300M-800M over the program lifecycle.

## 5. Ramboll / EYP Mission Critical Facilities

**Positioning:** Specialist DC design consultant, working **directly for owners**. The only Nordic-headquartered firm with a dedicated DC acquisition strategy comparable to what Norconsult could pursue.

**Acquisition path:** Ramboll acquired **EYP Mission Critical Facilities** (50 employees) in February 2022. EYP MCF had designed over **70 million sq ft of critically-powered space**, including numerous Tier 3/4 and LEED-certified facilities. ([Ramboll announcement](https://www.ramboll.com/en-us/news/ramboll-acquires-leading-data-center-consulting-company); [DCD coverage](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/ramboll-acquires-eyp-mission-critical-facilities/))

**Services:** Strategic planning, roadmaps, complete engineering design and integration, commissioning, operations and energy consulting. Combined entity has **100 dedicated DC experts globally**.

**Revenue:** Expected DC consulting revenue of **$60 million by 2025** -- a significant figure for a pure consulting practice.

**Commercial model:** [Inference] Fee-for-service design consultancy with strong advisory/strategic planning component. The EYP MCF heritage suggests lump-sum design packages and retainer-based advisory relationships.

---

## 6. COWI

**Positioning:** Nordic design consultant working **directly for DC owners/operators**. Most directly comparable to what Norconsult could do, given similar size (~7,000 employees), Nordic base, and multidisciplinary profile.

**Services:** End-to-end consulting for Nordic DC projects: site selection/acquisition, due diligence, EIA, design, permitting, masterplanning, construction-phase design support, waste heat recovery, and renewable energy integration. They emphasize their **"European Mission Critical" team** with **20+ years of experience**. ([COWI Data Center page](https://www.cowi.com/data-center); [COWI Skygard project](https://www.cowi.com/projects/skygard-data-centre/))

**Key project:** **Skygard data centre in Oslo** -- COWI was contracted to deliver a "complete solution" covering design, sustainable energy, engineering, and architecture for this urban colocation facility (part of a planned 40 MW, 3-site portfolio).

**Commercial model:** [Inference] Design consultant engaged by the owner (Skygard) on a fee-for-service basis covering multiple disciplines. COWI's broad in-house capability (structural, geotechnical, electrical, civil, environmental) allows them to offer a single-contract multidisciplinary design package -- reducing the owner's coordination burden.

## 7. Commercial Model Comparison

| Model | Typical Fee Range | Used By | Description |
|-------|------------------|---------|-------------|
| **Design Consultant** (fee-for-service, per-phase) | 4-8% of construction cost | Arup, WSP, HDR, Ramboll/EYP, COWI | Most common. Per-phase fees (concept, FEED, detailed design). Owner retains all risk. |
| **EPCM** (% of construction cost) | 3-8% of construction cost | Jacobs, (WSP for some) | Consultant manages E, P, and CM on owner's behalf. All subcontracts held by owner. Higher fee but more scope. |
| **Owner's Engineer / Representative** | 1-5% of construction cost, or $100-350/hr | Arup (advisory), Ramboll/EYP (strategic planning) | Retainer or T&M. Technical oversight, vendor evaluation, QA. Often supplements design consultant. |
| **Lump-Sum Design Package** | Fixed price per deliverable | HDR (govt), COWI | Used when scope is well-defined upfront. Lower risk for owner, higher risk for consultant. |

## 8. Key Takeaways for Norconsult

1. **Acquisition is the fast path.** WSP, Ramboll, and Accenture all acquired specialist DC engineering firms (kW MCE: 175 people; EYP MCF: 50 people; DLB Associates: 620 people). This instantly provides credibility, reference projects, and Uptime Institute-certified personnel.

2. **COWI is the closest Nordic precedent.** Similar firm size, Nordic base, multidisciplinary profile. They won the Skygard engagement by offering a "complete solution" -- leveraging breadth across architecture, MEP, energy, and environmental disciplines. Norconsult has equivalent or greater breadth.

3. **The owner's side is where the margin is.** All six firms position primarily as advisors to owners/operators, not as subcontractors to general contractors. This is where strategic relationships, repeat business, and premium fees exist.

4. **Specialist DC credentials matter.** Uptime Institute tier certifications, reference MW designed, and named hyperscale client relationships are table stakes. Norconsult would need to either acquire or rapidly build these credentials.

5. **Revenue potential is significant.** Ramboll targets $60M in DC consulting revenue from ~100 dedicated staff -- implying ~$600K revenue per dedicated DC consultant, well above typical engineering consultancy averages.
