# Research Scope

**Scope:** Data center engineering and design process — how it works, delivery models, standardized vs. custom design, role of engineering consultants (Norconsult), vendor reference designs (Schneider), and contractor/supplier (leverandør) relationships. Focused on positioning Metier's services in the DC engineering value chain.

**Project:** DC GTM (dc-gtm)
**Date:** 2026-04-16

## Specific Questions

1. How has CTS managed to offer standardized designs? Is this something Metier could replicate or offer?
2. Can Metier offer "basis of design" services via Norconsult? What does basis of design mean in the DC context?
3. Can you take a design from Schneider Electric and just do detailed engineering from there? How does that work in practice?
4. How does it work with leverandører (suppliers/contractors)? Norconsult is normally a sub-supplier to MEP contractors — how does this relationship work in DC projects?
5. What is the full engineering and design process for a data center? What are the different delivery models (design-bid-build, design-build, EPC, turnkey, etc.)?

## Context

This research is for Metier, a Norwegian project management consultancy exploring go-to-market strategies for data center services. They want to understand how engineering and design works in the DC space so they can position their services correctly.
