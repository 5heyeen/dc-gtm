# Research Breakdown — DC Engineering & Design Process

**Approved:** 2026-04-16
**Execution mode:** Sequential (one at a time)

## Wave 1 — Independent Research (subtasks 1-5)

### 01 - CTS Standardized Design Model `[independent]`
How CTS Nordics built their standardized design offering (NordicEPOD, Eaton partnership, in-house production, digitalized commissioning). What enables them to do this. Whether a PM consultancy like Metier could replicate or partner.

### 02 - Basis of Design in DC Context `[independent]`
What "basis of design" (BoD) means for data centers, what it typically contains, who produces it, and whether Metier could offer this via Norconsult or similar engineering partners.

### 03 - Vendor Reference Designs to Detailed Engineering `[independent]`
How Schneider Electric (and similar vendors like Vertiv, Huawei) provide reference/modular designs, and the process to go from a vendor design to detailed engineering for a specific site.

### 04 - Leverandør and Engineering Consultant Relationships `[independent]`
How Norconsult and similar engineering consultancies operate in DC projects. Their typical role as sub-to-MEP-contractor vs. owner's engineer. How the leverandør chain works in Norwegian DC construction.

### 05 - DC Engineering and Design Process and Delivery Models `[independent]`
The full engineering lifecycle (concept → schematic → detailed design → construction docs → commissioning). Different delivery models: design-bid-build, design-build, EPC, EPCM, turnkey.

## Wave 2 — Synthesis (subtask 6)

### 06 - Metier Positioning Synthesis `[sequential]`
Based on findings from subtasks 1-5, map where Metier can insert itself in the DC engineering value chain. Which services align to which gaps. Specific recommendations. Depends on 1-5.
