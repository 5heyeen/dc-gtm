# The Investor's Evaluation Framework for Data Center Investments

*Sourced from public investor presentations, fund publications, analyst coverage, and industry reports. March 2026.*

---

## 1. MARKET & DEMAND THESIS

**How investors assess demand-supply:**
- Investors look at absorption rates (MW leased per quarter) vs. new supply coming online
- Pre-lease commitments are the strongest demand signal — investors strongly prefer pre-committed capacity
- Hyperscaler RFPs and letters of intent are treated as near-certain demand
- AI compute demand is the dominant growth driver — data center-related PE deals reached nearly $108 billion in four years to September 2024, more than double the prior four-year period ($50 billion)
- Source: [Morgan Lewis](https://www.morganlewis.com/pubs/2025/09/in-the-know-private-equity-in-data-centers-growth-risks-and-opportunities), [Data Center Frontier](https://www.datacenterfrontier.com/colocation/article/55288208/private-equity-and-infrastructure-what-the-new-investment-landscape-means-for-data-centers)

**Norway-specific demand assessment:**
- 3,300 MW of DC grid reservation requests to Statnett (53 registered DC projects, 3.4 GW reserved) — strong demand signal
- Google, OpenAI/Microsoft, TikTok already committed — validation of Norway as a market
- Nordic DC power demand could rise fourfold by 2032, growing 14-19% annually
- Source: [TechPolicy Press](https://www.techpolicy.press/despite-plenty-of-renewable-energy-data-centers-split-norwegian-society/), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-09-22/sweden-norway-finland-power-firms-to-cash-in-on-europe-data-center-boom)

**Speculative vs. pre-committed risk:**
- Investors strongly prefer build-to-suit or pre-leased capacity
- Speculative builds require higher return thresholds and often milestone-based capital release
- In Norway, speculative developers (Segment 1 "Finansgutta") face higher investor scrutiny on demand risk

---

## 2. POWER & SITE FUNDAMENTALS

**Grid connection criticality:**
- A confirmed, energized grid connection is the single most value-accretive milestone for a DC investment
- The gap between a Statnett grid RESERVATION (conditional, subject to "first mature, first served" and maturity requirements) and an ENERGIZED CONNECTION (certain, physical infrastructure complete) represents the largest risk discount in investor pricing
- Projects with only reservations trade at significant discounts to projects with confirmed power
- Grid execution risk is often underestimated by investors unfamiliar with the Norwegian NVE concession process and substation build-out timelines (5-10 year wait times for high-voltage feeds)
- Source: [TechPolicy Press](https://www.techpolicy.press/despite-plenty-of-renewable-energy-data-centers-split-norwegian-society/)

**Power cost evaluation:**
- Norwegian wholesale electricity price is among the lowest in Europe — key competitive advantage
- Investors model power cost as % of OPEX (typically 30-50% of DC operating costs)
- PPA structures (fixed-price, indexed) provide revenue certainty that lenders value
- Southern Norway (NO2) typically has higher power prices than Northern Norway — affects site economics
- Source: [Statkraft](https://www.statkraft.com/what-we-offer/data-center-solutions/)

**Site characteristics that move the needle:**
- Zoning complete (reguleringsplan approved) — eliminates permitting risk
- Expansion optionality (adjacent land, capacity for future phases)
- "Powered land" with existing grid infrastructure commands a significant premium over greenfield
- Proximity to fiber/IX connectivity
- Municipal support (Kommune actively supportive vs. opposition risk)

## 3. EXECUTION CAPABILITY & DELIVERY RISK

**How important is execution capability?**

This is where Metier's positioning is strongest. Evidence from the market:

- **A one-year energization delay can cut IRR by 400 to 800 basis points.** A six-month slip can cut returns almost in half. For a 60 MW AI DC, one month delay = ~$14.2M in losses.
- Source: [DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/opinions/everyday-counts-on-a-project-the-financial-impact-of-time-on-data-center-construction/), [The Fast Mode](https://www.thefastmode.com/expert-opinion/47210-what-we-learned-in-2025-about-data-center-builds-why-delays-will-persist-in-2026-without-greater-visibility)

- Construction costs for hyperscale facilities run $650-$850 million. A 70/30 debt-equity split means equity checks of $200-250M. Cost overruns directly erode equity returns.
- A 75-basis-point cap-rate expansion reduces exit values by >12%
- Source: [Accordant Investments](https://www.accordantinvestments.com/blog/the-numbers-supporting-30-irr-returns-in-hyperscale-data-center-development)

**What investors look for as "execution readiness":**
- Named, credible project management and EPCM team
- Detailed project execution plan with realistic Norwegian market assumptions
- Contractor prequalification and evidence of market capacity assessment
- Independent cost estimate validation (not just the developer's own numbers)
- Proven delivery framework (stage gates, governance, reporting)
- Evidence that the team understands the LOCAL market — Norwegian contractors, NS contracts, NVE processes

**How investors price execution risk:**
- Higher return requirements for unproven operators in new markets
- Milestone-based capital release (equity drawn down against construction milestones, not upfront)
- Completion guarantees or parent company guarantees
- Independent construction monitoring (lender's requirement)
- Escrow arrangements for completion risk

**Evidence of execution failures:**
- Lead times for transformers (12-14+ months), HV/MV switchgear, generators, and cooling equipment are a major bottleneck
- "What we learned in 2025: Data center builds face persistent delays due to lack of visibility into supply chain, permitting, and workforce availability"
- Source: [The Fast Mode](https://www.thefastmode.com/expert-opinion/47210-what-we-learned-in-2025-about-data-center-builds-why-delays-will-persist-in-2026-without-greater-visibility)

## 4. FINANCIAL STRUCTURE & RETURNS

**IRR targets:**
- Hyperscale DC development can support 30%+ IRR returns with favorable assumptions (strong pre-lease, on-time delivery, competitive construction costs)
- Infrastructure fund target returns typically 10-15% net IRR for core-plus DC assets, 15-25%+ for development/value-add
- Source: [Accordant Investments](https://www.accordantinvestments.com/blog/the-numbers-supporting-30-irr-returns-in-hyperscale-data-center-development)

**Capital stack:**
- Typical 70/30 debt-equity split for established operators with pre-leases
- Higher equity requirements (50/50 or more) for speculative or first-time developers
- Construction debt vs. permanent financing (refinancing at stabilization)

**PE market scale:**
- In 2025, $170 billion in DC asset value required development or permanent financing
- Blackstone: $25B for DC + power in Pennsylvania; KKR/ECP: $50B for DC + power; Microsoft/BlackRock Global AI Infrastructure Fund: $30B target
- Source: [Schneider Electric](https://blog.se.com/datacenter/2025/01/23/unlocking-data-center-ecosystems-the-power-of-private-equity/), [Morgan Lewis](https://www.morganlewis.com/pubs/2025/09/in-the-know-private-equity-in-data-centers-growth-risks-and-opportunities)

**Cost overrun / delay impact on financial model:**
- Every month of delay: lost revenue + continued carry cost of debt/equity
- Cost overruns require additional equity (dilutive) or increased debt (covenant risk)
- For leveraged developers, delays can trigger debt covenant breaches
- Cash flow timing is critical — construction debt has drawn cost; revenue only starts at commissioning

## 5. REGULATORY & POLITICAL RISK ASSESSMENT

**Norway-specific regulatory risk:**
- Energy policy debate is LIVE: tension between DC power consumption vs. domestic industry, electrification, green hydrogen
- 53 DCs requesting 3.4 GW from Statnett — potential for political pushback on DC power allocation
- NVE (Norwegian Water Resources and Energy Directorate) controls concession process — regulatory risk if policies change
- Municipal-level: attitude varies widely. Some municipalities actively court DC operators; others resist
- Source: [TechPolicy Press](https://www.techpolicy.press/despite-plenty-of-renewable-energy-data-centers-split-norwegian-society/), [AlgorithmWatch](https://algorithmwatch.org/en/data-centers-norway-renewable-energy-conflict/)

**EU Taxonomy / ESG:**
- Increasingly important for institutional investors
- EU Taxonomy alignment affects "green" financing eligibility (lower cost of debt)
- CSRD reporting requirements create due diligence obligations
- Investors seeking ESG-compliant assets may prefer Norwegian DCs (renewable power) IF sustainability reporting is robust

## 6. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING & MOAT

**What makes a DC investment defensible:**
- **Grid capacity scarcity** — 5-10 year wait for new high-voltage connections creates a barrier to entry for new competitors. Operators with confirmed grid connections have a natural moat.
- **Site location** — proximity to connectivity, population centers, or unique advantages (hydropower, cool climate)
- **Municipal relationships** — an operator with a supportive Kommune has a faster path to expansion than a newcomer
- **Sustainability credentials** — renewable power, waste heat reuse, low PUE — increasingly important for tenant selection and investor preference
- **Tenant lock-in** — long-term lease agreements (5-15 years) with creditworthy tenants

**Oversupply concern in Norway:**
- 3.4 GW reserved but grid capacity is the bottleneck — oversupply is constrained by grid availability, not land or demand
- Current installed capacity ~300 MW vs. 3.4 GW reserved — enormous growth potential but physically constrained by power delivery timeline

## 7. EXIT STRATEGY

**Common exit routes:**
- Sale to another PE/infrastructure fund (secondary buyout) — most common
- Sale to a REIT (Digital Realty, Equinix, etc.)
- Sale to hyperscaler (build-to-suit conversion)
- IPO (less common, requires scale)
- Hold-to-income (core infrastructure funds)

**What makes an asset attractive to the next buyer:**
- Stabilized, long-term lease agreements with creditworthy tenants
- Quality of construction (design, documentation, commissioning quality)
- Expansion potential (remaining capacity, adjacent land)
- Sustainability credentials (increasingly important)
- Clean documentation and operational readiness

**Build quality impact on exit value:**
- Poor construction quality, incomplete documentation, or unresolved defects can reduce exit value by 5-15% or delay exit entirely
- Institutional buyers (especially REITs) conduct thorough technical due diligence
- A well-documented, professionally managed build commands a premium at exit

**Norwegian examples:**
- Lefdal Mine: Columbia Threadneedle acquired (2020), now selling to 3i Infrastructure for €300M — a successful exit cycle
- Bulk: BGO equity investment (€350M) positioned for future exits as assets stabilize
- Source: [DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/3i-infrastructure-acquires-majority-stake-in-norways-lefdal-mine-datacenter/), [BGO](https://bgo.com/press-release/bulk-infrastructure-announces-350msup1/sup-equity-investment-by-bgo-and-plans-for-over-1bn-of-investments-by-2026-to-grow-sustainable-data-centre-l-1717514270601)
