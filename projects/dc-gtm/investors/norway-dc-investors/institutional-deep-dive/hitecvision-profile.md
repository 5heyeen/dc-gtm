## 2. Energy-to-Digital Thesis

HitecVision has decided that **all new investments will be in companies that contribute to the energy transition**. Their strategic rationale for pivoting from oil & gas to digital infrastructure rests on several pillars:

1. **Energy-digital convergence:** Data centers are among the largest energy consumers. HitecVision sees digital infrastructure as sitting at "the intersection of digitalization and the green transition."
2. **Norwegian competitive advantage:** Norway's surplus of low-cost renewable power (primarily hydro), cool climate, and political stability create ideal conditions for sustainable data center operations.
3. **Security demand:** The current geopolitical situation increases the need for secure data storage on Norwegian soil — creating demand for Norwegian-owned, high-security DC operators.
4. **Connectivity infrastructure:** Data centers need fiber connectivity — HitecVision's Altibox Carrier investment provides the fiber backbone connecting Nordic DCs to Europe.
5. **Capital-intensive growth:** DCs and fiber networks require exactly the type of large capital deployments (EUR 150-500M) that match HitecVision's investment model.

**Key quote:** HitecVision describes the Altibox Carrier investment as "an opportunity to build a scalable platform at the intersection of digitalization and the green transition."

### Skygard Facilities

- **Phase 1 (Hovinbyen/Haraldrud, Oslo):** 20 MW, construction started March 2024, operations launched H1 2025.
- **Full Oslo cluster target:** 40 MW across three planned data centers in the capital region.
- **Acquisitions:** In December 2025, Skygard acquired OSL3 and OSL5 data center facilities from Orange Business Services Norway (located in Grorud and Lørenskog), significantly expanding existing capacity.

### Strategic Positioning

Skygard differentiates on: Norwegian ownership, energy efficiency (aims to be Norway's most energy-efficient DC), high-security credentials, and surplus heat recapture into Oslo's district heating network. Designed for customers with high security requirements (government, defense, critical infrastructure).

## 10. Future DC Plans — Assessment

### Evidence of Further DC Ambitions

1. **Skygard expansion:** Already expanded beyond Phase 1 via the Orange Business acquisition. Plans for three Oslo data centers totaling 40MW, plus "further growth ambitions for the Nordics."
2. **Altibox Carrier:** Building the fiber connectivity backbone specifically to serve the DC sector — you don't invest in DC fiber connectivity unless you expect DC volume growth.
3. **Hiring signal:** Recruiting from Brookfield Infrastructure Partners (digital infrastructure specialist).
4. **Fund capacity:** NEF II at USD 1.64B has significant undeployed capital.
5. **Thesis alignment:** Data centers sit perfectly at the intersection of HitecVision's three competencies: energy, infrastructure, and the green transition.

### Inference (Medium-High Confidence)

HitecVision is likely building a **Nordic digital infrastructure platform** with data centers (Skygard) and fiber connectivity (Altibox Carrier) as the two pillars. Further DC investments — either through Skygard expansion or new platform acquisitions — are highly probable given:
- The strategic narrative (energy-digital convergence)
- The capital available (NEF II)
- The infrastructure play (Altibox Carrier as DC enabler)
- Market timing (Nordic DC market growing at ~29% CAGR)

**Risk factor:** HitecVision is an energy investor learning the DC business, not a DC specialist. Execution risk on complex DC construction projects could be a pain point — and a Metier opportunity.
