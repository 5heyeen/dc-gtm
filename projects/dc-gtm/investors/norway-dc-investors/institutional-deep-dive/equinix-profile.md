## 2. Nordic Strategy

### Pre-atNorth Nordic Presence

Before the atNorth acquisition, Equinix operated **8 data centers** in the Nordics:
- **Helsinki, Finland:** 5 facilities
- **Stockholm, Sweden:** 3 facilities
- **Norway:** No direct presence
- **Denmark:** No direct presence
- **Iceland:** No direct presence

**Nordic MD (Sweden):** Maria Sundvall (joined 2017), overseeing Swedish operations.

### Post-atNorth: Transformative Nordic Expansion

The atNorth acquisition (announced Feb 27, 2026) is Equinix's largest ever Nordic move, adding:
- 8 operational data centers across Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, and Sweden
- Multiple sites under development
- ~800 MW development pipeline (coming online over next 5 years)
- 1 GW of secured power capacity
- Liquid cooling capabilities for AI/HPC workloads
- First entry into Norway, Denmark, and Iceland

**Strategic rationale:**
1. Nordic region offers abundant renewable energy and natural cooling
2. AI/HPC demand requires high-density, liquid-cooled facilities
3. Data sovereignty demand from European enterprises
4. Immediately accretive to AFFO per share
5. Extends Equinix's interconnection ecosystem into new Nordic markets

## 4. xScale Program

### Overview

xScale is Equinix's hyperscale joint venture program, enabling large-scale deployments for hyperscale cloud and AI companies alongside Equinix's existing IBX retail colocation platform.

### JV History & Partners

| JV | Year | Partners | Size | Geography |
|----|------|----------|------|-----------|
| **xScale Europe/APAC** | 2019 | GIC (Singapore sovereign wealth fund) | USD 7.5B+ committed | UK, Japan, France, Brazil, South Korea, etc. |
| **xScale US** | Oct 2024 | GIC (37.5%), CPP Investments (37.5%), Equinix (25%) | USD 15B+ | US — multi-100MW campuses |
| **atNorth JV** | Feb 2026 | CPP Investments (60%), Equinix (40%) | USD 4B | Nordics (Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, Sweden) |

### Total xScale Commitment

- Pre-2024 xScale portfolio: ~USD 8B committed, 725+ MW capacity across 35+ facilities
- US JV (2024): USD 15B+, adding 1.5+ GW of new US capacity
- atNorth (2026): USD 4B+
- **Total xScale/JV commitments: ~USD 27B+**

### How xScale Works

1. Equinix identifies land/sites adjacent to or near existing IBX facilities
2. JV partners (sovereign wealth funds, pension funds) provide majority of equity capital
3. JV takes on project-level debt
4. Equinix operates and manages the facilities
5. Hyperscale customers get dedicated capacity while accessing Equinix's interconnection ecosystem
6. Equinix keeps asset-light balance sheet while growing capacity

### How atNorth Fits

The atNorth acquisition follows the xScale playbook: institutional capital (CPP) provides majority funding, Equinix provides operational expertise and customer relationships, assets operate semi-independently. However, atNorth is an **acquisition** of existing assets, not a greenfield development — it's a faster route to Nordic capacity than building from scratch.

**Note:** ADIA (Abu Dhabi Investment Authority) was not found as a direct xScale partner. The primary JV partners are GIC and CPP Investments.

## 6. Nordic Expansion Pipeline (Beyond atNorth)

No specific announced Nordic expansions **beyond** the atNorth deal were identified. However:

- atNorth itself has an **800 MW development pipeline** across Nordic countries that will come online over 5 years
- The USD 4.2B financing package includes capital for expanding atNorth's portfolio
- Equinix's existing Stockholm and Helsinki facilities may see incremental expansion
- The atNorth deal gives Equinix a platform for further Nordic M&A or greenfield development

**Inference:** The atNorth platform is likely to serve as Equinix's primary Nordic expansion vehicle for the foreseeable future, with organic buildout of the 800 MW pipeline being the near-term focus.

## 10. Summary: Equinix in the Norwegian Context

### What Equinix Brings to Norway

1. **Global interconnection ecosystem:** 499,000+ interconnections, attracting hyperscalers and enterprises to Norwegian facilities
2. **Massive capital access:** $27B+ in JV commitments, $3.5B+ annual CapEx
3. **AI/HPC expertise:** Liquid cooling, high-density deployments
4. **Customer base:** 10,000+ customers globally, many needing Nordic presence
5. **Sustainability leadership:** 100% renewable in Europe, AAA ESG ratings

### What Equinix Lacks in Norway

1. **Local track record:** No prior Norway presence
2. **Local relationships:** Dependent on atNorth's existing team
3. **Norwegian ownership narrative:** Cannot compete with Skygard's "Norwegian ownership, Norwegian security" pitch
4. **Regulatory familiarity:** New to Norwegian permitting, zoning, grid connection processes
5. **Construction execution:** 800 MW pipeline requires massive construction execution in Nordic markets

### Implications for Metier

Equinix/atNorth's 800 MW Nordic development pipeline represents a significant opportunity for project management and advisory services. Key pain points likely include:
- Complex multi-country construction programs
- Grid connection and power procurement in Norwegian markets
- Local regulatory navigation
- Scaling construction teams in a tight Nordic labor market
- Integration of atNorth operations with Equinix standards
