# Norconsult Data Center Capabilities: Research Report

## Key Findings Summary

1. **Norconsult has a nascent but growing DC practice** positioned under its "Industry" business area, leveraging expertise from power-intensive industrial facilities rather than a dedicated mission-critical/DC team.
2. **The Bulk N01 EPCM contract is their flagship (and likely only major) DC engagement.** The scope covers power infrastructure engineering, project management, procurement, construction management, HSE, and systematic commissioning for a NOK 3B+ campus.
3. **Their DC engineering strength is heavily weighted toward power infrastructure** -- high-voltage grid connections (420 kV / 145 kV transformer stations, transmission lines) rather than white-space DC design (cooling architecture, UPS, power distribution within halls, DCIM).
4. **Norconsult acquired Metier Group in December 2025 for NOK 480M.** Metier now operates as an independent entity within Norconsult. This fundamentally changes the competitive dynamic: Metier's DC-oriented project management capabilities are now inside Norconsult.
5. **Compared to international competitors (COWI, Ramboll, Sweco), Norconsult lacks a dedicated DC/mission-critical practice.** COWI has a "European Mission Critical team" with 20+ years of DC experience. Ramboll acquired EYP Mission Critical Facilities (300+ dedicated DC experts). Sweco has a dedicated DC design team led from the UK.

## 1. Norconsult's DC Project Portfolio

**Confirmed DC projects:**

- **Bulk N01 Datasentercampus, Stoleheia/Vennesla** -- EPCM contract (2023-2026). This is Norconsult's major DC reference. The scope focuses on power supply infrastructure: engineering two transformer stations (420 kV / 145 kV), new 420 kV lines, 145/24 kV transformers, switchgear, and cable connections between stations. Full EPCM responsibilities include project management, engineering management, procurement, construction management, HSE/SHA, and systematic completion. ([Norconsult press release](https://norconsult.no/aktuelt/norconsult-soerger-for-groenn-kraftforsyning-til-bulk-infrastructure-groups-nye-datasenter-i-vennesla/); [Byggeindustrien](https://www.bygg.no/norconsult-fikk-datasenter-kontrakt/1548271!/); [Norsk Infrastruktur](https://www.norskinfrastruktur.no/norconsult-sa-rger-for-gra-nn-kraftforsyning-til-bulk-infrastructure-groups-nye-datasenter-i-vennesla))

- **Datasentral Telemark (Gromstul, Skien)** -- Norconsult prepared the regulatory plan (reguleringsplan) with environmental impact assessment for a large DC site (~180 hectares), on behalf of Statkraft AS. This was a planning/permitting engagement, not engineering or EPCM. The site was subsequently acquired by Google. ([Norconsult project page](https://norconsult.no/prosjekter/datasentral-telemark/); [Skien kommune](https://www.skien.kommune.no/by-og-naeringsutvikling/datasenter-paa-gromstul/informasjon-om-datasenter/))

**[Inference]** No other DC-specific projects were found in public sources. The Bulk N01 contract appears to be Norconsult's first and only full EPCM engagement for a data center. The Gromstul project was limited to zoning/planning work.

## 2. Norconsult's DC-Specific Capabilities

Norconsult positions its DC services under the **"Industry"** business area, specifically the sub-category "Datasentre" (Data Centres). Their website states they "assist developers of data centres with interdisciplinary expertise and experience from power-intensive industrial facilities and associated security requirements." ([Norconsult DC services page](https://norconsult.com/services/industry/data-centres/))

**Disciplines demonstrably applied to DC work:**
- High-voltage power systems engineering (420 kV/145 kV transformer stations, transmission lines, switchgear, cable systems) -- this is their core strength, rooted in decades of hydropower/grid work ([Norconsult Power Systems](https://norconsult.com/services/renewable-energy/power-systems/))
- Regulatory/planning/environmental impact assessment
- Project management and EPCM delivery
- Construction management, HSE, systematic completion

**Disciplines available in-house but not confirmed on DC projects:**
- MEP-mechanical (HVAC/cooling) -- Norconsult has VVS (HVAC) departments in regional offices
- MEP-electrical (low-voltage, distribution within buildings)
- Structural engineering
- Fire safety (brannrådgivning)
- Geotechnical/environmental
- BIM -- Norconsult has strong BIM capabilities demonstrated on other project types ([Norconsult BIM](https://norconsult.com/services/digitalisation/bim/); [Autodesk case study](https://www.autodesk.com/customer-stories/norconsult-bim-story))

**[Inference]** Norconsult does **not** appear to have dedicated DC-specific competence in: power distribution design within data halls, cooling architecture (hot/cold aisle, liquid cooling), UPS/generator system design, BMS/DCIM, or Tier classification/Uptime Institute standards. Their DC capability is an extension of their energy infrastructure and industrial facility expertise, not a purpose-built mission-critical practice.

## 3. The Bulk N01 EPCM Scope in Detail

The EPCM contract covers the full project lifecycle for the power supply infrastructure to the N01 campus:

| Responsibility | Details |
|---|---|
| **Engineering** | Design of two transformer stations (420 kV / 145 kV transformers), new 420 kV lines, 145/24 kV transformers, associated switchgear, cable connections |
| **Project management** | Project management and steering (prosjektledelse og styring) across all phases |
| **Procurement** | Procurement management (anskaffelse) |
| **Construction management** | Follow-up of project suppliers, construction management (byggeledelse) |
| **HSE** | Health, safety & environment (HMS/SHA) |
| **Commissioning** | Systematic completion (systematisk ferdigstillelse) |

The project runs 2023-2026. The campus will ultimately accommodate up to 800,000 sqm of data halls. Investment for this phase is estimated at NOK 3B+. ([BuildPilot](https://www.buildpilot.com/en/project/datacentercampus-utviklingstrinn-stoleheia-230555461); [Bulk Infrastructure](https://bulkinfrastructure.com/newsroom/bulk-infrastructure-investerer-ytterligere-milliardbel%C3%B8p-og-bygger-et-av-norges-st%C3%B8rste-datasenteranlegg-p%C3%A5-n01-datasentercampus-p%C3%A5-agder))

**[Inference]** The EPCM scope appears focused on the **power supply infrastructure** (grid connection, transformer stations, transmission) rather than the data halls themselves. It is unclear whether Norconsult's EPCM role extends to the white-space design and construction of the actual DC buildings, or whether this is handled by separate contractors. The phrasing "kraftforsyning" (power supply) in multiple sources suggests their primary engineering contribution is the grid connection and power delivery infrastructure.

## 5. Organizational Structure

Norconsult (as of 2024) is organized into **nine business areas**, with services delivered across **20+ specialist disciplines**. Key business areas include: Infrastructure, Buildings & Architecture, Renewable Energy, Industry & Water, and operations in Sweden, Denmark, Iceland, and Poland. The company has approximately **7,000 employees** across 140+ offices. ([About Norconsult](https://norconsult.com/about-us/); [New organization announcement](https://norconsult.com/news/new-organization-and-group-management-in-norconsult/))

Data centre services sit within the **"Industry"** business area, led by EVP Ola Kveli (Renewable Energy, Industry & Water). There is **no dedicated "mission critical" or "data center" practice** visible in public materials -- DC is positioned as a sub-service within industry.

**The Metier acquisition (closed December 2025):** Metier Group (250 employees, NOK 494M revenue) now operates as an independent entity within Norconsult. Metier specializes in project management, early-phase consulting, business development, digitalisation, and PM training. ([Norconsult investor announcement](https://investor.norconsult.com/stock-exchange-and-press-releases/norconsult-to-acquire-metier-group-as/); [Closing announcement](https://investor.norconsult.com/stock-exchange-and-press-releases/norconsult-closing-of-acquisition-of-metier-group-as/))

## Implications

**Critical finding: Norconsult acquired Metier in December 2025.** This has three major implications:

1. **Metier's DC project management capabilities now sit inside a competitor.** Any Metier DC engagements going forward may naturally pull in Norconsult's engineering disciplines, and vice versa.

2. **Norconsult's gap is white-space DC design, not PM.** With Metier's PM capabilities added, Norconsult's remaining gap is DC-specific engineering (cooling architecture, power distribution within halls, UPS/redundancy design, DCIM). This is precisely where international specialists (COWI, Ramboll/EYP) differentiate.

3. **The complement vs. overlap question has changed.** Pre-acquisition, Metier could position as PM complement to Norconsult's engineering. Post-acquisition, external DC operators engaging Metier are engaging a Norconsult subsidiary, which shifts the relationship fundamentally.
