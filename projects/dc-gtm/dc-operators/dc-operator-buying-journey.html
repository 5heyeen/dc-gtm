Non-linear feedback loops & re-entry points

↩ Feasibility rejected → return to Opportunity Recognition; re-scope segment, site, or power assumption
↩ Grid connection delay (Statnett queue) → process pauses at Requirements; restarts 12–18 months later
↩ Budget cut / IC rejection → return to Strategy & Feasibility to re-model IRR or reduce scope
⤵ Anchor tenant signs LOI → jumps from Recognition directly to Requirements (collapses Stage 2)
↩ Contractor market unavailable / supply chain flag → return to Delivery Model (re-scope EPC vs. EPCM)
↩ New CEO or leadership change → can restart journey from Stage 1 or 2 as mandate is re-evaluated
⤵ PE exit pressure / fund deadline → accelerates from any stage directly to FID; compresses due diligence
↩ Permitting rejected → return to Requirements; may require full site re-selection and OPR revision

How different operator archetypes enter the journey

Finance-First Developer ("Finansgutta")

Entry: PE fund closes / thesis approved

Starts at Opportunity Recognition with capital ready but no execution team. Hires VP Construction mid-journey. Moves fast to FID but risks under-specifying requirements.

Pain: No PM capability, no procurement expertise, IC pressure on timeline

Utility / Energy-Backed Platform

Entry: Power asset or grid capacity confirmed

Has the infrastructure advantage (land, power, grid connection). Jumps into feasibility quickly but lacks DC market knowledge. Strategy choice (segment, product) is the critical blocker.

Pain: Unclear market segment, DC-specific knowledge gaps, translating power into product

Domestic Operator Scaling (Bulk, Green Mountain)

Entry: Customer demand signal / capacity constraint

Has operational credibility. Skips Opportunity Recognition — enters at Strategy / Requirements. Pain is delivery bandwidth: existing team is fully occupied running the operating site.

Pain: Design-to-build handover, cost/schedule control, procurement performance

Global Operator Entering Norway (Hyperscaler / Colo)

Entry: Market analysis decision at HQ level

Has capital and delivery playbook but needs local expertise. Often enters at Requirements with a fixed design template that may conflict with Norwegian permitting, grid reality, and HSE rules.

Pain: Local permitting, Statnett grid queue, contractor market, community acceptance

Real-Estate / Industrial Park Developer Pivoting

Entry: Site opportunity or customer enquiry

Has land, construction experience, and capital access but underestimates DC technical requirements. Often discovers the complexity late — in Requirements or even Contractor Selection.

Pain: DC-specific contracting, MEP interfaces, schedule risk, cost overruns

Regulated / Sovereign Operator (Gov, Defence, Finance)

Entry: Mandate or compliance requirement

Journey is driven by a regulatory mandate or government directive. Buying group is large and slow-moving. Security clearance requirements constrain the contractor pool at Partner Selection.

Pain: Cleared contractor scarcity, security-by-design, governance documentation, change control
