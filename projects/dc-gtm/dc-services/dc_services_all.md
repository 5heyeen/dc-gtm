### Electricity Tax & Fiscal Structure Optimization

**Theme:** Financial / Commercial, Regulatory
**Potential for Metier:** No
**Potential for Norconsult:** No

Navigating Norway's DC-specific fiscal landscape: standard electricity tax now ~16.58 øre/kWh after reduced DC rate removed in 2023 (was 0.55 øre/kWh — a 30x increase). No CO2 compensation for DCs unlike traditional power-intensive industry. ICT equipment eligible for 2-year accelerated depreciation. Property tax (eiendomsskatt) varies by municipality — former 'verk og bruk' regime reformed but tax clarity should be negotiated pre-construction. Resource rent tax on hydro/wind PPAs affects counterparty willingness. Operators modeling on pre-2023 tax treatment will materially underestimate OPEX.

### Electricity Zone Selection & Power Economics (NO1–NO5)

**Theme:** Financial / Commercial, Regulatory
**Potential for Metier:** Yes
**Potential for Norconsult:** Yes

Strategic advisory on selecting among Norway's five electricity bidding zones (NO1–NO5), which create fundamentally different economic propositions — Q2 2025 prices ranged from 5 øre/kWh (NO4) to 68 øre/kWh (NO2), a 12–14x differential. Zone choice is effectively irreversible: PPA strategy, grid queue, and site acquisition all flow from it. Operators from Ireland/Netherlands/US treat Norway as a single electricity market — it is not. Must model zone-specific power costs, transmission bottleneck risk (e.g. Ofoten corridor capped at 1,200 MW), cable interconnector exposure (NO2 tracks continental prices), and potential policy shifts (Stortinget consumption cap debate).

### Uavhengig Kontroll (Independent Third-Party Review)

**Theme:** Governance & QA, Regulatory
**Client Archetypes:** Colocation Operator, PE-Backed Developer, Hyperscaler, EPC Contractor
**Competitive Threat:** Medium
**Potential for Metier:** No

Mandatory independent third-party control under SAK10 / TEK17 for construction quality verification. On Skygard OSL1, Rambøll A/S serves as uavhengig kontrollør reviewing structural, fire safety, and other critical technical areas. Required by Norwegian building regulations for specified responsibility areas — the controller must be independent of the firm that designed/executed the work. Covers RI Bygg (structural), RI Brann (fire), geotechnical, and other mandatory control areas. For DC projects, this extends to critical power infrastructure, fire suppression systems, and structural load capacity for heavy equipment. Not a Metier service but a required role that creates engagement opportunities for engineering firms.
