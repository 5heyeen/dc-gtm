# Mitchell McDermott's data center consulting services: a complete inventory

Mitchell McDermott (MMCD), an Irish construction consultancy founded in 2015, operates one of Europe's most comprehensive data center consulting practices spanning every phase from portfolio strategy through live operations. Their dedicated Mission Critical division — led from offices in Dublin, Limerick, London, Amsterdam, and Kuala Lumpur — delivers **four core service lines** (Project Management, Quantity Surveying/Cost Management, Funder Advisory, and Specialist Services) plus newly launched Sustainability Services, all tailored for data center clients. A 2025 private equity investment from MML Capital specifically targets expanding this European DC pipeline. What follows is a granular inventory of every identified service, organized by a lifecycle framework that covers both corporate/portfolio-level and project-level activities.

---

## Corporate and portfolio-level services span strategy through investment

Mitchell McDermott offers several services aimed at operators building their company, evaluating portfolios, and making investment decisions — activities that precede or run parallel to individual project execution.

### Market intelligence and benchmarking

MMCD maintains a **proprietary cost database called Feasa©** that powers much of their strategic advisory. Their global benchmarking service draws on an extensive database of mission-critical projects to compare cost, programme, and design efficiency across data center builds worldwide. They publish annual **Data Centre InfoCards** with market intelligence, including a DC-specific "Jargon Buster" and sector trend analysis. This capability helps operators evaluate market positioning, validate development budgets, and identify cost anomalies before committing capital.

### Due diligence and investment advisory

Their due diligence practice explicitly names data centers as a core focus sector. Services include **pre-acquisition technical due diligence** (evaluating sites, facilities, and development potential before purchase), construction due diligence for financial institutions, red flag reports, contractor and developer reviews, statutory consent reviews, construction programme benchmarking, and development budget appraisals covering both hard and soft costs. For funders specifically, the Funder Advisory team provides project monitoring, monthly drawdown recommendations, cash flow monitoring, invoice tracking, construction progress reporting, and risk assessments — all applicable to DC investment portfolios.

### Sustainability and ESG services

A newly launched dedicated sustainability offering includes **Whole Life Carbon Assessments**, Embodied and Operational Carbon Assessments, Carbon Baseline Establishment, Carbon Forecasting & Reduction Modelling, Carbon Engineering, Digital Carbon Dashboards, **EU Taxonomy Alignment**, Low Carbon Material Specification, Sustainability Benchmarking, Environmental Product Declaration Tracking, Green Finance Alignment, and **Certification Pathway Management** for HPI, BREEAM, and LEED. These apply directly to data center operators navigating ESG reporting requirements and green financing.

## Specialist and cross-cutting capabilities add further depth

Beyond the phase-specific services, several capabilities cut across multiple project stages or serve specialist functions that data center operators should note.

**Next-generation AI and liquid cooling expertise** stands out as a distinct differentiator. MMCD explicitly states it works "at the cutting edge of next generation AI deployment in data centre construction, with **direct-to-chip liquid cooling**." This covers integrating advanced cooling technologies for high-density AI workloads, future-proofing facilities against evolving hardware demands, and reducing cooling costs — a capability increasingly critical as rack densities climb above **30 kW per rack** in AI-focused facilities.

Their **Funder Advisory division** operates as a distinct service line serving financial institutions that fund data center construction. Key sub-services include red flag reports, pre-acquisition due diligence, initial due diligence reports, statutory compliance reviews, benchmarking via Feasa©, programme assessment, cash flow monitoring, construction monitoring with monthly site visits, invoice tracking and verification, facility agreement review, commercial insurance advice, development budget assessment (hard costs, soft costs), contractor review, developer review, and construction contract review for financial impact. This makes MMCD a dual-sided player — advising both DC developers and their lenders.

The **Specialist Services** group handles insolvency and restructuring (relevant for distressed DC assets), independent commercial dispute assessment, dispute resolution support, PPP/PFI advice, project audits, bid management, insurance reinstatement valuations, and **capital allowances** — the latter being particularly valuable for DC operators seeking tax-efficient treatment of substantial equipment investments.

## Five service pillars define their DC practice structure

Stepping back from the granular detail, Mitchell McDermott's data center consulting practice organizes around five pillars, each containing the services detailed above:

1. **Project Management** — 22 sub-services from brief development through post-completion, with DC-specific overlays for site selection, utilities/power analysis, permitting, OFCI procurement, construction oversight, and commissioning coordination
2. **Cost Management / Quantity Surveying** — 12 sub-services powered by the Feasa© proprietary database, covering benchmarking, cost planning, design-stage cost control, tendering, construction cost management, and final account negotiation
3. **Funder Advisory** — Project monitoring and due diligence services for financial institutions funding DC developments, with approximately 21 distinct sub-services
4. **Specialist Services** — Dispute resolution, insolvency, capital allowances, insurance, bid management, and value management
5. **Sustainability Services** — 13 carbon and ESG-focused sub-services including EU Taxonomy Alignment and certification pathway management for BREEAM/LEED

Their case study portfolio demonstrates these services in action across projects ranging from **6.84 MW single-building facilities** to **61 MW colocation wholesale campuses**, with geographic reach spanning Ireland, the UK, the Netherlands, broader Europe, the Middle East, and Asia. The firm's explicit mention of DC conversions (repurposing existing buildings), substation delivery, and power line infrastructure work on LinkedIn further extends their service envelope beyond what the main website pages formally catalogue.
