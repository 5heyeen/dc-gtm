# Prompt 03: focus-always-data-centers-or-broader

> **Prompt:** Was MMCD always focused on data centers, or did it start with a broader construction/engineering scope? Look for earliest press mentions, early project case studies on their website, and any archive versions of their website (Wayback Machine). When did "data center" language first appear prominently in their materials?

---

## Answer: Not Exclusively DC from Day One — Multi-Sector Founding

Mitchell McDermott was **not exclusively a data center firm from founding**. The evidence clearly shows a multi-sector construction consultancy launch:

### Evidence of Multi-Sector Launch (2015)

Staff bios on the MMCD careers/about pages reveal the early hires worked across multiple sectors:
- Staff joining in **2015** are described as working on "residential, hospitality, retail, commercial and student accommodation" alongside data centers
- One 2015-era hire "specialises in mission critical, life sciences & data centre projects" — indicating this was already a focus from the start but alongside other sectors
- Another 2015 hire "predominantly works on residential, hospitality, retail and commercial projects" — no data center mention at all

The firm's original strapline described it as delivering "construction projects across the Commercial, Industrial, Pharmaceutical, Data Centre, Residential, and Cultural sectors."

- Source: [Mitchell McDermott — Careers page (staff bios)](https://mitchellmcdermott.com/careers/)
- Source: [Mitchell McDermott — About page](https://mitchellmcdermott.com/about/)

### When Did Data Center Language Become Prominent?

The MMCD website today has dedicated landing pages for data centers, and they publish quarterly "Data Centres Infocard" reports. The earliest confirmed Infocard is from **January 2019**, which implies the data center thought-leadership publishing began around 2018–2019.

- Source: [Data Centres Infocard Jan '19 (PDF)](https://mitchellmcdermott.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Mitchell-McDermott-Data-Centres-Infocard-Jan19.pdf)
- Source: [Data Centres Infocard January 2025 (PDF)](https://mitchellmcdermott.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/Data-Centres-Infocard-FINAL.pdf)

*Inference:* The progression from multi-sector generalist (2015) to increasingly DC-specialist (2018–2020+) likely tracked Ireland's hyperscale DC construction boom. Ireland became the dominant European hyperscale hub during 2016–2020, when Amazon, Google, Meta, and Microsoft significantly expanded data center footprints in Dublin. This created a pull toward DC specialization for any Irish construction consultancy with early DC exposure.

### Summary

| Period | DC Focus |
|--------|----------|
| 2015 founding | Multi-sector, DC among equals |
| 2016–2018 | DC growing as primary focus |
| 2018+ | Active DC thought leadership (Infocards published) |
| 2020+ | Explicit DC consultancy positioning |
| 2025 | DC is co-equal flagship sector with residential |
