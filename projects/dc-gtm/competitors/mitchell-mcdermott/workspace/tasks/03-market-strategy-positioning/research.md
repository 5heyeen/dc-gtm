## Core Positioning Statement

Mitchell McDermott positions itself as a **"data-led, director-led, independent construction consultancy"** — a deliberate contrast to the large global firms (AECOM, Turner & Townsend) that dominate the sector. Each element is load-bearing:

- **Data-led:** Proprietary market intelligence as a client-value tool and marketing differentiator
- **Director-led:** Senior founders/directors personally involved in every client engagement
- **Independent:** No global parent, no conflict of interest, full alignment with client

Source: [Mitchell McDermott homepage](https://mitchellmcdermott.com/), [About page](https://mitchellmcdermott.com/about/)

## Dual Flagship Market Strategy

MMCD deliberately built **two flagship sectors** rather than going mono-sector:

1. **Data Centres** — high-fee, complex, internationally mobile clients
2. **Residential** — high-volume, government-supported, geographically sticky

This dual-track strategy provides counter-cyclical resilience: when Ireland's DC moratorium (2022–2024) paused Dublin DC work, residential demand kept the firm busy. And when residential slowed during COVID, DC demand surged.

## Market Intelligence as Strategy: The Infocard Engine

**The Infocards are not just marketing — they are the strategy.**

MMCD publishes quarterly "InfoCards" covering construction cost benchmarks, inflation, residential, data centres, hotels, student accommodation, ESG, and more. These are distributed via:
- Free downloads from their website
- The **MMCD Intel app** (available on iOS/Android — includes a cost calculator and construction benchmark tools)

**Why this matters:**
1. Infocards establish MMCD as the authoritative voice on Irish construction costs — a position previously owned by AECOM/Davis Langdon
2. They are used by developers, funders, and lenders to benchmark projects — creating a natural pipeline for MMCD's Funder Advisory and Due Diligence services
3. They generate media coverage (RTÉ, Irish Times, Business Post routinely cite MMCD data)
4. They differentiate from global firms whose market data is global rather than Irish-specific

The MMCD Intel app adds a "Development Stack" cost modelling tool — *inference:* this turns market intelligence into a lead-generation tool for developers assessing project viability.

## Geographic Market Strategy: Follow the Hyperscalers

MMCD's international expansion tracks hyperscale DC capital flows:

1. **Ireland (2015–present):** Ireland's hyperscale DC boom — Amazon, Google, Meta, Microsoft building in Dublin
2. **UK (~2019–2021):** London Docklands + M25 corridor DC expansion
3. **Malaysia (~2022–2024):** Microsoft $2.2bn commitment; Amazon, Google expanding APAC
4. **Netherlands (2025):** Dublin moratorium redirects hyperscale investment to Amsterdam/Middenmeer
5. **Germany + Spain (2023–2025):** Frankfurt and Madrid emerging hyperscale hubs

*Inference:* MMCD does not proactively enter markets — it follows clients. This is a deliberate "client-first" internationalisation strategy that minimises market risk.

## Niche Entry Strategy: Funder Advisory Flywheel

*Inference:* One underappreciated element of MMCD's strategy is the **Funder Advisory service**, which means MMCD advises banks and financial institutions that are lending to construction projects (not just developers/operators).

This creates a **flywheel:**
- Lender hires MMCD for due diligence/monitoring → MMCD learns what lenders need → MMCD advises developers on what will satisfy lenders → MMCD gets hired as PM/QS on the project → MMCD's Infocard data validates the project economics

Being on both sides of the capital table (operator advisory + funder advisory) gives MMCD market visibility and deal flow that pure PM/QS firms cannot match.
