# Prompt 03: limerick-amsterdam-office-openings-drivers

> **Prompt:** When did MMCD open offices in Limerick and Amsterdam? What drove each expansion?

---

## Limerick Office — September 2024

**Opening date:** September 19, 2024 (marked with a launch event)
**Location:** Bedford Row, Limerick city centre
**Head:** Eoin McEnery (Divisional Director of Cost Management, 14+ years experience)
**Staff at launch:** 4; grew to 8 within weeks of opening

### What Drove the Limerick Opening?

MMCD cited growing demand for construction consultancy services in the Munster and Mid-West region. At the time of opening, MMCD was advising on:
- ~2,400 residential units across the region (predominantly apartments, public and private sector)
- 500+ hotel rooms under development
- 270+ purpose-built student beds
- 40,000 m² of commercial, mixed-use and event space

The Limerick expansion was **primarily residential and commercial-driven**, not data center-driven. It reflects MMCD's ambition to be the leading Irish-national construction consultancy rather than a Dublin-only firm. The Irish government's regional housing development push and University of Limerick expansion were likely tailwinds.

**Key quote (Eoin McEnery):** The company was "seeing growing demand for its services, especially with regard to cost management, project management, bank monitoring, financial modelling and due diligence."

## Amsterdam Office — Early 2025 (Approximate)

**Opening date:** Announced via LinkedIn post circa early 2025 (exact date not confirmed in public sources — LinkedIn post ID suggests late 2024 / early 2025)
**Location:** Amsterdam, Netherlands
**Lead:** Ronan Tynan (Director and Head of Project Management; also leads Mission Critical team with Anthony McDermott)

### What Drove the Amsterdam Opening?

**Entirely data-center driven.** The Amsterdam office was explicitly described as enabling expansion in the Benelux region and mainland Europe for PM and Cost Management services. The Netherlands is a Tier 1 European hyperscale data center market (Amsterdam, Middenmeer) — home to major campuses from Microsoft (Middenmeer), Meta, Amazon, and Google.

**Key quote (Ronan Tynan):** "Our Amsterdam office strengthens our ability to expand further in the Netherlands and Benelux region."

The Netherlands makes strategic sense as a European DC hub because:
1. AMS-IX (Amsterdam Internet Exchange) is one of the world's busiest internet exchanges
2. Several hyperscalers have multi-hundred-MW campus projects underway in the Netherlands
3. Amsterdam gives MMCD a foothold for German, Belgian, and Nordic DC project pursuits

The October 2025 MML Ireland PE investment was subsequently announced with the explicit goal of "expanding its European project pipeline in the data centre sector" — reinforcing that Amsterdam is the beachhead for that strategy.
