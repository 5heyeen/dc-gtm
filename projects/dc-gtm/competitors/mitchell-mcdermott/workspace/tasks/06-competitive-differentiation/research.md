## MMCD vs. Global Firms (AECOM, Turner & Townsend)

### Global Firms' Advantages
- Brand recognition (easier to sell to procurement committees at hyperscalers)
- Global data benchmarks (cost data from 50+ countries)
- Larger teams for mega-projects (>500 MW campuses)
- Existing relationship with client's global procurement team

### MMCD's Advantages vs. Globals

| Dimension | MMCD | Global Firms |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| Director involvement | Founders personally on mandates | Junior staff manage day-to-day |
| Irish market intelligence | Deepest — Infocards, MMCD Intel app | Global data, less Irish granularity |
| Responsiveness | Small firm agility | Corporate approval chains |
| Independence | No global parent interests | May have conflicts (other clients/services) |
| Cost | Mid-market fees | Global firm premium rates |
| Culture fit (Irish market) | Native Irish firm | Overseas HQ |

The "director-led" claim is MMCD's most important differentiator — it means Paul Mitchell or Anthony McDermott personally engage with the client, which large-firm equivalents cannot match after a certain project volume.

## MMCD vs. Linesight (Closest Irish Peer)

Linesight is MMCD's most direct Irish competitor — also Dublin-headquartered, also QS/PM focused, also strong in DC. Key differences:

- **Linesight** has a dedicated global DC practice, is larger (~700+ staff globally), and has made DC its primary market internationally. Present in US, Middle East, APAC as well as Europe.
- **MMCD** is smaller, more Irish-rooted, dual-sector (DC + residential), with a stronger funder advisory service line.
- **MMCD's edge over Linesight:** The Infocard / MMCD Intel app ecosystem gives MMCD a proprietary data advantage in the Irish market. Linesight does not publish comparable open-access cost data.
- **Linesight's edge over MMCD:** Scale, global DC brand, US market access.

*Inference:* MMCD and Linesight likely compete for mid-size Irish DC mandates (10–100 MW range) but Linesight probably wins the largest global framework contracts where scale and global presence matter more.

## The Five Distinctive Capabilities (MMCD's Moat)

### 1. Proprietary Market Intelligence (Infocards + MMCD Intel App)
No Irish competitor publishes construction cost data at MMCD's depth and accessibility. The MMCD Intel app with a Development Stack calculator turns intelligence into a lead-generation tool — developers use it to assess project viability before engaging any consultancy.

### 2. Dual-Sided Capital Advisory (Developer + Funder)
Most PM/QS firms advise either the developer/operator OR the funder — not both. MMCD explicitly serves both sides. This gives MMCD market visibility that single-sided advisors cannot match and reduces client concentration risk.

### 3. Culture as Talent Strategy (GPTW, DEI)
In a market where senior QS/PM professionals are scarce, MMCD's ranked workplace culture (1st in Ireland 2022, top 30 in Europe) is a structural advantage in hiring. Global firms spend more on salaries; MMCD competes on meaning and workplace quality.

### 4. Irish Policy Advocacy (Infocards cited in legislation, media)
Paul Mitchell's SCSI chairmanship and Anthony McDermott's DCD/Business Post visibility make MMCD a policy-shaping actor — not just a service provider. This creates credibility with public sector clients and regulatory-savvy private clients that value a consultancy with policy influence.

### 5. Long-Tenured Internal Team
Four founding-year (2015) staff promoted to Divisional Director in 2025 signals deep institutional continuity. Clients who started with MMCD in 2015 are still working with the same people in 2025 — a powerful retention mechanism.

## What MMCD Does NOT Do (and Why That's Strategic)

- **Does not do EPC/contracting** — stays in the owner's advisory lane, no conflict with contractors it monitors
- **Does not do architecture/engineering design** — pure PM/QS/advisory, no design liability
- **Does not pursue global framework contracts at mega-scale** — focuses on delivering well at its size rather than overstretching

This deliberate scope control is itself a differentiation: MMCD is not trying to be Turner & Townsend. It is trying to be the best mid-market Irish construction consultancy for clients who want director attention and Irish market depth.
