# Global Data Center Investment Landscape: 2025-2026

**Report Date:** April 2026
**Purpose:** Factual overview of global data center capital expenditure, market size, capacity pipeline, and regional investment trends.

---

## 1. Total Global Data Center Capital Expenditure

### 2024 (Baseline)
- Global data center investments surged **51% year-over-year in 2024 to $455 billion** ([CIO Dive](https://www.ciodive.com/news/data-center-ai-cloud-infrastructure-capex-gpu-servers/743002/))
- The top 5 hyperscalers spent ~$256 billion in capex in 2024, up 63% YoY ([IEEE ComSoc](https://techblog.comsoc.org/2025/12/22/hyperscaler-capex-600-bn-in-2026-a-36-increase-over-2025-while-global-spending-on-cloud-infrastructure-services-skyrockets/))

### 2025 (Actual)
- Infrastructure capital expenditures grew an additional **30%+ over 2024** levels ([CIO Dive](https://www.ciodive.com/news/data-center-ai-cloud-infrastructure-capex-gpu-servers/743002/))
- Global data center capex surged **57% in 2025**, driven by AI infrastructure build-outs ([Brightlio](https://brightlio.com/data-center-stats/))
- The top 4 hyperscalers (Amazon, Google, Meta, Microsoft) collectively spent approximately **$413 billion** on data center capex in 2025, an 84% increase from $224 billion in 2024 ([Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/north-america-data-center-market))
- U.S. spending on data centers topped **$425 billion** in 2025, with ~70% from hyperscalers ([Programs.com](https://programs.com/resources/data-center-statistics/))
- Data center deal value hit a record **$61 billion** in 2025 (M&A and financing transactions), per S&P Global ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/19/data-center-deals-hit-record-amid-ai-funding-concerns-grip-investors.html))

### 2026 (Projected)
- Dell'Oro Group forecasts full-year 2026 data center capex to **surpass $1 trillion** ([Brightlio](https://brightlio.com/data-center-stats/))
- The top 5 hyperscalers (Amazon, Alphabet, Microsoft, Meta, Oracle) have committed to **$660-$690 billion** in capex for 2026 ([Futurum](https://futurumgroup.com/insights/ai-capex-2026-the-690b-infrastructure-sprint/))
- FMI forecasts data center construction spending to increase **24.9% in 2026**, following a 33.4% increase in 2025 ([ConstructConnect](https://news.constructconnect.com/january-2026-data-center-report-spending-surges-fivefold-in-two-years))
- Average global construction cost projected to reach **$11.3 million per MW** in 2026 (6% increase), with 60% of industry professionals expecting 5-15% cost increases ([Avid Solutions](https://avidsolutionsinc.com/13-data-center-growth-projections-that-will-shape-2026-2030/))

### Long-Term Projections (to 2030)
- **McKinsey:** $5.2 trillion in data center investment needed by 2030 for AI alone (156 GW of AI capacity); accelerated scenario: $7.9 trillion ([McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/the-cost-of-compute-a-7-trillion-dollar-race-to-scale-data-centers))
- **JLL:** ~$3 trillion total investment by 2030, including $1.2 trillion in real estate asset value creation, $870 billion in debt financing, and $1-2 trillion in GPU/networking upgrades ([DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/not-a-bubble-3-trillion-data-center-investment-supercycle-expected-by-2030-despite-challenges-jll/))
- Global data center capex projected to more than double from $430 billion (2024) to **$1.1 trillion by 2029** ([Network World](https://www.networkworld.com/article/4110602/data-center-investments-break-all-records.html))

## 4. Regional Breakdown

### 4.1 North America

- **Market share:** 41% of global DC market in 2025 ([Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/data-center-market-report))
- **Market size:** ~$153.9 billion in 2025, growing at CAGR of 10.5% to $253.4 billion by 2030 ([Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/north-america-data-center-market))
- **US dominance:** 76.5% of North American market; U.S. spending topped $425 billion in 2025
- **Colocation vacancy:** All-time low of **1.4%** in 2025 ([CBRE](https://www.cbre.com/insights/books/north-america-data-center-trends-h2-2025))
- **Absorption:** Record 2,497.6 MW absorbed in primary markets in 2025, up 38% from 1,809.5 MW in 2024 ([JLL](https://www.jll.com/en-us/insights/market-dynamics/north-america-data-centers))
- **Virginia** accounts for 6.3 GW of capacity under construction (~25% of Americas pipeline) ([CBRE](https://www.cbre.com/insights/books/north-america-data-center-trends-h2-2025))

### 4.2 Europe

- **Market share:** 25.6% of global DC market in 2025, generating ~$69 billion ([Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/data-center-market-report))
- **Market size:** $52.1 billion (2025) growing to $108.9 billion by 2031 at CAGR of 13.1% ([Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/europe-data-center-market))
- **Investment forecast:** EUDCA projects cumulative investment of **EUR 176 billion ($208B) between 2026 and 2031** — effectively doubling the EUR 80 billion forecast made just one year earlier ([EUDCA](https://www.eudca.org/new-2026-state-of-european-data-centres))
- **IT power capacity:** Grew from 10,539 MW (2023) to **14,784 MW** (2025), with 17% CAGR projected through 2031 ([EUDCA](https://www.eudca.org/new-2026-state-of-european-data-centres))
- **Colocation market:** $9.45 billion (2024), projected to reach $35.73 billion by 2030 (CAGR 24.8%) ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/europe-data-center-colocation-outlook-155400653.html))
- **Vacancy:** Projected to decline to all-time low of **6.5%** by end of 2026 ([CBRE](https://www.cbre.com/insights/books/european-real-estate-market-outlook-2026/data-centres))
- **Key constraint:** Grid congestion is the primary limiting factor, not capital availability
- **Leading market:** Germany (15% share in 2025); UK fastest-growing at 19.5% CAGR through 2031

### 4.3 Asia-Pacific

- **Market share:** 22.6% of global DC market in 2025, generating ~$61 billion ([Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/data-center-market-report))
- **Market size:** $78.3 billion (2025), projected to reach $151.1 billion by 2030 at CAGR of 14.1% ([Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/horizon/outlook/data-center-market/asia-pacific))
- **Investment forecast:** ~**$800 billion** in cumulative DC investment expected across APAC by 2030 ([CBRE](https://www.cbre.com/insights/articles/asia-pacific-data-centre-boom-to-continue-in-2026))
- **IT power capacity:** Current ~12.7 GW, expected to reach 14 GW by end of 2025; IDC projects 142,600 MW by 2029 (22% CAGR) ([IDC](https://my.idc.com/getdoc.jsp?containerId=prAP53964925))
- **Financing:** $15.2 billion raised through 21 transactions for DC financing in APAC in first 7 months of 2025 ([CBRE](https://www.cbre.com/insights/articles/asia-pacific-data-centre-boom-to-continue-in-2026))
- **Key growth markets:** India (highest CAGR; crossing 2 GW from 350-400 MW pre-COVID), Japan, Malaysia, Indonesia, Australia
- **India:** Grew from 350-400 MW pre-COVID to 1.2 GW, expected to cross 2 GW within 2-3 years ([Light Reading](https://www.lightreading.com/data-centers/2025-in-review-asia-emerges-as-the-hub-of-the-data-center-boom))

### 4.4 Nordics

- **Construction market:** $2.21 billion (2024), expected to reach **$7.83 billion by 2030** at CAGR of 23.4% ([Arizton](https://www.arizton.com/market-reports/nordic-data-center-construction-market-2025))
- **Total market:** ~$14.9 billion projected by 2030 at CAGR of 12.8% ([MarkSpark Solutions](https://marksparksolutions.com/reports/nordics-data-center-market))
- **Norway supply:** Qualified supply has grown at **43% five-year CAGR** since 2019 and now exceeds 1 GW ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/nordics-data-center-portfolio-report-080700385.html))
- **Major investments:**
  - OpenAI Stargate Norway: 100,000 GPUs on renewable hydroelectric power
  - CoreWeave: $2.2 billion Nordic program
  - Google: EUR 600 million facility in Skien, Norway
  - Brookfield: $10 billion AI hub in Sweden
  - XTX Markets: EUR 1+ billion mega-campus in Kajaani, Finland (250 MW at full capacity)
  - Areim: $481 million sustainability fund targeting 200 MW in Sweden by 2027
- **Competitive advantages:** Power costs 40-50% lower than European average; 95%+ renewable energy (Norway hydro); 400 kV transmission corridors; deployment timelines 24 months shorter than Frankfurt/London ([Pexapark](https://pexapark.com/blog/prmc-breaking-down-the-data-center-surge-in-the-nordics-key-players-trends-and-ppas/), [DC Byte](https://www.dcbyte.com/news-blogs/nordic-data-centre-market-sustainability/))
- **~137 existing data centers** in the region ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/nordics-data-center-portfolio-report-080700385.html))

## 5. Global Capacity Pipeline (Under Construction / Planned)

### Global
- **Over 23 GW** of data center capacity was under construction globally at end of Q3 2025, with ~75% in the US ([BloombergNEF](https://about.bnef.com/insights/commodities/ai-data-center-build-advances-at-full-speed-five-things-to-know/))
- **550 planned projects** totaling 125 GW in the global pipeline ([BloombergNEF](https://about.bnef.com/insights/commodities/ai-data-center-build-advances-at-full-speed-five-things-to-know/))
- **3.8 GW** of new IT capacity entered construction phase in Q3 2025, up 58% on the quarterly average this decade ([BloombergNEF](https://about.bnef.com/insights/commodities/ai-data-center-build-advances-at-full-speed-five-things-to-know/))
- Global capacity forecast to nearly double from **103 GW to 200 GW by 2030** ([Avid Solutions](https://avidsolutionsinc.com/13-data-center-growth-projections-that-will-shape-2026-2030/))

### United States
- US disclosed project pipeline reached **241 GW** at end of 2025 (up from 93 GW at end of 2024), with 33% under active development ([Wood Mackenzie](https://www.woodmac.com/press-releases/newly-added-us-data-center-capacity-slows-down-considerably-in-q4-2025-as-market-struggles-to-keep-up-with-explosive-demand/))
- **50+ GW** of data center capacity operating in the U.S. at end of 2025 (24% CAGR since 2020) ([FERC / Utility Dive](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/data-centers-miso-ferc-market-report/815831/))
- **25.3 GW** under construction in the Americas by end of H2 2025, with 89% pre-commitment rates ([CBRE](https://www.cbre.com/insights/books/north-america-data-center-trends-h2-2025))
- **8.9 GW** across 105 projects targeting operation by end of 2026 (47 already under construction) ([BloombergNEF](https://about.bnef.com/insights/commodities/ai-data-center-build-advances-at-full-speed-five-things-to-know/))
- ~3.9 GW of new colocation supply delivered in 2025; vacancy near historic lows at **4.2%** ([JLL](https://www.jll.com/en-us/insights/market-dynamics/north-america-data-centers))
- U.S. power capacity projected to jump from ~30 GW (2025) to **90 GW+ by 2030** (22% yearly growth) ([Avid Solutions](https://avidsolutionsinc.com/13-data-center-growth-projections-that-will-shape-2026-2030/))

### Power Demand
- U.S. data centers consume approximately **176 TWh** annually — 4.4% of total U.S. power ([Tech Insider](https://tech-insider.org/ai-data-center-power-crisis-2026/))
- Non-AI workloads: ~38 GW; AI workloads: ~44 GW in 2026, with 18 additional GW expected by end of year ([CTVC](https://www.ctvc.co/the-data-center-report-we-promise-you-havent-read/))
- Large load capacity that has signed construction/electricity supply agreements reached **183 GW** — equivalent to 22% of 2025 US peak load ([Wood Mackenzie](https://www.woodmac.com/press-releases/newly-added-us-data-center-capacity-slows-down-considerably-in-q4-2025-as-market-struggles-to-keep-up-with-explosive-demand/))

## 7. Key Themes and Risks

### Demand Drivers
- **AI is the primary catalyst:** AI infrastructure accounts for ~75% of hyperscaler capex. By 2030, AI could represent half of all data center workloads.
- **Shift from training to inference:** Inference workloads require proximity to end users, driving demand for distributed/edge capacity in secondary markets.
- **Cloud adoption and digitalization** continue to provide baseline growth independent of AI.

### Constraints
- **Power availability** is the #1 bottleneck globally. Grid congestion, lengthy interconnection timelines, and permitting delays constrain build-out.
- **Construction costs rising:** 60% of industry professionals expect 5-15% cost increases in 2026.
- **Talent shortages** for specialized construction and operations roles.
- **Regulatory complexity:** Zoning, environmental reviews, and grid connection approvals vary widely by jurisdiction.

### Capital Structure Shift
- Data center investment is shifting from equity-funded to **heavily leveraged.** Debt issuance nearly doubled in 2025 ($92B to $182B). UBS forecasts $900B in new tech sector debt in 2026.
- Big Tech capex has risen to approximately **1.9% of U.S. GDP** ([Programs.com](https://programs.com/resources/data-center-statistics/)).
