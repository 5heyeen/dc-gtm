# Task 02: DC Specialist Builders — Alliance Models (Turner, DPR, Holder)

**Completed:** 2026-04-21
**Scope:** Alliance and partnership programs, delivery models, named clients, and governance structures for Turner Construction, DPR Construction, and Holder Construction in the DC sector.

---

## Executive Summary

- **Turner Construction** is the dominant DC builder by revenue ($6.4B DC revenue in first 9 months of 2025, ~37% of $39B backlog) and operates a named "Critical Facilities Market" sector with an advanced-tech leadership revamp; no formal named alliance program disclosed but clear repeat-hyperscaler relationships (Meta, Vantage, CoreWeave, QTS).
- **DPR Construction** brands itself as "largest data center contractor in the nation," leads with a **self-perform model** (GC controls critical scopes in-house) and frames its governance as a single integrated schedule tying owner/vendors/GC/trades/commissioning; named clients include Meta (multiple campuses, incl. $10B Louisiana JV) and NSA.
- **Holder Construction** has 250+ DC projects over 10 years and a "Data & Telecom" service line; most visible public relationship is the $1.9B EdgeCore campus in Arizona; no named hyperscaler partnerships found publicly.
- All three firms share a common pattern: **confidential client identities** for most hyperscaler work, repeat/framework relationships inferred from project sequences, no publicly named alliance programs.
- **Meta Louisiana** ($10B, begun 2024) is the clearest example of large-GC consortium delivery: Turner + DPR + Mortenson as a formal joint venture, 5,000 peak construction workers.

## 2. DPR Construction

### Scale and Market Position

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| DC market position | ENR Top 5 US data center builders since 2016; self-describes as "largest data center contractor in the nation" |
| Ownership | Employee-owned (private); no public revenue figures |
| Geographic focus | US primary; some international |

### Delivery Model — Self-Perform as Core Differentiator

DPR's most distinctive feature is its **self-perform model**: unlike most GCs that subcontract all trade work, DPR directly employs crews for critical scopes. This means:
- GC (and owner) retain direct control over scheduling, quality, safety, and cost on critical-path activities.
- Reduces dependency on subcontractor availability and pricing.
- Greater schedule certainty for hyperscaler clients who face hard commissioning deadlines.

### Governance and Program Management Framing

DPR explicitly publishes a governance philosophy:
- **Single integrated schedule** tying together owner, vendors, GC, trades, commissioning, and all other stakeholders.
- **"Alignment with financial institutions, insurance entities and private equity partners"** explicitly noted as essential for large DC programs.
- **Prefabrication** used extensively to reduce site time and improve quality consistency.
- Services include: construction management, design-build, integrated project delivery (IPD).

### Named Client Relationships

| Client | Project | Value | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|-------|
| Meta | Fort Worth, Texas | Not disclosed | Long-standing relationship |
| Meta | Richland Parish, Louisiana | $10B | **JV: DPR + Turner + Mortenson** |
| Meta | Abilene, Texas | Not disclosed | ENR case study project |
| NSA | Camp Williams, Utah | $1B | DPR-led JV for US government |

**No named commercial framework agreements** publicly disclosed. The Meta multi-project pattern (Fort Worth, Abilene, Louisiana) strongly implies a preferred-contractor or master services relationship.

| Service line | "Data & Telecom" — a defined practice area on holderconstruction.com |
| Geographic focus | Southeastern US primary; national reach |

### Named Client Relationships

| Client | Project | Value | Notes |

- OSHA partnership for DC site safety program — demonstrates safety governance as a differentiator.
- No published self-perform model or distinct program governance framework found.
