# Task 06: Training, Academy & Capability-Building Programs in the DC Sector

**Completed:** 2026-04-21
**Scope:** Formal training programs, certifications, academies, and capability-building alliances offered or co-developed by major construction and PM firms, industry bodies, and hyperscalers in the DC sector.

---

## Executive Summary

- **No major GC** (Skanska, Turner, DPR, Hochtief) has published a client-facing DC training product; GC training programs are entirely internal workforce development, not client capability-building.
- **Training is dominated by three independent bodies**: Uptime Institute/CNet Training, BICSI, and DCD Academy — each offering globally-recognised certifications that DC operators, designers, and contractors rely on for staff development.
- **Hyperscalers run the most structured client/community-facing programs**: Microsoft's Datacenter Academy (community college partnerships, free curriculum) and Google's STAR Program (local skilled trades training near DC sites) are the most visible — but these serve their own labour pipelines, not the broader industry.
- **No Nordic-specific DC training initiative** was found — Uptime/BICSI/DCD serve globally but have no named Nordic DC program.
- **Whitespace**: A Nordic PM/governance training product co-branded or aligned with BICSI, Uptime, or DCD Academy, targeted at DC project teams and operators in Norway/Nordics, would fill a visible gap.

## 1. Industry Training Bodies

### 1.1 Uptime Institute / CNet Training

**Background**: Uptime Institute is the global authority on data centre operational standards (Tier certification). In 2024 it acquired **CNet Training** (previously self-described as the world's largest DC training provider), which now operates as "CNet Training, an Uptime Education Company."

**Key certifications offered:**

| Certification | Acronym | Target audience | Duration/format |
|--------------|---------|----------------|----------------|
| Certified Data Center Design Professional | **CDCDP®** | Design, construction, engineering | 5-day; classroom or remote |
| Certified Data Center Technician Professional | **CDCTP®** | Operations and maintenance staff | Multi-day; classroom or remote |
| Uptime Accredited Tier Professional | **ATP** | Architects, engineers, consultants | Based on Tier Standard knowledge |

**Programme content (CDCDP)**: Best practice principles for design, construction and operation of data centers; physical infrastructure, electrical distribution, air-conditioning (HVAC), data cabling, building support systems.

**Re-certification**: Required every 3 years via online learning management system.

**Governance**: Based on Uptime's own Tier Standard and the Tier Certification framework.

**Sources:**
- [Uptime Institute Education](https://uptimeinstitute.com/education)
- [CNet Training CDCDP](https://cnet-training.com/programs/certified-data-centre-design-professional-cdcdp/)
- [CNet Training acquisition announcement](https://uptimeinstitute.com/education/cnet-training-is-now-an-uptime-education-company)

### 1.2 BICSI — Data Center Design Consultant (DCDC®)

**Background**: BICSI is a global professional association for ICT design and installation. Its **DCDC®** certification is government-recognized (US DoD's COOL program) and increasingly required by operators and designers.

**DCDC® certification:**

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Full name | Data Center Design Consultant |
| Standard | ANSI/BICSI 002-2024 (updated Jan 2025 — all new applications use this edition) |
| Exam | 100 questions, 2 hours; multiple choice, drag-and-drop, hot spot, enhanced matching |
| Eligibility paths | (1) Current RCDD®; (2) 2 years DC experience + existing BICSI certification or engineering degree; (3) 3 years DC experience in last 7 years |
| Scope | DC design, coordination, management; integration of documentation, plans, specs, construction, operational management; stakeholder management (architects, engineers, contractors, operators) |
| Training course | DC102 Applied Data Center Design and Best Practices (5-day face-to-face) |
| Publication | "Essentials of Data Center Projects" — available as a training publication |

**Key note**: The DCDC® is the most relevant credential for a PM or project governance professional working in DC construction — it explicitly covers stakeholder management, construction integration, and operational management, not just technical design.

**Sources:**
- [BICSI DCDC certification](https://www.bicsi.org/education-certification/certification/dcdc)
- [BICSI DC102 course](https://www.bicsi.org/education-certification/education-@-bicsi-learning-academy/courses/face-to-face/dc102)
- [BICSI Essentials of DC Projects](https://www.bicsi.org/education-certification/education-@-bicsi-learning-academy/technical-publications/other-specialty-publications/essentials-of-data-center-projects)

### 1.3 DCD Academy (Data Center Dynamics)

**Background**: DCD Academy is the training arm of *Data Center Dynamics* (the sector's leading trade publication), operating since its DCPro predecessor. Now 25 years in the industry.

**Offering:**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Platform | Workforce Development Platform — tracks training ROI, benchmarks team skills, closes skills gaps |
| Accreditation | IACET (continuing education units, ANSI/IACET standard) |
| Format | Mix of classroom and online learning |
| Named courses | Data Center Design Awareness; The Business of Data Centers; Energy & Sustainability Professional |
| Target | Both technical and non-technical staff; suits multiple functions and skillsets |

**Commercial model**: DCD Academy partners/sponsors are typically vendors and operators; GC training partnerships not found publicly.

**Sources:**
- [DCD Academy](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/dcdacademy/)
- [DCD Academy course catalog](https://learn.dcdacademy.com/catalog)
- [DCD Business of Data Centers course](https://learn.dcdacademy.com/courses/bodc-en)

## 2. Hyperscaler-Run Training Programs

### 2.1 Microsoft Datacenter Academy

**Structure**: Microsoft partners directly with community colleges to deliver free DC training:

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Partners | Glendale Community College; Estrella Mountain Community College (Arizona); expanding to others |
| Microsoft provides | Curriculum; hands-on lab equipment; scholarships; mentorship |
| Training content | IT skills for DC careers; directly aligned with skills needed for Microsoft DC roles |
| Cost to students | Free |
| Outcome | Pipeline of trained talent for Microsoft DC operations teams |

**Model**: Microsoft as curriculum owner and equipment funder; community colleges as delivery vehicle. This is **client-to-community**, not client-to-contractor training.

**Sources:**
- [Microsoft Datacenter Academy](https://careers.microsoft.com/v2/global/en/datacenteracademy.html)
- [Glendale Community College Datacenter Academy](https://www.gccaz.edu/academics/departments/business/microsoft-datacenter-academy)

---

### 2.2 Google STAR Program (Skilled Trades and Readiness)

**Structure**: Short-term training program preparing new-to-industry talent for entry-level careers in construction and skilled trades near Google DC sites.

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Programme name | STAR — Skilled Trades and Readiness |
| Duration | Multi-week; location-specific |
| Target | New-to-industry talent; local communities near Google DCs |
| Content examples | Fiber optics (Omaha); project management readiness (Northern Virginia Community College) |
| Partners | Local community colleges; local workforce development bodies |
| Norway relevance | Google DC in Skien creates 2,000 construction jobs (peak) + 100 permanent — but no named STAR programme for Norway found |

**Model**: Google as funder/program owner; local colleges/workforce bodies as delivery. Focus on local community investment, not construction contractor capability.

**Sources:**
- [Google DC Workforce Development Program](https://datacenters.google/workforce-development-program/)
- [Google DC Workforce Development & Education](https://datacenters.google/workforce-development-and-education/)

### 3.1 Skanska — Internal Programs Only

| Program | Target | DC-specific? |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| CCTP (Core Competency Training Program) | Recent graduates; rotational across construction projects | No |
| CMBB (Construction Management Building Blocks) | Subcontractor diversity development | No |
| Supply Chain Sustainability School | Supply chain partners | No |

**Conclusion**: No Skanska client-facing DC training product found. No DC-specific certification partnership with BICSI, Uptime, or DCD Academy found.

### 3.2 Turner Construction

No client-facing or DC-specific training program found publicly. Turner's "Join the Largest Data Center Builder" career content targets potential employees, not clients or operators.

### 3.3 DPR Construction

No client-facing or DC-specific training program found publicly. DPR publishes thought leadership (ENR articles, conference participation) but no formal training product.

### 3.4 AECOM

No client-facing DC training product found. AECOM's training is internal (Project Management Academy for staff) and not marketed to DC operators.

### 3.5 Turner & Townsend

T&T's PMO model explicitly mentions "curating and building local skillsets on the ground for the long term" as part of its DC PMO service — this implies embedded knowledge transfer is part of programme management delivery, but it is not packaged or sold as a standalone training product.

## 5. Whitespace Observations

1. **No GC or PM firm offers a standalone client-facing DC training product** — the training market is entirely owned by independent bodies and hyperscalers.
2. **No Nordic-specific DC training program** exists at the professional level — the industry bodies serve globally but have not created localised Nordic programs.
3. **PM/governance training for DC project managers** is not offered by anyone as a packaged product — BICSI/Uptime/DCD cover design and operations, not programme governance, milestone management, or investor reporting.
4. **The "local skillsets" gap**: Turner & Townsend acknowledges the need to "build local skillsets on the ground" via PMO delivery, but this is not a packaged training product — it is an embedded service. A standalone training product for this need does not exist in the market.

**Metier relevance**: A PM/governance training offering for DC project teams in Norway — co-designed with BICSI or DCD Academy content, adapted to Norwegian regulatory and procurement context — would be a first-mover product in the Nordic market.
