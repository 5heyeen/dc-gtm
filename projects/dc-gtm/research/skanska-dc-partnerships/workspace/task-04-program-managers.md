# Task 04: Program Manager DC Partnership Models (AECOM, Bechtel, Jacobs)

**Completed:** 2026-04-21
**Scope:** How AECOM, Bechtel, and Jacobs structure partnerships, alliances, and program management/governance services for large DC programs and portfolios.

---

## Executive Summary

- **AECOM** is the most philosophically explicit about partnerships: it has published content arguing that DC delivery requires partnerships to allocate "system-level risk across power, permitting, capital, and community over decades"; it has a named modular DC alliance (Project Frog "Rapid Deployment Team"), a named client (Nostrum, Spain, €1.9B/500MW), and a dedicated Global Program Management service with explicit governance and reporting language.
- **Bechtel** has the most significant named alliance: a formal partnership with **NVIDIA** to modularize the "AI Factory" blueprint for gigawatt-scale DC construction globally — making Bechtel NVIDIA's preferred construction partner for standardized AI infrastructure; also has an alliance with Nautilus Data Technologies for water-efficient DC cooling.
- **Jacobs** operates an **EPCM model** (Engineering, Procurement and Construction Management) as its differentiator — not a GC or a pure PM firm, but an integrated delivery lead; its Hut 8 River Bend engagement demonstrates the full "institutional-grade execution model" with blue-chip counterparties (Vertiv, J.P. Morgan, Goldman Sachs, Entergy) that establishes a "repeatable framework" across a client's broader portfolio.
- All three firms position themselves as **risk-allocation and governance intermediaries** between owners/investors and contractors — which is the same value proposition Metier can offer, but scaled down.

### Published Partnership Philosophy

AECOM published an explicit thought-leadership piece: **"The size of the prize is getting bigger and more complex: The case for partnerships in data center delivery"**. Key arguments:
- Partnerships are now essential, but **the challenge has shifted** — it is no longer about collaboration for speed or efficiency alone, but about **how technical, financial and regulatory risk is allocated across parties over the full life of the asset**.
- Risk must be structured early across: **power, permitting, capital, and community considerations over decades**.
- DC delivery requires "structuring the right partnerships early to manage system-level risk."

This represents the most sophisticated published framing of the DC partnership problem by any construction-adjacent firm found in this research.

### Named Partnerships and Alliances

| Partner | Type | Scope |
|---------|------|-------|
| **Project Frog** | Named commercial alliance | "Rapid Deployment Team" — deploys Project Frog's Converge data center kit (modular, 1MW+); AECOM provides design and construction management around the modular kit; targets speed-to-market |
| **Nostrum Data Centers** | Client engagement | AECOM selected to lead design and construction management for a new DC in Badajoz, Spain: €1.9B investment, 500MW capacity (Dec 2025) |

### Program Management and Governance Services

AECOM's **Global Program Management** is a dedicated service line:
- Acts as program manager / owner's representative on large capital programs.
- Handles multi-billion-dollar programs with multiple concurrent projects.
- Provides owner's engineering: technical advisor checking design and construction meet specifications.
- **Governance and reporting**: "robust governance and transparent reporting to track how clients' investment, program and responsibility are developing."
- Also delivers: full water lifecycle management for hyperscale DC campuses (water/wastewater as a service to DC operators).

| Partner | Type | Scope |
|---------|------|-------|
| **NVIDIA** | Strategic partnership (late 2025) | Bechtel to modularize NVIDIA's "AI Factory" blueprint; standardize and accelerate construction of gigawatt-scale AI infrastructure globally; off-site fabrication and repeatable components; enables "new AI capacity deliverable faster across the globe through scalable, repeatable formats" |
| **Nautilus Data Technologies** | Commercial alliance | Build data centers using Nautilus' water-cooling technology that uses 70% less power for cooling and doesn't consume drinking water |

### NVIDIA Partnership — Key Detail

The NVIDIA-Bechtel alliance is the most significant named GC-technology company alliance found in this research:
- Bechtel modularizes NVIDIA's "Omniverse Blueprint" — NVIDIA's reference design for AI factory construction.
- Mirrors techniques from Bechtel's mining and energy projects (off-site fabrication, repeatable components).
- Directly targets ROI: the strategic focus is on minimizing time to **"first revenue token"** — the moment an AI facility processes its first live workload — which directly affects IRR for DC investors.
- Makes Bechtel a standards-setter for global AI DC construction at scale.

### Delivery Model and Governance

- **EPC/EPCM**: Bechtel aligns engineering, procurement, construction, and commissioning as an integrated single-firm service.
- **Procurement innovation**: explicitly highlighted as a differentiator — Bechtel sees procurement as a strategic tool, not a back-office function.
- **4 game-changing technologies for AI factory construction**: published thought leadership on construction methods (liquid cooling, modular power, prefabrication, digital twins).

### Hut 8 River Bend — Institutional-Grade Execution Model

The Hut 8 River Bend engagement is the clearest published example of how EPCM firms structure DC delivery governance:

**Governance structure for River Bend:**

| Role | Party |
|------|-------|
| EPCM lead | **Jacobs** |
| Critical infrastructure | **Vertiv** |
| Power utility | **Entergy** |
| Financial institution | **J.P. Morgan** (co-investor/financing) |
| Financial institution | **Goldman Sachs** (co-investor/financing) |
| DC operator | **Hut 8** |

Key governance principles:
- "**Institutional-grade execution model** designed to de-risk project delivery."
- "**Partnership-driven execution model** — de-risk delivery and establish a **repeatable framework** as Hut 8 scales across its broader development pipeline."
- Realistic, rigorously developed timelines.
- Comprehensive and integrated plan across the project lifecycle.
- Alignment with financial institutions baked in from day one.

This model demonstrates that EPCM firms serve as the **glue** between the financial investors (Goldman Sachs, J.P. Morgan), the operator (Hut 8), the technology provider (Vertiv), and the utility (Entergy) — a governance function, not just a construction function.

### Program Management Positioning

Jacobs positions itself as "end-to-end EPCM and program management":
- Integrated planning, architecture, engineering, construction management, and commissioning.
- Delivers "precision and predictability" — the language of investor governance, not just project delivery.
- 30+ years of DC design experience.

## Key Insight for Metier

All three firms position **program management and governance as the mechanism by which financial investors and DC operators are de-risked from project delivery**. The language is explicitly investor-facing:
- AECOM: "allocate risk over the full life of the asset"
- Bechtel: "first revenue token" (directly an IRR concept)
- Jacobs: "institutional-grade execution model" with J.P. Morgan and Goldman Sachs as named counterparties

This confirms that **governance and program management for DC delivery is explicitly a capital-market-facing service**, not merely a construction management tool.
