**Source document:** 25-74 KL-sak om forsvarssatsing april 2025 (Kathrine Duun Moen, 9 April 2025)
**Prepared for:** Datacenter GTM — Metier + Norconsult collaboration
**Date:** 2026-04-17

## Part A: Elements of a Good Strategy Document

The forsvarssatsing document is an exceptional example of an internal sector-entry strategy presented for board-level decision (KL = Konsernledelse). It contains 11 structural elements and 5 analytical frameworks worth replicating.

### A1. Structural Elements

**1. Governance Context & Mandate (Bakgrunn)**
Opens by stating the originating board decision (12.12.2024), names the two working groups (offentlig + forsvarsindustri) and the steering committee. This legitimizes the work and establishes accountability before a single analysis slide appears.

**2. Explicit Scope Boundaries (Avgrensninger)**
Dedicates a slide to what is *not* in scope (Sweden and Denmark at this stage). Prevents scope creep in the discussion and signals that geographic rollout is phased.

**3. Discussion Framing + Resolution Proposal (Innspill til KL)**
Every strategy section is bracketed by: (a) "here are the discussion questions we want your input on" and (b) "here is the formal resolution we are asking you to approve." This is the mechanism that converts a presentation into a decision. Most strategy documents skip this entirely.

**4. Market Mapping — Two-Segment Structure**
The market is split into two distinct sub-markets with different dynamics, customer behaviors, and Norconsult positions:
- Forsvar offentlig (Forsvarsbygg, FFI, Finansdepartementet)
- Forsvarsindustri (Kongsberg, Nammo, Chemring)

Each segment gets its own analysis slide using a consistent 4-column template: Customer Needs | Our Position | Competitors | Market Characteristics.

**5. Competitive Intelligence — Facts Not Opinions**
Page 9 presents actual spend data from Forsvarsbygg (2024): NOK 991M total, Multiconsult NOK 329M (#1), Sweco NOK 184M (#2), Norconsult NOK 67M (#3). Page 10 tracks media mentions by company (Jan 2024–Mar 2025). This grounds the strategy in observable market reality, not just internal beliefs.

**6. Strategic Rationale — Explicit "Why This Sector"**
A dedicated slide lists 7 reasons why defense is strategically attractive, including cyclical risk reduction and "part of Norconsult's societal mission." This is the slide the CFO and skeptical board members need before they will engage with the rest.

**7. Three-Part Strategy Summary (Trender / Mål / Tilnærming)**
The core strategy slide presents in three parallel columns:
- *Trender*: What is happening in the market (external)
- *Mål og konkurransefortrinn*: What we want to achieve and what we have that others don't (internal)
- *Tilnærming*: How we will get there (execution)

This three-column structure is reusable for any sector-entry strategy.

**8. Prioritized Customer List — Named and Reasoned**
6 named priority customers across two segments, with explicit rationale for each. Crucially, secondary "stepping stone" value is also articulated: being present at FFI positions Norconsult for FMA, FLO, NATO, Forsvarsdepartementet down the line.

**9. Internal Organization — Named, Scoped, and Resourced**
Three distinct organizational bodies are defined, each with:
- Mål/ambisjon
- Scope (what they do and don't do)
- Tentative membership (named individuals)
- Prerequisites for success

This level of specificity is what makes a strategy executable rather than aspirational.

**10. Strategic Actions Table**
A structured action plan in 3-column table format: Action | Goal/Ambition | Description. Six named actions: Bygge kompetanse, Utforske partnerskap/oppkjøp, Forsterke oppdrags-/prosjektledelse, Oversikt over sikkerhetsklareringer, Referansedatabase, Sikkerhetstiltak.

**11. Risk Matrix — Four Categories**
Risks are organized into four named categories (Kunde og marked, Operasjonell gjennomføring, Organisasjon og kultur, Lønnsom vekst og styring), each with specific risk descriptions and mitigations. This structure forces the strategy team to have thought through failure modes.

---

### A2. Analytical Frameworks Used

| Framework | Where Used | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| Ambition level spectrum (low/medium/high) | Bakgrunn slide | Forces explicit choice of ambition before market analysis |
| 4-column customer template (behov / posisjon / konkurrenter / karakteristika) | All market slides | Ensures consistent analysis across customers and segments |
| 3-column strategy summary (trender / mål / tilnærming) | Core strategy slide | Connects external context to internal goals to execution |
| Per-customer deep-dive (6 dimensions) | Appendices | Pågående oppdrag / Referanser / Mål og suksesskriterier / Kommende investeringer / Vår posisjon / Inngangsbarrierer |
| Risk matrix (4 categories × description × mitigation) | Risk slide | Structured risk management |

---

### A3. Key Quality Signals

- **Quantified targets**: 600 MNOK annual revenue by 2028, split 350-400 MNOK offentlig and 200-250 MNOK industri
- **Named accountability**: Every team and action has a named individual responsible
- **Market share data**: Actual spend statistics from Forsvarsbygg (not estimated)
- **Competitor benchmarking**: Media mention counts, named competitor strengths
- **Explicit prerequisites for success**: Each organizational body states what must be true for it to work
- **Geographic phasing**: Norway now, Sweden + Denmark by Q3 2025 — acknowledged uncertainty built in
- **Reporting cadence**: First KL report Q3 2025, semi-annual thereafter

## Part B: Recommended Elements for a Metier + Norconsult Data Center Strategy

### B1. Direct Parallels — Apply Without Modification

The following elements from the forsvar document translate directly to a DC strategy:

**The sector-entry context is identical.** Norconsult+Metier entering DC is structurally the same as Norconsult entering defense: an established multi-discipline consultancy entering a high-growth sector where it has adjacent competence but no formal market position. The same document architecture applies.

---

**Element 1: Governance Context & Mandate**

For DC: State the originating decision that triggered the strategy work. Who asked for it (Metier CEO? Norconsult board?)? Who was in the working group? What is the steering committee? This is especially important because *two companies* are involved — establishing the governance model upfront prevents the strategy from dying in "who owns this" ambiguity.

> Recommended addition vs. forsvar: An explicit section on the **Metier+Norconsult collaboration model** — how the two companies will coordinate on bids, revenue sharing, and client ownership. This does not exist in the forsvar document because it is a single-company strategy.

---

**Element 2: Two-Segment Market Structure**

For DC, the natural segmentation is:

| Segment | Equivalent to forsvar |
|---|---|
| **Hyperscale & cloud operators** (Microsoft, Google, AWS building in Norway) | Forsvar offentlig — large procurement, framework agreements, experience-driven selection |
| **Nordic colocation & independent operators** (Skygard, Fossefall, Bifrost Edge, Kitebrook, NRC etc.) | Forsvarsindustri — relationship-driven, rapid decision-making, lacking internal PM/procurement competence |

> **Critical overlap discovered**: Forsvarsbygg's own investment pipeline (page 32 of the forsvar document) explicitly lists **"Datasentre - kommunikasjonssentra"** as a priority service area for the 2025-2036 period. This means the same Forsvarsbygg customer relationships that Norconsult is building for defense can be activated for DC advisory. A DC strategy should explicitly call this out as a bridgehead.

---

**Element 3: Strategic Rationale ("Why DCs for Metier+Norconsult")**

Directly mirroring the forsvar rationale slide, 7 reasons for DC:

1. High investment tempo — Norway/Nordic DC capacity is growing 3-5x over next decade (AI, hyperscaler expansion, digitalization of public sector)
2. Long contracts — DC operators buy on framework agreements; once inside, volume follows
3. Large volume once inside — a single hyperscale campus = 500-2000 MNOK in design and PM fees
4. Part of societal mission — digital infrastructure is as critical as physical infrastructure
5. Relatively low risk — DC projects have well-understood execution risk; not novel technology
6. Few conflicts — no regulatory or ESG contradictions for an engineering/advisory firm
7. Cyclical hedge — DC demand is counter-cyclical to property/construction downturns; government and hyperscaler spending is durable

---

**Element 4: Market Mapping — 4-Column Template per Segment**

Apply the identical template:

For each of the two segments (hyperscale + Nordic operators), produce:
- **Kundebehov**: What are their project execution pain points? (For operators: no PM capability, unfamiliar with construction procurement, timeline pressure from investors. For hyperscalers: need local engineering compliance knowledge, Norwegian regulatory navigation)
- **Vår posisjon**: Where are Metier and Norconsult already positioned? What existing relationships exist?
- **Konkurrenter**: Who else is competing for this work? (Multiconsult has engineering; Advansia does owner's engineer; international PM firms like Turner & Townsend are entering)
- **Karakteristika**: How does procurement actually work in this segment? Direct negotiation vs. tender? Experience-weighting?

---

**Element 5: Competitive Intelligence — Data Not Opinions**

For DC, the equivalent of the Forsvarsbygg spend statistics would be:
- Published project awards to competitors (DCD, Byggeindustrien)
- Job ad analysis (which firms are hiring DC-specific roles in Norway?)
- Known reference projects by competitor (Multiconsult's DC work, Sweco's DC work)
- Media mentions per company in DC context

The forsvar document demonstrates that this analysis is achievable in 4-6 weeks of desk research.

---

**Element 6: Three-Part Strategy Summary**

| Column | DC Content |
|---|---|
| **Trender** | AI-driven demand surge; hyperscaler Norway expansion; Norwegian power advantage; defense DC investment (Forsvarsbygg LTP); EU AI Act compliance driving private DC investment; government cloud mandates |
| **Mål og konkurransefortrinn** | Be top 3 advisory+engineering firm for DC in Nordics; X MNOK revenue by [year]; Competitive advantage: only firm combining Norconsult's MEP+civil engineering depth with Metier's PM/advisory/Owner's Engineer capability — no competitor offers this integrated offering |
| **Tilnærming** | Active client development on 6-8 priority operators; build DC-specific competence (power systems, cooling, commissioning); develop joint go-to-market model between Norconsult and Metier; establish reference database; communications positioning |

---

**Element 7: Prioritized Customer List**

Recommend 6-8 named priority clients across two segments. Based on Metier's existing GTM research:

*Segment 1 — Nordic operators (relationship-driven, urgent, under-resourced):*
- Skygard (high-security, existing Metier engagement)
- Fossefall (PE-backed, ambitious build program, no PM capability)
- Bifrost Edge (multi-site expansion underway)
- Kitebrook (early stage, strategic advisory needed)
- NRC (existing Norconsult relationship)

*Segment 2 — Institutional/government DC:*
- Forsvarsbygg (via forsvar relationship — "datasentre - kommunikasjonssentra" explicitly in their LTP pipeline)
- Statsbygg (government cloud infrastructure projects)
- A named hyperscaler (Microsoft/Google/AWS — one to prioritize based on Norway expansion plans)

---

**Element 8: Internal Organization**

This is the most important adaptation point. The forsvar document has one company with market groups and customer teams. A DC strategy for Metier+Norconsult needs to address **two companies**:

Recommended structure:
```
DC Steering Group (joint)
├── Norconsult representative (engineering lead)
└── Metier representative (PM/advisory lead)

Market Group DC (cross-company)
├── Market coordinator (KAM role — which company owns this?)
├── Norconsult engineering disciplines rep
├── Metier advisory/PM rep
└── Regional leads (Norconsult regions where relevant)

Customer Teams (per priority client)
├── Lead: alternates between Norconsult and Metier depending on entry point
└── Members: relevant technical and PM disciplines from both companies
```

Prerequisites for success (directly mirroring forsvar format):
- Explicit revenue split model agreed before client pursuit begins
- Clear "who owns the client" designation per customer
- Shared CRM/pipeline visibility
- Joint bid templates and reference database

---

**Element 9: Strategic Actions Table**

| Action | Goal | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Build DC competence** | Competitive capability | Identify and develop internal DC expertise: MEP for DC (Norconsult), DC-specific PM (Metier); consider hiring 3-5 with DC track record |
| **Joint go-to-market model** | Coordination | Define how Metier and Norconsult will pursue, bid, and deliver DC projects together; agree revenue attribution framework |
| **Explore partnerships/acquisitions** | Capability extension | Evaluate specialist DC firms for partnership or acquisition (commissioning agents, IT infrastructure specialists, DCIM vendors) |
| **Reference database** | Sales efficiency | Build DC-specific reference library covering both Norconsult engineering and Metier PM work — frame as combined capability |
| **Positioning & communications** | Market visibility | Neither Norconsult nor Metier currently has any DC-specific market positioning. This is the same gap identified for Norconsult in defense — and the same opportunity to be first |
| **Security clearance track** | High-security segment | For defense DC and government DC work: map existing security-cleared staff across both companies; develop protocol for cleared project teams |

---

**Element 10: Communication Plan**

Apply the forsvar communication structure:

- **Positioning strategy**: Define the joint Metier+Norconsult DC value proposition — "Norway's only fully integrated DC advisory and engineering team"
- **Website**: Neither company currently has DC-specific service pages. This is an identical gap to the forsvar finding — "none of the competitors have defense-specific service offerings on their websites"
- **Press & media**: DC project wins, expert commentary on Norwegian DC market (power, security, AI demand)
- **Events & arenas**: Nordic Data Center Summit, DCD Nordic, Nor-Stella, DataSenter konferansen; Kongsberg Agenda (overlap with forsvar relationships)
- **Partnerships & memberships**: Green Mountain (reference customer), Norwegian Data Center Industry Association

---

**Element 11: Risk Matrix**

Directly mirror the four categories:

| Risk Category | Key DC Risks |
|---|---|
| Kunde og marked | DC market consolidating fast; if one hyperscaler picks a non-Norconsult engineering partner, we lose that segment |
| Operasjonell gjennomføring | DC construction moves 2-3x faster than traditional projects; our PM and engineering teams may not have DC-specific commissioning competence |
| Organisasjon og kultur | Two-company model creates coordination overhead; risk of Metier and Norconsult competing rather than collaborating on the same client |
| Lønnsom vekst og styring | DC operators are cost-sensitive on advisory; risk of being undercut by single-discipline firms offering "just PM" or "just engineering" at lower rates |

---

**Element 12: Per-Customer Deep Dives (Appendix)**

Use the exact 6-dimension template from the forsvar appendices for each of the 6-8 priority customers:

1. Kommende investeringer og behov (relevant services)
2. Vår posisjon og konkurransesituasjonen
3. Inngangsbarrierer og innkjøpsrutiner
4. Pågående oppdrag
5. Referanser
6. Mål og suksesskriterier (quantitative + qualitative)

### B2. Key Adaptations vs. Forsvar Document

| Forsvar Element | DC Adaptation Required |
|---|---|
| Single company (Norconsult only) | Two companies (Metier + Norconsult) — collaboration model must be made explicit |
| Security clearance focus is operational | Security clearance focus is also market access (high-security DC is a customer segment, not just a compliance requirement) |
| Competition is primarily domestic (Multiconsult, Sweco) | Competition includes international players (Turner & Townsend, Linesight, international EPCs) |
| Public procurement rules dominate (LOA, anbud) | DC procurement is primarily private/negotiated — relationship and reference matter more than formal procurement rules |
| Revenue target is portfolio-level (600 MNOK for Norconsult) | Revenue target must be split between two companies; set separately for Metier and Norconsult portions |
| "Samfunnsoppdrag" framing (defense is a duty) | DC societal framing: digital infrastructure, AI sovereignty, energy transition, security of supply |

---

### B3. The One Structural Element to Prioritize Above All Others

In the forsvar document, the most commercially decisive element is the **per-customer deep dive** (appendices pp. 32-44). This is where abstract market strategy becomes actionable: named individuals, existing contracts, current market share estimates, and specific revenue targets per client.

For the DC strategy, the equivalent material already exists in Metier's operator research files (`projects/operators/*/research/`). The primary task is to organize this material into the same 6-dimension format and use it to anchor the strategy in concrete client-level action plans rather than sector-level generalities.

**The forsvar document is ultimately a collection of 6 account plans held together by a strategic narrative. The DC strategy should be the same.**

## Summary: DC Strategy Document Structure Recommendation

```
1. Background & Mandate (governance, working group, ambition spectrum)
2. Scope (what's in/out — Norway first, then Nordics)
3. Discussion Topics + Resolution Proposal (for board/leadership approval)
4. Market Overview
   4a. Segment 1: Nordic operators (colocation, high-security, PE-backed)
   4b. Segment 2: Hyperscale & institutional (incl. Forsvarsbygg DC)
5. Competitive Intelligence (spend data, competitor analysis, media visibility)
6. Strategic Rationale (7 reasons)
7. Ambition & Main Goals (revenue targets, market position aspiration)
8. Strategy Summary (Trender | Mål | Tilnærming — 3 columns)
9. Prioritized Customers (6-8 named)
10. Relevant Service Portfolio (Norconsult engineering + Metier advisory mapped to DC phases)
11. Internal Organization
    - Joint steering group
    - DC market group (cross-company)
    - Per-customer teams
12. Strategic Actions (6 named actions in table format)
13. Communication Plan
14. Budget (FTE + marketing spend)
15. Risk Matrix
16. Resolution Proposal (formal)

Appendices:
A. Per-customer deep dives (6-dimension template, 1-2 pages per customer)
B. DC pipeline (large projects in Norway + Nordics, with Norconsult/Metier positioning)
C. Investment map (Norwegian DC investment geography — analog to Forsvarsbygg map on p.50)
```
