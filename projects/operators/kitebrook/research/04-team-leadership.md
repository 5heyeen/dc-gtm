### Team Analysis

#### Strengths
- **Deep data center heritage:** Murphy and Conway built DigiPlex into Scandinavia's largest DC operator over 20 years. Formidable track record.
- **Capital/investment expertise:** Conway is Carlyle Group co-founder. Murphy has 40+ years in international real estate development. Capital access is not a bottleneck.
- **Legal/regulatory coverage:** Torleif Dahl provides experienced Norwegian corporate law and M&A support, with direct DigiPlex heritage.
- **Local operational leadership:** Per Espen Edvardsen provides Norway-based CEO leadership with technical/engineering background (oil & gas patents).
- **Sustainability narrative:** Kitebrook's entire positioning is around sustainable, hydro-powered data centers.

#### Critical Gaps
- **No CTO or VP Engineering** — No publicly identified senior technical data center design/build leader. Edvardsen's background is oil & gas, not DC construction.
- **No Head of Construction / Project Director** — For a company developing greenfield 100+ MW sites, this is a significant capability need.
- **No project management or controls function** — No PM, cost controller, or scheduler visible.
- **No procurement/contracts team** — No one visible to manage EPC selection or equipment procurement.
- **No commercial/sales director** — May be intentional if targeting single hyperscale tenant model.
- **Norway operations are Bergen-based** (Nesttun), not Oslo-based. Sites (Bordalen, Sogn) are in western Norway.
