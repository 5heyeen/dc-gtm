# News, Ownership & Funding Analysis: Kitebrook
## Date: 2026-04-08

### Ownership Structure

#### Corporate Hierarchy

**US Parent Level:**
- **Kitebrook Partners LLC** — Washington, DC. Led by J. Byrne Murphy. Registered in Maryland.
- **Kitebrook Energy Norway 1 JV LLC** — Delaware. JV structure for Norwegian energy/DC investments.

**Norwegian Operating Level:**
- **Kitebrook AS** (Org. nr. 917 810 214) — Registered 02.11.2016, Nesttun (Bergen). 1-4 employees. Part of group of 6-7 companies with 3-4 subsidiaries.
  - CEO: Per Espen Edvard Edvardsen
  - Board Chairman: James Byrne Murphy (b. 1959)
  - Board Member: Torleif Peder Dahl

**Key Subsidiaries:**
1. **Kitebrook DC Bordalen AS** (922 363 307) — Founded 13.02.2019. Holds Bordalen site.
2. **Kitebrook Sogn Datasenter AS** (925 885 037) — Established 25.09.2020. Sogn region project.
3. **Kitebrook Infrastructure AS** (920 772 331) — Energy supply sector. Manages power/grid infrastructure.

#### Ownership Interpretation

Kitebrook is **privately held by its principals** — primarily J. Byrne Murphy, with Bill Conway as co-investor/partner. It is **not PE-backed** in the traditional sense; rather, a **principal-led investment vehicle** where Murphy and Conway invest their own capital (likely including proceeds from the DigiPlex sale in 2021).

The Delaware JV entity ("Kitebrook Energy Norway 1 JV LLC") suggests there may be additional co-investors or a structured JV for the Norwegian projects, but no external PE fund or institutional investor has been publicly identified.

### Investor Requirements & Constraints

Given **principal-led, self-funded** ownership, Kitebrook operates with **fewer external constraints** than PE-backed operators:

- **IRR Targets:** Not externally imposed. No fund hurdle rate or distribution waterfall.
- **Exit Horizon:** Flexible. DigiPlex held 20 years. No 5-7 year PE timeline.
- **Milestone Gates:** No evidence of externally gated capital. Pre-development phasing appears deliberate.
- **ESG Mandates:** Self-imposed — sustainability is core to value proposition (hydropower, "lowest PUE in the world" heritage). Commercial differentiation, not LP mandates.
- **Reporting Burden:** Minimal external requirements compared to PE/fund-backed peers. Norwegian AS reporting applies.
- **Governance:** Board controlled by principals (Murphy chairman). No external institutional board seats.
- **Debt Covenants:** No evidence of project finance or debt facilities. May seek project finance at construction stage.
- **Capital Deployment Pressure:** Low. No fund lifecycle pressure. 2024 platform launch timed to AI demand surge.

### Regulatory & Permitting Status

**Bordalen (Samnanger) — 100-200 MW:**
- Zoning plan approved after county governor withdrew objections.
- 60,000 m², owned by Kitebrook AS. 100 MW available, upgradeable to 200 MW.
- In power capacity queue for 100 MW at Samnanger.
- Status: **Zoned and approved, no construction commenced.**

**Matre (Masfjorden) — 30 MW:**
- 30 MW power reservation.
- Ground investigations May-July 2025.
- Status: **Pre-development / site investigation.**

**Sogn:**
- Limited public information. Entity registered Sep 2020.
- Status: **Early stage.**

**Regulatory Risk:**
- Norwegian parliament debating **national concession system for data centers** (SV proposal, March 2026). Could impose additional permitting.
- Power grid queue management is a known bottleneck.
- Political debate about DCs consuming power that could serve other industries.

### Key Takeaways for Metier Pitch

1. **Self-funded principal investor, not PE-backed** — Fewer external constraints but limited internal PM bandwidth. No fund manager pushing timeline, but principals have deep DC expertise and will demand execution quality.

2. **Pre-development stage with multiple parallel sites** — 3+ sites at various stages. Managing parallel pre-development across municipalities, grid operators, and regulatory contexts is where Metier's project advisory and owner's engineering add value.

3. **Deep DC expertise but new organizational build** — 20 years of DigiPlex experience, but Kitebrook AS is 1-4 employees. CEO background is oil & gas. Scaling from pre-development to 100+ MW construction requires significant PM capacity.

4. **Regulatory complexity** — Proposed concession system, grid queue management, multi-municipality permitting create project risk that Metier can help navigate.

5. **"DigiPlex 2.0" urgency** — 2024 platform launch + 2025 ground investigations = transition from land banking to active development. This inflection point is when PM advisory becomes essential.
