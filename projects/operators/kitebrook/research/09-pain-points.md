# Pain Point Analysis: Kitebrook
## Date: 2026-04-08

### Operator Archetype

**Primary: Pattern A — "Finance-First Developer"** with significant modifications.

Kitebrook fits Pattern A ("Founded by financial/investment professionals. Ambitious capacity targets. Strong on deal-making and capital raising, weak on execution") with these nuances:

- **Matches Pattern A**: Founded by investment professionals (Murphy/Conway), ambitious MW targets (130+ MW), very small team relative to ambition (1-4 employees), no visible PM or procurement function, no visible engineering team.
- **Differs from Pattern A**: NOT PE-backed — self-funded by principals with patient capital. NOT first-time DC developers — Murphy/Conway built DigiPlex over 20 years. NOT seeking PE investment (as far as publicly known).

This means: They have the **expertise** to know what good DC delivery looks like (unlike a typical Pattern A operator), but they lack the **organizational capacity** to deliver it with their current team. The pitch must respect their deep domain knowledge while addressing the execution gap.

**Secondary traits from Pattern D ("Established Operator Scaling Up")**: The DigiPlex experience gives them operational knowledge, but Kitebrook is a new organization building from scratch — they're not scaling an existing team.

### Top Pain Points (ranked by urgency)

#### 1. No Project Execution Capability for Imminent Construction Phase
- **Description:** Kitebrook has 130 MW of grid capacity across 3 sites, with Bordalen zoning approved and Matre ground surveys completed. They are transitioning from land banking to active development — but have only 1-4 employees in Norway with no PM, no project controls, no construction management, and no engineering staff. The CEO's background is in oil & gas, not DC construction.
- **Evidence:**
  - Zero job postings found on any platform (02-hiring.md)
  - Only 4 identifiable people across all entities (04-team-leadership.md)
  - No EPC contractor, no engineering firm, no PM firm engaged (07-contractors-partners.md)
  - No public tenders on Doffin/TED (07-contractors-partners.md)
  - Ground surveys completed at Matre 2025; Bordalen zoned since 2019 (06-dc-assets-pipeline.md)
- **Urgency:** **HIGH** — The transition from pre-development to construction is the most critical inflection point. If they have or secure a tenant, construction must start quickly. Without PM capability, they cannot deliver.
- **Metier service:** **Project Execution & PM** (PRIMARY), **Owner's Engineer / PMC** (PRIMARY)
- **Confidence:** **HIGH** — Multiple independent signals: no team, no hires, no partners, no procurement activity. The gap is conclusive.

#### 2. EPC/Contractor Procurement Without Internal Procurement Function
- **Description:** When construction begins, Kitebrook will need to select and manage EPC contractors, design consultants, and equipment suppliers for 100+ MW facilities. They have no visible procurement or contracts team, and no EPC relationships yet established. The Bordalen project alone is estimated at NOK 1 billion.
- **Evidence:**
  - No procurement/contracts team in org chart (02-hiring.md, 04-team-leadership.md)
  - No EPC contractor or engineering firm identified (07-contractors-partners.md)
  - No Doffin/TED tenders published (07-contractors-partners.md)
  - ~1 billion NOK project value at Bordalen alone (06-dc-assets-pipeline.md)
- **Urgency:** **HIGH** — Procurement decisions are among the most consequential in DC construction. Getting contract packaging wrong determines risk exposure, schedule, and cost. This needs to happen before construction starts.
- **Metier service:** **Contract, Procurement & Equipment Planning** (PRIMARY)
- **Confidence:** **HIGH** — Confirmed by absence of any procurement activity or personnel.

#### 3. Multi-Site Pre-Development Management Across Municipalities
- **Description:** Kitebrook is managing 3 parallel development tracks (Bordalen, Matre, Sogn) across different municipalities with different grid operators, regulatory contexts, and stakeholders. With 1-4 employees, this coordination burden will only grow as projects advance. The proposed national DC concession system adds regulatory complexity.
- **Evidence:**
  - 3 separate project entities across 3 municipalities (03-company-structure.md)
  - Bordalen NVE application pending since 2020 (06-dc-assets-pipeline.md)
  - Proposed DC concession system debated in parliament Mar 2026 (05-news-ownership-funding.md)
  - Grid queue management across Eviny/Statnett (06-dc-assets-pipeline.md)
- **Urgency:** **MEDIUM-HIGH** — Already relevant now. Each site has different permitting status, grid timeline, and stakeholder context. Delays at one site can ripple to others.
- **Metier service:** **Early-Phase Advisory** (PRIMARY), **Portfolio & Project Owner Governance** (SECONDARY)
- **Confidence:** **MEDIUM** — The multi-site complexity is clear, but the internal response (whether they're coping or struggling) is not visible from public data.

### Unaddressed Pain Points
(Pain points that don't map to Metier services)

1. **Tenant/customer acquisition** — Kitebrook needs hyperscale or large enterprise tenants to trigger construction. Greenbyte partnership addresses this, but it's not a Metier service area.
2. **Project finance** — When construction begins, they may need project finance or additional JV capital. Financial advisory is not Metier's domain.
3. **Grid connection timeline uncertainty** — Statnett's capacity queue and transformer upgrades are outside anyone's control. The Samnanger upgrades are prerequisites for Kitebrook's full capacity access.
4. **Political/regulatory risk** — The proposed DC concession system is a national policy issue. Kitebrook can engage politically but this is beyond consulting services.

### Key Uncertainties
(Questions to validate — these could change the analysis)

1. **Is Kitebrook intentionally staying lean until they have a signed tenant?** If so, the timing of Metier's pitch matters — too early and they'll say "we're not ready yet."
2. **Are there undisclosed external advisors or partners?** The company is very private. There may be engineering or PM relationships not visible publicly.
3. **What is the actual construction trigger?** Is it tenant commitment, project finance closing, or a board decision? This determines urgency.
4. **Does Murphy plan to build the internal team or outsource execution permanently?** His DigiPlex experience (which had a large internal team) might suggest he intends to build internally.
5. **Is the Bordalen timeline delay (2019 zoning → still no construction in 2026) by choice or due to obstacles?** If by choice (waiting for market), less urgent. If due to execution gaps, very relevant for Metier.
6. **What is the relationship with Kjetil Ertnaes/DLE Consulting?** If Ertnaes is already advising on DC strategy, Metier should position complementarily (execution vs. strategy).
