### Implications for Metier

1. **Wide-open PM/Owner's Engineer opportunity.** Most important finding. No visible PM, owner's engineer, or construction advisory partner. 1-4 employees and oil-and-gas CEO running 130 MW pipeline across 3 sites. Need for external PM is acute. No competitor visible.

2. **Pre-construction sweet spot.** Transitioning from land banking to active development (2025 ground surveys, zoning approved). Inflection point where PM advisory becomes essential — before EPC selection, during detailed design, through construction.

3. **No EPC or engineering incumbents to displace.** No major contractor or engineering firm engaged. Metier would fill a vacuum, not compete with an incumbent.

4. **Rime is the only professional services partner.** Legal is the one area with a clear senior advisor (Dahl). Metier would be the first non-legal external advisory firm — both an opportunity and a signal that Kitebrook may be reluctant to engage external advisors.

5. **Greenbyte partnership signals commercial intent.** Sales partnership indicates active marketing to tenants. Securing a tenant triggers construction — and the PM need.

6. **DigiPlex heritage creates high expectations.** Murphy ran one of Europe's most successful DC operators for 20 years. He knows what good delivery looks like. Metier's pitch should respect deep domain expertise while emphasizing dedicated PM capacity that the tiny team cannot provide internally.
