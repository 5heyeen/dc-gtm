# DC Assets & Pipeline: Kitebrook
## Date: 2026-04-08

### Operational Data Centers

**None found.** Kitebrook has no currently operational data center facilities. All activities are in pre-development/planning stages.

### Sites Under Construction

**None confirmed.** No sites appear to be under active construction as of April 2026.

### Development Pipeline

| Site | Location | Planned Capacity | Status | Key Details |
|------|----------|-----------------|--------|-------------|
| **Børdalen** | Samnanger, Vestland (40 min from Bergen) | 100 MW (upgradeable to 200 MW) | Zoning approved; pre-development | 60,000 m² greenfield. Dual-feed electrical power. Redundant fiber 300m from site. Adjacent to Samnanger transformer station. Est. NOK 1 billion investment, ~100 jobs. Suitable for hyperscale, colo, HPC. NVE application submitted end of 2020 — still awaiting case assignment as of March 2022. |
| **Matre** | Masfjorden, Vestland | 30 MW (reserved) | Early-stage planning; ground surveys done | Land purchased for ~NOK 23 million. Ground investigations May-July 2025. Zoning plan revision underway for ~85-hectare business area. Near historic Matre kraftverk (hydropower plant). |
| **Sogn** | Likely Sogn region, Vestland | Unknown | Entity established, no site details public | Kitebrook Sogn Datasenter AS established Sep 2020. Likely project vehicle for Sogn area DC. |

### Statnett / Grid Status

| Site | Grid Queue/Reservation | Details |
|------|----------------------|---------|
| **Børdalen/Samnanger** | **100 MW in capacity queue** | Adjacent to Samnanger transformer station. Statnett adding new transformer (construction started). Samnanger-Øygarden new power line and Sauda-Samnanger upgrade are prerequisites for additional capacity. |
| **Matre/Masfjorden** | **30 MW reserved** | Confirmed allocation from grid operator. |
| **Total** | **130 MW** | Across both sites |

**National context:** As of January 27, 2026, **2,483 MW is reserved nationally for data centers** (31% of all 8,043 MW total reservations). All grid capacity in the Bergen area transmission grid is currently reserved (1,200 MW total across the region).

**Statnett infrastructure projects relevant to Kitebrook:**
- Samnanger transformer station — new transformer being installed (construction started)
- Samnanger-Øygarden new power line — being planned (increases regional capacity)
- Blåfalli-Mauranger-Samnanger power line upgrade — in planning
- These upgrades are prerequisites for Kitebrook (and others) to access their full reserved capacity

### Power & Energy Strategy

- Strategy built around **Norwegian hydropower** as primary power source
- Develops "renewable sources in **100+ MW tranches**"
- Both sites near hydropower production areas
- Børdalen has dual-feed electrical power in a hydropower production area
- Matre is near the historic Matre kraftverk
- **No specific PUE targets** publicly stated (REGN Datacenters heritage claimed "lowest PUE in the world")
- Grid operator for Bergen/Vestland region: **Eviny** (formerly BKK), Norway's second-largest

### Geographic Footprint

**Norway (confirmed activity):**
- Børdalen, Samnanger — main planned site (100-200 MW)
- Matre, Masfjorden — secondary planned site (30 MW)
- Sogn region — project entity established
- Corporate HQ: Nesttun, Bergen

**UK:**
- Website mentions "engineering services in the UK" and "data centers in Scandinavia and the UK" — but **no specific UK sites or capacity identified**

**Sweden, Finland, Denmark:**
- **No presence found.** All identified activity is in Norway.

**US:**
- Kitebrook Partners LLC: Washington, DC
- Kitebrook Energy Norway 1 JV LLC: Delaware (financing vehicle)

### Key Assessment

Kitebrook is an **early-stage data center developer** with DigiPlex pedigree but **no operational infrastructure**:
- 130 MW of grid capacity reserved/queued
- Two development sites in Vestland county, Norway
- No construction commenced at any site
- Børdalen has been in planning since 2019-2020 (originally under REGN Datacenters) — prolonged development timeline
- Matre advancing with 2025 ground surveys and land acquisition
- Gap between announced ambition ("100+ MW tranches," AI-enabled DCs) and visible progress is notable

### Ownership Note
Per Espen Edvardsen holds **8.02%** of Kitebrook AS. Enrec Ltd (UK) is the largest shareholder.
