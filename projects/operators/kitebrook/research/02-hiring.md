## Hiring Pattern Summary

**The complete absence of job postings is the most significant finding.** Kitebrook has:
- Zero active listings on finn.no, LinkedIn, Indeed, NAV/arbeidsplassen, or any other job board
- No careers page on their website
- Only 1–4 registered employees in Norway (per proff.no) across a group of 7 companies
- Only 3 identifiable team members across the entire global organization
- 10–19 total employees globally (per ZoomInfo estimate)

Yet simultaneously, Kitebrook has:
- 130+ MW of data center capacity in development (30 MW Matre + 100 MW Samnanger queue)
- Active ground survey work happening (Matre, May–July 2025)
- A stated ambition to deliver "100+ MW tranches" of AI-enabled data center capacity
- A Børdalen project valued at ~1 billion NOK

**This is an extreme mismatch between ambition/project scope and organizational capacity.**

## What the Pattern Tells Us

The hiring pattern (or lack thereof) points to one of several scenarios:

### Most Likely: Asset-Light Developer / Outsourcing Model
Kitebrook appears to be operating as an **asset-light developer** — a small principal team (Byrne Murphy as capital/strategy, Per Espen Edvardsen as local CEO, Torleif Dahl in a legal/advisory role) that:
- Secures land, power, and permits
- Outsources engineering, construction, and project management to third parties
- May intend to bring in a JV partner, EPC contractor, or sell/develop-to-suit for hyperscalers

This is consistent with Byrne Murphy's track record — DigiPlex was also started small and scaled through partnerships (e.g., with Carlyle Group capital).

### Alternative: Very Early Stage / Pre-FID
The ground surveys at Matre (May–July 2025) suggest Kitebrook may still be in **pre-Final Investment Decision (FID)** phase, not yet ready to hire. They are validating sites before committing capital.

### Alternative: Hiring Through Intermediaries
Kitebrook may be using recruitment agencies, headhunters, or consulting firms to fill roles without public job postings. This is common for small, well-connected firms in Norway.

## Implications for Metier

The absence of hiring combined with ambitious development plans creates a **strong opportunity** for Metier across multiple service lines:

| Metier Service | Relevance | Rationale |
|----------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Project Management** | **Very High** | No visible PM capability for 130+ MW of projects. Kitebrook likely needs external PM either as a bridge (until they hire) or as their primary delivery model. |
| **Project Controls (Cost & Schedule)** | **Very High** | Billion-NOK capex with no visible cost controller or planner. Investor-grade reporting will be required. |
| **Procurement & Contract Management** | **High** | Major EPC/equipment procurement needed for data center builds with no visible procurement team. |
| **Owner's Engineer / Technical Advisory** | **High** | Ground surveys suggest early feasibility stage — Kitebrook needs technical advisory to move from feasibility to FID. |
| **Project Governance & Stage-Gate** | **High** | With Washington D.C.-based ownership and Norwegian operations, there is likely a governance gap. Investor-grade stage-gate processes would be expected by any PE/institutional co-investors. |
| **Risk Management** | **Medium-High** | 100+ MW projects in Norway involve grid connection risk, permitting risk, construction risk, and schedule risk — all requiring structured risk management. |
| **Organizational Development** | **Medium** | If Kitebrook plans to build an internal team, Metier could advise on project organization design. If they plan to stay asset-light, Metier could serve as their outsourced project delivery arm. |

### Key Pitch Angle

Kitebrook is a **textbook case** for Metier's value proposition: a capital-backed developer with ambitious plans but no internal project delivery capability. Metier can position as:

1. **Immediate bridge** — Providing PM/controls capacity while Kitebrook focuses on capital, land, and power
2. **Credibility layer** — Demonstrating to investors/hyperscaler customers that professional project governance is in place
3. **Speed-to-market enabler** — Kitebrook's stated goal is "short-term delivery schedules"; professional PM accelerates this

The conversation should explore: *"How are you planning to deliver these projects? What is your execution model?"* — because from the outside, there is no visible execution capability.
