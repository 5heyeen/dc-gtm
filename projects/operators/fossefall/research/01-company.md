# Company Research: Fossefall

## Date: 2026-03-31

### Company Overview

Fossefall AS is a Norwegian AI infrastructure startup founded on 25 June 2025, registered in Oslo (Org.nr. 935 794 854). The company positions itself as a builder of "AI factories" — next-generation data centers specifically engineered for high-density, GPU-optimized operations demanded by modern AI workloads, rather than traditional data storage. Fossefall's core thesis is converting Norway's renewable hydroelectric energy surplus, cold climate, and political stability into the foundation of Europe's AI economy. The company operates under the tagline of building "Europe's next industrial growth platform, powered by clean energy and artificial intelligence."

Fossefall launched publicly in November 2025 with an ambitious target of developing up to 500 MW of clean AI infrastructure across Norway and Sweden by 2030. The company has raised approximately NOK 60 million in seed capital from investors including Norwegian tech entrepreneur Stian Rustad (founder of 24SevenOffice and Done AI), investment firm Ravenger Establishment, US AI firm Seekr (an AMD Ventures company), Swedish multi-billionaire Robert Samuelsson, and Valinor (the investment firm of Norwegian "wind power king" Lars Helge Helvig). Done.ai also holds a minority stake of approximately 2.4% of shares.

As of March 2026, Fossefall has 6–7 employees, is headquartered at Haakon VIIs gate 10, 0161 Oslo, and is registered under NACE code 62.200 (IT consultancy). The company's parent entity is Ølv Holding AS, and Fossefall AS has 2 subsidiaries (including at least one SPV, Fossefall SPV 1). The company website is at https://www.fossefall.ai/ (note: the fossefall.com domain redirects or is secondary). The team claims over fifty years of combined experience in data centers and infrastructure, having delivered more than 321 MW of power capacity across twenty-five countries.

### Stated Strategy

Fossefall's strategy rests on three pillars:

1. **Clean energy arbitrage for AI compute:** Exploiting the massive electricity cost differential between continental Europe (>USD 0.15/kWh) and Northern Norway's NO4 region (~USD 0.009/kWh from renewable hydropower). The company frames this as enabling AI compute at "industrial electricity prices."

2. **Data sovereignty as a differentiator:** Positioning Nordic-located infrastructure as the answer to European concerns about data sovereignty. Fossefall states: "Data sovereignty is not an add-on — it is the foundation of everything we deliver." This targets enterprises and governments wanting data stored, processed, and governed within stable Nordic democracies rather than US hyperscaler infrastructure.

3. **Vertically integrated AI factory model:** Unlike traditional colocation, Fossefall aims to combine power generation/procurement, storage, GPU infrastructure, and AI cloud services into a single value chain. Through the Seekr partnership, they plan to offer a complete enterprise AI stack (infrastructure + AI software). Through the Done.ai partnership, they are exploring GPU-as-a-Service offerings.

The company targets 11+ sites in Norway (and an unknown number in Sweden), with total pipeline exceeding 385 MW across these sites. First projects are expected operational by end of 2026.

### Key Observations for Metier Engagement

1. **Finance-first team confirmed:** CEO's background is investment/finance (Glacier Link, Valinor). The "finansgutta" characterization in the seed data is accurate. The team has strong deal-making and capital-raising skills but limited in-house construction/engineering capability.

2. **Execution gap is real:** Only 6–7 employees. Karl Inge Halleraker (from Aker Nscale) was brought in specifically to lead project execution — this is a very recent hire (Jan 2026). Engineering Manager position is still open. The company is clearly in the early stages of building execution capability.

3. **Ambitious pipeline vs. thin team:** 11+ sites, 385+ MW pipeline, multiple municipalities in active discussions — but a team of under 10 people. This creates enormous demand for external project management, owner's engineer services, and procurement support.

4. **Local opposition emerging:** Both Harpefoss and Rollag projects face local debate and community pushback. The Sør-Fron municipality postponed its agreement signing and held a public meeting. This adds complexity requiring stakeholder management and permitting expertise.

5. **Seekr/US defense connection is a sensitivity:** Local media in Sør-Fron flagged that Seekr develops technology for the US military. This could become a recurring issue in community engagement for future sites.

6. **Project finance structure:** SPV model suggests site-by-site financing, which means each project needs its own business case, permitting timeline, and construction plan — exactly the type of work where Metier's services (owner's engineer, project execution, contract strategy) are most relevant.
