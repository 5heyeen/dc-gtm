# Task Breakdown: Series A Funding Requirements for a Forest Reforestation Program (Indonesia)

**Scope:** What is required to raise Series A funding for a pre-revenue forest reforestation program operating in Indonesia, covering carbon credit certification, investor expectations, financial metrics, due diligence, governance, ESG standards, the investor landscape, and pitch positioning.
**Date:** 2026-04-30
**Project:** Bifrost Edge

---

### Task 01: Carbon Credit Standards and Certification Requirements
Research the main voluntary carbon credit standards applicable to Indonesian reforestation projects: VCS/Verra, Gold Standard, Plan Vivo, and any others. Cover what certification each requires, typical timelines to first issuance, MRV (monitoring, reporting, verification) system requirements (including drone-based MRV), verification costs, and what proof-of-concept or pilot scale is needed before a project is certifiable. Completion: a sourced summary of the top 2–3 applicable standards, their requirements, timelines, and costs for an Indonesian reforestation project.

### Task 02: Series A Financial Metrics and Traction Benchmarks for Nature-Based Solutions
Research what financial metrics, traction signals, and KPIs Series A investors expect from a pre-revenue or early-revenue nature-based solutions (NbS) or reforestation company. Cover carbon credit pipeline size (tonnes CO2e), land area secured, verified vs. forward credits, revenue projections, cost-per-tonne benchmarks, and comparables from recent Series A raises in the NbS/reforestation sector. Completion: a sourced set of benchmarks and traction thresholds, with examples from comparable companies that have raised Series A.

### Task 03: Due Diligence Requirements for Land Acquisition and Indonesian Operations
Research what legal, regulatory, and operational due diligence investors expect for a reforestation program acquiring land and operating drones in Indonesia. Cover: land title types (HGU, HGB, community forest rights), environmental impact assessment (AMDAL/UKL-UPL) requirements, Ministry of Environment and Forestry (KLHK) permits, drone operation licensing (DGCA Indonesia), community benefit-sharing agreements, and anti-corruption compliance (FCPA, UK Bribery Act). Completion: a sourced checklist of legal and regulatory requirements investors will scrutinise in due diligence.

### Task 04: ESG and Impact Measurement Standards for Institutional Investors
Research what ESG reporting frameworks and impact measurement standards institutional investors (development banks, impact funds) require from reforestation/NbS investments. Cover IFC Performance Standards (especially PS6 Biodiversity), IRIS+ metrics for forestry, GRI Standards, TCFD climate disclosure, the Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures (TNFD), and co-benefit standards (biodiversity, community livelihoods). Completion: a sourced list of the key frameworks required or expected by institutional investors in this space.

### Task 05: Governance and Corporate Structural Requirements for Series A
Research what governance and legal entity structures Series A investors require for an Indonesia-based reforestation business. Cover: holding company structure (e.g. Singapore HoldCo + Indonesian PT), board composition (independent directors, investor board seats), ESOP/cap table norms, shareholder agreement provisions (pro-rata rights, drag-along, information rights), and any Indonesia-specific foreign ownership restrictions (PMA rules) relevant to a reforestation/forestry business. Completion: a sourced description of the corporate structure and governance provisions investors typically require.

### Task 06: Investor Landscape for Reforestation and Carbon Credits in Indonesia / Southeast Asia
Research and map the active investors funding reforestation, nature-based solutions, and carbon credit programs in Indonesia and Southeast Asia. Include: impact VCs (e.g. Mirova, LeapFrog, Circulate Capital, Clime Capital), development finance institutions (ADB, IFC, Norfund, FMO), carbon market buyers acting as investors (South Pole, Xpansiv, voluntary market corporates), Norwegian/Nordic impact investors (Norfund, Grieg Foundation, Nordic Impact), and any government grant programs (NICFI, Green Climate Fund, REDD+). For each, note their typical ticket size, stage focus, and any known requirements. Completion: a sourced investor map with at least 8–10 named investors/funders and their key criteria.

### Task 07: Series A Pitch Narrative and Positioning for Reforestation / Carbon Credit Companies
Research how reforestation and nature-based solutions companies have successfully positioned for Series A. Cover: common pitch narrative structures (problem/solution/market/traction/team/ask), how to present a carbon credit pipeline as a revenue model, how to address key investor objections (permanence risk, additionality, land tenure risk, regulatory risk), what the competitive landscape looks like, and examples of Series A decks or fundraising stories from comparable companies (e.g. South Pole, Terrasos, Pachama, Terraformation, Propagate Ventures). Completion: a sourced guide to the pitch narrative with examples from comparable fundraises.

### Task 08: Pre-Series A Preparation Checklist (Synthesis)
Synthesise findings from Tasks 01–07 into a concrete, prioritised checklist of everything that needs to be in place before approaching Series A investors. Organise by category: (a) carbon certification progress, (b) legal/regulatory compliance in Indonesia, (c) financial model and carbon pipeline, (d) governance and corporate structure, (e) ESG/impact reporting readiness, (f) team composition, (g) investor materials. For each item, note whether it is a hard requirement (deal-breaker if missing) or a soft expectation (strengthens the raise). Completion: a structured checklist ready for use as a fundraising preparation roadmap.
