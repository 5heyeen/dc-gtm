# Socratic Research Prompts: Data Center Go-To-Market Plan for Metier + Norconsult

> **Topic:** Design a go-to-market strategy for a joint Metier (project consultancy) + Norconsult (engineering consultancy) data center offering in Norway — defining what services to sell, to whom, when, why, and how.
> **Use these prompts in sequence in any AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) for deep, structured research.**

---

## Principles — What Makes a B2B Professional Services GTM Plan Work

- `[independent]` What are the foundational principles of a successful go-to-market strategy for B2B professional services in capital-intensive infrastructure sectors? What separates a GTM plan that actually drives revenue from one that sits on a shelf?

- `[independent]` In professional services (consulting, engineering advisory), what makes a value proposition genuinely compelling to buyers — as opposed to a list of capabilities? What's the difference between "we can do X" and "you need us because Y"?

- `[independent]` What principles govern successful market entry when a firm already has adjacent expertise (project management, engineering) but is building a sector-specific practice (data centers)? How does a "credibility transfer" from adjacent work actually function?

- `[independent]` What are the principles of effective service packaging in consulting? When should services be sold as discrete deliverables vs. ongoing retainers vs. embedded teams? What determines the right model for infrastructure project advisory?

- `[independent]` What makes a partnership or alliance between two professional services firms (one focused on management/PM, one on engineering) work commercially in practice? What are the governance, revenue-sharing, and client-ownership principles that prevent internal friction from destroying external value?

## Framework — The Components That Matter

- `[independent]` What is the standard framework for analyzing a professional services market opportunity? Map the components: market sizing, customer segmentation, competitive positioning, service-market fit, pricing, channel strategy, capability gap analysis.

- `[independent]` What framework should be used to map consulting services onto a client's project lifecycle? How do you identify which phases have the highest willingness-to-pay, the lowest competitive intensity, and the strongest lock-in effects?

- `[independent]` What is the right framework for assessing a data center operator's strategic position — specifically their access to capital, customers, land, and power — and how does each factor shape their execution strategy and consulting needs?

- `[independent]` How should a consulting firm evaluate "white space" in a competitive market? What's the analytical framework for distinguishing genuine unmet needs from needs that are unmet because they're not valuable enough to serve?

- `[sequential]` Given the components above, what is the right sequencing for building a data center consulting practice? What do you prove first, what do you build second, and what do you scale third? How does the sequence differ if you already have live client engagements (as Metier does)?

- `[independent]` What metrics and signals indicate that a professional services GTM strategy is working in its first 12-18 months? What are leading indicators vs. lagging indicators? How do you avoid vanity metrics (e.g., "pipeline created") and focus on real traction?

- `[independent]` What framework maps regulatory complexity to consulting demand? How do you quantify the advisory revenue that a specific regulatory change creates — and how do you time your market entry to that regulatory window?

## Competitive Dynamics

- `[independent]` Map the competitive landscape for data center advisory services in the Nordics. For each competitor type — Nordic engineering firms (Rambøll, Sweco, WSP, Multiconsult, AFRY, Granlund), international specialists (Turner & Townsend, Faithful+Gould, Arcadis, Uptime Institute), management consultancies (Big 4, MBB), and EPCM contractors (Bilfinger, ABB) — what services do they offer, and what is their positioning?

- `[independent]` What is the typical competitive dynamic in a data center consulting engagement in Norway? Is it usually a competitive tender, a relationship-based appointment, or a framework agreement call-off? How does this vary by operator type and engagement size?

- `[independent]` Where are the genuine gaps in the current competitive landscape? What do data center operators in Norway say they cannot easily buy from existing providers? Is it specific expertise, capacity/availability, understanding of Norwegian regulations, or project management capability?

- `[sequential]` How does the combined Metier (PM, project controls, execution models, gap analysis) + Norconsult (engineering, permitting, grid connection, EPCM) capability set compare to each competitor type identified above? - `[independent]` What partnership or alliance models do competitors use in this market? Who teams with whom, and why? Are there exclusive partnerships, or is it fluid and project-by-project?

## Objection Pre-Empt — What the Steering Group Will Challenge

- `[independent]` "We're too late — the market is already sewn up by incumbents." Is this true? What evidence supports or refutes the idea that the Norwegian DC advisory market still has room for a new entrant?

- `[independent]` "We should just do more of what we're already doing — why do we need a formal GTM plan?" What is the risk of organic, opportunistic growth vs. a deliberate practice-building strategy in a market that's rapidly scaling?

- `[independent]` "Norconsult already does data center work — why does Metier need to be involved?" What is the specific incremental value that Metier's PM/advisory capability adds on top of Norconsult's engineering? When does a client need both vs. just one?

- `[independent]` "The power grid bottleneck will slow everything down — should we wait?" How should grid connection delays be factored into the GTM timeline? Does the bottleneck reduce demand for advisory, or increase it (because navigating the bottleneck IS the advisory need)?

- `[independent]` "Our existing clients (Bulk, Green Mountain, Google) will just give us more work — we don't need to pursue new operators." What is the concentration risk of depending on 3-4 clients? What happens if one relationship ends or a project is delayed?

- `[sequential]` For each objection above, what is the strongest evidence-based rebuttal that would convince a skeptical C-suite executive?

## Application — Metier + Norconsult Specific

- `[sequential]` Now apply all of the above to design the go-to-market plan for Metier + Norconsult's joint data center offering:

  - **Our current position:** Metier has live DC engagements with Bulk (project controls, execution model, gap analysis), Green Mountain (PM), and Google/Gromstul (PM). Norconsult has 15+ DC engineering projects across Norway and Sweden (Bulk, Green Mountain, Google, Stack/Digiplex, Lefdal Mine, Evroc, Telenor, TikTok/Bytedance). We have relationships but no formal joint DC practice.

  - **Our combined capabilities:** Metier brings PM, project controls, execution models, gap analysis, strategy advisory, competence development. Norconsult brings engineering design, building physics, electrical/HVAC, geotechnics, grid connection/licensing, EPCM, construction management, Norwegian code compliance.

  - **Our constraints:** Norway-first market. Must justify to a cross-company steering group. Need to show ROI and phased investment. Must navigate internal Metier-Norconsult organizational dynamics.

  - **Our goal:** A service catalog with: what to sell, to which operators (specific companies), in which project phases, why (justified with evidence), how (delivery model, pricing, partnerships), and a phased roadmap for building the practice.

  - **Specific questions to answer:**
    1. Which 3-5 services should we lead with in the first 12 months, and why?
