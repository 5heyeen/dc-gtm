## Service: Contract, Procurement & Equipment Planning

**Pain points addressed:**
1. First-time DC developers don't know how to split MEP scopes — defaulting to full EPC when a split-package delt entreprise model would give them more control and better pricing.
2. Long-lead equipment (transformers 18–36 months, generators 12–18 months, UPS/switchgear 12+ months) must be procured before FID — operators without procurement experience miss this critical parallel track.
3. PE-backed operators face debt covenant risk if procurement is poorly structured — cost overruns from bad contract strategy directly threaten DSCR ratios.
4. High-security operators (Pattern B) face a severely restricted contractor pool due to sikkerhetsklarering requirements — standard competitive tendering processes don't work when only 3–4 contractors can legally bid.
5. International DC developers entering Norway don't know NS 8405/8407 contracting norms, how to structure bonus/malus provisions, or how Norwegian contractor market pricing works.

**Operator archetypes that buy this:**

| Archetype | Fit | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Pattern A — Finance-First Developer | PRIMARY | No procurement/contracts team; will select EPC contractor without any framework |
| Pattern B — High-Security Operator | PRIMARY | Cleared contractor procurement is uniquely constrained — standard processes fail |
| Pattern D — Established Operator Scaling Up | HIGH | Multi-site procurement needs standardised frameworks and OEM rammeavtaler |
| International DC developers | HIGH | Don't know Norwegian contracting norms; need NS/FIDIC translation |
| Debt-funded (funding-driven) | HIGH | Covenant protection requires airtight cost control from contract structure |

**Entry trigger (observable signals):**
- No procurement/contracts team visible in org chart or job ads
- EPC contractor selection underway without visible contract advisory
- Rapid expansion plans announced (need procurement frameworks for multiple builds)
- High-security DC announced (Pattern B) → cleared contractor pool challenge immediately apparent
- International operator entering Norwegian market for first time

**Decision-maker:** VP Construction / CPO for procurement decisions; CEO for contract strategy; CFO/PE sponsor for covenant-linked decisions

**Metier pitch (2 sentences):**
"How you split your contracts determines your risk exposure, your schedule, and your final cost — and most first-time DC developers get this wrong before they've even broken ground. We design the contract strategy, run the tender, qualify the contractors, and manage the procurement of long-lead equipment so that the decisions made in month one don't become the problems you're explaining in month eighteen."

**Key objection to pre-empt:**
*"Our EPC contractor handles procurement."*
Counter: When your EPC contractor procures, they optimise for their margin and schedule — not yours. Long-lead equipment procured by the contractor arrives to the contractor's spec, not yours. Owner-procured long-lead (OFCI model) gives you specification control, lead time visibility, and the ability to switch contractors without equipment risk. The 5% advisory fee on procurement is recovered in the first equipment negotiation.

**Competitive differentiator vs. Mitchell McDermott, Turner & Townsend:**
- Mitchell McDermott has the deepest DC-specific OFCI package management capability globally (specialist package managers, proprietary procurement methodology).
- T&T has scale and DC reference base but no Norwegian footprint.
- Metier's differentiator: Norwegian contractor market knowledge (who can deliver, at what price, with what lead times), NS standards expertise, and — for Pattern B — understanding of the sikkerhetsklarering procurement constraint that no international firm can match.
