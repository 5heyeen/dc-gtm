## Service: Owner's Engineer / PMC

**Pain points addressed:**
1. Non-technical owners (PE funds, real estate developers, energy companies) building their first DC have no ability to evaluate whether the EPC contractor is doing the right thing — they need eyes and voice on site.
2. EPC/EPCM contractors self-certify quality; without an independent OE, the owner has no early warning on scope creep, design deviations, or commissioning risks.
3. Long-lead equipment (transformers, UPS, generators) arrives at the wrong spec or late — an OE tracking FAT witness and expediting prevents this.
4. MEP interface failures (power/cooling/IT fit-out interfaces) are the most common cause of DC commissioning delay — an OE coordinates across workstreams.
5. PE-backed operators need independent technical validation for board CAPEX approvals and lender drawdown requests.

**Operator archetypes that buy this:**

| Archetype | Fit | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Pattern A — Finance-First Developer | PRIMARY | Founders from finance/consulting have zero construction DNA; OE is non-negotiable |
| Pattern B — High-Security Operator | HIGH | Cleared contractors and complex security requirements need independent technical oversight |
| Pattern C — Utility Spin-off | MEDIUM | Parent has engineering capability but DC-specific oversight gaps |
| Pattern D — Established Operator Scaling Up | MEDIUM | Parallel sites stretch internal oversight capacity |
| PE-backed (funding-driven) | HIGH | Investors require independent technical representation for CAPEX gate approvals |

**Entry trigger (observable signals):**
- PE investment closed or announced → operator now accountable to investors; needs independent eyes
- EPC contract awarded or tendering in progress → OE needed before design freeze
- VP Construction hired but PMO not yet formed → gap visible in org chart
- First-ever DC project announced → classic Pattern A signal
- Announced timeline seems aggressive relative to team size

**Decision-maker:** CEO / project owner (Pattern A); Board / PE sponsor co-decision (PE-backed operators); VP Construction for operational scope

**Metier pitch (2 sentences):**
"Data center projects fail at the interface between your contractor's interests and yours — when design decisions get made that cost you flexibility, when equipment specs drift, when commissioning milestones slip before you even know there's a risk. We act as your technical proxy throughout the build: independent, owner-aligned, and on site when it matters."

**Key objection to pre-empt:**
*"We have a VP Construction — we don't need an OE."*
Counter: A VP Construction manages the process; an OE represents the owner's technical interests in contractor disputes, design reviews, and CAPEX gate decisions. They are complementary, not competing. On a NOK 1B+ project, the OE fee is a rounding error against the cost of a missed commissioning date.

**Competitive differentiator vs. COWI, Turner & Townsend:**
- COWI (Skygard) and T&T bring DC-specific engineering depth; Metier brings owner-side advisory DNA and Norway-specific regulatory context.
- Metier's strength: independence (no design or construction revenue to protect), 40 years of owner representation, Norwegian contractor market knowledge.
- Positioning: "We are not engineers who also do OE — we are owner representatives who ensure engineers and contractors deliver what you paid for."
