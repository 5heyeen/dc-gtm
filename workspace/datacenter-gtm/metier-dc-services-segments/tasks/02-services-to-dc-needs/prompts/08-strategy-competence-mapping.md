# Chunk 08: strategy-and-competence-mapping

> **Topic:** Service-to-need mapping for Strategy Advisory + Competence Development

---

## Service A: Strategy & Business Development Advisory

**Pain points addressed:**
1. New DC entrants (Pattern A utility spin-offs, international developers) often position broadly ("we do everything") without a defined market niche — this makes customer acquisition and pricing impossible.
2. AI-driven demand shift is forcing established colo operators to rethink their offering (air-cooled colo → liquid-cooled AI/HPC) without internal strategic competence to manage the transition.
3. Operators need organisational design that matches their strategic ambition — a 20-person team announcing a 1GW DC portfolio needs a scaling plan before they can hire credibly.
4. Competitive positioning in Norwegian DC market requires understanding of who the real buyers are (hyperscalers, sovereign cloud, enterprise) and what genuinely differentiates Norwegian locations vs. Ireland/Netherlands.

**Operator archetypes that buy this:**
| Archetype | Fit | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Pattern C — Utility Spin-off | PRIMARY | Translating infrastructure assets into DC market position requires strategic framing |
| Pattern A — New DC Entrant | HIGH | Defining market position before first customer conversations |
| Pattern D — Scaling Up | MEDIUM | Pivoting strategy (colo → AI/HPC) or defining multi-country expansion |

**Entry trigger:** New market entrant without DC industry experience; strategy announcements that don't match visible capabilities; "we do everything" positioning in early conversations; significant AI/HPC pivot announced; first fundraise being prepared (investors want to see a coherent strategy).

**Decision-maker:** CEO (always); Board for significant strategy pivots; PE sponsor for strategy validation pre-investment.

**Metier pitch (2 sentences):**
"The Norwegian DC market is maturing fast — the operators who will win the next 5 years are the ones who pick a lane (hyperscale, sovereign/high-security, AI/HPC) and build an organisation that can actually execute it, not the ones who say yes to every RFQ. We help DC operators develop a clear market position and the organisational model to deliver on it."

**Key objection:** *"We have a strategy — we need execution help, not more strategy."*
Counter: We agree — strategy without execution support is consulting theatre. Offer Metier's strategy work as an accelerator for the PMO and OE engagement: "We define the model so the execution we do together is pointed at the right target." Strategy as a door-opener, not a standalone.

**Note on positioning risk:** Leading with strategy advisory to a DC operator with no Metier DC references is a credibility risk. This service should follow, not precede, an operational entry (OE, PM, procurement). The exception is Pattern C utility spin-offs, where strategic framing of the DC opportunity is genuinely the first question.

## Service B: Competence Development & Training (Metier Academy)

**Pain points addressed:**
1. Fast-growing DC operators hire 20–50 people quickly but have no structured onboarding — new hires arrive without a shared project management methodology, vocabulary, or toolset.
2. Mix of experienced and junior hires means project delivery quality varies dramatically across teams and sites.
3. Operators building internal PM capability want to reduce long-term dependency on external consultants — a training program builds this without requiring immediate departure of Metier resources.
4. DC-specific PM knowledge (MEP package management, commissioning planning, equipment procurement, security compliance) doesn't exist in standard PRINCE2/PMI curricula — operators lack this even after certification.

**Operator archetypes that buy this:**
| Archetype | Fit | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Pattern D — Established Operator Scaling Up | PRIMARY | Rapid hiring creates the training demand; 10+ roles open is the trigger |
| Pattern A — Finance-First (post-entry) | MEDIUM | After first OE/PM engagement, build internal capability |

**Entry trigger:** 10+ project-related roles open simultaneously; mix of experienced and junior hires; no visible internal training or development programs; desire to reduce consultant dependency stated explicitly.

**Decision-maker:** COO / Head of People & Organisation; sometimes VP Construction for PM-specific training.

**Metier pitch (2 sentences):**
"Hiring fast is easy — building a team that actually delivers consistently is the hard part, and the difference is whether your new hires share a methodology or improvise one. Metier Academy has trained 16,000 professionals annually and can design a custom onboarding program that gives your growing DC team the project management backbone they need to deliver reliably from day one."

**Note on entry sequencing:** Competence development is rarely a cold-entry service — it almost always follows a PM or OE engagement where Metier has demonstrated the methodology they're now teaching. Treat as a follow-on and relationship-deepener, not a door-opener.
