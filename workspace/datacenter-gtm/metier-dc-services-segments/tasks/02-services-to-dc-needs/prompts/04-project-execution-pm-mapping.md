## Service: Project Execution & PM (Byggherreombud / PMO)

**Pain points addressed:**
1. Finance-First Developers announce ambitious MW targets but have no internal project management capability — there is no one to own schedule, cost control, or contractor interface management.
2. DC construction schedules are commissioning-driven (everything reversed from energisation date); generic planners default to forward scheduling and miss the critical path.
3. Phased delivery (2MW live → 4MW → 8MW) means live phases exist alongside active construction — interface management requires dedicated PMs who understand DC sequencing.
4. Equipment-heavy cost structure (~40–50% equipment, not construction) means traditional cost controllers tracking civil progress will miss the dominant cost driver entirely.
5. PE exit horizon pressure: a 6-month construction delay directly erodes IRR and threatens the exit window — professional PM is the primary risk mitigation against this.

**Operator archetypes that buy this:**

| Archetype | Fit | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Pattern A — Finance-First Developer | PRIMARY | Has vision and capital, zero PM capability — this is the #1 service they need |
| Pattern D — Established Operator Scaling Up | HIGH | Going from 1 to 3+ sites simultaneously stretches internal PM capacity |
| Pattern C — Utility Spin-off | MEDIUM | Parent has PM capability but DC-specific knowledge gaps; bring in DC-experienced PMs |
| PE-backed (funding-driven) | PRIMARY | IRR-driven timeline pressure makes professional PM economically essential |

**Entry trigger (observable signals):**
- Aggressive timeline announced publicly (e.g., "first MW live by Q3 2026") with no visible PM team
- Hiring for VP Construction but not for project managers or planners
- Multiple sites under development simultaneously (Pattern D signal)
- Construction start announced within 6–12 months with no project controls function visible
- PE round closed → capital now deployed → execution risk now real

**Decision-maker:** VP Construction / COO (operational decision); CEO/CFO for large PMO engagements; PE sponsor may require PM capability as investment condition

**Metier pitch (2 sentences):**
"Data center construction schedules are decided in the commissioning room, not on the building site — and the teams that understand this difference deliver on time while everyone else explains their delays. We provide the project management backbone from contract award through commissioning, so your first MW generating revenue happens when you told your investors it would."

**Key objection to pre-empt:**
*"We're building the team internally."*
Counter: Internal teams take 6–12 months to become effective on a new project; the critical procurement and design lock-in decisions happen in the first 90 days. Metier bridges the gap while you build, and we can transfer methodology so your team becomes self-sufficient. The cost of getting procurement wrong in month 1 is a year of delay and 10–20% CAPEX overrun.

**Competitive differentiator vs. COWI, Turner & Townsend:**
- T&T (500+ DC projects, 10GW) has unmatched DC PM depth globally but no Norwegian footprint — they will bring an international team unfamiliar with NS standards, Norwegian contractors, and local regulatory processes.
- COWI holds the byggherreombud role at Skygard (43 companies, 18 trade packages) — this is a direct competitor match. Metier's counter: 40 years of building Norway's PM profession, deeper relationships with Norwegian contractors, and comparable capability without COWI's engineering conflict of interest.
- Metier differentiator: the combination of project management AND methodology (VPU, Safran, Omega 365) AND competence building — T&T and COWI don't offer to build the client's internal capability.
