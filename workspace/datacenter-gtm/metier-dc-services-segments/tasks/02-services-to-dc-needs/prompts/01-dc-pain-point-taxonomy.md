# Chunk 01: dc-pain-point-taxonomy
> **Topic:** DC operator pain points by phase and archetype
---

## Part A: Pain Points by Development Lifecycle Phase

### 1. Concept / Feasibility Phase
| Pain Point | Urgency | Feels It Most | Consultancy Entry Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Site selection with no DC-specific criteria (power, latency, risk) | HIGH | Finance-First Developer, Utility Spin-off | Announced capacity targets but no site confirmed |
| Technology choices made without benchmarking (tier level, cooling, redundancy) | HIGH | Utility Spin-off, Finance-First Developer | Board asking for investment case before concept is fixed |
| No structured concept-selection methodology (options compared by gut, not framework) | MEDIUM | Finance-First Developer | Investor requiring IRR/NPV model before committing capital |
| Phasing strategy undefined — full buildout vs. incremental MW | MEDIUM | All archetypes | PE investor asking what triggers Phase 2 spend |

### 2. Pre-Construction / Planning Phase
| Pain Point | Urgency | Feels It Most | Consultancy Entry Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Contract packaging wrong — scope split between civil, MEP, IT creates interface risk | HIGH | Finance-First Developer | EPC tender about to launch with no contract strategy |
| Cleared contractor pool too thin for security requirements | HIGH | High-Security Operator | Security clearance processing time threatens schedule |
| No procurement plan for long-lead equipment (transformers, generators, UPS — 18-36 wk lead times) | HIGH | Finance-First Developer, Utility Spin-off | Equipment procurement not started; schedule already at risk |
| No one to review contractor bids technically — owner lacks DC construction expertise | HIGH | Finance-First Developer | Contractor selection in progress with no internal QA |
| Permitting and zoning complexity underestimated | MEDIUM | Finance-First Developer, Utility Spin-off | Permit delays surfacing; schedule slipping |

### 3. Construction Phase
| Pain Point | Urgency | Feels It Most | Consultancy Entry Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| No cost control or schedule tracking — owner flying blind | CRITICAL | Finance-First Developer | Contractor claims increasing; owner has no counter-data |
| Interface failures between civil, power, cooling, IT workstreams | HIGH | All archetypes | Commissioning date at risk; blame being passed between contractors |
| Owner has no technical representative on site — contractor decisions go unchallenged | HIGH | Finance-First Developer, High-Security Operator | Quality issue discovered late; variation orders spiking |
| No progress data to satisfy investor milestone reporting requirements | HIGH | Finance-First Developer (PE-backed) | Upcoming capital tranche linked to milestone sign-off |
| Workforce capacity — PMs stretched across too many parallel builds | MEDIUM | Established Operator Scaling Up | Second site starting before first is commissioned |

### 4. Operations / Ramp-up Phase
| Pain Point | Urgency | Feels It Most | Consultancy Entry Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| New hires lack DC project management competence — delivery quality degrades at scale | MEDIUM | Established Operator Scaling Up | 10+ PM/engineering roles hired within 12 months |
| No governance framework across multiple sites — decisions inconsistent | HIGH | Established Operator Scaling Up | Portfolio growing; board asking for standardised reporting |
| DCIM and operational systems not integrated with project delivery data | LOW-MEDIUM | Established Operator Scaling Up | Expansion planned; operational data not feeding next concept |

## Part B: Pain Points by Operator Archetype

| Archetype | Top 3 Pain Points | Primary Services |
|---|---|---|
| **Finance-First Developer** | No execution capability (PM, procurement, Owner's Engineer); timeline pressure from PE; no cost control | Project Execution & PM, Contract & Procurement, Owner's Engineer |
| **High-Security Operator** | Cleared contractor pool thin; procurement constrained by security rules; schedule risk from clearance lead times | Contract & Procurement, Project Execution & PM |
| **Utility Spin-off** | Concept not yet translated into viable DC product; no DC market positioning; staff need DC upskilling | Early-Phase Advisory, Strategy Advisory, Competence Development |
| **Established Operator Scaling Up** | Governance gaps as portfolio grows; procurement not yet standardised; PM capacity stretched | Portfolio Governance, Contract & Procurement, Competence Development |

## Part C: Funding-Driven Pain Points

These cut across archetypes. Strongest for PE-backed and infrastructure-fund-backed operators. Often the most urgent entry point.

| Funding Condition | Pain Point | Urgency | Entry Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PE exit horizon (3-7 yr)** | Every month of delay erodes IRR — schedule slippage is a financial event, not just an operational one | CRITICAL | PE investor asking for accelerated commissioning plan |
| **Milestone-gated capital** | Next tranche withheld if milestone slips — project controls are survival-critical | CRITICAL | Capital freeze risk identified in board papers or investor comms |
| **ESG mandates (EU Taxonomy, PUE targets)** | Must document renewable sourcing, energy efficiency, and climate alignment for investor reporting | HIGH | Investor requiring Taxonomy alignment before next close |
| **High quarterly reporting burden** | Operator lacks capacity to produce investor-grade cost/schedule variance reports | HIGH | Investor complaining about report quality or frequency |
| **Board governance provisions (CAPEX thresholds)** | Investors hold board seats and require third-party validation for material CAPEX decisions | HIGH | Board meeting approaching; CAPEX item needs independent backing |
| **Debt covenants / DSCR requirements** | Cost overruns directly threaten financial ratios — procurement discipline is not optional | HIGH | Lender requesting evidence of cost control processes |
| **First-time capital raise** | Operator needs to demonstrate execution credibility to attract investors or lenders | MEDIUM | Fundraising roadshow or debt facility negotiation in progress |
| **Multiple capital classes (equity + debt)** | Equity investors and lenders want different cuts of the same data — unified reporting is missing | MEDIUM | Lender audit or investor update requiring consolidated project data |
