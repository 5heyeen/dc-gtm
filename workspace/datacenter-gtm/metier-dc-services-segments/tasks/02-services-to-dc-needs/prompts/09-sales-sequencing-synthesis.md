## Sales Sequencing: Entry Points vs. Follow-Ons

The core insight from the per-service mappings: **not all services are created equal as entry points**. Some require pre-existing trust; others are sold on the strength of a scoped proposal. The following sequence maximises win rate and revenue per client.

### Phase 1 — Entry Services (cold engagement, no prior relationship required)

These services address a specific, near-term, high-stakes problem the operator has right now. They can be scoped narrowly, require no track record in the sector, and deliver visible value quickly.

| Service | Why it works as entry | Best archetype |
|---------|----------------------|----------------|
| Owner's Engineer / PMC | Fills a structural gap; no internal alternative; scoped by project phase | Pattern A (PE-backed) |
| Contract & Procurement Advisory | Pre-tender window is time-bound; operators know they need it | Pattern A, B, International |
| Project Execution & PM | Visible org chart gap; concrete deliverable (schedule, cost report) | Pattern A, D |
| Uncertainty Analysis (UA) | Triggered by FID/investor requirement; scoped deliverable; no prior relationship needed | PE-backed (all archetypes) |

### Phase 2 — Relationship-Deepening Services (once trust established)

These services require the operator to grant Metier access to governance processes, strategy, and internal data. They follow naturally after a successful Phase 1 engagement.

| Service | Natural precursor | Revenue potential |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|
| Portfolio Governance | After OE or PM engagement on first site | HIGH — recurring, multi-year |
| Early-Phase Advisory / KS | After UA or governance work; when next site is being evaluated | HIGH — per project |
| Strategy Advisory | After any engagement; when operator faces positioning decision | MEDIUM — episodic |
| Competence Development | After PMO establishment; when operator wants to build internal capability | MEDIUM — recurring |

## Key Sales Guidance

1. **Lead with a scoped problem, not a service portfolio.** "We can help with your procurement strategy for the transformer and generator packages" wins faster than "we offer 8 services across the DC development lifecycle."

2. **Target the pre-tender and pre-FID windows.** These are the natural buying moments — operator has committed to building but hasn't yet locked in the decisions that matter most.

3. **Use the PE sponsor as an entry channel.** For PE-backed operators, the investor often has the clearest view of what governance and execution risk management the portfolio company needs. A relationship with the sponsor is worth more than 10 cold calls to the VP Construction.

4. **Don't pitch strategy to operators in execution.** They don't want it. Save strategy for operators at inflection points (entering the market, pivoting strategy, planning multi-country expansion).

5. **Reference the KS track record explicitly.** "We've reviewed investment decisions worth over 800 billion NOK" is a credibility signal no DC-specialist competitor can match in Norway. Use it early and often.
