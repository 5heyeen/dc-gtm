## Service: Portfolio & Project Owner Governance

**Pain points addressed:**
1. PE-backed operators must produce investor-grade project reports (typically monthly or quarterly) tracking MW commissioned, schedule adherence, cost-to-complete, and risk — most lack the reporting infrastructure to do this.
2. Milestone-gated capital structures mean a missed milestone triggers a capital freeze — without a governance system tracking milestones in real time, operators discover the breach after the fact.
3. Multi-site operators building 3+ DCs simultaneously have no consistent governance framework — each site runs its own tracking, reporting style, and risk management process.
4. Board governance provisions (PE investor board seats, CAPEX approval thresholds) require the operator to produce independent, auditable project status for every board decision — without a governance framework, these become fire drills.
5. EU Taxonomy and ESG reporting mandates from institutional investors require PUE tracking, renewable sourcing verification, and GHG accounting — operators have no system for this before Metier designs it.

**Operator archetypes that buy this:**

| Archetype | Fit | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Pattern D — Established Operator Scaling Up | PRIMARY | Moving from single-project to multi-project is the governance inflection point |
| Pattern A — Finance-First Developer (PE-backed) | PRIMARY | PE investors require governance; operators lack it entirely |
| Pattern B — High-Security Operator (state-adjacent) | HIGH | Institutional owners require structured reporting |
| Milestone-gated capital (funding-driven) | PRIMARY | Governance is the mechanism for unlocking next funding tranche |
| ESG-mandated investors (funding-driven) | HIGH | EU Taxonomy compliance requires embedded reporting from day one |

**Entry trigger (observable signals):**
- PE or infrastructure fund investment announced or closed
- Multiple sites under development simultaneously (2+ sites)
- Rapid organisational growth (10+ project roles hired in 6 months)
- History of project issues (delays, cost overruns) suggesting governance gaps
- Institutional investor ESG requirement announced
- Board level milestone/CAPEX approval process described but no system visible

**Decision-maker:** CFO / COO (operational governance); CEO for strategic governance design; PE sponsor often the forcing function behind the purchase

**Metier pitch (2 sentences):**
"When your investors ask for a project status update, the answer shouldn't take three days to produce — and when capital for your next site depends on hitting milestones at this one, the system tracking those milestones needs to be more than a spreadsheet. We design governance frameworks that serve both your operational needs and your investors' reporting requirements, turning project data into the instrument you use to run your portfolio."

**Key objection to pre-empt:**
*"We have a project management system — we don't need governance consulting."*
Counter: A project management system captures data; governance defines what data matters, who decides what, at what thresholds, with what escalation paths. Without the governance layer, your PMIS is a database no one trusts. Investors don't want to see Primavera screenshots — they want stage-gate decisions, risk quantification, and cost-to-complete in a format they can act on.

**Competitive differentiator vs. Turner & Townsend, Hill International:**
- T&T offers programme governance as part of a larger PMC engagement — Metier can offer it as a standalone advisory product or embedded within the PMO role.
- Hill has strong governance methodology but no Norwegian presence.
- Metier's differentiator: Metier's VPU/CVP framework is a proprietary stage-gate methodology with 40 years of Norwegian capital project application — not a generic PMO template. For PE-backed Norwegian operators, Metier's track record reviewing 800+ billion NOK in KS2 decisions is a credible governance signal no competitor can match.
