## Service A: Early-Phase Advisory (VPU / Concept Selection / KS)

**Pain points addressed:**
1. Technology choices made in Phase 1 (cooling topology, Tier level, redundancy architecture, power density per rack) are effectively irreversible once committed — operators without advisory make these based on gut or contractor recommendation.
2. Site selection is not just a real estate decision — electricity zone (NO1–NO5 price differentials up to 14x), grid connection queue position, and Statnett timeline directly determine project economics and viability.
3. Phasing strategy (build 2MW then expand vs. build 20MW and phase leasing) has major implications for financing, contract strategy, and IRR — most first-time developers don't model this rigorously.
4. Investors and boards require a rigorous decision-making framework before committing capital — ad hoc concept selection doesn't survive institutional due diligence.
5. KS2 (investment decision quality assurance) is a Norwegian governance standard that provides the third-party validation boards need before approving CAPEX above threshold.

**Operator archetypes that buy this:**
| Archetype | Fit | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Pattern A — Finance-First Developer | PRIMARY | Pre-construction phase; need rigour before capital commitment |
| Pattern C — Utility Spin-off | HIGH | Translating power/land assets into DC concept requires structured methodology |
| Board/investor governance (funding-driven) | HIGH | KS2 provides the third-party CAPEX validation investors require |

**Entry trigger:** Company is pre-construction or in feasibility phase; multiple sites being evaluated; board or investor pressure for rigorous decision-making; publicly announced plans but no visible construction activity yet; PE/institutional investor requiring FID readiness package.

**Decision-maker:** CEO/Board for concept selection; CFO/PE sponsor for KS2 commissioning

**Metier pitch (2 sentences):**
"The most expensive decision you'll make isn't which contractor to hire — it's which site, which cooling system, and which phasing strategy you choose in the first six months, because those decisions cost you nothing to change before commitment and everything afterwards. We apply Norway's most battle-tested investment decision framework (800+ billion NOK reviewed at KS level) to ensure the concept your board approves is the one worth building."

**Objection:** *"We have our own technical team for this."*
Counter: Technical teams optimise within a concept; Metier challenges whether you have the right concept in the first place. Our value is not engineering — it's structured uncertainty reduction before you lock in.

## Service B: Uncertainty Analysis (UA)

**Pain points addressed:**
1. Operators present point estimates (e.g., "NOK 800M, 18 months") to investors; lenders and PE sponsors require probabilistic ranges (P50, P80) to set contingency and covenant levels.
2. Grid connection timeline uncertainty (12–18+ months, and highly variable under Statnett's new maturity-based queue system) is the single largest schedule risk for Norwegian DC projects — most operators don't quantify it.
3. Long-lead equipment (transformers, generators) drives schedule risk more than construction progress — traditional schedule risk analysis misses this.
4. CAPEX covenant levels set at P50 without Monte Carlo analysis are routinely breached; banks now require quantified risk analysis before drawdown.

**Operator archetypes that buy this:**
| Archetype | Fit | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Pattern A — PE-backed Finance-First | PRIMARY | Investors require P50/P80 at FID |
| Pattern D — Scaling Up | HIGH | Multi-site portfolio CAPEX risk needs portfolio-level UA |
| Debt-funded (funding-driven) | PRIMARY | DSCR covenant protection requires quantified risk |

**Entry trigger:** FID approaching; lender technical advisory engagement; institutional investor due diligence process started; CAPEX estimate produced but without quantified uncertainty.

**Decision-maker:** CFO / PE sponsor (who require it for financing); VP Construction (who commissions it)

**Metier pitch (2 sentences):**
"Your investor doesn't want to know what your project will cost — they want to know the range it could cost and the probability you stay within budget, because that's what determines covenant levels and contingency reserves. Our Monte Carlo simulation methodology has been applied to 800+ billion NOK in Norwegian capital projects and translates directly to the DC context where grid connection timing and long-lead equipment are the variables that drive the widest ranges."

**Competitive differentiator:** Metier's UA methodology is proprietary and validated at scale — no Norwegian competitor offers the same analytical depth. T&T offers schedule risk analysis but lacks Metier's Norwegian regulatory risk parameterisation (Statnett queue timelines, konsesjon uncertainty).
