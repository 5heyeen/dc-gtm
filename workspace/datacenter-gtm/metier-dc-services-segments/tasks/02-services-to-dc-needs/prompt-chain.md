# Socratic Research Prompts: Mapping Metier Services to DC Operator Needs

> **Topic:** For each of Metier's 8 DC-framed services, identify which DC operator pain points it addresses, which operator archetypes would buy it, what the entry trigger looks like, and what the Metier pitch should be.
> **Reference files:** tasks/01-metier-service-portfolio/research.md | projects/dc-gtm/dc-services/dc_services_metier_pain_point.md
> **Use these prompts in sequence in any AI tool for deep, structured research.**

---

## Principles

- `[independent]` What makes a service-to-pain-point mapping effective for B2B consultancy sales? What separates a map that drives real conversations from one that looks rigorous on paper but fails in the field?
- `[independent]` In professional services, what is the difference between a pain point a client is willing to pay to solve vs. one they acknowledge but tolerate? How do you identify the former?
- `[independent]` What makes a consultancy pitch land with a capital project owner vs. falling flat? What language, framing, and specificity are required?
- `[independent]` How do operator archetypes (finance-first developer, high-security operator, utility spin-off, established operator scaling up) differ in what they prioritise, how they buy, and who the decision-maker is?

---

## Framework

- `[independent]` What are the core pain points that DC operators face at each phase of the development lifecycle (concept, pre-construction, construction, operations)? Which are universal vs. archetype-specific?
- `[independent]` How do funding structure and investor type (PE fund, infrastructure fund, utility balance sheet, self-funded) affect which pain points are most acute? What does "investor pressure" translate to in operational terms?
- `[sequential]` Given the pain points above, how should a consultancy prioritise which services to lead with vs. introduce later in the relationship? What sequencing maximises both win rate and revenue per client?
- `[independent]` What are the observable entry triggers — specific, detectable events or signals — that indicate a DC operator is ready to buy a given service? (e.g., board announcement of expansion, PE investment close, hiring a VP Construction)
- `[independent]` What does a high-quality service pitch look like for a capital project advisory service? What structure, language, and evidence resonate with infrastructure owners vs. financial buyers?

---

## Expert Simulation

- `[independent]` You are a senior business development director at a Scandinavian project management consultancy targeting data center operators. Before a first meeting with a new DC operator prospect, what do you need to know about them to deliver a relevant pitch?
- `[sequential]` Given that knowledge, how do you tailor the same service (e.g., Owner's Engineer) differently for a PE-backed first-time developer vs. a utility entering DCs vs. a high-security operator targeting government clients?
- `[independent]` What questions do DC operators typically ask during a first meeting with a new advisory firm? What are they really testing for? What answers build credibility immediately?
- `[sequential]` Based on those questions, what does Metier need to prepare or demonstrate for each of its 8 DC service lines to answer confidently and credibly?
- `[independent]` What do DC operators rarely articulate but consistently need? What pain points are they too busy, embarrassed, or unaware to surface directly?

---

## Failure Analysis

- `[independent]` Why do consultancy pitches to DC operators typically fail? What are the most common reasons a technically competent firm loses to a weaker competitor?
- `[independent]` What happens when a consultancy maps its services to pain points incorrectly — leading with the wrong service for the wrong archetype? What does that cost in terms of relationship and opportunity?
- `[independent]` What makes DC operators dismiss advisory firms as "not DC enough"? What specific knowledge gaps or language mistakes reveal a lack of sector depth?
- `[sequential]` Given those failure modes, what guardrails should Metier apply when pitching each of its 8 services to DC operators?

---

## Objection Pre-Empt

- `[independent]` What objections will DC operators raise to a Norwegian PM consultancy with no published DC references? How should each be pre-empted or addressed?
- `[independent]` What will a PE-backed DC developer say when told they need Owner's Engineer oversight? Why might they resist, and how do you reframe the value?
- `[independent]` Why might a DC operator prefer a specialist DC advisory firm (Mitchell McDermott, Turner & Townsend) over Metier? What is Metier's genuine competitive response?
- `[sequential]` Combining the objections above: what is Metier's differentiated positioning statement for the DC sector — one that acknowledges gaps while leading with genuine strengths?

---

## Buyer Psychology & Buying Process

- `[independent]` How do DC operators typically procure advisory services — RFP, direct appointment, framework agreements, referrals? What does this mean for how Metier should develop business?
- `[independent]` Who are the real decision-makers for each of Metier's 8 services within a DC operator organisation? (CEO, CFO, VP Construction, Board, PE sponsor?) How does the decision-maker change the pitch?
- `[independent]` What is the typical sales cycle length for each type of advisory service in the DC sector? When in the operator's development timeline does the buying window open and close?

---

## Application

- `[sequential]` Now apply all of the above to Metier's 8 DC-framed services. For each service, produce a structured entry in the following format:

**Service name:**
**Pain points addressed:** (list 3–5 specific DC operator pain points this service solves)
**Operator archetypes that buy this:** (reference Pattern A/B/C/D from dc_services_metier_pain_point.md; add funding-driven pain points where relevant)
**Entry trigger:** (what specific, observable event signals this operator is ready to buy?)
**Decision-maker:** (who approves this purchase within the operator organisation?)
**Metier pitch (2 sentences):** (what is the compelling, specific pitch Metier should make?)
**Key objection to pre-empt:** (what will the operator push back on, and how does Metier answer?)
**Competitive differentiator:** (why Metier over Turner & Townsend, COWI, Mitchell McDermott, or doing it in-house?)
