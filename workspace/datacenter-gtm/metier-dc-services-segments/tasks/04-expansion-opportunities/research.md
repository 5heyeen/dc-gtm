## Part 2: Partnership Model — Metier + Norconsult

**Recommended structure:** Integrated joint pitch with Metier as prime and Norconsult as named sub-consultant, backed by a pre-agreed scope allocation matrix and teaming agreement with exclusivity clauses.

**Role division:**
- Metier: client relationship, PMO, OE/owner representation, governance, cost/schedule, procurement advisory
- Norconsult: MEP engineering, grid connection, fire protection, uavhengig kontroll

**Why not a formal JV:** Unnecessary overhead for an emerging market. A teaming agreement is sufficient and faster to activate.

**Key risks:**
1. Client confusion about accountability → mitigate with single point of contact (Metier as prime)
2. Fee dilution → Norconsult sub-contracting reduces Metier's net margin; must be priced in
3. Independence conflict → Norconsult as both design engineer and Metier sub-consultant on same project is a governance risk; must be managed through project-level exclusions
4. Competitive displacement → Norconsult may build its own DC advisory offering and no longer need Metier; exclusivity in teaming agreement is critical

## Part 3: Top 3 Expansion Opportunities

### 1. Lender's Technical Advisory (LTA) — Highest Priority

**What:** Independent technical DD and construction monitoring for project finance lenders. Monthly drawdown recommendations, risk assessments, covenant compliance monitoring.

**Why Metier:** ~80% existing capability (KS2 methodology, owner-side advisory, cost/schedule analysis) reframed for the lender-side context. Requires a structured drawdown process and familiarity with project finance covenant structures — addressable through a project finance legal partner (Wikborg Rein, BAHR).

**Market gap:** Mitchell McDermott's Funder Advisory division is the benchmark globally. In Norway, this role is not yet systematically filled by any local firm. First-mover advantage is available.

**Revenue model:** Monthly retainer for the construction period (typically 18–24 months); direct access to PE sponsors and lenders creates follow-on advisory with the operator.

**What's needed to launch:** 1 senior hire with lender-side construction monitoring experience; legal partnership for covenant advisory; 3–6 month service design and marketing.

---

### 2. Cleared PM Cadre for High-Security Segment — Strategic Priority

**What:** A team of 3–5 Metier project managers holding personal sikkerhetsklarering, enabling Metier to provide PM services for Pattern B (government/defense/NATO) DC projects.

**Why Metier:** Pattern B is growing (NSM requirements, sovereign cloud, NATO alignment). No Norwegian PM consultancy has built this capability systematically. The cleared advisory supply is structurally constrained — anyone who builds it first creates a durable moat.

**What's needed:** Apply for clearance for identified PMs (6+ months processing); develop cleared project delivery protocols; track record through a first cleared DC project (could start with an existing Metier client in the public sector).

**Timeline:** 12–18 months to first cleared PM delivery capability.

---

### 3. Metier Academy DC Track — Brand and Pipeline

**What:** DC-specific PM curriculum: MEP package management, commissioning planning, long-lead equipment procurement, Norwegian DC regulatory context. Delivered via existing Metier Academy infrastructure.

**Why Metier:** No Norwegian provider offers DC-specific PM training. International DC certifications (BICSI, Uptime Institute AOS) cover technology, not project management. This is an unclaimed niche that plays to Metier Academy's core strength.

**What's needed:** 2–3 DC-specific modules; 1–2 SMEs with DC PM track records to design content; 3–4 months curriculum development.

**Value beyond revenue:** Positions Metier as DC PM thought leader; trained operators refer Metier to peers; academy alumni become a pipeline of warm prospects.

---

## Strategic Recommendation

Metier should pursue a **two-track expansion**:

**Track A (immediate, 0–6 months):** Activate Metier + Norconsult partnership model on first DC opportunities. Use existing capabilities to win OE, PM, and procurement engagements. Build DC project references.

**Track B (strategic, 6–18 months):** Simultaneously develop Lender's Technical Advisory capability (new service line) and Cleared PM Cadre (new capability). Develop Metier Academy DC Track.

The three expansion opportunities together create a DC advisory position that no existing Norwegian firm can replicate: Metier as PM/governance backbone + Norconsult as technical engineering partner + Metier LTA for lenders + cleared PM capability for Pattern B + Metier Academy for competence transfer.
