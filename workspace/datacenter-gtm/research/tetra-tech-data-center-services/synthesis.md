# Synthesis: Tetra Tech Data Center Services — Norway, Nordics, Global

**Scope:** What does Tetra Tech (tetratech.com) do for data centers — specifically in Norway, the Nordics, and globally? Cover: their DC services/capabilities, any Nordic/Norwegian projects or clients, key offerings (design, engineering, sustainability, construction management, etc.), and how they position themselves in the DC market.
**Date:** 2026-04-19
**Tasks synthesised:** 7

---

## Executive Answer

Tetra Tech is a $5.4B US-headquartered engineering consultancy with a genuine but opaque data center engineering practice. Globally, they offer a credible full-service engineering proposition — power and energy systems, cooling (including liquid cooling for AI workloads), water management, sustainability/ESG, commissioning, and digital engineering — organized under the Mission Critical Buildings (MCB) sub-practice within their High Performance Buildings solution area. Their "Leading with Science®" brand translates into the DC sector as CFD-modeled cooling design, digital twins, and AI/ML simulation tools. Technical depth is evidenced by three substantive white papers on AI-era cooling published in 2024–2025 and the May 2024 acquisition of Convergence Controls & Engineering, which added dedicated commissioning capability. However, no named DC clients, no MW-scale portfolio figures, and no completed project case studies are publicly available anywhere in the world — a significant credibility gap compared to specialist DC design firms.

In Norway and the broader Nordics, Tetra Tech has **no identifiable data center presence**. Their Norwegian footprint was built entirely through RPS Group (acquired 2023 for $691M) and Metier (inherited via RPS from the 2015 RPS acquisition). Both assets were oriented toward oil and gas project management and environmental consulting, not mission-critical or data center engineering. The Metier divestiture to Norconsult (December 2025, NOK 475M) removed the only Norwegian asset with genuine client-facing depth. As of April 2026, any Tetra Tech engagement in the Nordic DC market would start from scratch — most likely staffed by UK-based RPS engineers with no Norwegian DC track record or local regulatory relationships.

For Metier, the key strategic finding is that the Tetra Tech relationship was never organic. Tetra Tech inherited Metier through the RPS acquisition and sold it two years later as a portfolio rationalisation. During the shared ownership period (January 2023–December 2025) there is no evidence of joint DC projects, co-branded activity, or operational integration. The clean sale at NOK 475M — a significant premium over the NOK 267M RPS paid in 2015 — leaves no residual commercial ties. The historical connection provides a conversation opener but no preferential commercial position. A Metier–Tetra Tech alliance today would need to be built from scratch on the basis of genuine complementarity: Tetra Tech's MEP engineering depth (power, liquid cooling, water, commissioning) filling a real gap in Norway's DC engineering supply, with Metier holding the client relationship and OE/PMC role that Tetra Tech cannot credibly occupy from London or Columbus, Ohio.

## Key Findings

### 1. Global DC Service Portfolio — What Tetra Tech Actually Offers

Tetra Tech's DC practice is organized around eight service lines, which sit within the MCB (Mission Critical Buildings) sub-practice inside CIG (Commercial/International Services Group):

| Service | Evidence Quality | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|-------|
| **A. Feasibility & Site Due Diligence** | Medium | Stated capability; grid modeling, site risk assessment; no named project evidence |
| **B. Power & Energy Engineering** | Strong | Core competency; HV/MV/LV design, grid interconnection, on-site generation; white paper evidence; USACE contract |
| **C. Cooling Engineering (incl. DTC/liquid)** | Medium-Strong | Three technical white papers on AI-era cooling; DTC and liquid cooling stated with technical specifics; no named project refs |
| **D. Water Management** | Strongest | Tetra Tech's primary global identity; ZLD, water treatment, WUE optimization; deep bench |
| **E. Sustainability & ESG** | Strong | PUE/WUE modeling, CSRD/EU EED alignment, LEED/BREEAM, carbon reporting |
| **F. Commissioning (Cx/IST)** | Medium-Strong | Convergence Controls & Engineering acquisition (May 2024) added verified Cx capability; Level 1–5 IST stated |
| **G. Planning & Permitting** | Strong in UK/Europe | RPS heritage; environmental permitting, grid connection applications; unverified in Nordics specifically |
| **H. Digital Engineering** | Medium | CFD modeling, digital twins, myProjects PM platform, "Tetra Tech Delta" tooling; AI/ML simulation stated |

**Confidence: Medium** — Service catalog is well-evidenced through public content; delivery at commercial scale cannot be confirmed from public sources.

### 2. Organizational Structure — A Sector Overlay, Not a Standalone BU

Tetra Tech's DC practice is a **sector overlay within CIG**, not a freestanding business unit with its own P&L. This has practical consequences:

- **MCB practice leadership** (Sam Khalilieh, National Director Advanced Manufacturing & Mission Critical, Columbus, Ohio) is a small expert nucleus providing thought leadership and standards. Actual project delivery draws on shared CIG engineering staff across power, water, environmental, and buildings disciplines.
- **No dedicated European DC practice leader** has been identified in any public source. European DC work would flow through RPS Group (UK, Netherlands, Ireland), with no identified specialist DC team.
- **myProjects PM platform** (a proprietary project information management tool from the RPS era) was deployed for the Norwegian Defence Materiel Agency — the only confirmed Norwegian delivery — but this is a PM software implementation, not mission-critical design work.

The overlay model means Tetra Tech's DC capability is real but potentially thin on the ground in any given geography. A large Nordic project would require mobilising US MCB leadership alongside UK-based RPS engineering staff — a coordination model that has not been publicly tested in the DC sector.

**Confidence: Medium-High** — Structural facts (GSG/CIG, MCB, Khalilieh's role) are confirmed across multiple public sources. Practice depth and European staffing depth are inferred.

### 3. Project References — The Critical Gap

No named DC clients, no MW-denominated portfolio, no project case studies with measurable outcomes, and no Uptime Institute or BICSI credentials are publicly available for Tetra Tech anywhere in the world.

What IS evidenced:
- Three technical white papers (2024–2025) on liquid cooling, DTC cooling with PG-25, and CFD modeling for AI DCs — authored by named practitioners, technically substantive
- Sam Khalilieh as a named expert practitioner with genuine DC energy domain knowledge
- Convergence Controls & Engineering acquisition (May 2024) adding verified commissioning depth
- US Army Corps of Engineers $248M multi-award A-E contract (2025) for mission-critical facilities — plausibly includes DC/classified computing facilities, but specific projects are not disclosed
- RPS myProjects deployment for Norwegian Defence Materiel Agency — government delivery in Norway, not DC

What is NOT evidenced (standard proof points for a credible DC firm):
- No named hyperscale, colocation, or enterprise DC clients
- No European DC project references of any kind
- No Tier III/IV certification projects
- No BICSI or ASHRAE committee participation
- No named technology vendor partnerships (Schneider, Vertiv, Eaton)

**Inference:** Given Tetra Tech's scale ($5.4B revenue, ~28,000 staff) and the depth of their technical content, it is plausible that they have delivered US federal/government DC work that is contractually restricted from public disclosure. This is inference-grade, not fact-grade. A client conducting commercial due diligence cannot verify any DC project delivery from public sources.

**Confidence: Low-Medium** — Technical capability is plausible; project delivery at scale is unconfirmed.

### 4. European and Nordic Presence — Effectively Zero for DC

Tetra Tech's European engineering presence runs almost entirely through **RPS Group** (acquired January 2023, $691M). RPS employs ~5,000 people across UK, Europe, Asia Pacific, and North America. RPS lists "data centres" as a named property sector, but this appears to be advisory and feasibility work, not MEP design production at scale.

**Norway specifically:**
- RPS built a Norwegian footprint through two acquisitions: OEC Group (Nov 2013, energy/environmental) and Metier Holding AS (Apr 2015, NOK 267M, project management)
- Combined RPS Norway + Metier was positioned as "the leading project management organisation in Norway" — PM advisory, not engineering design
- Documented Norwegian projects: City of Oslo Water Treatment programme (PM), Norwegian National Police Emergency Response Centre (early-phase), Norwegian Defence Materiel Agency (myProjects platform) — none are data centers
- **No DC projects identified in Norway in any public source**
- Metier divested to Norconsult December 2025 (NOK 475M), removing the deepest Norwegian client relationships from Tetra Tech entirely

**Broader Nordics (Sweden, Denmark, Finland):**
- No Tetra Tech or RPS DC projects, client relationships, or offices identified in any Nordic country outside Norway

**Post-divestiture status:** What remains of Tetra Tech's direct Norwegian presence after the Metier exit is unclear. Residual RPS Norway staff (energy/environmental) may exist, but scope and client-facing capability is uncertain. Any Nordic DC engagement would start from scratch, staffed by UK-based teams with no local supply chain, regulatory relationships, or DC experience in the region.

**Confidence: High** — Absence of Nordic DC presence is confirmed across multiple research angles; not a research gap.

### 5. Metier Historical Relationship — Inherited, Not Built

The full Metier ownership chain:

| Date | Event | Price |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1976 | Metier AS founded (Oslo, PM consultancy) | — |
| April 2015 | RPS Group acquires Metier Holding AS | NOK 267M (~£22.3M) |
| January 2023 | Tetra Tech acquires RPS Group; Metier inherited | $691M (full RPS) |
| December 2025 | Norconsult ASA acquires Metier from Tetra Tech RPS Energy Ltd | NOK 475M |

**Key finding:** Tetra Tech never organically built a relationship with Metier. They inherited it through the RPS acquisition and divested it 23 months later as a portfolio rationalisation — retaining engineering, divesting PM consultancy. During the shared ownership period there is **no public evidence** of:
- Joint DC project delivery
- Co-branded go-to-market activity
- Shared client references
- Operational integration of any kind

Metier operated under its own brand and leadership throughout. The clean sale at NOK 475M (77% premium over the NOK 267M RPS paid) implies no distress and no ill will. No non-compete, preferred supplier arrangement, or ongoing commercial tie has been announced.

**Implication for Metier today:** The shared ownership history is a genuine conversation opener at executive level — both organizations know each other's capabilities and can reference common context — but creates no preferential legal, commercial, or contractual position. Any commercial arrangement with the current Metier (now under Norconsult) would require fresh negotiation, and anything exclusive would require Norconsult's approval.

**Confidence: High** — Ownership timeline confirmed from multiple primary sources (Norconsult investor releases, Tetra Tech press releases, RPS news).

### 6. Alliance Relevance — Where Complementarity Is Genuine

The service portfolios of Tetra Tech and Metier are **genuinely complementary** in the DC sector, with manageable but real conflict zones.

**Strongest complementarity (additive, low conflict):**

| Alliance Angle | Tetra Tech Provides | Metier Provides |
|---------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Technical design + PM wrapper | Power, cooling, water, sustainability, Cx engineering | Project execution, Owner's Engineer oversight |
| Feasibility + business case | Site assessment, grid capacity, technical feasibility | Concept selection, VPU, investment optimization |
| Permitting + procurement strategy | Environmental permitting, grid connection applications | Contract strategy, procurement planning, supplier qualification |
| Commissioning + project controls | IST/FAT/SAT, CxA | Schedule, cost control, commissioning interfaces |
| ESG design + governance | PUE/WUE modeling, carbon reporting, CSRD | Portfolio governance, investor reporting frameworks |

**Conflict zones requiring explicit scope boundaries:**
- **Early-phase advisory:** Both firms engage in pre-investment advisory; risk of scope conflict on who "leads" the front end
- **Digital project tools:** Tetra Tech's myProjects PM platform may compete with Metier's digital PM advisory
- **Owner's engineering:** Most sensitive — if Tetra Tech pitches OE role, it directly competes with Metier's core offering. Mitigation: Metier holds OE/PMC role and client relationship; Tetra Tech is always a technical sub-consultant, never the client-facing advisor.

**What Tetra Tech has that no Norwegian firm currently offers at depth:**
- MEP engineering for AI-era DC (power, liquid cooling, water treatment)
- Grid interconnection studies (power availability is Norway's #1 DC constraint)
- Environmental permitting (NVE concession, EIA — via RPS European heritage)
- Advanced cooling design (DTC, liquid cooling for 50–100 kW/rack GPU clusters)
- IST-level commissioning (Level 1–5, full independent CxA)

**What Metier has that Tetra Tech cannot replicate in Norway:**
- Norwegian client relationships (trust built over years)
- Norwegian procurement law fluency (LOA/FOA, NS 8401 contract forms)
- Portfolio governance frameworks (multi-site operators)
- Metier Academy / PM training (16,000 participants/year)
- Local language and regulatory fluency (NVE, Statsforvalteren, Statnett)

**Three alliance models, ranked by viability:**
1. **Model A (Recommended): Project-by-project subcontracting** — Metier wins OE/PMC mandate, brings Tetra Tech as named technical engineering sub-consultant. No exclusivity, no Norconsult approval needed, easy to test.
2. **Model B (Selective): Joint bid/consortium** — Metier as PMC/OE lead, Tetra Tech as technical engineering lead on larger mandates. Requires formal teaming agreement; Norconsult approval likely needed.
3. **Model C (Entry point): Knowledge exchange/referral** — Informal arrangement, no legal complexity, builds trust before deeper commitment.

**Confidence: Medium** — Complementarity analysis is well-grounded; alliance viability depends on Tetra Tech's actual European DC staffing depth, which cannot be confirmed from public sources.

## Contradictions & Tensions

**1. Technical depth vs. project opacity**
Tetra Tech's white papers and named practitioners signal genuine engineering capability. The complete absence of named clients or project references is unusual for a firm of their scale. These two facts are in tension: either the capability exists but is legally restricted from disclosure (federal classified work), or it exists primarily as thought leadership without equivalent delivered project volume. Both are plausible; neither can be confirmed.

**2. Global ambition vs. European DC thinness**
Tetra Tech's website positions DC services for AI, hyperscale, colocation, and edge clients globally. But the organizational reality is that the specialist MCB practice is US-based (Columbus, Ohio), the European footprint (via RPS) has no identified DC specialist team, and the only confirmed European DC-adjacent delivery was a PM software implementation in Norway. The global positioning and the European delivery infrastructure are not aligned.

**3. Metier divestiture as signal**
The divestiture of Metier — Tetra Tech's strongest Norwegian asset — could be read two ways: (a) rational portfolio focus (retain engineering, divest PM), suggesting a disciplined strategy; or (b) signal that Tetra Tech sees limited commercial opportunity in the Norwegian market, making any Nordic DC alliance less strategic for them. The first interpretation is more consistent with the stated rationale, but the second cannot be ruled out.

## Gaps & Limitations

1. **No confirmed DC project references anywhere**: The single biggest gap in this research. Without named projects, any alliance proposal to Tetra Tech must include a request for non-public references or a direct meeting conversation to establish delivery track record.

2. **European DC staffing depth unknown**: Who are Tetra Tech's European DC engineers? Are they in the UK, Germany, Netherlands? How many? Can they staff a Norwegian project without flying in from the US? This is critical for any alliance model and cannot be answered from public sources.

3. **Norconsult's strategic posture unclear**: Norconsult is now Metier's parent. If Norconsult is building out its own engineering practice to compete with firms like Tetra Tech in DC, a Tetra Tech alliance may create internal political friction. This requires direct intelligence, not public research.

4. **Post-divestiture RPS Norway status**: What RPS Norway staff and client relationships remain after the Metier exit is not documented in public sources. The residual Norwegian operation could be significant or negligible.

5. **Tetra Tech Delta tooling depth**: "Tetra Tech Delta" is cited as a digital innovation unit powering DC design tools. What these tools actually are, how mature they are, and whether they add genuine value vs. standard BIM/CFD workflows is unclear from public sources.
