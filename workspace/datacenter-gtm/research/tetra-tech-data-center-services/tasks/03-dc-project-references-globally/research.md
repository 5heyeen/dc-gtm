# Research: Tetra Tech DC Project References Globally

**Subtask:** 03 — DC Project References Globally
**Executed:** 2026-04-18
**Sources:** Public sources only

## What IS Evidenced

### 1. White Paper and Technical Publication Programme

Tetra Tech's High Performance Buildings / Mission Critical Buildings (MCB) group has published a substantive body of technical content demonstrating genuine engineering depth in data center design:

- **"Taking the Heat Out of AI: Sustainable Solutions for Liquid-Cooled AI Data Centers"** (white paper, 2024/2025) — addresses the transition from air-cooled to liquid-cooled infrastructure for high-density AI workloads, including thermal load management and sustainability implications.
- **"A Practical Design Guide to Optimizing Data Center Direct-to-Chip Liquid Cooling with PG 25"** (green paper) — best practices for corrosion-free direct-to-chip (DTC) liquid cooling systems, covering propylene glycol formulation and system longevity.
- **"Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) as a Strategic Enabler for AI-Driven Data Centers"** (white paper) — covers CFD modelling for airflow optimisation, heat transfer analysis, and thermal management in high-density DC environments.

These publications are authored by a named internal team and address specific, current engineering problems (liquid cooling for AI GPUs, CFD-led design). They are not generic marketing material — they indicate practitioners who have worked through these problems. However, publications are evidence of knowledge, not necessarily evidence of delivered projects.

### 2. Sam Khalilieh — Named Expert Practitioner

Sam Khalilieh is featured on Tetra Tech's website as a subject matter expert, discussing "Innovative Energy Sources to Power AI Data Centers" and co-authoring insight on "Fueling the Growth of Data Centers with Natural Gas Power." His presence as a named practitioner (rather than an anonymous marketing author) indicates real engagement with DC energy infrastructure challenges, including on-site generation, power quality, and grid interconnection for large-scale facilities.

### 3. Commissioning Capability — Insight Article and Acquisition

The Tetra Tech insight piece "Effective Commissioning for Data Centers" signals an active commissioning (Cx) practice. This is corroborated by the acquisition of **Convergence Controls & Engineering** (May 2024), which added verified commissioning, controls, and building systems integration staff to Tetra Tech's headcount. Commissioning is one of the highest-value, most measurable services in the DC lifecycle and is difficult to fake at scale.

### 4. RPS myProjects — Norwegian Government Delivery

RPS (a Tetra Tech subsidiary acquired 2021) implemented their **myProjects** project information management platform for the **Norwegian Defence Materiel Agency (Forsvarsmateriell)**. This is not a data center project, but it establishes: (a) active delivery capability in Norway, (b) a relationship with Norwegian defence/government, and (c) familiarity with Norwegian regulatory and procurement environments. Mission-critical government facilities — including secure communications infrastructure — are adjacent to data center work.

### 5. US Army Corps of Engineers — Federal Mission-Critical Design

Tetra Tech holds a **$248M multi-award Architect-Engineer (A-E) design contract** with the US Army Corps of Engineers (USACE), awarded 2025. USACE designs and builds a wide range of US federal facilities, including classified computing infrastructure. While no specific DC projects under this contract are publicly named, the scale and scope of USACE work plausibly includes mission-critical and data/communications facilities. Government clients routinely restrict public disclosure of facility specifics.

## What Is NOT Evidenced — Critical Gaps

The following would be standard proof points for a credible DC engineering firm — none are publicly available for Tetra Tech:

- **No publicly named data center clients** — no hyperscale operators (AWS, Microsoft, Google, Meta), no colocation providers (Equinix, Digital Realty, Eviden), and no named enterprise DC clients anywhere in the world.
- **No MW-denominated portfolio** — no published figure for total IT load designed, commissioned, or under management.
- **No project case studies with measurable outcomes** — no PUE figures achieved, no capacity delivered (MW or rack count), no schedule or cost performance data.
- **No European DC references** — no named projects in the UK, Germany, the Nordics, the Netherlands, Ireland, or any other European market where DC construction is currently concentrated.
- **No Uptime Institute Tier certification projects listed** — Tier III/IV certification projects are a standard credential for serious DC design firms; Tetra Tech lists none.
- **No BICSI or ASHRAE committee participation cited** — industry body engagement is a common signal of serious DC practice; absent here.
- **No partner ecosystem visibility** — no co-marketed relationships with MEP subcontractors, rack/cooling OEMs, or commissioning bodies specific to DC work.

---

## Assessment

Tetra Tech's data center project portfolio is **opaque by design or by limitation**. Given their scale ($5.4B revenue, ~28,000 staff globally) and the breadth of their acquisition history, it is plausible — particularly inference-grade, not fact-grade — that they have delivered meaningful DC work in the US federal/government sector, where client names are routinely classified or contractually restricted from disclosure. The Convergence Controls & Engineering acquisition further suggests real operational capability in commissioning.

However, a client conducting due diligence from public sources **cannot verify any DC project delivery**. This is a significant credibility gap compared to specialist DC practices that publish named client references and project metrics — for example, DLR Group's mission-critical practice (named hyperscaler and colo clients), Corgan (published MW figures and named clients), and WSP's mission-critical group (Uptime Institute-certified projects, named European references).

For a prospective client in Norway or Northern Europe, the absence of any European DC reference is the most material gap. Tetra Tech's strongest verifiable DC-adjacent credential in the region is the RPS Norway government delivery — which, while not a data center, demonstrates local delivery infrastructure.

**Confidence level: Low-Medium.** Technical publications confirm genuine engineering capability. Project delivery at commercial scale cannot be confirmed from public sources.
