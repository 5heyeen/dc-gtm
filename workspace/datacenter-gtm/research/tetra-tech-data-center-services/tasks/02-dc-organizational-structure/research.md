## 1. Corporate Segment Structure

Tetra Tech operates under two primary business segments:

- **Government Services Group (GSG):** Serves federal, state, and local government clients. Core work spans water resources, environmental monitoring, waste management, government consulting, and facilities management for public-sector clients.
- **Commercial/International Services Group (CIG):** Serves commercial and international clients. Core work spans natural resources, energy, utilities, sustainable infrastructure, and facilities engineering design.

Data center work sits within **CIG**. Data centers are commercial infrastructure projects, not government-facing contracts, placing them squarely under the commercial group's mandate. This is consistent with Tetra Tech's positioning of data center services alongside other large-scale commercial and industrial engineering programs.

Source: Tetra Tech Wikipedia / annual reports — GSG/CIG structure confirmed.

## 2. Mission Critical / Data Center Practice Structure

### High Performance Buildings — Solution Area

Within CIG, Tetra Tech maintains a named solution area called **High Performance Buildings**, with a dedicated web presence at `tetratech.com/solutions/high-performance-buildings/`. This is one of several portfolio solution areas that cross-cut the broader CIG service lines.

Within High Performance Buildings, the sub-practice specifically covering data centers and mission critical facilities is labeled **MCB (Mission Critical Buildings)**. This is where Tetra Tech's dedicated data center engineering capability is organized and branded.

The High Performance Buildings group is also the organizational home for Tetra Tech's data center technical content — including published white papers on liquid cooling, CFD modeling for AI data centers, and direct-to-chip (DTC) cooling. This signals a team with genuine technical depth and a mandate to develop thought leadership in the sector.

### Key Practice Leader: Sam Khalilieh

The publicly identified leader of the MCB practice is **Sam Khalilieh**, who holds the titles:

- National Director, Advanced Manufacturing and Mission Critical
- Managing Director, MCB (Mission Critical Buildings) at Tetra Tech

Sam is based in the **Columbus, Ohio** area. His background spans 25+ years in architecture and engineering, with credentials including PE (Professional Engineer) and LEED AP. He has led large programs across the US, China, Canada, Dubai, India, Puerto Rico, and Saudi Arabia. His prior employers include AECOM, Tyco, and Jones & Henry Engineers — a pedigree typical of senior mission critical practice leaders.

## 3. Structural Model Assessment: Sector Overlay, Not Standalone BU

A key structural finding is that **data centers at Tetra Tech are organized as a sector overlay, not as a standalone business unit with its own P&L**.

The MCB/Mission Critical group provides specialist leadership, technical standards, and thought leadership. However, project delivery is executed by CIG's broader engineering teams — the same teams that deliver across energy, utilities, water, and sustainability programs. This explains why data center capability appears distributed across multiple service pages (energy, water, sustainability, commissioning) rather than being concentrated in a single DC-dedicated section of the website or organization chart.

No dedicated data center business unit with a separate P&L has been identified in public sources.

This structure has practical implications:

- **Integrated delivery model:** A data center project at Tetra Tech draws on cross-disciplinary CIG resources. For a client, this can be an advantage (access to deep expertise in power systems, water/cooling, environmental permitting) but may also mean that the MCB practice leadership is a relatively small core group coordinating a larger, shared workforce.
- **Thought leadership concentrated:** The MCB practice and its leader (Khalilieh) are the primary public face for DC expertise — white papers, expert articles, conference presence. This is consistent with the overlay model: a small expert nucleus doing outward-facing positioning, backed by CIG delivery capacity.

## 4. Geographic and Leadership Footprint for Nordic/European DC Work

The MCB practice leadership is **US-headquartered** (Columbus, Ohio). No dedicated European data center practice leader has been identified in public sources.

European data center work would most likely run through **RPS Group** (now fully integrated as Tetra Tech), which brought UK and European engineering teams into the Tetra Tech structure. RPS maintained Norwegian offices in Oslo, Stavanger, Trondheim, and Alesund — but those offices were focused on **energy consulting and project management, primarily oil and gas**, not mission critical or data center work.

This creates a structural gap for Nordic DC engagements: the specialist MCB capability is US-based and US-market-oriented, while the European engineering footprint (via RPS) does not appear to have a dedicated data center track. European DC projects would likely require either coordination with the US MCB practice or upskilling of the UK/European CIG teams.

This gap is a relevant factor when assessing Tetra Tech's competitive position in the Nordic data center market and when considering where Metier's services could add value alongside or in place of what Tetra Tech can deliver locally.
