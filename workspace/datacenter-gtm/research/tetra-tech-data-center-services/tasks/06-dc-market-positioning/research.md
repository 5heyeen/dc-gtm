## Go-to-Market Message

Tetra Tech's firm-wide positioning — "Leading with Science®" — is applied to the data center market through a lens of data-driven design and digital innovation. In the DC context this translates to: computational fluid dynamics (CFD) modeling to optimise cooling layouts before construction, digital twins to simulate facility performance, AI/ML simulation to forecast capacity and energy use, and rigorous life-cycle analysis to reduce both capital expenditure and operational expenditure over the facility's life.

The stated DC tagline is "scalable, next-generation data center consulting and engineering solutions that help clients launch high-performance facilities quickly and sustainably." Three value pillars organise the positioning:

1. **Speed-to-market** — integrated delivery model, pre-validated design configurations, reduced iteration cycles through digital simulation
2. **Sustainability and efficiency** — energy efficiency, water use efficiency, alignment with EU Energy Efficiency Directive and CSRD reporting requirements
3. **Digital innovation** — CFD, digital twins, Tetra Tech Delta tooling as accelerators throughout design and commissioning

## Practice Branding

The data center practice sits under Tetra Tech's "High Performance Buildings" solution area, with Mission Critical Buildings (MCB) as the named sub-practice. Notably, data centers are not marketed as a standalone Tetra Tech business unit — they are positioned as a capability within the broader buildings and infrastructure portfolio. This has implications for how the practice is resourced and prioritised relative to Tetra Tech's larger environmental and infrastructure lines of business.

Tetra Tech published three white papers between 2024 and 2025 specifically on data center engineering topics:

- Liquid cooling solutions for AI data centers (cooling technology overview)
- Direct-to-chip (DTC) liquid cooling optimisation using PG-25 coolant (technical deep-dive)
- CFD modelling for AI data centers (published in collaboration with Datacenter Technology)

These publications position Tetra Tech as a thought leader specifically in the AI data center engineering space — a deliberate positioning choice given the rapid shift in the market toward high-density AI compute. The white papers demonstrate technical depth in thermal and cooling engineering, which is the dominant engineering challenge for AI-era DC operators.

## Target Client Segments

Tetra Tech's DC content explicitly names the following client segments:

- **Hyperscale and colocation operators** — referenced in the context of advanced cooling capability for high-density deployments
- **Edge facilities** — named alongside hyperscale and colocation as a distinct segment
- **AI data center operators** — explicit and prominent; "AI" appears throughout their DC content and white papers, indicating this is the primary growth segment they are targeting
- **Government and federal** — evidenced by the US Army Corps of Engineers $248M indefinite-delivery, indefinite-quantity (IDIQ) Architectural-Engineering contract for mission critical facilities; federal IT and mission critical is a meaningful segment for Tetra Tech in the US
- **Sustainability-driven operators** — referenced through EU Energy Efficiency Directive and CSRD compliance framing, suggesting European regulatory alignment is part of the value proposition for European clients

## Partnerships and Standards Body Presence

No named technology vendor partnerships — with Schneider Electric, Vertiv, Eaton, or other major DC infrastructure vendors — appear in Tetra Tech's public DC content. This is notable; many engineering firms in the DC space prominently reference vendor relationships as evidence of technical currency.

No publicly stated affiliations with the Uptime Institute or BICSI have been identified. Both are standard credentialing bodies in the mission-critical sector; their absence from public communications may reflect a US-centric delivery model or a conscious choice to position capability as vendor- and standard-agnostic.

**Tetra Tech Delta** is the firm's internal digital innovation unit. It is referenced in the DC context as the source of digital twin and simulation tooling, but it functions as an internal delivery accelerator rather than an external partnership.

**Convergence Controls & Engineering**, acquired by Tetra Tech in May 2024, adds commissioning, controls, and systems integration capability. In practice this functions as an internal DC commissioning partner — the acquisition internalised a capability that would otherwise have required subcontracting.

## Competitive Differentiation vs. Peers

Tetra Tech's stated differentiation, as readable from public content, clusters around three claims:

- **vs. large multidisciplinary firms (AECOM, WSP, Jacobs):** Tetra Tech claims tighter sustainability integration — water, energy, and environmental services within a single firm rather than assembled from separate practices. Digital twin is positioned as a delivery tool embedded in the engineering process, not simply a visualisation deliverable at the end.
- **vs. DC specialists (DLR Group, Corgan, Syska Hennessy):** Tetra Tech claims breadth — grid interconnection, water systems, environmental permitting, and broader infrastructure alongside MEP design. AI/digital tools are positioned as delivery accelerators that specialists without Tetra Tech's technology investment cannot match at the same depth.
- **On speed and cost:** Tetra Tech claims the ability to "radically reduce capital expenditures" through optimised design. No benchmarks, case studies, or quantified comparisons are published to support this claim.

**Key credibility gap:** No named clients, no publicly described MW-scale project portfolio, and no Tier certification track record are visible in Tetra Tech's public DC content. For an operator evaluating engineering partners, this absence of project references is a significant unknown. The white papers and digital capability claims are substantive, but unanchored to delivered projects.

## How Tetra Tech Goes to Market

Tetra Tech's observable DC go-to-market channels:

- **Thought leadership:** White papers and expert profiles (notably Sam Khalilieh, who appears in interviews and expert commentary on AI DC energy sourcing)
- **Website solution pages:** Data Center Solutions and High Performance Buildings pages serve as the primary discovery surface
- **US federal procurement vehicles:** Army Corps of Engineers multi-award contract and similar IDIQ vehicles are a significant channel for US government and defence DC work
- **Through RPS in Europe:** Property and infrastructure advisory relationships in the UK and Western Europe provide the entry point for European DC opportunities; no dedicated European DC sales function is evidenced
- **No identified direct sales activity in the Nordic DC market**
