## European Structure via RPS

Tetra Tech's European engineering footprint is delivered almost entirely through RPS Group, acquired in January 2023 for $691M and now operating as "RPS, a Tetra Tech company." RPS brings approximately 5,000 employees across the UK, Europe, Asia Pacific, and North America. Within Europe, the primary hubs are the UK (the dominant engineering delivery centre), the Netherlands, and Ireland — the latter reinforced in March 2025 with the acquisition of Carron + Walsh, an Irish infrastructure consultancy.

A legal entity called "Tetra Tech Europe" exists but is principally used for international development projects (USAID, FCDO-funded programmes), not for commercial engineering services. European commercial engineering — including any data centre work — runs through RPS.

RPS's DC-relevant capability in the UK/Europe context sits within its property and technical advisory services. RPS describes this as covering design, technical due diligence, and advisory services for the property sector, which explicitly includes data centres. However, this capability is relatively narrow compared to full-service MEP engineering firms: RPS is stronger in environmental, energy, and project advisory than in mission-critical MEP/ELV design.

## Norway and Nordic Specifics

RPS established a Norwegian presence through two sequential acquisitions. In November 2013, RPS acquired OEC Group, a Norwegian energy consultancy, creating an initial Norwegian office network. In April 2015, RPS acquired Metier Holding AS for NOK 267M (~£22.3M), adding 160 employees across Oslo, Stavanger, Trondheim, and Alesund. Combined, RPS described itself as "the leading project management organisation in Norway" in its acquisition press release.

RPS Norway's publicly documented project references are concentrated in public sector project management and energy:

- **City of Oslo Water Treatment Plant** — project management services
- **Norwegian National Police Emergency Response Centre** — early phase advisory
- **Norwegian Defence Materiel Agency** — deployment of the myProjects PM platform

No data centre projects have been identified for RPS Norway in any public source. This is consistent with the character of RPS's Norwegian practice: it was built around oil and gas consulting, public sector project management, and environmental services — not mission-critical building engineering or DC infrastructure.

## Metier Divestiture and Its Impact

In November 2025, Norconsult ASA announced an agreement to acquire 100% of Metier Group AS from Tetra Tech RPS Energy Limited (the legal entity holding Norwegian operations). The transaction closed in December 2025 at a final payment of NOK 475M (enterprise value NOK 480M). Metier had grown to approximately 250 employees and NOK ~494M in revenue during the Tetra Tech ownership period.

With the Metier divestiture complete, Tetra Tech has effectively shed its primary Norwegian client-facing asset. What remains of RPS Norway outside Metier — energy consultants, environmental staff — is unclear from public sources, and no public statements have been made about the residual Norwegian footprint.

Across the broader Nordic region (Sweden, Denmark, Finland), no RPS or Tetra Tech offices, projects, or capabilities have been identified in public sources. There is no Nordic DC project reference, no named Nordic DC client, and no evidence of active Nordic business development in the mission-critical sector.

## Assessment

As of April 2026, Tetra Tech has no publicly evidenced presence in the Nordic data centre market. The Norwegian footprint they built through RPS and Metier was oriented toward project management consultancy and energy advisory — not the MEP engineering, commissioning, or technical design services that DC operators procure. The December 2025 Metier divestiture removed their strongest Norwegian relationship asset entirely.

Any future Nordic DC engagement by Tetra Tech would need to be built from scratch, most likely delivered by UK-based RPS engineering teams operating on a fly-in basis — a model that carries commercial, logistical, and client-relationship disadvantages relative to locally embedded competitors.

**Confidence level:** High for structural facts (acquisitions, divestiture). Low-to-medium for residual Norwegian headcount post-divestiture — no public disclosure found.
