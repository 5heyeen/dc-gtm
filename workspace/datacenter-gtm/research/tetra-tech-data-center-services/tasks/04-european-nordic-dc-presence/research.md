## European Structure via RPS

Tetra Tech's European engineering presence is built almost entirely on RPS Group, which Tetra Tech acquired in January 2023 for $691M. RPS now operates as "RPS, a Tetra Tech company" and serves as the primary vehicle for Tetra Tech's European client relationships. RPS employs approximately 5,000 people across the UK, Europe, Asia Pacific, and North America.

RPS's DC-relevant service lines in the UK and European context include design, technical, and advisory services for the property sector — with data centres listed as a named sector. This positions RPS as capable of feasibility, site selection, and technical due diligence for DC clients in the UK and Western Europe, though the depth of MEP design capability (as distinct from advisory) is not clearly evidenced in public sources.

A separate "Tetra Tech Europe" entity exists but is primarily oriented toward international development projects (EC-funded, DG DEVCO work) rather than commercial engineering. It is not the vehicle for Tetra Tech's European DC work.

Key European engineering hubs for RPS include the UK (primary, with offices in multiple cities), the Netherlands, and Ireland. The Ireland footprint was strengthened in March 2025 with the acquisition of Carron + Walsh, a Dublin-based engineering and project management firm — though this acquisition appears oriented toward the Irish construction and infrastructure market rather than DC specifically.

## Norway / Nordic Specifics

RPS built its Norwegian presence through two acquisitions:

1. **November 2013**: RPS acquired the OEC Group in Norway, establishing an initial Norwegian footprint in energy and environmental consulting.
2. **April 2015**: RPS acquired Metier Holding AS for NOK 267M (~£22.3M). Metier had approximately 160 employees at acquisition, with offices in Oslo, Stavanger, Trondheim, and Alesund. Approximately 52% of Metier was held by external investors and 48% by 64 Metier staff at the time of sale. Following the acquisition, RPS described the combined RPS Norway + Metier entity as "the leading project management organisation in Norway."

RPS Norway's publicly documented project portfolio covers: project management for the City of Oslo Water Treatment programme, early-phase engagement on the Norwegian National Police Emergency Response Centre, and the myProjects PM platform deployment for the Norwegian Defence Materiel Agency.

**No data center projects have been identified for RPS Norway in any public source.** RPS Norway's core competencies were concentrated in energy (oil and gas project management), government infrastructure project management, and environmental services — not mission-critical or DC engineering.

## Metier Divestiture Impact

In November 2025, Norconsult ASA announced its agreement to acquire 100% of Metier Group AS from Tetra Tech RPS Energy Limited at an enterprise value of NOK 480M. The transaction closed in December 2025 at a final payment of NOK 475M. Metier is now wholly owned by Norconsult ASA.

This divestiture represents a full exit from Tetra Tech's strongest Norwegian client-facing asset. What remains of Tetra Tech's direct presence in Norway post-divestiture is unclear from public sources. RPS Norway may retain some energy and environmental consulting staff, but the scope and client-facing capability of any residual Norwegian operation is uncertain.

Across the broader Nordic region — Sweden, Denmark, Finland — no Tetra Tech or RPS DC projects, office presences, or client relationships have been identified in public sources.

## Assessment

As of April 2026, Tetra Tech has no publicly evidenced Nordic data center presence. The Norwegian footprint they built through RPS and Metier was oriented toward project management and energy consulting, not mission-critical engineering. The Metier divestiture removed the asset with the deepest Norwegian client relationships. Any future Nordic DC engagement by Tetra Tech would effectively start from scratch, most likely delivered by UK-based RPS teams on a fly-in basis, with no established local supply chain, regulatory relationships, or DC client references in the region.
