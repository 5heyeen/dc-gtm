## 3. Service Overlap Map

### A. Complementary (Low Conflict — Alliance Opportunity)

These are the clearest alliance angles where Tetra Tech and Metier are additive, not competing:

| Alliance Angle | Tetra Tech Provides | Metier Provides | Client Value |
|---------------|---------------------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Technical design + PM wrapper** | B/C/D/E/F: Power, cooling, water, sustainability, commissioning engineering | 2/4: Project execution, owner's engineer oversight | Client gets independent PM watching the technical consultant — best practice structure |
| **Feasibility + business case** | A: Site assessment, grid capacity, technical feasibility | 3: Concept selection, VPU, investment optimization | Technical feasibility (Tetra Tech) + decision framework for investor/board (Metier) |
| **Permitting + procurement strategy** | G: Environmental permitting, grid connection applications | 1: Contract strategy, procurement planning, supplier qualification | Integrated approach: permitting shapes procurement strategy; together they cover the full pre-FEED risk |
| **Commissioning + project controls** | F: IST, FAT/SAT, CxA | 2: Schedule, cost control, progress planning | Tetra Tech runs technical testing; Metier manages commissioning schedule and interfaces with IT go-live |

| **Long-lead equipment spec + procurement** | B: Equipment specification (generators, UPS, switchgear) | 1: Tendering, supplier qualification, procurement planning | Tetra Tech specifies what to buy; Metier manages how to buy it |

### B. Overlap / Potential Conflict Areas

These areas require clear scope boundaries in any alliance agreement:

| Area | Tetra Tech's Offering | Metier's Offering | Risk |
|------|----------------------|-------------------|------|
| **Early-phase advisory** | A: Feasibility engineering, site assessment, due diligence | 3: Concept selection, Pre-FEED, PDRI | Both firms engage in pre-investment advisory; risk of scope conflict on who "leads" the front end |
| **Digital project tools** | H: myProjects PM platform, digital twins | 8: IT architecture, DCIM, BIM, agile | myProjects (Tetra Tech's PM platform) may compete with Metier's digital PM advisory; needs a clear boundary |
| **Owner's engineering** | A+F: Tetra Tech sometimes acts as "owner's technical advisor" in feasibility and Cx | 4: Owner's Engineer / PMC is Metier's core offering | The most sensitive overlap — if Tetra Tech pitches OE role, it directly competes with Metier |

**Key mitigation:** Define the boundary explicitly — Metier holds the OE/PMC role and client relationship; Tetra Tech is always a technical sub-consultant, never the client-facing advisor.

## 4. Most Credible Alliance Models

### Model A: Project-by-Project Technical Subcontracting (Recommended)

**Structure:** Metier wins owner's engineer or PMC mandate → brings Tetra Tech as named technical engineering sub-consultant for specific disciplines (power design, cooling engineering, sustainability/permitting)

**Why it works:**
- Metier holds the client relationship (solves the trust problem)
- Tetra Tech fills genuine technical gaps (solves the MEP depth problem)
- No exclusivity required — each project is its own commercial arrangement
- Norconsult does not need to approve (not an exclusive alliance)
- Low commitment, easy to test

**Best fit clients:** Financial investor entering DC for first time; colocation operator with aggressive build timeline needing independent OE + technical design

### Model B: Joint Bid / Consortium (Selective)

**Structure:** Metier + Tetra Tech bid together on larger mandates — Metier as PMC/OE lead, Tetra Tech as technical engineering lead

**Why it works on the right project:**
- Combined offer covers full lifecycle from feasibility through commissioning
- Tetra Tech's global brand + Metier's local credibility = stronger consortium than either alone
- Hyperscale or large colocation clients who need both PM depth and MEP engineering depth

**Risks:** Requires formal teaming agreement; Norconsult approval likely needed; risk of scope conflict if both firms are pitching to the same client independently

### Model C: Knowledge Exchange / Referral (Low Commitment Entry Point)

**Structure:** Informal arrangement where Metier refers clients needing MEP engineering depth to Tetra Tech, and Tetra Tech refers Nordic clients needing PM/OE services to Metier

**Why it matters as a starting point:**
- No legal complexity, no Norconsult approval needed
- Builds trust and familiarity before deeper commitment
- Reveals whether Tetra Tech is genuinely interested in the Nordic market or just wants a distribution relationship

## 5. Strategic Assessment

**Is a Tetra Tech alliance worth pursuing?**

**Yes, conditionally.** The complementarity is genuine — Tetra Tech has technical engineering capabilities that no Norwegian firm currently offers at depth for AI-era data centers (liquid cooling design, grid studies, water treatment, commissioning engineering, sustainability permitting). Metier has the market relationships and PM backbone Tetra Tech lacks in Norway.

**The key conditions:**
1. Tetra Tech must demonstrate real European DC project references (not just US federal classified work) — ask this in the first meeting
2. Norconsult's strategic posture must be assessed — if Norconsult is building out its own engineering practice, a Tetra Tech alliance may be politically difficult
3. Start with Model A (project-by-project subcontracting) before any exclusive or branded partnership
4. Confirm that Tetra Tech's European DC engineering team is accessible and available — not just US staff flying in

**The biggest risk:** Tetra Tech's DC capability may be real in the US but thin in Europe. If they cannot staff a Nordic project with engineers who have genuine DC experience in European regulatory environments, the alliance value proposition collapses.

## 6. Questions to Open With in a First Meeting with Tetra Tech

1. Since divesting Metier in December 2025, what is Tetra Tech's current strategy for Norway and the Nordics?
2. Who leads your European data center engineering practice, and where are they based?
3. Can you share 2-3 European DC project references — MW scale, client type, scope?
4. Have you delivered DTC or liquid cooling projects in Europe?
5. How do you typically structure your relationship with the owner's engineer on a project — do you prefer to be OE yourself, or work under an independent OE?
6. What is your standard teaming arrangement for Nordic projects — UK staff, local hire, or partnership?
7. Is there appetite within Tetra Tech for a structured referral or teaming arrangement with Metier on Nordic DC opportunities?
