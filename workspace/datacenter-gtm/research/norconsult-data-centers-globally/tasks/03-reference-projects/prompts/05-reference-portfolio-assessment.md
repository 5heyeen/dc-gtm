## 1. Portfolio Strength vs. Competitors

| Firm | Confirmed DC Projects (Norway) | Global DC Portfolio | Key Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ramboll/EYP** | Active in Nordic (heat reuse advisory) | 70M+ sq ft designed globally; 1,000+ projects | Meta Odense (45MW heat reuse); commission/IST methodology |

**Assessment:** Norconsult's portfolio is thinner and narrower than COWI, Sweco, and Ramboll:
- Smaller volume (5 confirmed vs. 1,000+ for Ramboll)
- Narrower geographic spread (Norway only vs. pan-Nordic or global)
- No MEP design, commissioning, or owner's advisory credits in any project
- Two of five confirmed DC projects are regulatory planning — not engineering delivery

However, **Norconsult holds the most credible single Norwegian EPCM reference** of any consultancy: N01 at Støleheia is the largest Norwegian DC construction EPCM engagement and involves the most complex grid connection (420kV) in the market. No competitor can match this specific reference.

## 2. Over-represented vs. Absent Project Types

### Over-represented in Norconsult's portfolio:
- **HV power infrastructure and grid connection** — N01 power infra (420kV), Skillemoen (420kV Finnmark), INEOS Inovyn (industrial HV). This is Norconsult's core industrial DNA applied to DC.
- **Regulatory planning / land-use** — Telemark and Vestfold projects are essentially zoning exercises for Statkraft. Valuable but entry-level DC work; not differentiated.

### Absent from Norconsult's portfolio:
- **Critical MEP design** (UPS, CRAC/CRAH, redundancy architecture, busbar) — no evidence in any project
- **Commissioning / Integrated Systems Testing (IST)** — entirely absent
- **Owner's advisory / independent PMC** — Norconsult is always the engineering or EPCM contractor, never the independent advisor
- **Digital twin / DCIM integration design** — no reference
- **Sustainability / heat reuse design** — no confirmed heat recovery or district heating integration reference
- **Any Nordic project outside Norway** — geographic constraint
- **Any direct hyperscaler relationship** — always through an intermediary (Statkraft, Bulk)

## 4. Strategic Implication for Metier

Norconsult's reference portfolio confirms the structural complementarity identified in subtask 02:

1. **Norconsult wins work in the engineering/delivery layer** — grid, EPCM, regulatory. These are phases where the consultancy is doing the work, not advising the owner about the work.
2. **The independent advisory layer is structurally open.** On every confirmed Norconsult DC project, the role of independent owner's engineer, lender's technical advisor, or PMC sitting above the EPCM contractor is unoccupied (at least publicly).
3. **The Statkraft relationship is an indirect Metier opportunity.** Statkraft's DC sites (Telemark/Google and Vestfold) have Norconsult doing planning. If/when a Statkraft-linked operator (or Google directly) needs project governance advisory, Norconsult cannot serve that role — they are the site planner and have a pre-existing relationship with Statkraft as developer.
4. **The N01 lender's Independent Engineer role** (undisclosed for the €410M ABN Amro/Citibank/Nordea loan) remains the most immediately actionable Metier opportunity. If not already filled, this is a named, live, high-value engagement.
