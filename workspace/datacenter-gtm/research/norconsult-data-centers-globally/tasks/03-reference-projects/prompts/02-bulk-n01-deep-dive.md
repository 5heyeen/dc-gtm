# Prompt 02: Bulk N01 Støleheia — Full Scope and Contract Details

> **Prompt:** Research Norconsult's full scope of involvement in Bulk Infrastructure's N01 / Støleheia data center campus. Clarify the EPCM vs. power infrastructure scopes, contract value and duration, 420kV role vs. Statnett, current phase, and the €410M loan's Independent Engineer.

---

## Two Distinct Norconsult Scopes at N01

### Scope A — Campus EPCM (data center campus construction)
Norconsult holds the EPCM contract for construction of N01 Datasentercampus expansion at Støleheia. Scope covers:
- Project management and project controls
- Engineering and engineering management across all project phases
- Procurement management
- Construction management / site management
- Follow-up of project suppliers
- HSE/SHA oversight
- Systematic completion (commissioning/handover)

Norconsult explicitly uses its **UnoSys** project and contract management platform on this engagement.
Named person: **Sten-Ole Nilsen** (Group Director, Renewable Energy, Norconsult) — quoted in contract announcement.

### Scope B — Power infrastructure / grid connection engineering
A separate announcement describes Norconsult's role engineering the private on-site power supply:
- Engineering design for two new transformer stations (420kV/145kV)
- Engineering for new 420kV overhead lines (short: ~300m and ~1.4km)
- Engineering for 145kV/24kV transformers and associated switchgear
- Cable connections between transformer stations

**Inference (moderate-high confidence):** Two separate press releases with different framings suggest two distinct contract packages or work orders. Not confirmed as legally separate.

## The €410M Loan — Independent Engineer Gap

**Lenders:** ABN Amro Bank N.V., Citibank Europe plc, Nordea Bank Abp
**Advisors to Bulk:** Eastdil Secured (financial); White & Case + Schjødt (legal)
**Independent Engineer / Lender's Technical Advisor:** **Not publicly named in any source.**

This is a critical gap: for a €410M infrastructure loan, a Lender's Independent Engineer is standard practice. The absence from public sources likely means the role was not publicized, not that it doesn't exist. Norconsult's pre-existing EPCM role on the campus creates a conflict of interest that would typically disqualify them from also serving as lender's engineer — **a separate firm would normally be retained for this role. That is Metier's opening.**
