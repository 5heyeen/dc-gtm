# Socratic Research Prompts: Norconsult DC Service Offerings

> **Topic:** Map the full range of services Norconsult markets to data center clients — site selection, design, MEP engineering, sustainability, construction management, commissioning, etc. For Metier's DC GTM competitive intelligence.
> **Use these prompts in sequence in any AI tool for deep, structured research.**

---

## Principles

- `[independent]` What are the standard service categories that a credible multidisciplinary engineering consultancy offers to data center clients across the full project lifecycle (pre-development through operations)?
- `[independent]` How do engineering consultancies typically differentiate their DC service offering — on technical depth, sustainability, speed-to-market, or integrated delivery?
- `[independent]` What services are genuinely specialist (requiring dedicated DC expertise) vs. commoditized (standard engineering that any competent consultancy can deliver for DC clients)?

## Framework

- `[independent]` What is the full DC project lifecycle and which service types map to each phase (feasibility, site selection, FEED, detailed design, procurement, construction, commissioning, handover, operations)?
- `[independent]` How do DC clients (hyperscalers, colocators, enterprise DC owners) typically structure their consulting/engineering procurement — single EPCM, split packages, owner's engineer + specialist subs?
- `[sequential]` Given the above lifecycle and procurement structures, which service gaps are most commonly left unfilled by EPCM-led consultancies — and where does an independent PM advisory firm like Metier fit?

## Expert Simulation

- `[independent]` What would a DC client expect to see in a full-service engineering consultancy's DC offering — what services are table-stakes vs. differentiators?
- `[sequential]` Given that Norconsult is positioned as an EPCM-led industrial/power engineering firm (not a building MEP specialist or mission-critical IT infrastructure firm), what specific DC services are they likely strongest in, and which are likely absent or thin?
