# Prompt 05: dc-procurement-structures-where-independent-pm-fits

> **Prompt:** How do DC clients structure consulting/engineering procurement — single EPCM, split packages, owner's engineer + specialist subs? Where does an independent PM advisory firm like Metier fit?

---

## The Four Main Procurement Models

### 1. Single EPC (Turnkey)
Design, procurement, and construction responsibility transferred to single contractor. Owner specifies outcome; contractor assumes cost/schedule risk via lump-sum or GMP. "Nearly hands-off for the owner." Smaller facilities (<$500M) commonly on GMP; large AI/hyperscale ($5–10B) on target price with painshare/gainshare.

*When chosen:* Owner wants single accountability, proven/repeatable design, maximum speed, willing to pay risk premium. Hyperscalers with standardized hall designs (Microsoft, Google, AWS) often favor this.

*Pros:* Maximum schedule certainty, single accountability, fastest to commissioning.
*Cons:* Premium cost; owner loses sight of subcontractor performance; **no independent technical voice to challenge contractor claims**.

### 2. EPCM (Engineering, Procurement, Construction Management)
EPCM firm manages on behalf of the owner but does NOT do the actual construction. Design, procurement management, and construction coordination done in the owner's name. Owner holds direct contracts with each work package contractor. Typically reimbursable (cost-plus fee). Market is moving away from lump-sum EPC toward EPCM as contractors reach capacity limits (Freshfields 2025).

*When chosen:* Owner has (or can get) in-house capacity to hold multiple contracts; cost transparency preferred over risk transfer; market is hot and EPC contractors are booked out.

*Pros:* Full cost transparency, owner controls key decisions, flexibility in design changes, EPCM fees typically capped.
*Cons:* **Owner retains most construction risk** — cost overruns and schedule slippage fall on owner. Multiple counterparty contracts create interface management complexity owners frequently underestimate. Freshfields warns of "greater in-house costs for contract management, potentially uncapped fees, and the cost of managing multiple concurrent disputes."

### 3. Split Packages (Separate MEP + Civils + IT Fit-Out)
Owner directly splits work into discrete packages: civil/structural shell, electrical/power, mechanical/cooling, IT fit-out, commissioning, security. Owner or PM advisor manages interfaces directly. Hyperscalers with large in-house construction teams (AWS, Google, Meta) increasingly use this for mature, repeat campuses. Often combined with direct framework agreements with critical equipment suppliers (generators, transformers, UPS) to bypass EPC mark-ups on long-lead items.

*When chosen:* Owner has strong in-house PM/procurement capability, wants maximum competitive tension and cost control.
*Pros:* Maximum cost control, competitive tension between specialist contractors.
*Cons:* Highest management burden on owner — interface risk is entirely the owner's problem.

### 4. Design-Build (D&B)
Owner provides functional brief + performance spec; design-builder develops both design and construction. Sits between EPC (fully hands-off) and construction management (fully hands-on). Common for colocation operators building the first instance of a new design.

*Pros:* Single design-construction accountability, some design flexibility, faster than DBB.
*Cons:* Less risk transfer than full EPC; owner must still manage design reviews actively.

## Where Metier Fits — The Structural Gap in Every Model

**The key insight: the procurement model determines the form of the gap, but the gap always exists.**

| Model | Owner Visibility | Where Metier Fits | Key Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| EPC/Turnkey | Low | Owner's Engineer, PMC | No independent technical voice; change orders unchallenged |
| EPCM | Medium | Independent PM Advisor, Owner's Engineer | EPCM firm interests may diverge; owner lacks in-house capacity |
| Split Packages | High | PM/PMO, Interface Manager | Interface risk between packages; master programme ownership |
| Design-Build | Medium | Owner's Engineer, Early-Phase Advisory | Design quality and scope drift without independent review |

**In EPC/D-B:** Client transferred risk but lost visibility. Independent Owner's Engineer provides technical counterweight — reviewing contractor deliverables, challenging change orders, representing owner's interests at design reviews, verifying commissioning.

**In EPCM:** Structural trap — EPCM contractor ostensibly serves owner but as a reimbursable contractor may have interests that diverge when changes and overruns emerge. An independent PM advisor "has no commercial interest in the construction outcome, only in the owner's outcomes." Can challenge the EPCM contractor, validate its recommendations independently.

**In split packages:** Metier needed most operationally — holding interfaces, managing master programme, coordinating civil/MEP/IT fit-out contractors.

**Across all models:** Portfolio governance, reporting to PE/infrastructure fund investors, milestone tracking for tranched capital, and procurement strategy all sit squarely in the owner's advisory space.

---

## The EPCM Oversight Gap (Most Important for Metier)

The EPCM contractor wears two hats simultaneously — owner's agent AND reimbursable contractor. As CMAA notes: "If the job starts to get into trouble, the Construction Manager will, out of business necessity, have to look out for its own interests." When this happens, there is no independent technical voice representing the owner — unless the owner has engaged a separate Owner's Engineer or PM advisor with no construction contracts at stake.

**For Norconsult specifically:** Norconsult explicitly offers EPCM services and is acting as EPCM contractor on Bulk Infrastructure's Støleheia project. If Norconsult is the EPCM firm, **the independent Owner's Engineer role is structurally open**. A DC operator using Norconsult as EPCM provider has a clear need for a separate, independent advisory firm (Metier) to validate Norconsult's recommendations — particularly on contract strategy, change management, and commissioning.

## Nordic/Norwegian Examples

- **Green Mountain (Stavanger):** NCC held primary construction contract (NOK 60M initial scope), Green Mountain managed fit-out and specialized MEP separately — de facto split-package with operator retaining in-house coordination.
- **Lefdal Mine:** Skanska as main civil contractor for cave development; Lefdal managed IT infrastructure partnerships separately (IBM, Rittal). Two distinct procurement tracks (civil + IT fit-out).
- **STACK/DigiPlex (Fetsund):** "Campus approach" with modular design enabling proven EPC-style template for each incremental phase.
- **Norconsult/Bulk Støleheia:** Norconsult as EPCM = Metier's opening as independent Owner's Engineer.
