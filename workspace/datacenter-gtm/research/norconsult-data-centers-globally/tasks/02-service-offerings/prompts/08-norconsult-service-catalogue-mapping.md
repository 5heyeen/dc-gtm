## Norconsult DC Services — Competitive Intelligence Analysis

### What Norconsult Explicitly Claims (Website & Press Releases)

Norconsult's dedicated data centres page at norconsult.com/services/industry/data-centres/ makes the following explicit claims:

- **Full value chain coverage** "from studies to detailing and follow-up during construction" — a deliberately broad claim covering early-phase through construction administration
- **Interdisciplinary expertise** drawn from power-intensive industrial facilities and associated security requirements
- **Green/sustainable solutions** — active participation in sustainable DC projects including the Bulk Infrastructure N01 campus at Støleheia
- **Power supply / kraftforsyning** — explicitly named as a core competency in the Bulk Infrastructure press release, including grid connection applications and renewable power routing
- **EPCM contract delivery** — formally named as EPCM provider for Bulk N01: "project management, governance, engineering, and supervision throughout the construction process"
- **Prosjektledelse og styring, prosjektering og prosjekteringsledelse** (project management, engineering management, design coordination) — explicitly listed in N01 contract scope
- **Geotechnics and geo sciences** — foundations, ground investigations, excavation
- **Construction follow-up/supervision** — stated as part of EPCM scope at Støleheia

Norconsult does **not** explicitly claim on their DC page: commissioning/IST, owner's independent advisory/PMC, site selection advisory, or MEP-specific design (cooling, UPS, redundancy architecture).

---

### What Norconsult Implicitly Delivers (from N01 EPCM Scope)

Based on EPCM engagement at Bulk Infrastructure N01 (NOK 3+ billion project, 2023–2026):

- **High-voltage grid connection design** — Norconsult secured grid concession (konsesjon) for Bulk's power supply from Statnett to the campus, delivering transmission-level electrical engineering (420kV/145kV transformer stations)
- **Civil and structural engineering** — Campus-scale earthworks, foundations, and building structures for the data hall shells
- **Procurement support** — EPCM includes "anskaffelse" (procurement); managing tender processes for construction packages at N01
- **Schedule and cost control** — Standard EPCM deliverable; implied by "styring" (control) scope term
- **Regulatory approvals / planning** — Grid concession work implies permitting and municipal plan interface

---

### What Is Absent from Norconsult's DC Offering

Service areas where **no evidence exists** of Norconsult capability or marketing in the DC context:

1. **Critical MEP redundancy design (Tier/ANSI classification)** — No evidence of designing internal DC MEP systems (UPS, CRAC/CRAH cooling, generators, busway). The N01 EPCM scope is infrastructure/civil-focused; internal MEP design for data halls appears handled by Bulk's own team or specialist contractors.
2. **Commissioning / IST (Integrated Systems Testing)** — No evidence of DC commissioning engagement anywhere. Entirely absent from marketed services.
3. **Independent Owner's Advisory / PMC (Owner's Engineer)** — Norconsult acts as EPCM contractor at N01, not as an independent advisor. These are structurally different roles. No evidence of marketing a neutral owner's representative service for DC operators.
4. **Site Selection & Technical Due Diligence** — Urban planning capability exists but is not marketed as DC site selection. No press releases or case studies link them to greenfield DC site advisory work.
5. **Operations & Asset Management** — Not mentioned in any DC context. Consistent with Norconsult's engineering/design DNA.
6. **Nordic DC projects beyond Norway** — No confirmed DC project activity found in Sweden or Denmark. DC references are exclusively Norway-based.

### Competitive Positioning vs. COWI, Sweco, Ramboll

**COWI** — Strongest competitor in the Norwegian market based on the Skygard Oslo project. COWI explicitly markets "end-to-end consulting" for Nordic DC development with 900+ Norway employees. Skygard involved COWI in architectural design, sustainable energy systems, and full engineering delivery — indicating MEP design depth Norconsult lacks. Named vendor in Nordic DC Construction Market report alongside Arup and Caverion. Has a dedicated DC landing page (cowi.com/data-center) with explicit service framing.
- *COWI differentiation:* MEP/sustainability design, urban infill/complex site DC, multidisciplinary full scope including architecture. **Norconsult cannot match COWI on internal MEP design or commissioning.**

**Sweco** — Offers the broadest explicit DC lifecycle claim among Nordic firms: "all data center services under one roof — from feasibility studies to design, monitoring and commissioning." Explicitly markets site selection, lifecycle/environmental impact, Tier-class design (backup power/UPS), architectural design, structural engineering, and commissioning.
- *Sweco differentiation vs. Norconsult:* Sweco covers Phases 1, 2, 7 (commissioning), and internal MEP — all gaps in Norconsult's offering. Active in Norwegian colocation segment.

**Ramboll** — Deepest commissioning and IST capability via EYP Mission Critical Facilities acquisition. Explicitly markets IST, factory witness testing, HVAC commissioning, Owner's Engineer services (design + tender + construction management), MEP + fire protection detailed design, and sustainability/heat reuse consulting. 70M+ sq ft of critically-powered space designed globally. Norway-specific strength: heat reuse from DC to district heating (Meta Odense, Denmark reference).
- *Ramboll differentiation vs. Norconsult:* Phases 7 (commissioning/IST), owner's engineer positioning, sustainability/net-zero advisory — all gaps in Norconsult.

**Norconsult's defensible position:** Large-scale Norwegian grid infrastructure EPCM — the N01 Bulk engagement is a flagship Norwegian reference no competitor can match on HV grid connection scale. Geotechnics, civil, structural, and construction management on campus-scale projects. Strong project management and EPCM execution DNA. The Aas-Jakobsen acquisition (closed August 2025, NOK 1.43B, structural engineering specialist) deepens civil/structural capacity but does **not** close the MEP or commissioning gap.

### Key Insight for Metier Competitive Intelligence

Norconsult competes most directly with Metier on **Service 2 (Project Execution & PM)** and **Service 1 (Contract/Procurement)** in large EPCM-style engagements. However, Norconsult does so as an engineering firm delivering EPCM — meaning they are also providing the engineering, not just managing it. This creates a structural conflict of interest that Metier does not have when acting as **Owner's Engineer / PMC (Metier Service 4)** or **Portfolio Governance (Service 5)**.

**Norconsult cannot credibly position itself as an independent owner's advisor on a project where it is also the EPCM contractor.**

Metier's clearest differentiation from Norconsult: independence, owner-side advisory, governance frameworks, and competence development (Metier Academy) — all domains where Norconsult has zero DC market presence.
