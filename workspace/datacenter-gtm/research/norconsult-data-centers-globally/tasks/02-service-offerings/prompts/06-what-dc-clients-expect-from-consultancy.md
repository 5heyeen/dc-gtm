# Prompt 06: what-dc-clients-expect-from-consultancy

> **Prompt:** What would a DC client expect to see in a full-service engineering consultancy's DC offering — table stakes vs. differentiators? How do hyperscalers vs. colocators vs. domestic operators differ?

---

## Table Stakes (Minimum Viable Service Set)

1. **Integrated MEP Engineering** — N+1 or 2N redundancy design across power distribution, cooling systems, fire suppression. Uptime Institute Tier II–IV fluency mandatory.
2. **Civil and Structural Engineering** — Heavy point loads, raised floors, rooftop cooling plant structural capacity.
3. **Site Feasibility and Grid Assessment** — TSO (Statnett) engagement, power availability evaluation from day one. Now firmly table stakes in Norway.
4. **Permitting and Regulatory Navigation** — Zoning, construction permits, utility concessions (konsesjon). Norwegian grid concession processes can span several years.
5. **BIM-Based Design Delivery** — LOD 350+ BIM documentation. Hyperscalers have standardized BIM Level 2 as a contract requirement; inability to deliver eliminates at RFQ stage.
6. **Construction Administration and Site Supervision** — RFI response, shop drawing review, site observation.

---

## Differentiators (What Clients Actually Evaluate)

1. **Speed-to-Market Engineering** — Compressed design timelines through templated workflows, parametric/automated design, concurrent design-procurement packages. Market standard for hyperscale: 16–20 months for a full campus. Early procurement enablement (buying long-lead items before design finalized) provides measurable capex control.

2. **Modular and Prefabricated Design Capability** — Off-site prefabrication of power skids, utility zones, mechanical prefab reduces on-site labour, shortens schedules 30–50%. Requires established manufacturer relationships and factory-assembly coordination experience.

3. **Digital Twin and Lifecycle Continuity** — Unified digital twin from design through construction into operations. Enables predictive maintenance, capacity expansion planning, operational optimisation. Highest projected growth rate of any DC engineering service through 2030.

4. **Sustainability Engineering and ESG Integration** — Heat recovery into district heating, renewable PPA advisory, PUE/WUE optimisation, lifecycle carbon assessments. Must be embedded from concept stage. **COWI's win at Skygard** was attributed specifically to "broad experience within design of data centres, sustainable energy, engineering solutions, and architecture" + ability to design circular energy flows from inception.

5. **Technical Due Diligence and Owner's Engineer Advisory** — For PE-backed/fund-backed operators: independent OE providing TDD on acquisitions, validating contractor claims, protecting owner interests through construction.

6. **Power and Energy Strategy** — Grid scenario modelling, TSO negotiation, grid-interactive demand response, BESS integration. BCG 2025: power access is the single largest barrier to DC growth.

---

## Priorities by Client Type

**Hyperscalers** (Microsoft, Google, Amazon in Norway):
- Primary priority: **speed and replicability** — no learning curve on their standard reference designs, BIM/data formats, procurement templates, and preferred vendor lists
- Demand early procurement enablement; design packages sequenced for long-lead electrical gear orders before detailed design complete
- Sustainability is mandatory to their own standards (e.g., Google 24/7 carbon-free energy matching) — not a differentiator
- Key differentiator sought: concurrent design-procurement-construction at scale + automation enabling multi-site standardization

**Colocation operators** (Green Mountain, Bulk Infrastructure, STACK):
- Primary priority: **capital efficiency and operational performance** — highly flexible/scalable designs for multiple future tenants with unknown IT load profiles
- Want DC colocation commercial model expertise: mixed-load tenants, carrier-neutral MMRs, flexible power distribution at customer suite level
- Sustainability credentials matter commercially — Green Mountain sells "100% renewable" positioning to enterprise customers
- Norway: Sweco active on multiple Norwegian colocation projects; Green Mountain works with COWI

**Domestic operators** (Skygard, Lefdal Mine, Basefarm/Orange):
- Primary priority: **local regulatory navigation and integrated design** — especially for unconventional sites
- Have the least in-house engineering capability; rely most heavily on consultant as full-service partner
- **Skygard** (Oslo urban infill): COWI selected for complete solution — urban integration, district heating, Oslo municipality planning requirements. Multidisciplinary local capability was decisive.
- **Lefdal Mine** (underground conversion): IBM/CH2M Hill led cooling design (PUE <1.1 using fjord water), ABB for power. Mine structural envelope meant civil/MEP highly interdependent; single-discipline firms were unqualified.
- **Bulk Infrastructure** (N01, Kristiansand): adjacent to Europe's largest transformer station — engineering challenge is internal power distribution architecture at campus scale (300 ha) and capacity buildout sequencing

## Key Implication for Norconsult vs. Metier

Norwegian market evidence: domestic and colocation operators award full-service mandates to consultants who demonstrably cover sustainability + local regulatory navigation + integrated MEP/civil in a single engagement. **COWI's wins at Skygard and Green Mountain** are directly attributable to this combination. Norconsult's competitive position depends on whether it can match this integrated offering while adding something COWI cannot — most plausibly deeper Norwegian regulatory relationships (konsesjon, TSO engagement) or project management continuity. This is where a PM-oriented partner like Metier could complement rather than compete.
