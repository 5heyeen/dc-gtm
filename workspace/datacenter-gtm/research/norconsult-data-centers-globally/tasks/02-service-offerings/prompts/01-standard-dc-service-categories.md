# Prompt 01: standard-dc-service-categories-full-lifecycle

> **Prompt:** What are the standard service categories that a credible multidisciplinary engineering consultancy offers to data center clients across the full project lifecycle?

---

## Standard Service Catalogue — Data Center Engineering Consultancies

### Phase-by-Phase Service Catalogue

**1. Feasibility**
Demand/load forecasting, financial feasibility (capex/opex), power availability assessment, grid connection studies, permitting/regulatory mapping, environmental impact screening, Tier/uptime target definition. Almost always sold as a discrete advisory engagement, not bundled with later design.

**2. Site Selection & Technical Due Diligence**
Multi-site shortlisting, power grid/latency studies, fibre/connectivity mapping, geotechnical screening, flood/seismic risk, planning/zoning assessment, land acquisition advisory. For portfolio clients (hyperscalers), often sold as a **repeating retainer product** — a DC-specific bundling pattern.

**3. FEED / Concept Design**
Campus masterplanning, concept architectural design, MEP concept development, structural concept, IT load density definition, white-space layout, PUE/WUE target modelling, preliminary CFD analysis. EYP/Ramboll provides "all engineering services from conceptual facility design through all phases to stamped/sealed construction documents."

**4. Detailed Design (core revenue centre)**
Nearly always bundled as an MEP package:
- **Mechanical:** CRAC/CRAH sizing, chilled water plant, free-cooling, liquid/immersion cooling (increasingly standard for AI compute >30 kW/rack), adiabatic systems
- **Electrical:** HV/MV switchgear, UPS, PDU/busbar design, standby generators, earthing, lightning protection
- **Structural:** raised floor, roof loading, seismic bracing
- **Fire & Life Safety:** clean-agent suppression (FM-200/Novec), aspirating smoke detection, egress
- **Building Controls:** BMS/EPMS/SCADA design, DCIM integration strategy
- **ICT/Security:** cabling infrastructure, security design, CCTV/access control

**5. Procurement / Specification Support**
Technical specifications, equipment schedules, RFP/ITT preparation, vendor qualification, long-lead equipment tracking (generators, transformers, UPS). Increasingly critical — in 2025, >50% of DC projects faced 3+ month delays despite ordering materials 24 months early. Often an add-on module, not fully bundled.

**6. Construction / Construction Administration**
Site supervision, shop drawing review, RFI response, quality management, testing and inspection. Models range from traditional design-bid-build to progressive design-build and contractor-assist.

**7. Commissioning (Cx) / Integrated Systems Testing (IST)**
Commissioning plan development, component-level functional testing (L1–L4), integrated systems testing, failover/scenario testing, documentation, operator training. **Highly DC-sector-specific** — cascading failure risk and uptime requirements require staged IST protocols with no equivalent in standard commercial buildings. EYP/Ramboll described as "a pioneer and leader in commissioning and testing."

**8. Handover / Transition to Operations**
As-built documentation, O&M manual production, CAFM/DCIM data population, staff training. Often a short fixed-term add-on to commissioning.

**9. Operations & Asset Management**
Energy benchmarking and PUE/WUE optimisation, planned maintenance specification, incident investigation, lifecycle capex planning, capacity management consulting, operational carbon reporting (ESG), retrofits/upgrades. Usually sold as a **separate retainer or framework contract**.

---

### Bundling Patterns

| Cluster | Typically Bundled | Typically Separate |
|---|---|---|
| Feasibility + Site Selection | Sometimes together | Separate from design |
| FEED + Detailed Design | Nearly always bundled | Rarely split |
| MEP + Structural + Fire | Core bundled package | Rarely disaggregated |
| Procurement Support | Add-on to design | Often separate instruction |
| Commissioning | Add-on or separate | Frequently independently tendered |
| Operations advisory | Separate retainer | Rarely bundled with new-build |
| Technical Due Diligence | Standalone advisory | Always separate |

---

### DC-Sector-Specific Services (No Generic Engineering Equivalent)

1. **Global Reference Design / Design Replication** (Arup) — standardised design deployable across geographies
2. **Power and Cooling Analytics** (WSP/kW MCE) — IT load growth scenarios, stranded capacity, energy mix optimisation
3. **CFD Analysis** — hot/cold aisle containment optimisation
4. **Tier Certification Support** — Uptime Institute Tier III/IV preparation
5. **DCIM Integration Design** — real-time monitoring of power, cooling, IT assets
6. **AI / High-Density Compute Design** — liquid/immersion cooling for 30–130 kW racks (NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 scale)
7. **SCIF-Compliant Design** — for government/classified compute facilities
8. **Staff Augmentation / Embedded Advisory** (EYP/Ramboll) — skilled personnel embedded in client teams

---

### Competitive Positioning

- **Arup**: Owner-side advisory, masterplanning, reference design for portfolio clients, operational optimisation
- **WSP/kW MCE**: 100% DC-focused CoE; MEP engineering, power/cooling analytics, speed-to-market; 1,000+ DC projects
- **Ramboll/EYP MCF**: 70M+ sq. ft. designed; detailed engineering design, commissioning/IST, energy/sustainability
- **Sweco**: European generalist; cross-Nordic specialist networks; feasibility through commissioning
