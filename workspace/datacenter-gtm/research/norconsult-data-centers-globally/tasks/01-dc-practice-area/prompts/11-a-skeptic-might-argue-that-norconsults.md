# Prompt 11: a-skeptic-might-argue-that-norconsults

> **Prompt:** A skeptic might argue that Norconsult's DC work is too fragmented or opportunistic to constitute a "practice" — what evidence would confirm or refute this?

---

## Does Norconsult Have a Real Data Center Practice?

### What a Credible Practice Would Look Like
Named practice group with dedicated leadership; 5+ publicly referenced client engagements across DC segments; published methodology/frameworks; active participation in DC industry bodies (DCD, Norsk Datasenter Industri); recurring work with the same clients; DC-specific hiring.

---

### Evidence Against Fragmentation (Arguments for a Real Practice)

**Confirmed DC Projects (4–5 verifiable):**

1. **Bulk Infrastructure — N01 Støleheia, Vennesla (2023–2026):** Full EPCM contract covering project management, design management, procurement, and construction management. Engineering two 420 kV/145 kV transformer stations, new 420 kV transmission lines, cable infrastructure — for what is described as the world's largest renewable-powered DC campus at 800,000 m². Multi-year delivery responsibility on a flagship hyperscale project, not a one-off advisory engagement.

2. **Statkraft/Google — Datasentral Telemark, Gromstul, Skien:** Norconsult prepared the regulatory plan and environmental impact assessment (EIA) for what became Google's Skien DC campus. Planning work on behalf of Statkraft as facilitating landowner, covering a 4 km² site. Different service type from Støleheia (planning/permitting vs. EPCM) — suggests multi-service capability.

3. **Statkraft — Project Vestfold (Sundland, Sandefjord):** Second regulatory planning engagement for Statkraft for a ~150,000 m² DC development. Same client, same service type — consistent with a repeatable service model.

4. **Aure Municipality/Salfjord:** Feasibility study examining DC co-location with land-based aquaculture (circular economy model). Smaller engagement but shows DC knowledge applied in novel contexts.

**Repeatable Elements:** Telemark and Vestfold projects (same client, same service type) show detectable pattern. Støleheia EPCM draws directly on their 420 kV transmission grid expertise — specialist engineering that transfers from their renewable energy practice.

---

### Evidence Supporting the Skeptic's View

**Narrow service scope:** Confirmed DC work clusters in only two areas — high-voltage power infrastructure and land-use planning/permitting. No publicly documented evidence of: data hall MEP design, IT infrastructure design, cooling system engineering, security/resilience design, or commissioning.

**No published DC methodology or framework:** Unlike Arup, WSP, or Ramboll/EYP, Norconsult has published no DC white papers, technical standards, or design frameworks. Their cited VDC/BIM methodology is not DC-specific.

**No confirmed DC industry association membership:** No evidence of membership in Norsk Datasenter Industri or participation at DCD, DatacenterDynamics events, BICSI, or Uptime Institute working groups.

**Client concentration risk:** Confirmed client base is narrow — Statkraft (as Google facilitator) for planning work, and Bulk Infrastructure for EPCM. Two clients, two service types.

**Positioning via adjacency, not specialism:** DC expertise is substantially derived from the renewable energy and transmission grid practice. The Støleheia engagement is arguably a large transmission grid project that happens to serve a DC client.

**No dedicated DC hiring signal:** Searches on LinkedIn and Finn.no for Norconsult DC-specific roles returned no results — work is staffed from existing industrial/energy engineering pools.

---

### Verdict

The skeptic is **partially right**. Norconsult has a demonstrable DC track record in two specific areas (grid/power infrastructure and planning/permitting) anchored by at least 4 distinct client engagements over 5+ years. The Støleheia EPCM is a genuine, high-complexity, multi-year DC engagement.

However, what Norconsult has is **not a full-spectrum DC practice** but rather the application of pre-existing transmission grid and planning expertise to DC clients. It lacks the hallmarks of an organized practice: published frameworks, industry body engagement, dedicated headcount, and coverage of the full DC engineering value chain.

**For Metier:** Norconsult competes credibly for DC grid connection and permitting mandates, but **does not compete directly** for DC project management advisory or owner's project management (OPM) services — Metier's natural territory.
