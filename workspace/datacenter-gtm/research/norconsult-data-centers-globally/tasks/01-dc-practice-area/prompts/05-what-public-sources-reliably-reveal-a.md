# Prompt 05: what-public-sources-reliably-reveal-a

> **Prompt:** What public sources reliably reveal a consultancy's internal structure — annual reports, LinkedIn org charts, press releases, award submissions, job postings? How do you use each source type effectively, and what are the reliability limitations?

---

## Public Sources for Mapping a Consultancy's Internal Structure (Norconsult-Specific)

### 1. Annual Reports and Investor Documents (Highest Reliability)

Norconsult ASA (listed Oslo Børs 2021) discloses six reporting segments: Norway Head Office, Norway Regions, Sweden, Denmark, Renewable Energy, and Digital/Technogarden. Also reveals three market verticals: Buildings & Architecture, Infrastructure, and Energy & Industry — each ~one-third of revenue. Data centers fall inside "Energy & Industry," not a standalone segment.

*Technique:* Read MD&A, not just financials. Look for named segment leaders, growth narratives, and vertical-specific language. Q4 2024 earnings and November 2025 Capital Markets Day (investor.norconsult.com + Cision) both confirm data centers as a growth driver within Industry — not an independent P&L unit.

*Limitation:* Segments reflect legal/financial reporting, not how delivery teams are organized.

---

### 2. Press Releases on Contract Wins (High Reliability, High Specificity)

Best source for inferring internal structure — names the discipline responsible and often quotes a named executive. When Norconsult won the EPCM contract for Bulk Infrastructure's N01 Datasentercampus at Støleheia, Vennesla (2023–2026, NOK 3B+ investment), the press release named **Sten-Ole Nilsen** as "konserndirektør Fornybar energi" (Group Director, Renewable Energy) — revealing DC EPCM sits under the Renewable Energy division, not a dedicated DC practice.

*Technique:* Search `site:norconsult.no aktuelt datasenter` and norskinfrastruktur.no, bygg.no, elmagasinet.no. Cross-check spokesperson's title to identify which internal unit owns the client relationship.

*Limitation:* PR quotes are chosen for external image; quoted director may be senior rather than operationally responsible.

---

### 3. Project Portfolio Pages (Moderate-High Reliability)

Norconsult.no hosts a project library (`/prosjekter/`). Three confirmed DC entries: Bulk/Støleheia EPCM, Datasentral Telemark (Gromstul, Skien) regulatory plan, and Project Vestfold/Datasentral Sundland (Sandefjord). Reveals two modes of engagement: early-phase advisory/permitting (Planning & Environment team) vs. late-phase EPCM execution (Renewable Energy / Teknikk og Industri).

*Technique:* Look for discipline tags (VVS, elektro, reguleringsplan, EPCM), geographic office assignments, contact emails.

*Limitation:* Marketing assets — often outdated, rarely name the PM or delivery team lead.

---

### 4. Job Postings (Moderate Reliability, Leading Indicator)

Norconsult career portals (norconsult.com/career, finn.no) show "Prosjektledere Fornybar energi og Industri" and roles within "Divisjon Teknikk og Industri — Hovedkontor" (~350 staff in HVAC, electro, energy, automation). A 2024 PEX posting explicitly listed "datasenter" alongside hydropower and grid projects as example mandates — confirming shared capacity, not dedicated practice.

*Technique:* Search finn.no for `norconsult datasenter` and `norconsult energi industri`. Read reporting line, office location, and project-type list in each posting.

*Limitation:* Postings reflect short-term gaps. Norconsult has ~20 open positions in early 2026 with no DC-specific roles, yet is actively delivering a NOK 3B+ EPCM contract — meaning existing staff are covering demand.

---

### 5. Norwegian Company Registries — Proff.no / Brreg.no (High for Legal Structure, Low for Practice)

Confirms: Norconsult ASA (org.nr. 963 865 724) is listed parent; Norconsult Norge AS (org.nr. 962 392 687) is primary operating entity; CEO Bård Sverre Hernes; board chair Egil Olav Hogna. 17 group entities, 7 direct subsidiaries. Largest shareholder: Bank of New York Mellon SA/NV (nominee holder for dispersed retail/institutional investors post-IPO — not PE-driven governance).

*Technique:* Use brreg.no "Roller" section to map board and management. Use proff.no subsidiary tree to identify legal entities (Norconsult International AS, Norconsult Digital AS).

*Limitation:* Registry shows legal entities, not delivery divisions. No DC-specific legal entity exists.

---

### 6. LinkedIn (Moderate Reliability, High Noise)

Cannot be fetched directly. Search snippets via Google: `site:linkedin.com/in "Norconsult" "datasenter" OR "data centre"`. Roy Nyheim appears as "Senior Prosjektleder" at Norconsult. Eirin Smestu appears as an engineer. **Absence of "Head of Data Centres" or "Practice Lead" title in any snippet** strongly corroborates: no standalone DC practice with a dedicated leader.

*Limitation:* LinkedIn self-descriptions lag real org changes by 6–18 months; over-represent seniority.

---

### Cross-Reference Triangulation (Key Finding)

Three signals converge to the same conclusion:
1. **Annual report** → data centers in "Energy & Industry," not Buildings
2. **Contract press release** → Renewable Energy division director named as responsible, not a DC practice head
3. **Job postings** → DC projects inside PEX/Teknikk og Industri unit alongside hydropower and grid work

**Conclusion:** Norconsult's DC capability is a **cross-disciplinary competence cluster within Renewable Energy / Industry** — not an independent practice with own P&L. The decision-maker for DC mandates is the Renewable Energy group director or a senior EPCM project director.

---

### Common Misleading Signals

- **Dedicated web page** (`/services/industry/data-centres/`) ≠ dedicated practice. Delivery team is shared with hydropower and industrial projects.
- **Quoted executive in press release** may be segment director, not day-to-day team lead.
- **Bank of New York Mellon as top shareholder** = nominee/custodial holding post-IPO, not PE pressure.
- **No open DC-specific job ads** ≠ no DC activity — could mean existing staff are deployed on a major live contract.
