> **Prompt:** Apply all of the above to the specific case of Norconsult AS — determine if Norconsult has a named data center practice, which division owns it, who leads it, and which countries it actively operates in. Is Norconsult a credible, organized DC competitor to Metier — or a generalist that occasionally touches DC projects?

---

## Application: Norconsult's DC Practice Area — Full Assessment

### 1. Does Norconsult Have a Named DC Practice?

**No.** Norconsult does not have a named, standalone data center practice with dedicated P&L, a named practice head, published DC methodology, or independent market-facing identity. This was confirmed through:

- Annual report segment structure (6 segments; none named "Data Centres" or "Mission Critical")
- Website taxonomy (DC is one of ~11 sub-sectors under Industry, not a top-level division)
- LinkedIn searches (zero "Head of Data Centres" or equivalent titles among ~7,200 employees)
- Finn.no job searches (no DC-specific role postings over 18+ months)
- Norsk Datasenter Industri membership list (Norconsult not listed)

**Confidence: High.**

---

### 2. Which Division Owns DC Work?

**A two-division split — formal home vs. actual delivery:**

| Layer | Division | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Formal service taxonomy** | Industry (`/services/industry/data-centres/`) | Website URL path; page content framing DC as "power-intensive industrial facility" |
| **Actual EPCM delivery** | Renewable Energy / PEX unit | Bulk/Støleheia press release attributed to Renewable Energy division; PEX unit (Project Execution) confirmed as delivery vehicle |
| **NOT the vehicle** | Norconsult Digital / Technogarden | Software/staffing subsidiaries; no DC engineering delivery role |

DC is positioned as a power-intensive industrial plant (Industry framing) but delivered by the EPCM team that also handles hydropower, wind, and transmission grid projects (Renewable Energy/PEX delivery).

### 3. Who Leads It?

**Key named individuals (confirmed from press releases and LinkedIn):**

- **Håkon Bergsodden** — *EVP Renewable Energy* (since February 2025) — segment owner; most senior accountable executive for DC EPCM
- **Halvor Haugsvold** — *SVP Project Execution and Business Development — Renewable Energy & Industry* — operational owner of PEX; most directly responsible for DC EPCM delivery
- Former: **Sten-Ole Nilsen** — *konserndirektør Fornybar energi* — quoted in Bulk/Støleheia press release; has since been titled EVP with broader remit

**No named "DC Practice Lead" exists.** The closest is Haugsvold as the SVP running the PEX unit that happens to deliver DC EPCM.

---

### 4. Which Countries Does Norconsult Actively Operate DC Work In?

**Confirmed: Norway only.**

All identified DC projects are in Norway:
- N01 Støleheia, Vennesla (Bulk Infrastructure EPCM — ongoing 2023–2026)
- Datasentral Telemark, Gromstul, Skien (Statkraft/Google — planning/permitting)
- Project Vestfold, Sundland, Sandefjord (Statkraft — planning/permitting)
- Aure (feasibility study)

**No evidence of DC work in Sweden, Denmark, or internationally.** Norconsult has offices in Sweden and Denmark but no confirmed DC projects there. Their Renewable Energy division does operate internationally (hydropower in Africa, offshore wind studies) but no DC work outside Norway is documented.

**Market context:** Norway's DC construction market was valued at USD 3.04B in 2025, growing to USD 9.68B by 2031 (21% CAGR). New hyperscale entrants: OpenAI Stargate Norway (230 MW near Narvik), Google Skien (~EUR 600M hyperscale), Microsoft/Nscale Narvik. Norconsult has not been publicly confirmed on any of these next-wave projects, but their Bulk/Støleheia reference positions them credibly for future grid connection and EPCM mandates.

---

### 5. Is Norconsult a Credible, Organized DC Competitor to Metier?

**Verdict: Not a direct competitor — a complementary player at a different value-chain layer.**

**Where Norconsult is credible and organized:**
- High-voltage power grid infrastructure (420 kV transmission engineering, transformer station design)
- EPCM contracting for large industrial/energy-adjacent DC campuses
- Regulatory planning and permitting (land-use, EIA) for DC sites in Norway
- Multi-discipline engineering design for power-intensive facilities

**Where Norconsult is not organized and not competing:**
- Owner's project management (OPM) / owner's representative services
- Independent PMO advisory (separate from EPCM design authority)
- Milestone governance, investor reporting, programme controls
- DC-specific commissioning oversight (CxA)
- IT infrastructure and MEP systems integration inside the data hall

**Metier's competitive exposure:** Near zero for Norconsult's core DC offering (grid/EPCM). Norconsult may bundle basic project management within an EPCM contract — this is the only overlap zone. But Norconsult's EPCM PM is integrated with their design authority, whereas Metier's PM advisory is independent of design. These are structurally different propositions.

**Metier's opportunity with Norconsult:** Potential co-existence on the same project — Norconsult as EPCM engineer for grid/power infrastructure, Metier as the DC operator's owner-side PM/PMO. This is the Skygard/Bulk-type project model where the operator benefits from having an independent PM layer above the engineering EPCM contractor.

### 6. Classification

**Norconsult's DC practice status: Early Aggregation / Embedded Capability**

Using the practice maturity framework:
- ❌ Named practice with dedicated P&L
- ❌ Named practice head with market-facing mandate
- ❌ Published DC methodology or white papers
- ❌ DC industry association membership
- ✅ Real DC project references (4–5 confirmed engagements)
- ✅ Specialist engineering capability (420 kV transmission, industrial EPCM)
- ✅ Marquee anchor client (Bulk Infrastructure)
- ✅ DC listed as explicit service sub-sector on website
- ⚠️ Shared staffing with Renewable Energy / Industry teams (no dedicated headcount)

**Overall: Tier 3 — Embedded generalist with real DC references, not a specialist practice.**
