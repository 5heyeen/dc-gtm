# Socratic Research Prompts: Norconsult DC Practice Area & Business Unit

> **Topic:** Identify Norconsult's data center business unit or practice area — named practice, owning division, key leaders, and geographic scope. For Metier's DC GTM competitive intelligence.
> **Use these prompts in sequence in any AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) for deep, structured research.**

---

## Principles

- `[independent]` How do large multidisciplinary engineering consultancies typically organise specialised practices like data centers — as standalone business units, centres of excellence, sector overlays, or informal communities of practice?
- `[independent]` What are the structural signals that a consultancy takes a practice area seriously vs. treating it opportunistically (e.g., dedicated P&L, named leadership, published white papers, dedicated hiring)?
- `[independent]` How does a Nordic engineering consultancy's internal structure typically map to its external market-facing divisions? What names are commonly used?

## Framework

- `[independent]` What dimensions matter when mapping a competitor's practice area: division name, geographic ownership, headcount, leadership seniority, client base, and revenue attribution?
- `[independent]` What public sources reliably reveal a consultancy's internal structure — annual reports, LinkedIn org charts, press releases, award submissions, job postings?
- `[sequential]` Given the above source types, which are most likely to reveal whether Norconsult has a named DC practice vs. integrating DC into broader building technology, energy, or infrastructure divisions?

## Expert Simulation

- `[independent]` What would a competitive intelligence analyst ask first when trying to map a rival's practice area in a niche like data centers?
- `[sequential]` Given Norconsult is a large Norwegian consultancy (~5,000+ employees, operates across Nordics, Europe, and internationally), what hypotheses would an analyst form about where DC work likely sits in the org?
- `[sequential]` What LinkedIn search strings and job title patterns would an expert use to triangulate which division owns DC delivery at Norconsult?
- `[independent]` What do Norconsult's publicly named service lines and industry sectors reveal about how they segment their market? Is "data center" named explicitly?

## Failure Analysis

- `[independent]` What are common mistakes when trying to map a competitor's internal practice structure from public sources?
- `[independent]` What false signals might suggest a named DC practice when none exists (e.g., individual employees who happen to work on DC projects)?
- `[sequential]` If the research finds no named DC practice at Norconsult, what alternative interpretations are possible — and how would you distinguish them from genuine absence?

## Objection Pre-Empt

- `[independent]` A skeptic might argue that Norconsult's DC work is too fragmented or opportunistic to constitute a "practice" — what evidence would confirm or refute this?
- `[independent]` What if Norconsult's DC capabilities sit within a non-obvious division (e.g., "Industry & Energy" or "Digital Infrastructure") — how would you surface that?
