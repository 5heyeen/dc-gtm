## 1. How the Acquisition Changes Everything

**The "independent owner's advisor" positioning — the central finding of subtasks 01–06 — is structurally foreclosed on any project where Norconsult holds or bids an EPCM, design, or technical advisory role.**

Metier is now a wholly-owned Norconsult subsidiary. Legal separation (separate brand, separate entity) does not equal commercial independence. Sophisticated clients — hyperscalers, PE-backed operators, institutional lenders appointing a Lender's Independent Engineer — will identify the group-level conflict immediately via Brønnøysund/Euronext filings.

**Affected projects (Norconsult conflicts that close Metier's independent advisory path):**
- Bulk Infrastructure N01 (Norconsult holds EPCM) → Metier cannot serve as independent owner's advisor
- Google/Statkraft Telemark (Norconsult holds regulatory planning) → closed
- Statkraft Project Vestfold (Norconsult holds regulatory planning) → closed
- Any future project where Norconsult bids EPCM or technical design → requires active conflict check

## 2. What the Combined Group Has vs. What Remains Open

**Combined Norconsult + Metier strengths:**
- HV grid/power (420kV) + EPCM execution (Norconsult) + PM methodology and governance (Metier)
- Largest Norwegian engineering firm with PM advisory overlay
- BREEAM, EU Taxonomy, CSRD sustainability capabilities
- UnoSys platform + Metier OEC education/certification offering

**Remaining group gaps (neither entity covers):**
- Commissioning / IST — no confirmed capability in either entity
- EN 50600 / Uptime Institute Tier certification expertise — absent
- MEP redundancy design (UPS, CRAC, Tier III/IV fault tolerance) — absent
- Credible **lender's Independent Engineer** role on projects with Norconsult EPCM involvement — structurally unavailable

## 3. Three Most Actionable Metier Moves

**Move 1 — Conflict-mapping sprint (immediate):**
Map all active Norconsult DC bids and mandates (Bulk N01, Telemark, Vestfold, Aure, pipeline). This becomes the exclusion list for Metier's independent advisory pipeline. Not doing this is a reputational risk — sophisticated clients will discover the conflict independently.

**Move 2 — Target greenfield operators with no Norconsult touchpoint (0–6 months):**
Operators not yet engaged with Norconsult: incoming hyperscaler supply chain partners, new Nordic co-location entrants, Icelandic/Finnish operators expanding into Norway. These are clean-slate independent advisory opportunities. Metier's PM methodology, OEC, and programme governance framing are genuinely differentiated here — the Norconsult generalist model does not serve this segment well.

**Move 3 — Build commissioning/lender advisory capability (6–12 months):**
The combined group has no IST/commissioning capability and no credible Lender's Independent Engineer track record for DC assets. For future Norwegian DC financing rounds where Norconsult is not the EPCM contractor, a ring-fenced Metier lender advisory offering — potentially via partnership with a UK/US commissioning specialist — is the highest-value long-term positioning move. Requires legal ring-fencing from Norconsult group and careful conflict governance.

---

## 4. Two Viable GTM Postures

**Posture A — Group Synergy (internal cross-sell):**
Metier as PM/governance layer above Norconsult's technical DC work. Pitch: "Norconsult brings HV, grid, MEP feasibility, permits; Metier brings structured owner's programme management above the design/EPCM layer." Works where client is comfortable with integrated group delivery. Leans into single-group accountability rather than claiming independence.

**Posture B — Selective Independence (conflict-free projects):**
On projects where Norconsult has no role, Metier can still claim functional advisory independence. Requires active pipeline hygiene — tracking Norconsult bids and mandates as an exclusion list. Defensible but requires explicit governance to remain credible.

**What no longer works:** Positioning Metier *against* Norconsult as the "independent alternative to EPCM-integrated advice." Structurally unavailable on any project with Norconsult technical involvement.
