## 1. Norwegian DC Engineering Market Overview

Market size: ~USD 3.04B construction value in 2025; projected USD 9.68B by 2031 (CAGR ~21%). Main competing consultants for DC design/EPCM mandates: Norconsult, COWI, Sweco, Ramboll, WSP, Arup, Multiconsult.

---

## 2. Norconsult's Self-Claimed Differentiators

- **Industrial/power heritage** — "interdisciplinary expertise and experience from power-intensive industrial facilities and associated security requirements." Deliberate positioning vs. building-focused competitors.
- **Full value chain** — "studies to detailing and follow-up during construction."
- **Green/renewable** — "participation in several projects for the establishment of sustainable data centres."
- **Norwegian-local** — understands Norwegian grid, permitting, and NVE concession processes.

Evidenced by: Bulk Infrastructure N01 Støleheia EPCM (2023–2026) — largest DC EPCM contract in Norway, involving 420kV/145kV transformer stations and new HV lines for what Bulk markets as the world's largest renewable-powered DC campus.

## 4. Where Norconsult is Stronger

- **HV power and grid integration** — 420kV engineering beyond scope of building-focused competitors
- **EPCM delivery model** — offers full execution accountability, not just design advisory
- **Norway scale** — #1 Norwegian engineering firm (~7,200 employees); fastest to staff multidisciplinary DC project locally
- **vs. Multiconsult** — Multiconsult has pivoted toward defence/nuclear; no visible DC practice; effectively conceded the DC market

---

## 5. Where Norconsult is Weaker

- **International DC brand** — Ramboll's "300 DC specialists" and Arup's global profile are more credible to hyperscalers
- **Architecture/urban design** — COWI wins full-service urban DC briefs; Norconsult skews engineering/infrastructure
- **MEP interior design** — Sweco's Tier 1–4, UPS/backup positioning not matched by Norconsult
- **Commissioning/IST** — absent vs. Ramboll/EYP which pioneered DC commissioning methodology

---

## 6. Aas-Jakobsen Acquisition Impact (Aug 2025, NOK 1.43B)

Adds 230 structural/geotechnical engineers. Modest direct DC uplift — Aas-Jakobsen's focus is bridges and public infrastructure. Consolidates Norway structural engineering capacity, making it harder for rivals to staff comparable projects. Geotechnics credential useful for DC campuses in geologically complex Norwegian sites.

## 8. The Metier Acquisition — Strategic Implication (PENDING VERIFICATION)

If the December 2025 acquisition is confirmed: Norconsult now holds both the EPCM execution layer (Norconsult) AND the PM advisory/owner's engineer layer (Metier). This closes the structural gap that has been the central finding of this research — the independent advisory role Metier was positioned to fill above the EPCM contractor.

**Key tension:** Metier's value proposition as independent PM advisor is based on having no construction contracts at stake. Post-acquisition, Metier sits inside the same group as the EPCM contractor. Whether Metier can maintain credible independence — and whether DC clients will perceive it as independent — is the central strategic question for the combined entity's DC GTM.
