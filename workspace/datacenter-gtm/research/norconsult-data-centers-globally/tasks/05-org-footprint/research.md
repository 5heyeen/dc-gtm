# Subtask 05: Norconsult Organizational Footprint for DC Work — Research Synthesis

*Synthesised from agent research executed 2026-04-18.*

---

## 1. Two Divisions — No Named DC Practice

DC work sits across two divisions, not a unified practice:

**Divisjon Fornybar Energi / Renewable Energy (primary for EPCM/power)**
Home of the Norconsult Project Execution (PEX / EPCM) unit. Delivers large-scale HV power infrastructure and construction management contracts. Combined Renewable Energy + Industry headcount: **1,000+ employees** company-wide.
- Division head (from Feb 2025): **Håkon Bergsodden** (EVP Renewable Energy)
- Previous director (at time of Bulk N01 contract): **Sten-Ole Nilsen** (Group Director, Renewable Energy)

**Divisjon Teknikk og Industri (secondary — MEP/building systems)**
Hosts the norconsult.no/tjenester/industri/datasentre/ service page. ~370 staff at Sandvika HQ in HVAC, electrical, energy, and automation.
- Division head: **Henning Vellene** (SVP/Direktør Teknikk og Industri)

**Inference:** DC work is not managed as a single cross-disciplinary practice. RE division wins and delivers large EPCM contracts (power, grid, CM); T&I handles MEP building systems — structurally parallel to how Norconsult organizes industrial plant work.

## 2. No Named DC Practice Lead

No publicly identified DC practice lead or competence lead exists. Closest equivalents:

## 5. Key Organizational Events

**Pecom AS acquisition** — Norconsult acquired Pecom (Sunnfjord), an EPCM specialist, specifically to strengthen project execution capability. This introduced the **UnoSys** project/contract management tool now used on the Bulk N01 DC project. The Pecom acquisition is the organizational origin of Norconsult's PEX/EPCM unit.

**Aas-Jakobsen acquisition (Aug 2025, NOK 1.43B)** — structural engineering specialist. Deepens civil/structural capacity for large campus construction. Does not add MEP, commissioning, or DC-specific credentials.
