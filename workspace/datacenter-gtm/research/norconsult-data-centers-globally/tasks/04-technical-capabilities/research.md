## Strengths (Clearly Evidenced)

**Transmission-level power engineering (420kV)** — rare competency among consultants; critical path capability for large Norwegian hyperscale campuses where grid connection drives the timeline.

**UnoSys platform** — proprietary integrated EPCM execution tool covering contract management, document control, interface management, risk management, and systematic completion tracking. Gives Norconsult a digital EPCM backbone comparable to purpose-built construction management platforms.

**ISY software suite** (owned subsidiary Norconsult Informasjonssystemer / NoIS) — BIM-linked cost and document management. ISY Linker keeps BIM data and specifications in sync across project phases.

**BREEAM** — Norway's largest BREEAM practice (self-described); covers BREEAM-NOR, BREEAM In-Use, EU Taxonomy alignment, and CSRD reporting. Relevant as Norwegian DC operators face sustainability reporting obligations.

**Systematisk ferdigstillelse** — explicitly named in the Bulk N01 EPCM scope and listed as a standalone PEX competency. This is the Norwegian term for systematic commissioning/completion — closer to construction handover management than full IST, but a real credential.

---

## Gaps (Notable Absences)

**No Uptime Institute Tier methodology claim** — operators building certified Tier III/IV facilities may need to bring in an Uptime-accredited firm alongside Norconsult.

**No EN 50600 claim** — European DC standard for design, construction, and operations; not referenced anywhere.

**No CFD capability evidenced** — thermal and airflow modelling for DC cooling absent; Sweco (in-house CFD) and specialist firms have an advantage here.

**No DC MEP fit-out design references** — UPS, CRAC/CRAH, busbar, containment; primary reference (N01) is power supply and civil, not internal data hall MEP.

**No formal technology vendor partnerships** — no Schneider Electric, Vertiv, ABB, Uptime Institute, or cooling vendor relationships disclosed.

---

## Implication for Metier

Norconsult's technical toolkit is strongest in the civil, HV electrical, and EPCM execution layers. Their "systematic completion" capability (UnoSys + systematisk ferdigstillelse service) is the closest they come to commissioning — but it is construction handover management, not the IST (Integrated Systems Testing) that DC operators need for Tier certification. The absence of Uptime Institute methodology, EN 50600, and CFD creates specific technical gaps where Metier's independent advisory (working alongside specialist sub-contractors) adds value that Norconsult cannot self-supply.
