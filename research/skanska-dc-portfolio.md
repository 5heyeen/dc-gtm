# Skanska Global Data Center Portfolio Research

**Research date:** April 16, 2026
**Purpose:** Competitive intelligence — understanding Skanska as a construction contractor in the data center sector

## 1. Overview & Scale

Skanska is one of the world's largest construction and project development companies, headquartered in Stockholm, Sweden. Its data center construction business has become the **fastest-growing segment** within its construction division, driven primarily by AI-fueled demand for hyperscale infrastructure.

### Financial Scale
- **Group revenue (FY 2024):** SEK 177.2 billion (~USD 16–17 billion) — Source: [Skanska Year-end 2024](https://group.skanska.com/media/press-releases-articles/296658/Yearend-report,-January-December-2024)
- **Q4 2025 data center order intake alone:** ~SEK 9.5 billion (~USD 1.05 billion) in new DC projects in a single quarter — Source: [Construction Owners](https://www.constructionowners.com/news/skanska-eyes-ai-fueled-data-center-growth)
- **Full-year 2025 order backlog:** SEK 257.9 billion — Source: [Skanska Year-end 2025](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/skanska-year-end-report-januarydecember-2025-302681128.html)
- **FY2025 operating profit:** SEK 7.24 billion (+2.2% YoY) — Source: [IndexBox](https://www.indexbox.io/blog/skanska-2025-earnings-report-strong-growth-led-by-data-center-construction/)
- [INFERENCE] Data centers likely represent 15–25% of total US construction order flow by 2025, given the scale of disclosed DC contracts relative to reported US order bookings.

### Dedicated Data Center Division (US)
- **Skanska Advanced Technology (SAT):** Reorganized and expanded in September 2025, uniting mission-critical data center capabilities with semiconductor/cleanroom delivery
- **Headcount:** 285+ dedicated advanced technology, data center, and high-tech manufacturing professionals
- **Leadership:** Katie Coulson, EVP and General Manager, SAT

## 4. Delivery Models

Skanska uses several delivery models for data center work:

| Model | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Core & Shell (general contractor)** | Constructs the building envelope, foundations, civil works, access roads | Norway (Gromstul for WS Computing/Google) — NOK 1.7B total; Finland EUR 95M (announced 2025) |
| **Shell + Interior Fit-Out (integrated)** | Full data center building including shell and all technical interior spaces, data halls, support areas, office functions | USA $263M (Dec 2025); USA $256M (Nov 2025); Arizona $241M (2024); Georgia multiple contracts |
| **Tenant Improvement / Fit-Out within existing shell** | Interior buildout of data halls within a client-provided shell | Western USA $164M (2025) — 33,400 sqm, 36 MW, 4-phase TI; Virginia $105M + $58M (data hall fitout) |
| **Construction Management at Risk (CMAR)** | Construction management services with guaranteed maximum price | Commonwealth of Massachusetts Springfield data center |
| **Design-Build** | Full design responsibility plus construction | VIRTUS London11 (Slough) — "design and build of LONDON11 data centre"; Utah confidential client enterprise DC |
| **M&E / MEP Fit-Out (subcontract tier)** | Mechanical, electrical fit-out via Skanska Rashleigh Weatherfoil (UK subsidiary) | Telehouse South London — £158M M&E fit-out; Telehouse earlier fit-out contracts |
| **Developer-backed integrated delivery** | Skanska partners with developer, overlaps core/shell and fitout construction | Peak 10 / C&Y Partners Nashville |

**Key insight:** Skanska does NOT typically operate as an EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction) contractor in the energy/process industry sense. In data centers, they act as **principal contractor or construction manager**, with design typically led by the client or a specialist engineer. Design-build is used on some projects (notably UK colocation).

### Construction Approach — Modular & Prefabrication
Skanska increasingly uses skid-mounted MEP systems, precast concrete shells, and plug-and-play electrical rooms to accelerate data center delivery. This is positioned as a key differentiator for repeatability across multi-campus programs.
Source: [Skanska Advanced Technology press release](https://www.usa.skanska.com/who-we-are/media/press-releases/301891/Skanska-Advanced-Technology-Expands-to-Address-Data-Center-and-Semiconductor-Markets)

## 7. Key Observations & Competitive Position

### Strengths
1. **Repeat client relationships at scale:** The vast majority of US data center contracts are with "existing" or "repeat" clients, indicating deep embedded relationships with hyperscalers or large-scale DC developers. Multiple $200M+ contracts with the same undisclosed client in Arizona, Virginia, and Georgia suggest 1–2 very large hyperscaler relationships.
2. **Multi-geography capability:** Simultaneously active in USA, UK, Norway, Finland. Able to mobilize for large projects across markets.
3. **Full-spectrum delivery:** Can deliver groundworks, core & shell, full interior fitout, M&E fit-out (via SRW), and design-build.
4. **Dedicated advanced technology division:** SAT with 285+ specialists positions them as a focused player, not a generalist.
5. **Modular/prefab capability:** Increasingly important for hyperscale speed-to-power requirements.
6. **Nordic positioning:** Google engagement in Norway + Finland announcement signals credibility as a hyperscale contractor in the Nordics.

### Limitations / Gaps (relative to Metier's clients)
1. **Not a project manager / PMO / owner's representative:** Skanska is a general contractor and construction manager. They do not provide independent project advisory, owner's project management, or PMO services. This is Metier's core market.
2. **Client identity opaque:** Skanska rarely names its hyperscaler clients, making competitive intelligence on who they serve difficult.
3. **No disclosed data center projects in Sweden, Denmark, Poland, or CEE** — despite operating in these markets.
4. **Revenue contribution not disaggregated publicly:** Skanska does not break out data center revenue as a separate segment in financial reporting.

### Competitive Context (for Metier)
Skanska is a **potential client of Metier** (as a major construction company executing large DC projects in Norway and the Nordics that may need project management support), but is more accurately positioned as a **competitor to project delivery consultancies** only if they bundle PM services. For Metier, Skanska is primarily relevant as:
- A **market signal**: The scale of Skanska's DC investments in Norway (Google/WS Computing NOK 1.7B) and Finland (EUR 95M) confirms the Nordics as an active hyperscale construction market.
- A **reference point**: Skanska's disclosed project values and delivery models help benchmark Norwegian DC project complexity and scale.
