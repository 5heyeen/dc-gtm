# Advisory Firms & Investor-Grade Execution Confidence

---

## 1. PRE-INVESTMENT (Due Diligence Support)

**Role of PM/advisory during investor DD:**
- Independent project execution review — does the operator's build plan actually work?
- Cost estimate validation — are the CapEx numbers realistic for Norwegian market conditions?
- Schedule risk assessment — is the timeline achievable given grid lead times, contractor availability, permitting?
- Contractor market analysis — can the operator actually procure what they need in Norway's tight MEP market?
- Delivery strategy review — is the contract model (NS 8405/8407/FIDIC/samspill) appropriate?

**How firms like Turner & Townsend, Faithful+Gould, Linesight, and RLB position:**
- Turner & Townsend: "Independent cost and project management consultancy" — positions as the neutral third-party that investors trust. Global DC practice with project monitoring and cost management for institutional investors.
- Faithful+Gould (part of Atkins/SNC-Lavalin): Independent project and cost consultants. Positions as the "lender's friend" — their role is to protect capital.
- Linesight: Irish-origin firm with strong DC practice. Positions as end-to-end project delivery consultants with specific DC expertise.
- RLB (Rider Levett Bucknall): Global cost consultancy. Provides independent cost opinions that lenders and investors rely on.

**What "investor-grade" execution assessment looks like vs. standard PM plan:**

| Standard PM Plan | Investor-Grade Assessment |
|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Describes HOW to build | Assesses WHETHER the plan will work |
| Written by the operator's team | Written by an INDEPENDENT party |
| Presents one scenario | Presents scenarios (P50/P80/P90) |
| Focuses on execution | Focuses on RISK to financial returns |
| Uses project language | Uses INVESTMENT language (IRR impact) |
| Internal document | Designed for investment committee consumption |

## 2. INVESTMENT COMMITTEE SUPPORT

**Materials advisory team produces for IC:**
- Independent technical opinion on project feasibility and execution risk
- Cost estimate validation report with sensitivity analysis
- Schedule risk assessment with probabilistic outcomes (Monte Carlo simulation)
- Market analysis of contractor availability and pricing trends
- Regulatory and permitting risk assessment
- Comparison to benchmark projects (cost per MW, timeline benchmarks)

**"Comfort letters" and independent opinions:**
- Investors frequently require a formal letter from the technical advisor confirming the execution plan is credible
- Lenders always require an independent engineer's opinion before first drawdown
- The credibility of the advisory firm directly affects IC confidence — a report from Turner & Townsend or RLB carries weight that an unknown local firm's report does not

**Implication for Metier:** The Norconsult brand (Norway's largest engineering firm, publicly listed) provides the credibility anchor. Metier's Norwegian market knowledge provides the substance. Together, the IC sees an independent opinion from a national-scale firm with local expertise — stronger than either alone.

## 3. CONSTRUCTION MONITORING (Investor Oversight)

**What investors require:**
- Monthly independent construction monitoring reports
- Progress vs. plan (schedule, cost, quality)
- Identification of emerging risks and recommended mitigations
- Confirmation that milestone conditions for capital release are met
- Photographic evidence, site visit reports

**Independent monitor vs. operator's PM:**
- The operator's PM works FOR the operator and is incentivized to show progress
- The independent monitor works FOR the investor/lender and is incentivized to identify risk
- These are structurally different roles — both are needed, neither replaces the other
- Metier could serve either role, but the investor monitoring role is higher-margin and creates a structural client relationship with the capital provider (who does multiple deals)

**Milestone-based capital release:**
- PE funds typically don't fund 100% upfront — capital is released against construction milestones
- The independent monitor certifies that milestones are achieved
- This gives the monitor significant influence and ensures ongoing engagement throughout construction
- Typical milestones: foundation complete, structural frame, MEP rough-in, MEP commissioning, IT ready

## 4. RISK QUANTIFICATION

**Translating execution risk into investor language:**

The key shift: investors don't think in "risk register" terms. They think in financial impact terms.

| Project Manager Language | Investor Language |
|--------------------------|-------------------|
| "Schedule risk: medium" | "6-month delay erodes IRR by 400-800 bps" |
| "Cost overrun risk: high" | "20% overrun requires €50M additional equity, diluting returns by 35%" |
| "Contractor capacity risk" | "Norwegian MEP supply constraint adds 3-6 month procurement float, delay cost NOK 150M" |
| "Permitting timeline uncertain" | "NVE concession process adds 12-18 months of grid risk, carrying cost €2M/month" |

**Tools and methodologies:**
- **Monte Carlo simulation** — probabilistic cost and schedule modeling (P50, P80, P90 outcomes)
- **Earned Value Management (EVM)** — real-time cost and schedule performance tracking
- **AACE cost estimate classifications** — Class 5 (conceptual, ±50%) through Class 1 (detailed, ±10%)
- **P50/P80/P90** — the probability of completing within cost/schedule. Investors typically want P80 confidence.
