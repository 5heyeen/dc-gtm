## TIER 1: ACTIVE BUILD PROGRAMS IN NORWAY (Highest immediate opportunity)

### Google (Gromstul/Skien)

**Corporate Strategy in Norway:** Market entry for Google Cloud + AI workloads. First Norwegian facility. 240 MW Phase 1 with massive expansion potential on 2,000-decare site. €600M committed. Timeline: operational 2026. Norway fits as part of European DC portfolio (alongside Finland, Netherlands, Ireland, Denmark).

**Execution Model:** Global design standards localized for Norway. International design team with LOCAL adaptation required. Engaged Skanska (construction) and Martinsen & Duvholt (contractor). Norconsult already providing Norwegian standards adaptation (VA, structural, HVAC, electrical, geotechnics, hydrology). Google uses its own global PM playbook — the localization gap is the opportunity.

**Lifecycle Phase (March 2026):** Late construction → commissioning. Phase 1 nearing completion. **Next phase:** Phase 2 expansion planning (additional 120 MW to reach 240 MW total, then further expansion to 2030).

**Specific Pain Points:**
- Phase 2 expansion will repeat the localization challenge — international design adapted to Norwegian standards
- Grid infrastructure expansion for additional capacity beyond Phase 1
- Ongoing construction management and Norwegian contractor coordination
- Commissioning complexity at scale

**Persona:** Segment 2 — Global Operators entering Norway

**Consulting Gap:** Norconsult already embedded for engineering. Metier opportunity: expansion-phase PM, program governance if Google moves to multi-phase/multi-building program. The transition from single-build to campus program creates program management needs.

### Bulk Infrastructure (N01 Støleheia + expansion)

**Corporate Strategy:** Become Northern Europe's leading sustainable DC campus operator. N01 Campus targeting 400 MW by 2026, ambition for 1-2 GW. PE-backed (BGO €350M equity + €410M debt). Wholesale/colocation model serving hyperscalers and enterprise.

**Execution Model:** Norwegian operator with established delivery capability. Using Metier for project controls, execution framework, and board support. Using Norconsult for engineering (building physics, energy calculations) and grid connection (420 kV EPCM for two substations). Multi-site program emerging — N01 campus expansion plus potential Oslo-area sites.

**Lifecycle Phase:** Active construction (42 MW facility) + grid infrastructure build (substations). **Next phase:** 300 MW additional capacity in 2026, campus scale-up toward 1 GW.

**Specific Pain Points:**
- **Program-level governance** — transitioning from project-by-project to campus program management
- **Grid execution risk** — building two substations is a major parallel project (Norconsult doing EPCM, planned completion 2027)
- **Contractor capacity** — multiple simultaneous builds competing for Norwegian MEP contractors
- **Investor reporting** — BGO (PE) requires investor-grade reporting and governance
- **Cost control at scale** — keeping per-MW costs competitive as program scales

**Persona:** Segment 3 — Domestic Operators scaling (with Segment 1 elements given PE backing)

**Consulting Gap:** Metier ALREADY engaged — opportunity is to EXPAND scope from project controls to full program management as Bulk scales. The BGO investor relationship also opens the door for investor-grade advisory services (Prompt 5H territory). Norconsult grid EPCM is a deep, multi-year engagement.

### Green Mountain (Multi-site program)

**Corporate Strategy:** Norway's largest DC operator by installed capacity. Multi-site portfolio (Rjukan, Enebakk, Hamar + London). TikTok at Hamar (90 MW, option for 150 MW) is the flagship. Diversified tenant base. Growth through campus expansion at existing sites.

**Execution Model:** Norwegian operator with strong local knowledge. Uses Norwegian contractors. Metier providing PM. Norconsult providing engineering across sites (Rjukan: avalanche/electrical; Enebakk: electrical QA; Hamar: construction management). Established delivery processes.

**Lifecycle Phase:** Operational across sites. Enebakk Phase 1 completing Q1 2025, Phase 2 next. Hamar — TikTok expansion to 150 MW potential. Rjukan — ongoing operations with potential expansion. **Next phase:** Multi-site expansion program, new tenant acquisition.

**Specific Pain Points:**
- **Program management** — managing parallel construction across 3+ sites simultaneously
- **Tenant diversification** — high concentration on TikTok at Hamar creates risk
- **Rjukan expansion** — unique mountain facility requires specialized engineering (Norconsult already doing avalanche protection)
- **Attracting new hyperscale tenants** — need to demonstrate program delivery capability
- **Operational excellence** — commissioning, handover, and operational readiness as sites come online

**Persona:** Segment 3 — Domestic Operators scaling

**Consulting Gap:** Metier ALREADY engaged for PM. Expansion opportunity: program-level governance across sites, standardized delivery framework (capture learning from Hamar build for future sites), contractor strategy at program level. Norconsult deepening engineering across all sites.

### OpenAI / Nscale / Aker (Stargate Norway — Narvik + Korgen)

**Corporate Strategy:** AI-specific gigafactory. 100,000 NVIDIA GPUs by end 2026 at Narvik. 230 MW expandable to 520 MW. Korgen site for additional 250 MW. This is a strategic national-level project with Norwegian government visibility. Aker brings Norwegian industrial/political capital. Nscale brings AI infrastructure expertise.

**Execution Model:** JV structure (50/50 Nscale-Aker). ~$1B committed to initial 20 MW phase. Nscale designs and builds. Aker provides Norwegian industrial infrastructure expertise. OpenAI is anchor customer. Microsoft involvement ($6.2B deal). Northern Norway locations — different contractor market than southern Norway.
- **Contract model:** Likely bespoke given the JV structure. Aker has extensive experience with Norwegian construction contracts.
- **International design team + Norwegian execution** — the same localization pattern as Google but with AI-specific requirements (liquid cooling, extreme power density).

**Lifecycle Phase:** Early construction (Narvik). Pre-development (Korgen — awaiting Statnett allocation). **Next 6-12 months:** Narvik ramp-up, Korgen grid allocation decision, GPU deployment timeline pressure.

**Specific Pain Points:**
- **Extreme timeline pressure** — GPU delivery schedules drive construction timeline. AI compute demand doesn't wait.
- **AI infrastructure complexity** — liquid cooling, extreme power density (100+ kW/rack), Nvidia reference designs
- **Northern Norway execution** — different contractor market, logistics challenges, limited local MEP capacity
- **Grid execution** — Korgen awaiting Statnett allocation. Narvik grid capacity needs confirmation for full 520 MW.
- **Scale and speed simultaneously** — building the largest AI facility in Europe in Northern Norway, a region with limited DC construction experience

**Persona:** Segment 9 — AI/HPC specialists (primary), Segment 1 elements (Aker as financial sponsor)

**Consulting Gap:** Aker has Norwegian infrastructure PM capability in-house, but AI DC is new for them. Potential for Metier to provide DC-specific project controls and governance complementing Aker's industrial expertise. Norconsult opportunity: grid connection, environmental assessments for Northern Norway sites, Norwegian building code compliance for AI-specific designs. Key question: does the Aker-Nscale JV hire external PM or keep it in-house?

### Bitdeer (Tydal)

**Corporate Strategy:** Converting 175 MW Bitcoin mining facility to 180 MW AI data center (colocation for Nvidia Vera Rubin). Public company (BTDR). Targeting Q4 2026 completion. Engaged Data Center Installations (DCI) for development.

**Execution Model:** Brownfield conversion — existing energized site being repurposed. DCI as development partner. This is a conversion project, not greenfield — different risk profile.

**Lifecycle Phase:** Active conversion. Mining operations ongoing while AI DC construction proceeds. **Next phase:** AI DC commissioning Q4 2026.

**Specific Pain Points:**
- **Building while operating** — zero-downtime conversion challenges
- **AI-specific requirements** — Nvidia Vera Rubin reference design, liquid cooling, extreme density
- **Norwegian contractor adaptation** — DCI is the developer, but local Norwegian contractors needed for execution
- **Phased transition** — managing the mining-to-AI transition without disrupting revenue

**Persona:** Segment 8 — Brownfield expansions (primary), Segment 9 — AI/HPC (secondary)

**Consulting Gap:** Norwegian regulatory compliance for the conversion (building permits, electrical code changes), construction management for the phased transition, cost control for the conversion project. Norconsult opportunity: electrical engineering for the power system conversion, building physics for changed cooling requirements.

### TikTok / Bytedance (Green Mountain Hamar)

**Corporate Strategy:** European data sovereignty (Project Clover). All 3 buildings operational as of 2025. 90 MW used, option for 150 MW expansion. Data migration from other locations to Norway ongoing.

**Execution Model:** Green Mountain provides the facility. TikTok's international team manages the IT deployment. Norconsult previously provided construction management and stakeholder communication.

**Lifecycle Phase:** Operational (Phase 1). **Next phase:** Potential expansion to 150 MW (2 additional buildings).

**Specific Pain Points:**
- **Expansion decision** — will TikTok exercise the 150 MW option? Depends on regulatory/political environment (TikTok bans, data sovereignty requirements)
- **Waste heat utilization** — TikTok exploring waste heat reuse for local community
- **Geopolitical risk** — TikTok/Bytedance faces political headwinds globally that could affect Norwegian expansion

**Persona:** Segment 2 — Global Operators

**Consulting Gap:** Expansion-phase PM and engineering if 150 MW option exercised. Norconsult already has the relationship from Phase 1.

### Lefdal Mine Data Center (3i Infrastructure)

**Corporate Strategy:** Expanding from 37 MW operational to full 200 MW potential across 6 mine levels. New PE owner (3i Infrastructure, €300M) arriving summer 2026. This ownership transition will likely trigger accelerated development plans.

**Execution Model:** Unique mine conversion model. PE-driven (3i will want to deploy capital and realize returns within fund timeline). Norconsult has deep existing relationship (concessions, structures, geotechnics).

**Lifecycle Phase:** Operational (Level 1) + construction (43 MW). **Next phase:** New ownership → new expansion plan → potential rapid scale-up of remaining 5 mine levels.

**Specific Pain Points:**
- **New owner onboarding** — 3i will want execution plan, cost estimates, and delivery governance for expansion
- **Mine-specific engineering** — rock reinforcement, ventilation, underground construction is unique
- **Scaling from 37 MW to 200 MW** — massive expansion in unconventional facility
- **Investor-grade governance** — 3i as institutional PE investor will require professional reporting and governance

**Persona:** Segment 1 — Capital-led Developers ("Finansgutta") given PE ownership

**Consulting Gap:** HIGH opportunity. New PE owner will need: independent execution readiness assessment, expansion plan validation, cost estimate review, delivery governance framework, and potentially ongoing PM during construction. This is exactly the pre-FID and investor advisory play from Prompts 1D and 5H. Norconsult's existing deep relationship is the entry point.
